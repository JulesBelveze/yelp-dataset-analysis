Lawrence''' (born October 18, 1954) is an American politician and member of the [[United States Democratic Party|Democratic Party]] who served as the [[Mayor]] of [[Southfield, Michigan]] from 2001 to 2015. The Democratic nominee for [[Oakland County, Michigan|Oakland County]] [[County executive|Executive]] in 2008 and for [[Lieutenant Governor of Michigan|Lieutenant Governor]] in [[Michigan gubernatorial election, 2010|2010]], she was elected U.S. Representative for [[Michigan's 14th congressional district]] after winning the seat in the [[United States House of Representatives elections in Michigan, 2014|2014 elections]].\n\n==Early life, education, and early political career==\nLawrence grew up in Detroit's northeast side, on Lumpkin Street. She was raised by her grandparents after her mother died when she was three years old.<ref>Detroit News, 6/3/2008, Lawrence embraces challenge as Southfield mayor takes on 'iconic' Patterson</ref> She graduated from Detroit's [[Pershing High School]], and earned her [[bachelor's degree]] in Public Administration from [[Central Michigan University]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sentinel-standard.com/article/20100827/NEWS/308279995|title=AP Source: Bernero picks Lawrence as running mate|work=[[Ionia Sentinel-Standard]]|accessdate=4 February 2016}}</ref>\n\nIn the early 1990s, as an active member of the [[Parent Teacher's Association]] at her children's school, she sought and earned a seat on the Southfield Public Schools [[Board of Education]], serving as president, vice president, and secretary.\n\n==Southfield politics==\nIn 1997, she was elected to serve on Southfield's City Council and in 1999 she was elected council president.\n\nIn 2001, she defeated longtime Southfield Mayor Donald Fracassi, becoming the city's first [[African-American]] and first female Mayor. She was re-elected in 2005 without opposition.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=755371|title=Our Campaigns - Southfield, MI Mayor Race - Nov 08, 2005|publisher=ourcampaigns.com|accessdate=4 February 2016}}</ref> As Mayor, she was invited by the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. House]] Oversight Committee in 2008 to represent United States mayors in testimony about the mortgage crisis and its effect on American communities.<ref>[http://oversight.house.gov/story.asp?ID=1762 House Oversight Committee, 3/7/2008 Committee Holds Hearing on CEO Pay and the Mortgage Crisis] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081126185512/http://oversight.house.gov/story.asp?ID=1762 |date=November 26, 2008 }}</ref>  She returned to [[Washington, D.C.|Washington]] later that year to lobby [[United States Congress|Congress]] for a bridge loan for the [[Big Three automobile manufacturers#United States and Canada|American auto industry]].<ref>[http://www.hometownlife.com/article/20081120/NEWS20/811200543/1037/NEWS20 Observer and Eccentric, 11/20/2008 Mayor speaks up for local auto suppliers]{{dead link|date=November 2016 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>\n\nLawrence was a [[Michigan]] delegate to the [[2004 Democratic National Convention]]. As a [[superdelegate]] at the [[2008 Democratic National Convention]], she endorsed [[U.S. Senator]] [[Barack Obama]] for [[President of the United States|president]] in June 2008.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://brendalawrence.com|title=Brenda Lawrence for Congress|publisher=brendalawrence.com|accessdate=4 February 2016}}</ref>\n\nShe successfully sought a third term as mayor in 2009, defeating former Councilwoman Sylvia Jordan with nearly 80 percent of the vote.<ref name=\"ReferenceA\">Official Election Results Certified by the Southfield City Clerk</ref> She was re-elected to a fourth term unopposed in 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=791542|title=Our Campaigns - Southfield, MI Mayor Race - Nov 05, 2013|publisher=ourcampaigns.com|accessdate=4 February 2016}}</ref>\n\n==Campaigns for higher office==\n\n===2008 Oakland County Executive election===\nIn May 2008, Lawrence announced her candidacy for Oakland County Executive.  She was unopposed for the Democratic nomination to unseat the long-time [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] incumbent, [[L. Brooks Patterson]]. Patterson won re-election 58%-42%. Lawrence's challenge to the polarizing Patterson was identified as the strongest challenge he faced in his six elections for County Executive.<ref name=\"oakgov.com\">[http://www.oakgov.com/results_jp.html Election Results Certified by the Oakland County Clerk] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081218220215/http://www.oakgov.com/results_jp.html |date=December 18, 2008 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=690249|title=Our Campaigns - Oakland County Executive Race - Nov 04, 2008|publisher=ourcampaigns.com|accessdate=4 February 2016}}</ref>\n\n===2010 gubernatorial election===\n{{See also|Michigan gubernatorial election, 2010}}\nLansing Mayor [[Virg Bernero]] named Lawrence as his running mate in his bid for Michigan governor. She was formally nominated as the Democratic candidate for lieutenant governor at the Michigan Democratic Party convention in August 2010. They campaigned around the state promoting a 'Main Street Agenda' with emphasis on their shared backgrounds as mayors.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.freep.com/article/20100827/NEWS15/100827065/Bernero-picks-Brenda-Lawrence-as-running-mate |accessdate=August 31, 2010 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100830222532/http://www.freep.com/article/20100827/NEWS15/100827065/Bernero-picks-Brenda-Lawrence-as-running-mate |archivedate=August 30, 2010 }}</ref>\n\nAs with the national election results, the 2010 general election in Michigan saw strong turnout and enthusiasm by Republican voters. Political pundits attributed the losses by Democrats, in part, to voter reaction to Democratic President Barack Obama and term-limited Democratic Governor [[Jennifer Granholm]]. The Democratic gubernatorial ticket lost to Republican nominees [[Rick Snyder]], a businessman, and [[Brian Calley]], a State Representative, 58%-40%. No Democrats were elected statewide in 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=653125|title=Our Campaigns - MI Lieutenant Governor Race - Nov 02, 2010|publisher=|accessdate=4 February 2016}}</ref>\n\n===2012 Congressional election===\n{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Michigan, 2012#District 14}}\nIn late 2011, she announced she would be running in the newly redrawn [[Michigan's 14th congressional district|14th congressional district]]. She faced U.S. Representatives [[Hansen Clarke]] and [[Gary Peters (Michigan politician)|Gary Peters]], both of whom lived outside the district,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mlive.com/politics/index.ssf/2011/09/report_southfield_mayor_brenda.html|title=Report: Southfield Mayor Brenda Lawrence to run for Congress in Michigan's new-look 14th district|work=MLive.com|accessdate=4 February 2016}}</ref> and former State Representative Mary D. Waters. Peters won with 47%, Clarke took 35%, Lawrence 13% and Waters 3%. Peters went on to win the general election.\n\n===2014 Congressional election===\n{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Michigan, 2014#District 14}}\nIn May 2013, Peters announced that he would not be running for re-election in 2014. He instead [[United States Senate election in Michigan, 2014|ran for]] the U.S. Senate seat being vacated by retiring Democrat [[Carl Levin]]. On January 23, 2014, Lawrence announced that she would be running for the 14th district for the second time.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.detroitnews.com/article/20140123/OPINION01/301230003 |title=Southfield Mayor: Detroit, don't drop dead |work=Detroit News |date=January 23, 2014 |accessdate=January 24, 2014 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140202145558/http://www.detroitnews.com/article/20140123/OPINION01/301230003 |archivedate=February 2, 2014 |df= }}</ref>\n\nLawrence was the first candidate to submit signatures to the state in order to be on the August primary ballot, doing so in March. Other candidates that filed for the Democratic nomination were former Congressman Hansen Clarke of Detroit, State Representative Rudy Hobbs of Southfield and teacher Burgess D. Foster of Detroit.\n\nDuring the course of the campaign's contribution reporting, Hobbs raised a total of $607,806, Lawrence raised a total of $383,649 and Clarke raised a total of $173,124; Burgess reported no contributions to the Federal Election Commission, indicating that he raised or spent less than $5,000. Michigan Congressman Sander Levin's Political Action Committee, GOALPAC, also spent hundreds of thousands of dollars to help elect Hobbs, who was previously employed on Levin's congressional staff.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theoaklandpress.com/special-news-reports/20140808/winner-isnt-always-the-candidate-with-the-most-money|title=Winner isn\u2019t always the candidate with the most money|publisher=|accessdate=4 February 2016}}</ref>\n\nLawrence won the Democratic Party nomination on August 5, 2014, despite being outspent. Lawrence won with 36% of the vote, Hobbs took 32%, Clarke 31% and Foster 1%. She took the most votes in Oakland County, carrying the cities of Southfield, Pontiac and Oak Park, as well as Royal Oak Township. Although it was expected that Clarke would convincingly win the portion of Detroit within the district, where he lives and had previously held public office, Lawrence was competitive in the city and won more votes than all other candidates from voters who cast their ballot on Election Day in Detroit.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.freep.com/article/20140806/NEWS05/308060129/primary-election-michigan-lawrence-smith|title=It's official: Lawrence, Smith fend off challengers|work=Detroit Free Press|date=August 6, 2014|accessdate=August 6, 2014}}</ref>\n\nAs the Democratic nominee for Congress, she faced Republican nominee Christina Conyers of Detroit in the November general election. But Conyers withdrew from the race and Christina Barr of Pontiac was chosen as the Republican nominee.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.freep.com/article/20140912/NEWS06/309120188/ |accessdate=November 5, 2014 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140917051556/http://www.freep.com/article/20140912/NEWS06/309120188/ |archivedate=September 17, 2014 }}</ref> The district has a history of voting heavily for Democratic candidates. Also facing off against Lawrence in the November election was Libertarian Party nominee Leonard Schwartz of Oak Park and Green Party nominee Stephen Boyle of Detroit.<ref>http://www.oakgov.com/clerkrod/elections/Documents/2014_november_combined_list.pdf</ref> Lawrence won with 78% of the vote, Barr took 20%, Schwartz 1% and Boyle 1%.\n\n==U.S. House of Representatives==\n\n===Committee assignments===\n*'''[[United States House Committee on Oversight and Government Reform|Committee on Oversight and Government Reform]]'''\n**[[United States House Oversight Subcommittee on Interior|Subcommittee on Interior]] (Ranking Member)\n**[[United States House Oversight Subcommittee on National Security|Subcommittee on National Security]]\n*'''[[United States House Committee on Small Business|Committee on Small Business]]'''\n**[[United States House Small Business Subcommittee on Agriculture, Energy and Trade|Subcommittee on Agriculture, Energy and Trade]]\n**[[United States House Small Business Subcommittee on Contracting and Workforce|Subcommittee on Contracting and Workforce]]\n\n==Personal life==\nLawrence has been married to McArthur Lawrence for over 42 years. They were high school sweethearts, having met outside the Midway Market corner store where he worked on Detroit's east side. They bought their first home on Detroit's northwest side.  They have two children and a granddaughter.\n\nProfessionally, Lawrence worked for the federal government for 30 years in the United States Postal Service. She started as a letter carrier and later worked in human resources management; she retired in 2008.  Her husband is a [[United Auto Workers]] retiree from [[Ford Motor Company]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://brendalawrence.com/?q=node/2|title=Brenda Lawrence for Congress|publisher=|accessdate=4 February 2016}}</ref>\n\n==Electoral history==\n*'''2014 Election for Congress, Michigan 14'''\n{| class=\"wikitable\"\n|-\n!
