She is a member of the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]].\n\nThe district is based in [[Milwaukee]] and as a result of the 2011 [[redistricting]] also includes some nearby [[Milwaukee County, Wisconsin|Milwaukee County]] suburbs: [[Bayside, Wisconsin|Bayside]], [[Brown Deer, Wisconsin|Brown Deer]], [[Cudahy, Wisconsin|Cudahy]], [[Fox Point, Wisconsin|Fox Point]], [[Glendale, Wisconsin|Glendale]], [[St. Francis, Wisconsin|St. Francis]], [[South Milwaukee, Wisconsin|South Milwaukee]], [[West Milwaukee, Wisconsin|West Milwaukee]], [[Shorewood, Wisconsin|Shorewood]] and [[Whitefish Bay, Wisconsin|Whitefish Bay]]. She is the first woman to represent the district. She is also the second woman after [[Tammy Baldwin]] and the first [[African-American]] elected to Congress from Wisconsin.\n\n==Early life, education and career==\nMoore was born in [[Racine, Wisconsin|Racine]], but has spent most of her life in Milwaukee. She is the eighth of nine children; her father was a factory worker and her mother a public school teacher. Moore attended [[North Division High School (Milwaukee)|North Division High School]] and served as student council president.<ref name=\"jrank1\">{{cite web|url=http://biography.jrank.org/pages/2923/Moore-Gwen-S.html |title=Gwen S. Moore Biography |publisher=Biography.jrank.org |date= |accessdate=2012-05-28}}</ref> She later attended [[Marquette University]] and became a single mother and was for a while a [[Aid to Families with Dependent Children|welfare recipient]]. Nonetheless, she was able to earn a [[Bachelor of Arts]] degree in [[political science]], graduating in 1973.\n\nShe worked as an organizer with [[Volunteers in Service to America|Volunteers In Service to America]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nationalservice.gov/for_individuals/current/stories_detail.asp?tbl_stories_id=100 |title=AmeriCorps: Gwendolynne Moore |publisher=Corporation for National & Community Service |date= |accessdate=2012-05-28}}</ref> Through the program, she worked to establish the Cream City Community Development Credit Union to offer grants and loans to low-income residents to start businesses. For her work, she was awarded the national \u201cVISTA Volunteer of the Decade\u201d award from 1976 to 1986.<ref name=\"house1\">[http://gwenmoore.house.gov/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=17&Itemid=22 ] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110622221002/http://gwenmoore.house.gov/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=17&Itemid=22 |date=June 22, 2011 }}</ref> From 1985 to 1989, she worked for the City of Milwaukee as a neighborhood development strategist and for the state Department of Employment Relations and Health and Social Services. Moore also worked for the Wisconsin Housing and Economic Development Authority (WHEDA) as a housing officer.<ref name=\"jrank1\"/>\n\n==Wisconsin Legislature==\nMoore was elected to the [[Wisconsin Assembly]] in 1988 and served two terms. She was a prominent voice calling into an investigation into the case of sexual assault and serial killer [[Jeffrey Dahmer]], who lived two blocks away from Moore.<ref name=\"jrank1\"/>\n\nIn the election of 1992, Gwen Moore was elected to the [[Wisconsin State Senate]], in which she served the [[Wisconsin Senate, District 4|4th District]] from 1993 to 2005. Moore was the first [[African-American]] woman to be elected to the upper chamber of the Wisconsin legislature.<ref name=\"jrank1\"/> She became a prominent voice against mandatory ID security measures to enter the state capitol. She said  \"I am too often reminded [9/11 hijacker] Mohammed Atta had a photo ID. This will not tell people whether I am a terrorist. This disenfranchises people who come to their Capitol.\"<ref name=\"jrank1\"/>\n\n==U.S. House of Representatives==\n[[File:Gwen Moore, official 109th Congress photo.jpg|thumb|left|Congresswoman Gwen Moore]]\nMoore was elected to the [[United States House of Representatives]] in 2004, earning 69.6% of the vote and defeating [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] [[Lawyer|attorney]] Gerald Boyle in the general election. Moore was one of a handful of African-Americans to have been elected to Congress as freshmen in 2004, and she was the first [[African-American]] and second woman (after [[Tammy Baldwin]]) to represent Wisconsin in Congress.<ref>{{cite news|last=Sandler|first=Larry|title=Moore rewrites history: Mainstream appeal makes her state's first black congresswoman|url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=MWSB&p_theme=mwsb&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=10624A326F1A1F83&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM|newspaper=Milwaukee Journal Sentinel|date=November 3, 2004}}</ref>\n\nMoore is a prominent advocate for women\u2019s rights, releasing frequent statements on topics ranging from [[domestic abuse]] awareness to abortion rights. In January 2011, she was elected Democratic co-chair of the Congressional Women's Caucus to become a leader on health insurance reform and the protection of reproductive rights.<ref>{{cite news|title=Rep. Gwen Moore Weighs in on Birth Control Victory|url=http://msmagazine.com/blog/blog/2011/08/03/rep-gwen-moore-weighs-in-on-birth-control-victory/|newspaper=Ms. Magazine|date=August 3, 2011}}</ref>\n\nDuring the congressional debate in February 2011 on the Pence Amendment proposing to defund the health services organization Planned Parenthood, in response to comments from [[Paul Broun]] suggesting that Planned Parenthood promoted racist eugenics because more black women than white women have abortions, Moore spoke about her experience raising children on little money, and why \"''planned parenthood'' is healthy for women, it\u2019s healthy for children and it's healthy for our society\".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=j5GOCfpE4RQ |title=Rep. Gwen Moore (D-WI) In Opposition to the Pence Amendment |publisher=Youtube.com |date= |accessdate=2012-05-28}}</ref> She publicly opposed the investigation into the financial accounting of [[Planned Parenthood]], stating that the investigation \u201cis an unfortunate waste of taxpayer dollars.\u201d<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.votesmart.org/public-statement/642709/dem-leaders-to-stearns-stop-pointless-political-attack-on-planned-parenthood|title=Dem Leaders to Stearns: Stop Pointless Political Attack on Planned Parenthood|publisher=Project Vote Smart|date=October 11, 2011|accessdate=2012-05-28}}</ref> Moore voted \u201cnay\u201d on Amends Federal Health Care Law to Prohibit Abortion Coverage on October 13, 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.votesmart.org/bill/13922/36927/3457/amends-federal-health-care-law-to-prohibit-abortion-coverage|title=HR 358 - Amends Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act to Prohibit Abortion Coverage|publisher=Project Vote Smart|accessdate=2012-05-28}}</ref> In March 2012, during the House debate over re-authorizing the [[Violence Against Women Act]], she spoke about her own experience of being sexually assaulted and raped as a child and as an adult, criticizing the all-male Senate Judiciary Committee that voted no on the bill.<ref>{{cite web|first=Eric W.|last=Dolan|url=http://www.rawstory.com/rs/2012/03/28/rep-gwen-moore-recounts-being-raped-in-house-speech|title=In House speech, Rep. Gwen Moore recounts being raped|publisher=The Raw Story|date=March 28, 2012|accessdate=2012-05-28}}</ref>\n\nOver the first session of the [[109th Congress]], Moore earned 90% and higher legislative agenda approval scores from [[Planned Parenthood|Planned Parenthood of Wisconsin]], the [[National Association for the Advancement of Colored People]], the [[Sierra Club|Sierra Club of Wisconsin]], and the [[Service Employees International Union]]. Moore has focused herself legislatively on traditional Democratic and [[progressivism|progressive]] issues, believing that the [[Federal government of the United States|federal government]] should play a significant role in the amelioration of poverty and the resolution of difficult local problems. Moore has received support from interest groups ranging from the [[American Civil Liberties Union]] (93%), The [[Human Rights Campaign]] (100%), and The National Abortion and Reproductive Rights Action League ([[NARAL]]) (100%), to The National Farmer's Union (100%) and Defenders of Wildlife Action Fund (100%). She lacks support from those supporting hunting and sportsmen rights (0% support from Sportsmen and Animal Owner\u2019s Voting Alliance), pro-lifers (0% support from [[National Right to Life]]), and conservative tax reform stances (0% support from Americans for Tax Reform).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.votesmart.org/candidate/evaluations/3457/gwen-moore|title=Gwen Moore - Ratings and Endorsements|publisher=Project Vote Smart|accessdate=2012-05-28}}</ref>\n\nDuring her first term, Moore introduced legislation to provide economic incentives and [[tax cut]]s to small businesses to promote job creation, and also cosponsored legislation supporting community [[block grant]]s, continuing and expanding [[Medicaid]] funding, amending the [[Truth in Lending Act]] to prevent so-called \"[[predatory lending]],\" and removing troops from [[Iraq]]; Moore also cosponsored two prospective [[amendments]] to the [[United States Constitution|US Constitution]], providing for uniform national election standards and prohibiting gender discrimination under law.{{citation needed|date=May 2016}}\n\nOn May 6, 2006, Moore and eight fellow members of the [[Congressional Black Caucus]] were arrested and ticketed for [[unlawful assembly]] and [[disorderly conduct]] after they stepped onto the grounds of the [[Embassy]] of the [[Sudan]] to call attention to the ongoing [[Darfur conflict]] in Sudan. Moore said that the group expected ''ex ante'' to be arrested but that they were pleased to participate in a \"peaceful act of [[civil disobedience]]\".<ref>[http://www.jsonline.com/story/index.aspx?id=423968 JS Online: Moore expects arrest in protest] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070929132046/http://www.jsonline.com/story/index.aspx?id=423968 |date=September 29, 2007 }}</ref>\n\n===Committee assignments===\n*'''[[United States House Committee on the Budget|Committee on the Budget]]'''\n*'''[[United States House Committee on Financial Services|Committee on Financial Services]]'''\n**[[United States House Financial Services Subcommittee on Capital Markets and Government-Sponsored Enterprises|Subcommittee on Capital Markets and Government-Sponsored Enterprises]]\n**[[United States House Financial Services Subcommittee on International Monetary Policy and Trade|Subcommittee on International Monetary Policy and Trade]]\n**[[United States House Financial Services Subcommittee on Oversight and Investigations|Subcommittee on Oversight and Investigations]]\n\n==Electoral history==\n*'''2004 Race for U.S. House of Representatives \u2014 Democratic Primary'''\n**Gwen Moore (D), 64%\n**Matt Flynn (D), 25%\n**[[Tim Carpenter]] (D), 10%\n*'''2004 Race for U.S. House of Representatives \u2014 4th District'''\n**Gwen Moore (D), 70%\n**[[Gerald H.
