She is a member of the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]]. The district stretches from Kaptur's hometown of [[Toledo, Ohio|Toledo]] to [[Cleveland]].  It includes all of [[Ottawa County, Ohio|Ottawa]] and [[Erie County, Ohio|Erie]] counties, and parts of [[Lucas County, Ohio|Lucas]], [[Lorain County, Ohio|Lorain]] and [[Cuyahoga County, Ohio|Cuyahoga]] counties.\n\nServing her seventeenth term in the House of Representatives, Kaptur is the dean of Ohio's congressional delegation, the longest-serving woman in the House and the second longest-serving ever after [[Edith Nourse Rogers|Edith Rogers]]. In [[United States Congress|Congress]], she is tied for the second longest-serving woman with Senator [[Barbara Boxer]], behind Senator [[Barbara Mikulski]].<ref>{{Cite web|title = Sen. Kaptur started volunteering with the [[Ohio Democratic Party]] when she was 13.<ref>{{cite book|last1=Foerstel|first1=Karen|title=Biographical Dictionary of Congressional Women|date=1999|publisher=Greenwood Press|location=Westport, Connecticut|isbn=0-313-30290-1|page=141|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=A2CmFiw72okC&lpg=PA141&dq=%22marcy%20kaptur%22&pg=PA141#v=onepage&q&f=false|chapter=Marcy Kaptur}}</ref>\n\nKaptur graduated from [[St. Ursula Academy (Toledo, Ohio)|St. Ursula Academy]] in 1964 and became the first member in her family to attend college. She received her undergraduate degree from the [[University of Wisconsin\u2013Madison]] in 1968 and a [[Master of Urban Planning]] from the [[Taubman College of Architecture and Urban Planning|University of Michigan]] in 1974.<ref>{{cite web|author=[[Taubman College of Architecture and Urban Planning]] |url=http://taubmancollege.umich.edu/news_and_events/events/archives/2009-2010/?event=000000000000000000000000000000000000000003227876 |title=Lecture: Congresswoman Marcy Kaptur, An Urban Planner in Congress|accessdate=2011-10-10}}</ref> She did post-graduate study in urban planning at the [[Massachusetts Institute of Technology]] in 1981.<ref>{{cite book|last1=Schenken|first1=Suzanne O'Dea|title=From Suffrage to the Senate: An Encyclopedia of American Women in Politics|date=1999|publisher=ABC-Clio|location=Santa Barbara, California|isbn=0-87436-960-6|page=370|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Si6ZVqdOqIgC&lpg=PA370&dq=%22marcy%20kaptur%22&pg=PA370#v=onepage&q&f=false|chapter=Kaptur, Marcia (Marcy) Carolyn (b. 1946)}}</ref>\n\nKaptur served on the Toledo-[[Lucas County, Ohio|Lucas County]] Plan Commissions from 1969 to 1975. She was director of planning for the National Center for Urban Ethnic Affairs (1975\u20131977) founded by the late Msgr. [[Geno Baroni]]. She later served as a domestic policy advisor during President [[Jimmy Carter]]'s Administration.<ref>{{cite book|last1=Kouters|first1=Angela|title=Encyclopedia of Women and American defeated Weber 58%-39% in what was and still is considered an upset.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=37212 |title=OH District 9 Race |publisher=Our Campaigns Kaptur was instead challenged by Republican [[Rich Iott]], a [[Tea Party movement]] favorite. Kaptur won re-election to her 15th term with 59% of the vote,<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-srv/special/politics/election-results-2010/#/house/ |title=2010 Election: Live Results |work=The Washington Post |accessdate=2010-11-08}}</ref> which was her closest election since 1984.\n\n;2012\nFor her first three decades in Congress, Kaptur represented a compact district centered around Toledo.  Redistricting after the 2010 census extended the 9th District all the way to western [[Cleveland]].  The new map put the home of incumbent 10th District congressman [[Dennis Kucinich]] into the 9th as well, so they ran against each other in the Democratic primary. Graham Veysey, a small-business owner from Cleveland, also ran in the primary. Kaptur won the primary with 56% of the vote, while Kucinich received 40%.<ref>http://www.politico.com/2012-election/map/#/House/2012/Primary/OH</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=745617 |title=OH District 9 - D Primary Race |publisher=Our Campaigns |date=March 6, 2012 |accessdate=2012-08-30}}</ref> In the general election, she won a 16th term against Republican [[Joe the Plumber|Samuel \"Joe the Plumber\" Wurzelbacher]] and [[Libertarian Party (United States)|Libertarian]] Sean Stipe.<ref name=\"marcy kaptur coasts\">{{cite news |title=Marcy Kaptur coasts to win in 9th District congressional race |url=http://www.cleveland.com/sun/all/index.ssf/2012/11/marcie_kaptur_coasts_to_win_in.html |publisher=Sun News |first=Joe |last=Noga |date=November 7, 2012}}</ref>  However, the reconfigured 9th was no less Democratic than its predecessor, and Kaptur had effectively clinched reelection by defeating Kucinich in the primary.\n\n;2014\nKaptur's 2014 opponent was Richard May, a longtime Republican activist from west Cleveland, who beat Lakewood resident Robert C. Horrocks, Jr. in the May 6 primary.<ref>http://www.cleveland.com/open/index.ssf/2014/05/richard_may_of_cleveland_wins.html</ref> Kaptur won 68%-32%.\n\n===Tenure===\nKaptur is a member of the [[Congressional Progressive Caucus]]. She was once named \"Most Valuable Member\" of the House by ''[[The Nation]]''. In 1996, Kaptur was asked by [[Ross Perot]] to be his vice-presidential running mate. She eventually declined.<ref>{{cite web|author= |url=http://www.newsweek.com/id/103411/page/4 |title=Ross Reruns |work=Newsweek |date=November 18, 1996 |accessdate=2010-08-23}}</ref>\n\n====Patent reform====\nKaptur was a dedicated opponent of the [[America Invents Act]] that passed into law and changed the U.S. Patent System. Kaptur opposed changing from a \"first to invent system\" to a \"first to file system,\" claiming it hurt small businesses.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thehill.com/special-reports/patent-reform-june-2009 |title=Patent Reform |publisher=The Hill |date=June 2009 |accessdate=2012-08-30}}</ref> Kaptur stated \"Our patent system is the finest in the world... the proposed solutions are special fixes that benefit these few giants at the expense of everyone else.\"<ref>{{cite web|last=Kaptur |first=Marcy |url=http://thehill.com/special-reports/patent-reform-june-2009/47125-tech-giants-are-aiming-to-infringe |title=Tech giants are aiming to infringe |publisher=The Hill |date=June 22, 2009 |accessdate=2012-08-30}}</ref>\n\n====World War II Memorial====\nResponding to Roger Durbin, a [[World War II]] veteran and constituent, Kaptur first suggested the creation of a [[National World War II Memorial]] in [[Washington, D.C]].{{citation needed|date=April 2013}}\n\n[[Image:WWII Memorial Atlantic.jpg|thumb|The World War II National Memorial in Washington, D.C]]\n\nOn December 10, 1987, Kaptur introduced the World War II Memorial Act to the House of Representatives.  The legislation authorized the [[American Battle Monuments Commission]] to establish a World War II memorial, however, as the bill was not voted on before the end of the session it failed to be enacted into law. Kaptur introduced similar legislation in 1989 and 1991 but these bills also failed to become law.\n\nKaptur introduced legislation for the fourth time on January 27, 1993.  This time the legislation was voted on and passed in the House on May 10, 1993. After a companion bill was passed in the [[United States Senate]], President [[Bill Clinton]] signed the bill into law on May 25, 1993. Durbin died before the memorial was built, but Kaptur spoke at the memorial dedication ceremony, along with Durbin's granddaughter, on May 29, 2004.\n\nKaptur later said that she felt \"a great sense of fulfillment\" that the memorial was finally built.  \"This generation was the most unselfish America has ever seen,\" she said. \"They never asked anybody for anything in return.\"<ref>{{cite news| url=http://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9D02E7DD1F3EF933A05756C0A9629C8B63&sec=&spon=&pagewanted=print | work=The New York Times | first=Michael | last=Janofsky | accessdate=May 12, 2010 | title=Veterans Gather to Dedicate World War II Memorial | date=May 30, 2004}}</ref>\n\nOn April 12, 2011, Kaptur introduced H.R. 1489 to restore the [[Glass-Steagall Act]]. It states \"To repeal certain provisions of the [[Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act]] and revive the separation between commercial banking and the securities business, in the manner provided in the Banking Act of 1933, the so-called \"Glass-Steagall Act\", and for other purposes.\" There were 30 co-sponsors.\n<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.govtrack.us/congress/bill.xpd?bill=h112-1489|title=Return to Prudent Banking Act of 2011 (2011; 112th Congress H.R. 1489) - GovTrack.us|accessdate=10 April 2014}}</ref>\n\n====Abortion====\nKaptur holds a 95% approval rating from NARAL. She helped lead opposition to the [[North American Free Trade Agreement]], [[permanent normal trade relations]] for the People's Republic of China, and fast track authority for the president.\n\n====2008 economic crisis====\nMarcy Kaptur expressed strong opposition to the [[Emergency Economic Stabilization Act of 2008]], which provided a [[bailout]] for U.S. banks.<ref>[http://www.kaptur.house.gov/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=289&Itemid=1 THE LATEST REALITY GAME--WALL STREET BAILOUT] House of Representatives - September 22, 2008.</ref> Addressing [[Wall Street]] banks, she said, \"You have perpetrated the greatest financial crimes ever on this American Republic.\"\n\nShe also stated, \"America doesn't need to bail you out. It needs to secure real assets and property. Federal regional reserve banks should have a new job to help renegotiate . American people should get equity in any companies. Major job creation to rebuild our infrastructure. Regulate, we need a modern [[Glass\u2013Steagall Act]]. Refinancing must return a major share of profits to a new Social Security and Medicare lock box.\"\n\nKaptur's opposition to the [[Emergency Economic Stabilization Act of 2008]] was highlighted in [[Michael Moore]]'s 2009 documentary ''[[Capitalism: A Love Story]]''<ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.time.com/time/arts/article/0,8599,1920771,00.html | work=Time | title=Michael Moore's Capitalism Goes for Broke | date=September 6, 2009}}</ref>\n\n====The environment====\nMarcy Kaptur backed The [[American Clean Energy and Security Act]] in the U.S. House after she was able to insert an amendment that would authorize the Secretary of Energy to create power marketing authorities in regions where none exist. One such area would be the Great Lakes region. Kaptur said the St. Lawrence Seaway Development Corporation could serve as the vehicle for administering up to $3.5 billion in borrowing authority to stimulate economic development through creation of green energy such as solar power and wind power. Kaptur said the $3.5 billion in borrowing authority would promote \"regional equity\" and serve as a powerful engine for job creation in a region that has suffered from high energy costs, especially expensive electricity.\n\n==== Agriculture ====\nMarcy Kaptur helped to protect the rights of chicken farmers to speak up about mistreatment and unfair practices.
