From 2007 to 2013 she represented {{ushr|MA|5}}, the district her husband [[Paul Tsongas]] served prior to being elected to the [[United States Senate]]. She is a member of the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]]. Following [[John Kerry]]'s appointment as [[United States Secretary of State|Secretary of State]], she was widely expected to run in the [[United States Senate special election in Massachusetts, 2013|2013 special election]] for the Senate seat once held by her husband; she put such speculations to rest when she announced her endorsement of Representative [[Ed Markey]] instead.\n\n==Family, education, and career==\nTsongas was born '''Nicola Dickson Sauvage''' on April 26, 1946, in [[Chico, California]]. Her mother Marian Susan (n\u00e9e Wyman) was an artist and copywriter, and her father [[Colonel]] Russell Elmer Sauvage was an engineer in the [[United States Army Air Forces]] who survived the [[attack on Pearl Harbor]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bu.edu/phpbin/news-cms/news/?dept=634&id=56501|title=MA Congresswoman Niki Tsongas ('88) Running for Re-election|date=August 24, 2010|accessdate=January 11, 2013|work=[[Boston University School of Law]]|author=Elina Troshina}}</ref> Tsongas graduated in 1964 from Narimasu American High School in [[Japan]] while her father was stationed at Fuchu Air Force Base. Tsongas spent one year at [[Michigan State University]], then transferred to [[Smith College]] in [[Northampton, Massachusetts]], and graduated in 1968 with a [[Bachelor of Arts]] in [[religious studies|religion]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.womenspeecharchive.org/women/profile/index.cfm?ProfileID=250|title=Women Profiles: Niki Tsongas|accessdate=January 11, 2013|work=[[Iowa State University]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.usafa.edu/superintendent/pa/bov/style/support/bios/tsongas.html|title=The Honorable Niki Tsongas|accessdate=January 11, 2013|work=[[United States Air Force Academy]]}}</ref> After college she moved to [[New York City]], where she took a job as a social worker for the Department of Welfare.<ref name=\"about\">{{harvnb|Tsongas|2009}}.</ref> Tsongas earned her [[Juris Doctor]] from [[Boston University]] and started Lowell's first all-female law practice.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.telegram.com/article/20121102/COULTER01/111029917/0/COULTER02|title=Tsongas, Golnik compete in rematch|date=November 2, 2012|accessdate=January 11, 2013|author=Ken Cleveland|work=[[The Item]]}}</ref>\n\nTsongas interned in [[Arlington County, Virginia|Arlington, Virginia]], for [[Eugene McCarthy presidential campaign, 1968|presidential candidate]] [[Eugene McCarthy]] during summer 1967; at a party while there she met [[Paul Tsongas]], then an aide to Republican Congressman [[Frank B. Morse|Brad Morse]]. In 1969, she married Paul; they had three daughters: Ashley, Katina, and Molly.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2007/11/23/AR2007112301745.html|title=Mrs. Tsongas Comes to Washington|work=[[The Washington Post]]|date=November 24, 2007|accessdate=January 11, 2013|author=Sridhar Pappu}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.nytimes.com/1992/02/21/us/1992-campaign-man-paul-ethemios-tsongas-politician-who-thought-he-could.html?pagewanted=2|title=THE 1992 CAMPAIGN Man in the News: Paul Ethemios Tsongas; A Politician Who Thought He Could|date=February 21, 1992|accessdate=January 11, 2013|work=[[The New York Times]]|author=Karen De Witt}}</ref> A politician, Paul served in the House from {{ushr|MA|5}} from 1975 to 1979, and the [[United States Senate|Senate]] from 1979 to 1985. After being diagnosed with [[non-Hodgkin lymphoma]], Paul resigned from [[United States Congress|Congress]]. Tsongas moved their family from [[Washington, D.C.]], back to [[Massachusetts]] to care for Paul as he underwent treatments.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P2-7663123.html|title=NIKI TSONGAS STANDS BY HER MAN Paul Tsongas' wife says his cancer's the past, presidency is his future|work=[[The Boston Globe]]|date=June 4, 1991|accessdate=April 6, 2013|author=Carol Stocker}}</ref> After seemingly being cured of his disease, in 1992 Paul ran for the [[Democratic Party presidential primaries, 1992|Democratic Party nomination for President]]; he came in third behind former [[Governor of California|California Governor]] [[Jerry Brown]] and eventual winner [[Bill Clinton]]. Paul's cancer later returned; he died of [[pneumonia]] and [[liver failure]] on January 18, 1997.\n\nPrior to her election to the House, Tsongas worked as the Dean of External Affairs at [[Middlesex Community College (Massachusetts)|Middlesex Community College]],<ref name='about' /> as a Board Member of [[Fallon Health]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.achp.org/achp_news/niki-tsongas-board-member-of-fallon-community-health-plan-elected-to-congress/|title=Niki Tsongas, Board Member of Fallon Community Health Plan, Elected to Congress|date=October 24, 2007|accessdate=January 11, 2013|work=Alliance of Community Health Plans}}</ref> and served on the Lowell Civic Stadium and Arena Commission, which oversees several sites including the [[Paul E. Tsongas Center at UMass Lowell|Tsongas Arena]].<ref name=\"about\" /> In 2001, Representative [[Marty Meehan]] appointed Tsongas to head a foundation to provide education funding for children of the victims of the [[September 11 attacks]].<ref>{{cite news |first=Gloria |last=Negri |date=August 26, 2002 |url=http://pqasb.pqarchiver.com/boston/access/155340241.html?FMT=ABS |title=Scholarship fund helps 9/11 families |work=[[The Boston Globe]] |page=B3 |accessdate=September 26, 2010}}</ref>\n\n==U.S. House of Representatives==\n\n===Elections===\nAfter Marty Meehan resigned in 2007 to serve as Chancellor of the [[University of Massachusetts Lowell]], Tsongas ran in the [[Massachusetts's 5th congressional district special election, 2007|special election]]. Tsongas defeated four other candidates to win the Democratic primary with 36% of the vote.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.boston.com/news/local/articles/2007/09/05/tsongas_wins_primary_for_5th/?page=full|title=Tsongas wins primary for 5th|date=September 5, 2007|accessdate=January 11, 2013|author=Matt Viser and Eric Moskowitz|work=[[The Boston Globe]]}}</ref> During her initial campaign Tsongas received endorsements from ''[[The Boston Globe]]'', the ''[[Boston Herald]]'', and the ''[[The Sun (Lowell)|Lowell Sun]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nikitsongas.com/press/082807-GlobeandHerald.pdf|title=Niki Tsongas Endorsed by Boston Globe and Boston Herald|publisher=Niki Tsongas for Congress|accessdate=October 15, 2007|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20071013091644/http://nikitsongas.com/press/082807-GlobeandHerald.pdf|archivedate=October 13, 2007}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bluemassgroup.com/showDiary.do?diaryId=9007|title=Sun backs Tsongas | publisher=Blue Mass. Group | accessdate=2007-10-15}}</ref> During the general election, former [[President of the United States|President]] [[Bill Clinton]], who defeated her husband for the [[1992 Democratic National Convention|Democratic nomination in 1992]], campaigned for her. At an event in [[Lowell, Massachusetts|Lowell]] Clinton remarked: \"Congress will be a better place because she is there.\"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rollcall.com/issues/53_30/-20096-1.html|title=President Clinton Will Stump for Niki Tsongas|date=September 20, 2007|accessdate=March 19, 2013|author=Josh Kurtz|work=[[Roll Call]]}}</ref> Tsongas won the special election against Republican Jim Ogonowski with 51% of the vote on October 17;<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.boston.com/news/local/massachusetts/articles/2007/10/17/tsongas_wins_in_fifth_district/?page=full|title=Tsongas wins in Fifth District|date=October 17, 2007|accessdate=January 11, 2013|author=Eric Moskowitz|work=[[The Boston Globe]]}}</ref> she became the only female Representative from Massachusetts, and the first female representative from Massachusetts since the retirement of [[Margaret Heckler]] in 1983, who became [[Secretary of Health and Human Services]] under [[Ronald Reagan]].\n\nAfter running unopposed in 2008, in 2010 Tsongas faced Republican [[Jon Golnik]], a small businessman and former [[Wall Street]] currency trader. During the campaign Tsongas attacked Golnik's history as a Vice President of [[American International Group|AIG]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lowellsun.com/todaysheadlines/ci_16436062|title=Tsongas targets Golnik's work|author=Lyle Moran|date=October 26, 2010|accessdate=March 19, 2013|work=[[The Sun (Lowell)]]}}</ref> which Golnik called [[Hypocrisy|hypocritical]], as she had [[stock]] in AIG and other large corporations.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lowellsun.com/rss/ci_16415334|title=Golnik: Tsongas' former investments make her attacks 'hypocritical'|date=October 25, 2013|accessdate=April 5, 2013|author=Lyle Moran|work=[[The Sun (Lowell)]]}}</ref> Tsongas defeated Golnik with 52% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hgazette.com/local/x2073121697/Tsongas-defeats-Golnik-will-return-to-D-C-for-second-full-term/print|title=Tsongas defeats Golnik, will return to D.C. for second full term|date=November 4, 2010|accessdate=March 19, 2013|author=Ross Marrinson|work=[[Haverhill Gazette]]}}</ref> Following redistricting after the [[2010 United States Census|2010 census]], Tsongas ran for re-election in the reconfigured {{ushr|MA|3}} in 2012. In a rematch, she again defeated Golnick.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.eagletribune.com/election/x257825677/Tsongas-wins-over-Golnik-for-Congress|title=Tsongas wins over Golnik for Congress|date=November 6, 2012|accessdate=March 19, 2013|work=[[The Eagle-Tribune]]|author=Brian Messenger}}</ref>\n\n===Tenure===\n{{U.S. Congress committee assignments\n|congress = 114\n|ref      = <ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rollcall.com/members/26843.html|title=Rep. Upon returning, Tsongas spoke of the need for the involvement of women in rebuilding of government.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.boston.com/news/politics/politicalintelligence/2010/05/tsongas_returns.html|title=Tsongas returns from Afghanistan trip|author=Matt Viser|date=May 11, 2010|accessdate=January 13, 2013|work=The Boston Globe}}</ref>\n\nTsongas is an advocate for [[universal health care]] and supports a [[public health insurance option]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://bluemassgroup.com/2007/04/on-universal-health-care/|title=On Universal Health Care|date=April 25, 2007|accessdate=March 18, 2013|author=Niki Tsongas|work=Blue Mass Group}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wickedlocal.com/littleton/news/x1972890185/Rep-Tsongas-reports-to-district#axzz2Nr2HSfOg|title=Rep.
