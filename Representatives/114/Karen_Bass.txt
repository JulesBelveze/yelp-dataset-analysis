She represents [[California's 37th congressional district]] in the [[United States House of Representatives]]; she was first elected in 2010. In [[redistricting]] following the [[U.S. Census 2010|2010 census]], the district was renumbered from {{ushr|CA|33|33rd}} to {{ushr|CA|37|37th}}.  Bass represented [[California's 47th State Assembly district|the 47th district]] in the [[California State Assembly]] 2004\u20132010, and was [[Speaker of the California State Assembly]] 2008\u20132010 (second woman, third [[African American]] speaker).<ref name=\":0\">{{Cite news|title = L.A. woman to follow Nunez|url = http://articles.latimes.com/2008/feb/28/local/me-speaker28|newspaper = Los Angeles Times|date = 2008-02-28|access-date = 2015-12-21|issn = 0458-3035|language = en-US|first = Nancy|last = Vogel}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |title = African American Speakers of the California  |url = http://lasentinel.net/african-american-speakers-of-the-california.html|website = Los Angeles Sentinel|accessdate = 2015-12-21}}</ref>\n\n==Early life, education, and medical career==\nBass was born in Los Angeles, California, the daughter of Wilhelmina (n\u00e9e Duckett) and DeWitt Talmadge Bass.<ref>{{cite web\n |title=Karen Bass ancestry |accessdate=2013-10-01 |work=[[RootsWeb]]\n |url=http://freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.ancestry.com/%7Ebattle/reps/basskaren.htm}}</ref> Her father was a letter carrier.<ref name=\"Barone2014\"/>  She was raised in the Venice/Fairfax neighborhood and went to [[Alexander Hamilton High School (Los Angeles)|Hamilton High School]]. She studied [[philosophy]] at [[San Diego State University]] (1971\u20131973), then earned a B.S. in health sciences from [[California State University, Dominguez Hills]] (1990).<ref name=CQ>{{cite news |title=112th Congress: Karen Bass, D-Calif. (33rd District) |first=Kerry |last=Young |newspaper=[[Congressional Quarterly]] |date=November 6, 2010 |url=http://news.yahoo.com/s/cq/20101106/pl_cq_politics/politics000003759351_1 |accessdate=}}</ref>\n\nBass worked as a physician assistant and as a clinical instructor at the [[Keck School of Medicine of USC|USC Keck School of Medicine]] Physician Assistant Program.<ref name=\"about\">{{cite web|title=About Karen|url=http://karenbass.com/about-karen|publisher=KarenBass.com|accessdate=December 4, 2010}}</ref>  Bass founded the Community Coalition, an organization in South Los Angeles.<ref>{{cite web|title=About Us|publisher=Community Coalition|url=http://www.cocosouthla.org/about/ourmission|accessdate=2014-02-15}}</ref>\n\n==California Assembly==\nAs the [[California State Assembly|Assemblymember]] for the [[California's 47th State Assembly district|47th District]], Bass served the cities and communities of [[Culver City]], [[West Los Angeles]], [[Westwood, Los Angeles, California|Westwood]], [[Cheviot Hills]], [[Leimert Park]], [[Baldwin Hills]], [[View Park-Windsor Hills]], [[Ladera Heights]], the [[Crenshaw District]], [[Little Ethiopia]] and portions of [[Koreatown, Los Angeles, California|Koreatown]] and [[South Los Angeles]].{{citation needed|date=December 2015}}\n\nIn addition to her leadership of California African Americans for Obama and her post on [[Barack Obama]]'s national African American Leadership Council, Bass served as a California Co-chair of Obama's [[Barack Obama presidential campaign, 2008|2008 presidential campaign]].\n\n===Leadership prior to speaker election===\nSpeaker [[Fabian N\u00fa\u00f1ez]] appointed Bass [[California State Assembly]] Majority Whip (2005\u20132006), and Majority Floor Leader for 2007\u20132008 legislative session. She was chair of the Select Committee on Foster Care and vice chair of the Legislative Black Caucus. She succeeded N\u00fa\u00f1ez as Speaker on May 13, 2008; he was [[term limit|termed out]] of the Assembly November 30, 2008.<ref name=\":0\" />\n\nAs chair of the Legislative Black Caucus, she commissioned a report to research the basic demographic profile of Black Californians including the basic social and economic conditions.<ref>{{cite web|last=Bass|first=Karen|title=The State of Black California|url=http://www.asmdc.org/speaker/pdf/State_of_Black_California_Full_Report.pdf|work=February 2007|publisher=California Democratic Caucus|accessdate=11 September 2012}}</ref>  The State of Black California report included a statewide organizing effort to involve Black Californians in identifying their concerns and making legislative recommendations.<ref name=\"Karen Bass State of Blk CA\">{{cite web|last=Samad|first=Anthony  Asadullah|title=Between the lines|url=http://www.blackcommentator.com/216/216_between_the_lines_black_california_samad.html|work=8 February 2007|publisher=The Black Commentator|accessdate=11 September 2012}}</ref>\n\n===Speakership===\nWith the defeat of [[California Proposition 93 (2008)|Proposition 93]], Speaker [[Fabian N\u00fa\u00f1ez]] was termed out of the Assembly at the end of the 2007-2008 session. As the next-highest-ranking Democrat in the Assembly, Bass was well-positioned to take the post.  After consolidating the support of a number of Legislators who had previously also been seeking the Speakership, Bass was elected Speaker on February 28, 2008 and then sworn in as Speaker on May 13.<ref>{{cite news |first=Matthew |last=Yi |title=L.A. lawmaker first African American woman to lead state Assembly |url=http://www.sfgate.com/bayarea/article/L-A-lawmaker-first-African-American-woman-to-3226146.php |work=[[San Francisco Chronicle]] |date=29 February 2008 |accessdate=2008-03-01}}</ref>\n\nBass was criticized{{By whom|date=October 2013}} for the following statement to [[Los Angeles Times]] reporter [[Patt Morrison]]: \"The Republicans were essentially threatened and terrorized against voting for revenue. Now [some] are facing recalls. They operate under a terrorist threat: 'You vote for revenue and your career is over.' I don't know why we allow that kind of terrorism to exist. I guess it's about free speech, but it's extremely unfair.\"<ref>{{cite news\n   |authorlink=Patt Morrison |first=Patt |last=Morrison\n   |title=Madam Speaker: An interview with state Assembly Speaker Karen Bass.\n   |url=http://www.latimes.com/news/opinion/commentary/la-oe-morrisonbass27-2009jun27,0,4807376.story\n   |work=[[The Los Angeles Times]] |date=27 June 2009 |accessdate=2009-07-08 |archivedate=2009-06-30\n   |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090630040653/http://www.latimes.com/news/opinion/commentary/la-oe-morrisonbass27-2009jun27,0,4807376.story?}}</ref>\n\nSince leaving office, Bass was named Speaker Emeritus.<ref>{{cite web\n  |title=Speaker Emeritus Karen Bass |date=November 17, 2010 |publisher=California State Assembly Democratic Caucus\n  |accessdate=2010-10-01 |url=http://asmdc.org/members/a47/\n  |archivedate=2010-11-17 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20101117093913/http://asmdc.org/members/a47/}}</ref>\n\n==U.S. House of Representatives==\n\n===2010 election===\n{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in California, 2010#District 33}}\nBass was ineligible to run for reelection in 2010 due to term limits and on February 18, 2010, confirmed her candidacy to succeed retiring U.S. Representative [[Diane Watson]] in [[California's 33rd congressional district]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Merl|first=Jean|title=Karen Bass confirms candidacy for seat in Congress|publisher=|date=February 18, 2010|url=http://articles.latimes.com/2010/feb/18/local/la-me-bass-watson18-2010feb18|accessdate=March 3, 2010|work=Los Angeles Times}}</ref> Bass won the election with over 86% of the vote on November 2, 2010.<ref>{{cite news |last=Van Oot|first=Torey|title=Bass, Denham win seats in Congress |newspaper=[[The Sacramento Bee]] |date=November 3, 2010 |url=http://blogs.sacbee.com/capitolalertlatest/2010/11/nov3amatter---bass-denham-win.html|accessdate=2010-11-03}}</ref>\n\nBass raised $932,281.19 and spent $768,918.65. Her 2010 campaign contributions came from very different and diverse groups with none donating more than 15% of her total campaign funds. The five major donors to her campaign are Labor Unions with $101,950.00; Financial Institutions with $90,350.00; Health Professionals with $87,900.00; the Entertainment Industry with $52,400.00 and Lawyers and Law Firms with $48,650.00.<ref name=\"votesmart\" />\n\n===2012 election===\nIn 2012 she had no primary opponent, and carried the general election with 86%.<ref name=Barone2014/>  She raised $692,988.53 and spent $803,966.15, leaving $52,384.92 on hand and a debt of $3,297.59.<ref name=\"votesmart\">{{cite web |title=Representative Karen Bass' Campaign Finances -  Project Vote Smart |accessdate=2013-10-02 |url=http://www.votesmart.org/candidate/key-votes/28963/karen-bass}}</ref>\n\n===Committee assignments===\n* '''[[United States House Committee on the Judiciary|Committee on the Judiciary]]'''\n** [[United States House Judiciary Subcommittee on Courts, Intellectual Property and the Internet|Subcommittee on Courts, Intellectual Property and the Internet]]\n** [[United States House Judiciary Subcommittee on Crime, Terrorism, Homeland Security and Investigations|Subcommittee on Crime, Terrorism, Homeland Security and Investigations]]<ref name=Barone2014/>\n* '''[[United States House Committee on Foreign Affairs|Committee on Foreign Affairs]]'''\n** [[United States House Foreign Affairs Subcommittee on Africa, Global Health, and Human Rights|Subcommittee on Africa, Global Health, and Human Rights]] ([[Ranking Member]])<ref name=Barone2014/>\n<!-- **[[United States House Foreign Affairs Subcommittee on Oversight and Investigations|Subcommittee on Oversight and Investigations]] -->\n\n===Position on issues===\nBass is generally considered a liberal, with ratings of 100% or close from liberal interest group capitol Weekly Positions. Conservative groups like the California Republican Assembly Positions have consistently awarded her a 0%.<ref name=\"votesmart\" />\n\n===Guns===\nBass is a very strong supporter of gun control. The National Rifle Association Political Victory Fund Lifetime Score is an F. The Gun Owners of California Postitons on Gun Rights have also given Bass an F. Congresswoman Bass recently has voted against the National Right-to-Carry Reciprocity Act that would loosen general Gun Control laws. In 2010 while campaigning for Congress Bass supported Expanded Firearm Registration Bill that with other regulations would have made all gun dealers report their sales to the Department of Justice. Bass also supported the Amendment to the Penal Code regarding Firearms of 2010.<ref name=\"votesmart\" />\n\nCongresswoman Karen Bass voted once in a way that led to the claim that she favors the continuation of militarized local police forces, when in 2014 she voted against the Rep. Alan Grayson (D-FL) amendment that would have halted the Pentagon's 1033 program.<ref>{{cite web |title=Black Caucus member can't recall her vote on police militarization |author=Alice Salles |date=February 24, 2015 |publisher=Franklin Center for Government & Public Integrity |url=http://watchdog.org/202114/black-caucus-police-militarization/}}</ref>\n\n===International Policy/Foreign Affairs===\nBass serves in the Committee on Foreign Affairs. In her website, she supports \"working with our NATO allies and within the United Nations\". She also attributes significant importance to diplomacy and \u201ccreat[ing] jobs here in the US.\" Bass does so through supporting trade protectionism. She has voted against the Free Trade Treaty with South Korea and the Trade Promotion Agreements with Colombia and Panama.<ref name=\"votesmart\" /><ref>{{cite web|title=Foreign Policy|publisher=Karen Bass|url=http://karenbass.house.gov/issue/foreign-policy|accessdate=2014-02-15}}</ref>\n\n===The Budget, Taxes and Spending===\nBass, a social liberal, with Ratings around 100% by pro-gay marriage associations, can also be considered so in her fiscal positions. She has a rating of 10% from the very conservative California Tax Payers Association. However, the more liberal Consumer Federation of California gives her very high rankings. Besides the following positions on Taxing and Spending, She supports stimulus to create jobs.\n\nBass has been against general Budget-Wide Cuts like Resolution 38, which would reduce spending to 2008 levels. She has also opposed budgets with deep cuts like the 2011 Budget. However, she has supported the H Amendment 16 for Reducing Navy and Air Force Appropriations. She has supported mainly military cuts.\n\nBass has supported keeping taxes low for the middle class and \"tax credits for small businesses to hire new employees\". She states that the Bush tax cuts for the wealthiest should expire because \"the wealthy ... don't need these breaks.\"<ref name=\"votesmart\" /><ref>{{cite web|title=Jobs & the Economy|publisher=Karen Bass|url=http://bass.house.gov/issue/economy|accessdate=2014-02-15}}</ref>\n* '''112th Congress - 1st Session (2011)'''\nBass voted to authorize the United States military to participate in the [[2011 military intervention in Libya]].  The measure (H J RES 68) failed 123-295.<ref>\n {{cite web\n  |title=Authorizing the limited use of the United States Armed Forces in support of the NATO mission in Libya\n  |publisher=[[Clerk of the United States House of Representatives]] |date=June 24, 2011\n  |accessdate=2013-10-02 |url=http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2011/roll493.xml}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title = U.S. House of Representatives Roll Call Votes 112th Congress - 1st Session (2011)|accessdate = 2013-10-01|url = http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2011/index.asp}}<!-- Not too helpful - long list --></ref>\nIn 2011, Bass became a co-sponsor of Bill H.R.3261 otherwise known as the [[Stop Online Piracy Act]].<ref name=\"govtrack\">{{cite web |accessdate=2013-10-02 |url=http://www.govtrack.us/congress/bill.xpd?bill=h112-3261 |title=Stop Online Piracy Act (2011; 112th Congress H.R. 3261) |work=[[GovTrack.us]]}}</ref>\n\n==Personal life==\nFrom 1980 to 1986, Bass was married to Jesus Lechuga. Following their divorce, Bass and Lechuga jointly raised their daughter and four step-children together.<ref name=BlackVoices>{{cite news\n  |title=Karen Bass Makes United States History as the first African American Woman to be named to Speaker of (sic)\n  |date=March 6, 2008 |work=The Black Voice News |publisher=Brown Publishing Company |location=[[Riverside, California]]\n  |accessdate=2013-10-02\n  |url=http://www.blackvoicenews.com/news/41879-karen-bass-makes-united-states-history-as-the-first-african-american-woman-to-be-named-to-speaker-of.html}}</ref>\n\nBass suffered the loss of her only child, daughter Emilia Wright and son-in-law Michael Wright, in a car accident in 2006.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://articles.latimes.com/2006/oct/31/local/me-bass31 | work=Los Angeles Times | first=Stuart | last=Silverstein | title=Couple die in crash on 405 | date=October 31, 2006}}</ref>\n\n==References==\n{{reflist|2}}\n{{refbegin|2}}\n* {{cite news |newspaper=Loudmouth |pages=15\u201317 |number=6\n  |title=Is this democracy? |first=Jessica |last=Hoffmann |date=Summer 2004\n  |publisher=Women's Resource Center [[CSU Los Angeles]]\n  |accessdate=2013-10-01 |url=http://www.calstatelausu.org/books/loudmouth/issue06/550ffd.pdf}}\n{{refend}}\n\n==External links==\n* [http://karenbass.house.gov/ Congresswoman Karen Bass] official U.S. House site\n* [http://karenbass.com/ Karen Bass for Congress]\n*{{Dmoz|Regional/North_America/United_States/California/Government/Federal/US_House_of_Representatives/Karen_Bass_%5BD-37%5D|Karen Bass}}\n{{CongLinks | congbio = B001270 | votesmart = 28963 | politifact = | fec = H0CA33117 | congress = karen-bass/1996}}\n\n{{s-start}}\n{{s-par|us-ca-hs}}\n{{s-bef|before=[[Herb Wesson]]}}\n{{s-ttl|title=Member of the [[California State Assembly|California Assembly]]<br>from the [[California's 47th State Assembly district|47th]] district|years=2004\u20132010}}\n{{s-aft|after=[[Holly Mitchell]]}}\n|-\n{{s-bef|before=[[Lloyd E.
