The district includes many of the northern and western suburbs of [[Boston]], such as [[Medford, Massachusetts|Medford]], [[Framingham, Massachusetts|Framingham]], [[Woburn, Massachusetts|Woburn]] and Clark's hometown of [[Melrose, Massachusetts|Melrose]].  She is a member of the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]]. She was a member of the [[Massachusetts House of Representatives]] from 2008 to 2011, and a member of the [[Massachusetts Senate]] from 2011 to 2013.\n\nBorn in [[Connecticut]], Clark worked as an attorney in several states before moving to Massachusetts in 1995, where she worked in state government. She joined the Melrose School Committee in 2002, becoming committee chair in 2005. She was first elected to the state legislature in 2007, and contributed to legislation regarding criminal justice, education, and municipal pensions. She won the [[Massachusetts's 5th congressional district special election, 2013|2013 special election]] for the U.S. House of Representatives to succeed [[Ed Markey]] in the 5th district and sits on the [[United States House Committee on Natural Resources|House Natural Resources Committee]].\n\n==Early life and career==\nKatherine Marlea Clark<ref name=\"globe-alook\">{{cite news |url=http://www.boston.com/yourtown/news/2013/12/a_look_at_the_two_candidates_in_tuesdays_special_election_to.html |title=A look at the two candidates in Tuesday's special election |date=December 4, 2013 |work=The Boston Globe |author=Miller, John}}</ref> was born July 17, 1963 in [[New Haven, Connecticut|New Haven]], [[Connecticut]].<ref name=\"offic-2010\">{{cite book |url=http://archives.lib.state.ma.us/handle/2452/119267 |title=Public Officers of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts (2009\u20132010) |publisher=Commonwealth of Massachusetts |page=107 |chapter=Katherine M.
