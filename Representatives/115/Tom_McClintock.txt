He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]]. He is a former [[California Assembly|Assemblyman]] and [[California State Senate|state Senator]]. Aged 23, he was elected [[Chairman]] of the [[Ventura County, California|Ventura County]] Republican Party, and served until 1981. He was chief of staff to State Senator [[Edward M. Davis|Ed Davis]] from 1980 to 1982. From 1992 to 1994, he served as the director of the Center for the California Taxpayer.<ref>[https://mcclintock.house.gov/about/full-biography Biography | Congressman Tom McClintock]. Retrieved on December 22nd, 2015.</ref>\n\n==California Assembly (1982\u20131992, 1996\u20132000)==\n\n===Elections===\nMcClintock ran for [[California's 36th State Assembly district]], based in [[Thousand Oaks, California|Thousand Oaks]], in 1982 at the age of 26 after redistricting. He defeated [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrat]] Harriet Kosmo Henson 56\u201344%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=450790|title=Our Campaigns \u2013 CA State Assembly 36 Race \u2013 Nov 02, 1982|work=ourcampaigns.com}}</ref> In 1984, he won re-election to a second term, defeating Tom Jolicoeur 72\u201328%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=444938|title=Our Campaigns \u2013 CA State Assembly 36 Race \u2013 Nov 06, 1984|work=ourcampaigns.com}}</ref> In 1986, he won re-election to a third term, defeating Frank Nekimken 73\u201325%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=443080|title=Our Campaigns \u2013 CA State Assembly 36 Race \u2013 Nov 04, 1986|work=ourcampaigns.com}}</ref> In 1988, he won re-election to a fourth term, defeating George Webb II 70\u201329%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=441739|title=Our Campaigns \u2013 CA State Assembly 36 Race \u2013 Nov 08, 1988|work=ourcampaigns.com}}</ref> In 1990, he won re-election to a fifth term, defeating Ginny Connell 59\u201336%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=373460|title=Our Campaigns \u2013 CA State Assembly 36 Race \u2013 Nov 06, 1990|work=ourcampaigns.com}}</ref>\n\nAfter running for Congress in 1992 and for controller in 1994, he decided to run for the Assembly again in 1996. He ran for [[California's 38th State Assembly district]] and defeated Democrat Jon Lauritzen 56\u201340% to win his sixth assembly term.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=368955|title=Our Campaigns \u2013 CA State Assembly 38 Race \u2013 Nov 05, 1996|work=ourcampaigns.com}}</ref> In 1998, McClintock won re-election to a seventh term unopposed.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=280320|title=Our Campaigns \u2013 CA State Assembly 38 Race \u2013 Nov 03, 1998|work=ourcampaigns.com}}</ref>\n\n===Tenure===\nHe authored California\u2019s [[lethal injection]] use for [[Death penalty in California|California's death penalty law]]. He also opposed tax increases and supported spending cuts. He was a strong proponent of abolishing the car tax.<ref>[http://www.tommcclintock.com/meet-tom ] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121005210730/http://www.tommcclintock.com/meet-tom |date=October 5, 2012 }}</ref><ref name=\"nationaljournal.com\">{{cite web|url=http://www.nationaljournal.com/almanac/2010/person/tom-mcclintock-ca/|title=Login|work=nationaljournal.com}}{{Dead link|date=July 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=no }}</ref>\n\n==California Senate (2000\u20132008)==\n[[File:Tom McClintock.jpg|thumb|Tom McClintock as a California State Senator]]\n\n===Elections===\nIn 2000, he decided to retire from the California Assembly to run for [[California's 19th State Senate district]]. He ranked first in the May 7th open primary with 52% of the vote. In November, he defeated Democrat Daniel Gonzalez 58\u201342%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=5772|title=Our Campaigns \u2013 CA State Senate 19 Race \u2013 Nov 07, 2000|work=ourcampaigns.com}}</ref> In 2004, he won re-election to a second term, defeating Paul Joseph Graber 61\u201339%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=71750|title=Our Campaigns \u2013 CA State Senate 19 Race \u2013 Nov 02, 2004|work=ourcampaigns.com}}</ref>\n\n===Tenure===\nIn 2008, McClintock voted against Proposition 2, which prohibits confining calves, pigs and hens in small cages in which they cannot extend their limbs. \"Farm animals are food, not friends\", he said in response to backlash to his no vote. He also cited concern about increased grocery bills.<ref name=\"McGreevy102108\">{{cite web|last1=McGreevy|first1=Patrick|title=Tom McClintock speaks out on farm animal, gay marriage measures|url=http://latimesblogs.latimes.com/lanow/2008/10/mcclintock----n.html|website=LA Times Blogs - L.A. NOW|accessdate=22 December 2017|date=21 October 2008}}</ref>\n\nMcClintock has a long history of opposing various tax increases. During the 2000 [[dot-com bubble]], he was instrumental in proposing a two-thirds reduction in the vehicle license fee, or [[car tax]]. In 2003, he opposed then-Governor [[Gray Davis]]'s attempt to rescind a rollback of a vehicle license fee.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.signonsandiego.com/news/politics/recall/20031001-9999_1n1tom.html|title=GOP's McClintock sticks to his guns even if it costs him|publisher=SignOnSanDiego.com|date=2003-10-01|first=Michael|last=Gardner}}</ref> McClintock has also opposed deficit reduction efforts that would have increased taxes. He supported the Bureaucracy Reduction and Closure Commission and [[performance-based budgeting]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.signonsandiego.com/uniontrib/20040222/news_mz1e22state.html|title=Pressing measures|publisher=SignOnSanDiego.com|first=Robert M.|last=Stern|date=2004-02-22}}</ref>\n\n==Statewide elections==\n\n===1994 Controller election===\n{{Main|California State Controller election, 1994}}\nHe ran for [[California State Controller]] after incumbent Democrat Gray Davis retired. He won the Republican primary, defeating John Morris, 61\u201339%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=113667|title=Our Campaigns \u2013 CA Controller \u2013 R Primary Race \u2013 Jun 07, 1994|work=ourcampaigns.com}}</ref> In the general election, he faced [[Kathleen Connell]], former Special Assistant to [[Los Angeles Mayor]] [[Tom Bradley (American politician)|Tom Bradley]] and Director of [[Housing Authority of the City of Los Angeles|L.A. Housing Authority]]. Despite the fact that Connell outspent McClintock by a 3-to-1 margin, he only lost by two percentage points, 48\u201346%, with three other candidates receiving the other 6% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=40634|title=Our Campaigns \u2013 CA Controller Race \u2013 Nov 08, 1994|work=ourcampaigns.com}}</ref>\n\n===2002 Controller election===\n{{Main|California State Controller election, 2002}}\nMcClintock ran for State Controller again in 2002, facing Democratic nominee [[Steve Westly]], an [[eBay]] executive. Westly outspent him 5-to-1. McClintock's campaigns focused on increasing accountability for the state budget. The ads featured the character ''Angus McClintock'', a fictional cousin and fellow [[Scottish American]] extolling Tom McClintock's virtues of thriftiness and accountability with low-budget fifteen-second ads. He lost by a margin of just 0.2%, or 16,811 votes behind Westley, who won with a plurality of 45.3% of the vote. McClintock obtained 45.1% of the vote, while three other candidates obtained a combined 9.5% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=1608|title=Our Campaigns \u2013 CA Controller Race \u2013 Nov 05, 2002|work=ourcampaigns.com}}</ref>\n\n===2003 recall gubernatorial election===\n{{Main|California gubernatorial recall election}}\nIn 2003, he ran for the recall election against incumbent Democrat Gray Davis. Republican and film actor [[Arnold Schwarzenegger]] won the election with 49% of the vote. [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] [[Lieutenant Governor of California|Lieutenant Governor]] [[Cruz Bustamante]] finished second with 31% of the vote, which was about 17 points behind Schwarzenegger. McClintock finished in third place with 14% of the vote, which was about 35 points behind Schwarzenegger. Together, Republicans Schwarzenegger and McClintock were supported by 5,363,778 Californians, or 62.1% of the vote. 132 other candidates obtained the remaining 6.4% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=6312|title=Our Campaigns \u2013 CA Governor Recall Race \u2013 Oct 07, 2003|work=ourcampaigns.com}}</ref>\n\nMcClintock performed the best in [[Stanislaus County, California|Stanislaus County]], where he obtained 24% of the vote. He also cracked 20% or higher in several other counties: [[Mariposa County, California|Mariposa]] (23%), [[Tuolumne County, California|Tuolumne]] (22%), [[Tehama County, California|Tehama]] (21%), [[Calaveras County, California|Calaveras]] (20%), [[Madera County, California|Madera]] (20%), [[Modoc County, California|Modoc]] (20%), [[Shasta County, California|Shasta]] (20%), [[San Joaquin County, California|San Joaquin]] (20%), and [[Ventura County, California|Ventura]] (20%).<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20141021000759/http://www.sos.ca.gov/elections/sov/2003-special/gov.pdf ]</ref>\n\n===2006 gubernatorial election===\n{{Main|California lieutenant gubernatorial election, 2006}}\nHe ran for [[Lieutenant Governor of California|Lieutenant Governor]] in the 2006 elections. He defeated Tony Farmer in the Republican primary, 94\u20136%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=112292|title=Our Campaigns \u2013 CA Lieutenant Governor \u2013 R Primary Race \u2013 Jun 06, 2006|work=ourcampaigns.com}}</ref> In the general election, he lost to Democratic [[California Insurance Commissioner|State Insurance Commissioner]] [[John Garamendi]] 49\u201345%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=112362|title=Our Campaigns \u2013 CA  Lieutenant Governor Race \u2013 Nov 07, 2006|work=ourcampaigns.com}}</ref>\n\n==U.S. House of Representatives==\n\n===Elections===\n;1992\nAfter redistricting, State Assemblyman McClintock decided to retire in order to challenge Democratic U.S. Congressman [[Anthony C. Beilenson]] in [[California's 24th congressional district]]. He won the nine-candidate Republican primary with a plurality of 34% of the vote, beating second-place finisher Sang Korman by eleven percentage points.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=514570|title=Our Campaigns \u2013 CA District 24 \u2013 R Primary Race \u2013 Jun 02, 1992|work=ourcampaigns.com}}</ref> Beilenson defeated McClintock 56\u201339%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=27539|title=Our Campaigns \u2013 CA District 24 Race \u2013 Nov 03, 1992|work=ourcampaigns.com}}</ref>\n\n;2008\n{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in California, 2008#District 4}}\nOn March 4, 2008, McClintock announced his candidacy for the [[U.S. House of Representatives]] in [[California's 4th congressional district]], which is hundreds of miles away from the district McClintock represented in the state Senate. The district's nine-term incumbent, fellow Republican [[John Doolittle]], decided to retire. McClintock was unable to vote for himself in either the primary or the general election because the [[California Constitution]] required him to maintain his legal residence in his State Senate district until the end of his Senate term. Furthermore, in order to vote using a ballot in regards to a specific congressional district, one must live within that district. Because Thousand Oaks is outside of California's Fourth Congressional District, McClintock was thus ineligible to vote for himself.<ref name=\"SacBee030408\"/>\n\nUpon McClintock's entry into the race, fellow Republicans [[Rico Oller]] and Eric Egland withdrew from the Republican primary and endorsed McClintock.<ref name=\"SacBee030408\">{{cite news|author=Hecht, Peter |title=McClintock in, Oller out in race to replace Doolittle |work=[[Sacramento Bee]] |date=2008-03-04 |url=http://www.sacbee.com/111/story/759440.html |accessdate=2008-03-05 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20081206125635/http://www.sacbee.com/111/story/759440.html |archivedate=December 6, 2008 }}</ref><ref>{{cite news|author=Gunzberger, Ronecemb|title=California|work=Politics1.com|date=2008-03-05|url=http://www.politics1.com/blog-0308.htm#0305|accessdate=2008-03-05|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080310221242/http://politics1.com/blog-0308.htm#0305|archivedate=2008-03-10|df=}}</ref> McClintock was endorsed by the [[Republican Liberty Caucus]],<ref>[http://www.rlc.org/2008/11/05/rlc-election-results/ \"RLC Election Results.\" Republican Liberty Caucus (November 5, 2008).] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120212201341/http://www.rlc.org/2008/11/05/rlc-election-results/ |date=February 12, 2012 }}</ref> [[Club for Growth]], and U.S. Congressman [[Ron Paul]]. McClintock faced former U.S. Congressman [[Doug Ose]], a moderate represented the neighboring [[California's 3rd congressional district|3rd District]] from 1999 to 2005. Ose lived outside the district and was painted as a [[carpetbagger]] and a liberal who had voted to raise taxes and who voted for earmarks. McClintock defeated Ose 54\u201339%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=341896|title=Our Campaigns \u2013 CA District 04 \u2013 R Primary Race \u2013 Jun 03, 2008|work=ourcampaigns.com}}</ref>\n\nThe Democratic nominee was retired [[Usaf|Air Force]] [[Lieutenant Colonel (United States)|Lt. Col.]] [[Charles Brown (California)|Charlie Brown]], who ran an unexpectedly strong race against Doolittle in 2006. In March 2008, Ose's campaign commercials criticized McClintock for receiving payments totaling over $300,000 in per diem living expenses during his time in the [[California State Senate]], despite the fact that he lived in [[Elk Grove, California|Elk Grove]], near Sacramento, for most of the year. McClintock maintained that the payments were justified because his legal residence was in Thousand Oaks, in his State Senate district. He stated, \"Every legislator's [Sacramento area] residence is close to the Capitol. My residential costs up here are much greater than the average legislator because my family is here.\"<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.latimes.com/news/local/la-me-mcclintock23mar23,0,6751476.story|work=Los Angeles Times|first=Patrick|last=McGreevy|title=McClintock criticized for taking per diem housing allowance|date=March 23, 2008}}</ref> However, Ose's campaign commercials argued McClintock does not own or rent a home in the 19th district, but uses his mother's address. These attacks prompted a response from McClintock's wife, Lori, who said McClintock stays with his mother in order to better care for her after she fell ill and after the death of her husband.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.sacbee.com/111/story/963198.html|title=Ballot Watch: \"Ose's attack on McClintock for taking per diem riles rival's wife\"|work=[[Sacramento Bee]]|date=May 24, 2008|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20081206125641/http://www.sacbee.com/111/story/963198.html|archivedate=December 6, 2008|deadurl=yes|df=}}</ref> McClintock ran ads attacking Brown\u2019s participation at a 2005 protest by [[Code Pink]], an infamous [[anti-war]] group, and argued Brown supported [[Gay marriage in California|gay marriage]] but not the troops in Iraq. He also portrayed Brown as a clone of [[Speaker of the United States House of Representatives|Speaker]] [[Nancy Pelosi]].<ref name=\"nationaljournal.com\"/>\n\nBy November 23, 2008, McClintock led Brown by 1,566 votes (0.4% of the vote), 184,190 to 182,624. Subsequent returns expanded the margin slightly with the last returns coming in from [[El Dorado County]] shortly after [[Thanksgiving]]. On December 1, 2008, McClintock declared victory and on December 3, 2008, Brown conceded the race. McClintock defeated Brown by a margin of 0.5%, or 1,800 votes.<ref>{{cite book |title=2014 Almanac of American Politics |last2=Barone |first2=Chuck |last1=McCutcheon|first1=Michael |publisher=The University of Chicago Press |year= 2013}}</ref> He prevailed by a 3,500-vote margin in [[Placer County]], the largest county in the district. Brown won just three of the district's nine counties: Sierra (49.8%), Plumas (47.9%), and Nevada (42.3%).<ref>[http://www.sos.ca.gov/elections/sov/2008_general/23_34_us_reps.pdf \"United States Representative (final results)\"] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081221042514/http://www.sos.ca.gov/elections/sov/2008_general/23_34_us_reps.pdf |date=December 21, 2008 }} Office of the California Secretary of State, Retrieved on December 26, 2008</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.theunion.com/article/20081122/NEWS/811220440/1066&ParentProfile=1053&title=McClintock%20declares%20victory |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110524102543/http://www.theunion.com/article/20081122/NEWS/811220440/1066%26ParentProfile%3D1053%26title%3DMcClintock%20declares%20victory |archive-date=May 24, 2011 |title=McClintock declares victory |work=The Union |date=November 22, 2008 |accessdate=February 7, 2017 |deadurl=yes |df= }}</ref> Ultimately, McClintock won mainly on the strength of coattails from [[John McCain]], who carried the 4th with 54 percent of the vote, his fifth-best total in the state.\n\n;2010\n{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in California, 2010#District 4}}\nMcClintock was challenged in the Republican primary again, this time by Michael Babich. He easily defeated Babich 78\u201322%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=641309|title=Our Campaigns \u2013 CA District 04 \u2013 R Primary Race \u2013 Jun 08, 2010|work=ourcampaigns.com}}</ref> On November 2, he easily won re-election to a second term, defeating businessman [[Clint Curtis]] 61\u201331%, winning all of the counties in the district.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=539273|title=Our Campaigns \u2013 CA \u2013 District 04 Race \u2013 Nov 02, 2010|work=ourcampaigns.com}}</ref>\n\n;2012\n{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in California, 2012#District 4}}\nRedistricting pushed the 4th well to the south. However, it is as strongly Republican as its predecessor.\n\nHe easily won re-election to a third term, defeating Democrat [[Jack Uppal]] 61\u201339%. He won all but two of the district's ten counties: Nevada (37%) and Alpine (41%).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=768433|title=Our Campaigns \u2013 CA \u2013 District 04 Race \u2013 Nov 06, 2012|work=ourcampaigns.com}}</ref>\n\n;2014\n{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in California, 2014#District 4}}\nMcClintock won re-election, finishing first in California's \"top two\" primary, and defeating moderate Republican challenger, National Guard Major Art Moore in the general election, 60\u201340%.<ref>[https://www.nytimes.com/2014/04/21/us/politics/mcclintock-faces-bid-from-moderate-art-moore.html A Rare G.O.P. Retrieved on October 6, 2015.</ref>\n\n;2016\n{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in California, 2016}}\nMcClintock again finished first in the primary and subsequently defeated Democrat Robert W. Tom McClintock|work=[[The Sacramento Bee]]|date=February 22, 2016|accessdate=November 6, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://ballotpedia.org/California%27s_4th_Congressional_District_election,_2016|title=California's 4th Congressional District election, 2016\n|work=[[Ballotpedia]]|accessdate=November 6, 2017}}</ref>\n\n===Tenure===\nDuring the [[112th Congress]] McClintock was one of 40 \"staunch\" members of the [[Republican Study Committee]]<ref>{{cite web|title=Member List|author=|url=https://rsc-walker.house.gov/|format=|publisher=Republican Study Committee|date=|accessdate=21 December 2017}}</ref> who frequently voted against Republican party leadership and vocally expressed displeasure with House bills.<ref name=rsc>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2012/03/17/us/politics/house-freshmen-not-as-defiant-as-their-reputation-suggests.html?_r=1&hp|title=G.O.P.
