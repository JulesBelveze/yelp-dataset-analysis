Elected to the [[United States House of Representatives]] in 2014, he represents {{ushr|AL|6}}. Prior to his career as an elected official, Palmer co-founded and served as the long-time president of the [[Alabama Policy Institute]], a conservative think tank.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Cason |first1=Mike|title=Gary Palmer announces he will run for Congress in Alabama's 6th congressional district|url=http://blog.al.com/wire/2013/10/gary_palmer_announces_he_will.html|accessdate=4 March 2015 |publisher=AL.com| date=October 24, 2013}}</ref> He is a member of the [[Freedom Caucus]] in the House of Representatives.<ref>{{cite web| url= http://fivethirtyeight.com/datalab/the-hard-line-republicans-who-pushed-john-boehner-out/ | first1= Carl |last1=Bialik |first2=Aaron|last2= Bycoffe | title= The Hard-Line Republicans Who Pushed John Boehner Out | date= 25 September 2015 | work= [[FiveThirtyEight]]| access-date= 2015-09-28}}</ref>\n\n==Early life, education and career==\nPalmer was born in [[Hackleburg, Alabama]].<ref>http://info.cqrollcall.com/rs/cqrc/images/CQNews_NewMemberGuide2014.pdf</ref> He has a [[bachelor's degree]] in [[operations management]] from the [[University of Alabama]].<ref name=announces>{{cite web|url=http://blog.al.com/wire/2013/10/gary_palmer_announces_he_will.html |title=Gary Palmer announces he will run for Congress in Alabama's 6th congressional district|work=AL.com|accessdate=October 16, 2014}}</ref> In 1989, Palmer co-founded the Alabama Family Alliance, which later became the [[Alabama Policy Institute]]. In the ensuing [[runoff election]], Palmer picked up the support of the [[Club for Growth]].<ref name=rollcall>{{cite web|url=http://atr.rollcall.com/gary-palmer-club-for-growth-alabama-runoff-2014/|title=Gary Palmer Marks Second Chance for Club for Growth in Alabama Race|work=At the Races|accessdate=October 16, 2014}}</ref> Palmer won the runoff election by a margin of 64% to 36%.<ref name=victorious>{{cite web|url=http://www.al.com/news/birmingham/index.ssf/2014/07/gary_palmer_victorious_over_pa.html|title=Gary Palmer swamps Paul DeMarco in 6th District Republican runoff|work=AL.com|accessdate=October 16, 2014}}</ref> In the November 4, 2014 general election, Palmer defeated [[Democratic Party (US)|Democratic]] nominee Mark Lester, a history professor at [[Birmingham-Southern College]], 76% to 24%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.shelbycountyreporter.com/2014/11/04/gary-palmer-victorious-in-alabamas-6th-congressional-district-race/ |title=Gary Palmer victorious in Alabama\u2019s 6th congressional district race|work=Shelby County Reporter|date=November 4, 2014|accessdate=November 6, 2014}}</ref>\n\n===Tenure===\nPalmer took office on January 3, 2015, along with the other freshmen members of the [[114th United States Congress|114th Congress]].\n\n''[[Conservative Review]]'' has graded Palmer's voting record an A with a Liberty Score of 100%.
