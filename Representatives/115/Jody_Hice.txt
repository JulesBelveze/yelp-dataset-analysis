He was elected in 2014.\n\n==Early life==\nHice is a native of [[Atlanta]] and received his Bachelor of Arts degree from [[Asbury University|Asbury College]] in [[Wilmore, Kentucky]], a Master of Divinity degree from [[Southwestern Baptist Theological Seminary]] in [[Fort Worth, Texas]], and a Doctor of Ministry degree from [[Luther Rice Seminary]] in Atlanta, Georgia.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.jodyhice.com/JodyBio.php|title=Bio of Jody Hice|publisher=|accessdate=15 December 2017}}</ref>\n\nHice first served as senior pastor of Bethlehem First Baptist Church, until April 2010<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bethlehemfbc.org|title=Jody Hice Pastors First Baptist Church of Bethlehem, GA|publisher=}}</ref> in [[Bethlehem, Georgia]]. In addition, he served as first vice president of the Georgia Baptist Convention (2004\u201305) and Professor of Preaching at [[Luther Rice Seminary]]. Hice served as senior pastor at The Summit Church, a Southern Baptist church, in [[Loganville, Georgia]] from 2011 until December 2013, when he stepped down to run for office.\n\n==''The Jody Hice Show''==\nDuring the battle between the ACLU and Barrow County, Georgia, regarding the public display of the Ten Commandments in the County Courthouse, Hice was asked by a local radio station to provide weekly updates and to address various other issues.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.jodyhice.com|title=The Jody Hice Show|publisher=jodyhice.com|accessdate=February 2, 2018}}</ref>\n\nFrom that weekly program a daily show, ''Let Freedom Ring'', which was originally heard on [[WJBB (AM)|WIMO]] 1300 AM, [[Bethlehem, Georgia]] was born. The show is heard on about 400 stations,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.jodyhice.com/currentStations.php|title=Current Stations Airing The Jody Hice Show|publisher=|accessdate=15 December 2017}}</ref> the show focuses on Constitutional, moral, and religious liberty issues.{{citation needed|date=February 2018}}\n\n==U.S. House of Representatives==\n===2014 election===\nThe Congressional Seat for Georgia's 10th Congressional District opened up when the sitting representative, [[Paul Broun]], announced his bid for U.S. Senate in 2014.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Killough|first1=Ashley|title=Georgia Rep. Hice placed first in the primary on May 20, 2014 with 34% of the vote, followed closely by his run-off opponent Mike Collins who won 33% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://results.enr.clarityelections.com/GA/51345/132192/en/summary.html|website=GA Secretary of State Page|title=Ga Election Results|accessdate=February 2, 2018}}</ref>\n\nWith no candidate winning 50% of the vote, a run-off campaign was issued between the top two candidates, Hice and Mike Collins. The close race quickly grew heated amid accusations of campaign sign theft from both sides and reports of supporters being harassed at debates.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Cochran|first1=Kelsey|title=Hice, a Democratic opponent in November election, which he won (66.52%-33.48%) in a GOP wave on November 4, 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.covnews.com/section/1/article/56815|title=covnews.com, Jody auditing the [[federal reserve]] and its activities around mortgages. He co-sponsored the [[Federal Reserve Transparency Act]].<ref name=\"OTIBudget\">{{cite web|title=Doug Collins on Budget & Economy|url=http://www.ontheissues.org/GA/Doug_Collins_Budget_+_Economy.htm|website=On the Issues|accessdate=25 December 2017}}</ref>\n\nHice favors [[tax reform]] and voted in support of the [[Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017]].<ref name=\"Almukhtar\">{{cite web|last1=Almukhtar|first1=Sarah|title=How Each House Member Voted on the Tax Bill|url=https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2017/12/19/us/politics/tax-bill-house-live-vote.html?_r=0|website=The New York Times|accessdate=25 December 2017|date=19 December 2017}}</ref> He claims the new legislation creates a \"competitive and fair tax system.\" He says it will create an improved economy, more opportunities, and \"keep more money in the pockets of hardworking families and individuals.\" He also believes \"It will also encourage American businesses to keep their jobs and revenue here, and our job creators will once again be competitive internationally.\"<ref name=\"Yeomans122317\">{{cite web|last1=Yeomans|first1=Curt|title=POLITICAL NOTEBOOK: Gwinnett's Republican representatives in Washington celebrate tax bill passage|url=http://www.gwinnettdailypost.com/local/political-notebook-gwinnett-s-republican-representatives-in-washington-celebrate-tax/article_182cd456-d5fd-5f50-856e-f11d22b41f33.html|website=Gwinnett Daily Post|accessdate=December 25, 2017}}</ref>\n\n===Abortion===\nHice is [[pro-life]]. He opposes [[abortion]]s being used for race or sex selection. He believes that life begins at fertilization or cloning. He opposes family planning assistance that includes abortion.<ref name=\"OTIAbortion\">{{cite web|title=Doug Collins on Abortion|url=http://www.ontheissues.org/GA/Doug_Collins_Abortion.htm|website=On The Issues|accessdate=December 25, 2017}}</ref>\n\n===Interest group ratings===\n\nHice has a \"D\" rating from marijuana legalization advocacy organization [[NORML]] for his voting history regarding [[cannabis]]-related issues.<ref name=\"NORML\">{{cite web|title=Georgia Scorecard|url=http://norml.org/congressional-scorecard/georgia|website=NORML|accessdate=December 25, 2017}}</ref>\n\n===LGBT issues===\nHice opposes gay marriage.<ref>Jody Hice is likely headed to Congress, Sean Sullivan, 23 July 2014, Washington Post</ref> According to [[Right Wing Watch]], Hice compared homosexuality to alcoholism and opposed a ban on gay conversion therapy.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Sarlin|first1=Benjy|title=Anti-Islam pastor Jody Hice wins Georgia primary|url=http://www.msnbc.com/msnbc/ultra-conservative-jody-hice-wins-ga-10-primary|publisher=MSNBC}}</ref>\n\n===Religious issues===\n\nIn September 2008, Hice was one of 33 pastors across America who participated in \"Pulpit Freedom Sunday\",<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.onlineathens.com/stories/100608/ele_340689679.shtml|title=Jody Hice Returns To National Spotlight With Presidential Endorsement|publisher=onlineathens.com|accessdate=February 2, 2018}}</ref> an effort that challenged an [[Internal Revenue Service]] code threatening churches and pastors with loss of [[tax-exempt]] status and criminal behavior if they address [[Johnson Amendment|political issues from the pulpit]]. In that sermon, Hice endorsed [[United States Senate|Senator]] [[John McCain]] for [[President of the United States|President]]. The IRS never publicly responded to the event, and Pulpit Freedom Sunday has since grown to include over 450 churches.<ref name=\"MSN-IRS-R\">{{cite web|url=http://www.msnbc.msn.com/id/30391105/|title=Churches await IRS response after protest|date=April 24, 2009|publisher=MSNBC|accessdate=2009-05-20}}</ref>\n\nHice has argued that Christians have been \"tricked\" into a \"false belief\" in separation of church and state.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rightwingwatch.org/content/rep-jody-hice-church-state-separation-encourages-corruption|title=Rep.
