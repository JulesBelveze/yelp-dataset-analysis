(7th District))]{{dead link|date=November 2016 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} ''[[CQ Politics]]'' November 3, 2010</ref> is an American auctioneer and politician serving as the [[U.S. Representative]] for [[Missouri's 7th congressional district]] since 2011. The district includes much of the southwestern quadrant of the state and is anchored in [[Springfield, Missouri|Springfield]]. The district also includes [[Joplin, Missouri|Joplin]] and the popular tourist destination of [[Branson, Missouri|Branson]].\n\nA member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]], he was elected to fill the district's vacant seat in the 2010 midterm elections following the successful election of [[Roy Blunt]] to the [[United States Senate|U.S. Senate]].\n\n== Early life and education ==\nLong is a fourth-generation native of Missouri. He was born in [[Springfield, Missouri|Springfield]] in 1955. He attended the [[University of Missouri]] and was a member of the [[Delta Upsilon]] fraternity prior to dropping out. After taking 3 years off from school, Long decided to attend a 9 day training with the Missouri Auction School in [[Kansas City, Missouri|Kansas City]]. He received his Certified Auctioneer Institute designation via the National Auctioneers Association Education Institute and Trustees.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.congress.org/election/candidate/id/153758 |title=Election |publisher=Congress.org |date= |accessdate=2011-07-25}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://billylongforcongress.com/Groups/1000059963/Billy_Long_for/About_Billy_Long/About_Billy_Long.aspx |title=About Billy Long |publisher=Billy Long for Congress |date=2010-11-02 |accessdate=2011-07-25 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110910151639/http://www.billylongforcongress.com/Groups/1000059963/Billy_Long_for/About_Billy_Long/About_Billy_Long.aspx |archivedate=2011-09-10 |df= }}</ref>\n\n== Professional career ==\nLong owned Billy Long Auctions, [[Limited liability company|LLC]]. He was also a talk radio show host on the Springfield-based station [[KWTO (AM)|KWTO]]. He is a member of the [[National Association of Realtors]], [[National Auctioneers Association]], [[United States Chamber of Commerce|the Springfield Area Chamber of Commerce]], the [[National Rifle Association]], and the Greater Springfield Board of Realtors.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.billylongforcongress.com/Groups/1000059963/Billy_Long_for/About_Billy_Long/About_Billy_Long.aspx |title=About Billy Long |publisher=Billy Long for Congress |date=2010-11-02 |accessdate=2011-07-25 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110910151639/http://www.billylongforcongress.com/Groups/1000059963/Billy_Long_for/About_Billy_Long/About_Billy_Long.aspx |archivedate=2011-09-10 |df= }}</ref>\n\nDuring a September 2018 [[United States House of Representatives Committee on Energy and Commerce|House Committee on Energy and Commerce]] hearing on alleged anti-conservative bias on [[social media]], far-right [[internet personality]] [[Laura Loomer]] interrupted the meeting.
