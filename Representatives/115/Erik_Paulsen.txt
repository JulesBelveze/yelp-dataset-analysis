Paulsen''' (born May 14, 1965) is an American politician serving in the [[United States House of Representatives]] for {{ushr|MN|3}} since 2009. A member of the [[Republican Party of Minnesota]], he previously served in the [[Minnesota House of Representatives]] from 1995 to 2009 and as [[Minnesota House Majority Leader|Majority Leader]] from 2003 to 2007. His district in the western part of the [[Minneapolis-St. Paul|Twin Cities]] metropolitan area includes [[Bloomington, Minnesota|Bloomington]], [[Eden Prairie]], [[Edina, Minnesota|Edina]], [[Minnetonka]], [[Maple Grove, Minnesota|Maple Grove]], [[Plymouth, Minnesota|Plymouth]] and [[Wayzata]].\n\n== Early life, education, and career ==\nBorn in [[Bakersfield, California]], Paulsen graduated from [[Chaska High School]] in Minnesota in 1983.<ref name=\"leg.state.mn.us\">{{cite web|url=http://www.leg.state.mn.us/legdb/fulldetail.aspx?id=10508 |title=Paulsen, Erik - Legislator Record - Minnesota Legislators Past & Present |publisher=Leg.state.mn.us |date= |accessdate=January 11, 2018}}</ref> He attended [[St. Olaf College]], and received a [[Bachelor of Arts]] in [[mathematics]] in 1987. After college, Paulsen worked as an [[intern]] for Republican Senator [[Rudy Boschwitz]] from 1989 until Boschwitz was defeated by [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] challenger [[Paul Wellstone]] in 1990.<ref name=\"leg.state.mn.us\"/> Paulsen then took a staff position with Republican Representative [[Jim Ramstad]] in [[Washington, D.C.]] He worked on Ramstad's local congressional campaign in 1992 before seeking election to the [[Minnesota House of Representatives]] in 1994.<ref name=\"legistorm.com\">[https://web.archive.org/web/20090105163827/http://www.legistorm.com/blog/2008-election-big-for-former-congressional-staffers.html]</ref><ref>[http://www.leg.mn/legdb/fulldetail.asp?ID=10508 Minnesota Legislative Library for Erik Paulsen]</ref>\n\nFrom 2007 to 2009, Paulsen worked as a part-time business analyst for [[Target Corporation]] while a member of the [[Minnesota House of Representatives]].<ref name=\"leg.state.mn.us\"/>\n\n== Minnesota House of Representatives ==\nPaulsen was initially elected as an [[Independent-Republican Party|Independent Republican]].
