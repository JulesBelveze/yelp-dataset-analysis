She is a member of the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.newsnet5.com/politics/17893425/detail.html |title=Fudge Elected To Late Tubbs-Jones' Congressional Seat |publisher=[[WEWS-TV]] |date=November 4, 2008 |accessdate=November 4, 2008 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090106081251/http://www.newsnet5.com/politics/17893425/detail.html |archivedate=January 6, 2009 }}</ref>  The district includes most of the black-majority areas between [[Cleveland]] and [[Akron, Ohio|Akron]]. Fudge was Chairwoman of the [[Congressional Black Caucus]]<ref>{{cite web |title=Membership|author=|url=https://cbc.house.gov/membership/|format=|publisher=Congressional Black Caucus|date=|accessdate=7 March 2018}}</ref> in the [[113th United States Congress|113th Congress]]. Fudge was chosen to replace [[Debbie Wasserman Schultz]] as chair of the [[2016 Democratic National Convention]] after a leak of internal documents released by [[WikiLeaks]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://dailycaller.com/2016/07/24/marcia-fudge-to-replace-wasserman-schultz-as-chair-of-democratic-national-convention/ |title=Marcia Fudge To Replace Wasserman Schultz As Chair Of Democratic National Convention |work=[[Daily Caller]] |date=July 24, 2016 |accessdate=July 24, 2016}}</ref>\n\n==Early life, education and career==\n[[File:Marcia Fudge official portrait.jpg|thumb|Earlier official photo of Fudge]]\nFudge was born in Cleveland, Ohio. A 1971 graduate of [[Shaker Heights High School]],<ref name=alumnews /> she earned a [[Bachelor of Science]] degree in business from [[Ohio State University]] in 1975.<ref name=cauldron>{{cite web|url=http://www.csucauldron.com/home/index.cfm?event=displayArticlePrinterFriendly&uStory_id=c9200657-a38a-436d-8f86-c6175e650915 |title=Alumna Spotlight: Marcia L. Fudge, ESQ |work=The Cauldron |date=April 10, 2006 |author=Michelle McCafferty |accessdate=September 12, 2008}} {{Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot}}</ref> In 1983, she earned a [[Juris Doctor|J.D.]] degree from [[Cleveland State University]] [[Cleveland\u2013Marshall College of Law]].<ref name=cauldron /><ref>{{cite news |title=Mayor Marcia Fudge, Esq. |date=March 8, 2007 |work=[[Call and Post]] |page=6 }}</ref>\n\nImmediately after college, she worked as a law clerk and studied legal research. She also worked in the Cuyahoga County prosecutor's office.<ref>{{cite news |title=Cuyahoga County Prosecuting Attorney Press Conference Regarding Congressional Race |date=February 10, 1998 |publisher=[[PR Newswire]]}}</ref> While serving in the prosecutors' office, she worked on business aspects as she held the position of Director of Budget and Finance. Fudge has also worked as an auditor for the estate tax department and has occasionally served as a visiting judge and as a chief referee for arbitration.<ref name=bio>{{cite web|url=http://fudge.house.gov/about/index.shtml |title=Biography |accessdate=November 29, 2008 |publisher=[[United States House of Representatives]] |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20081126191854/http://fudge.house.gov/about/index.shtml |archivedate=November 26, 2008}}</ref>\n\n== Early political career ==\nFudge was the mayor of [[Warrensville Heights]], a middle-class and mostly [[African-American]] suburb of Cleveland, from January 2000 until November 18, 2008.<ref>{{cite web |title=Marcia Fudge, with style of her own, takes congressional seat |url=http://blog.cleveland.com/metro/2008/11/marcia_fudge_with_style_of_her.html |date=November 19, 2008 |accessdate=November 19, 2008 |author=Olivera Perkins |publisher=[[The Plain Dealer]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.accessmylibrary.com/coms2/summary_0286-27370874_ITM |title=Warrensville Heights, Ohio Mayor's Inauguration |date=January 11, 2000 |accessdate=September 12, 2008 |publisher=[[PR Newswire]]}}</ref> Her 1999 campaign was her first run for any elected office.  She was the town's first female and first African-American mayor.<ref name=about>{{cite web |url=http://www.cityofwarrensville.com/mayor.htm |title=About the Mayor |publisher=City of Warrensville Heights, Ohio|accessdate=September 12, 2008 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20070620223902/http://www.cityofwarrensville.com/mayor.htm |archivedate=June 20, 2007}}</ref>\n\nShe was chief of staff to 11th District Congresswoman [[Stephanie Tubbs Jones]] during Jones' first term in Congress.<ref>{{cite news |title=Stephanie Tubbs Jones: A servant of the people |work=[[Call and Post]] |date=October 26, 2006 |page=1B}}</ref> She has also served on the board of trustees for the [[Cleveland Public Library]].<ref name=about />\n\n==U.S. House of Representatives==\n\n===Committee
