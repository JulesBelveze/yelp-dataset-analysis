Foxx is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]] and served as [[Republican Conference Secretary of the United States House of Representatives|Secretary of the House Republican Conference]] from January 2013 until January 2017.<ref>Sherman, Jake. [http://www.politico.com/news/stories/1112/84293.html?hp=f3 \"House committee chairs all men\"] ''Politico.'' (Published 27 Nov 2012) <www.politico.com> Retrieved 28 Nov 2012.</ref> She is the current chair of the [[House Committee on Education and the Workforce]].\n\n==Early life, education and career==\nFoxx was born in [[The Bronx, New York]], to Dollie (n\u00e9e Garrison) and Nunzio John Palmieri. She was reared in a rural area of [[Avery County, North Carolina]]. Foxx grew up in a poor family and first lived in a home with running water and electricity at 14 years old.<ref name=\"news and observer\">{{cite web|title=Virginia Foxx |url=http://projects.newsobserver.com/dome/profiles/virginia_foxx |work=Raleigh News & Observer |accessdate=March 28, 2012 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120728014722/http://projects.newsobserver.com/dome/profiles/virginia_foxx |archivedate=July 28, 2012 }}</ref>\n\nWhile attending Crossnore High School in [[Crossnore, North Carolina]], she worked as a janitor at the school and was the first in her family to graduate from high school.<ref>{{cite web|title=Celebrities, Notable Public Figures Reveal Their Most Memorable Teachers|url=http://www.nea.org/grants/17345.htm|publisher=National Education Association|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120424201956/http://www.nea.org/grants/17345.htm|archivedate=2012-04-24|df=}}</ref> She graduated from the [[University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill]] with a bachelor's degree in 1968 and later earned both a [[Master of Arts]] in college teaching (1972) and [[Ed.D]] (1985) from the [[University of North Carolina at Greensboro]].<ref name=\"Bioguide\"/><ref name=\"About Foxx\">{{cite web|title=About Virginia Foxx|url=http://virginiafoxx.com/about|publisher=VirginiaFoxx.com|accessdate=March 28, 2012|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120216014452/http://www.virginiafoxx.com/about|archivedate=February 16, 2012|df=}}</ref> With her husband, Virginia Foxx owned and operated a [[Plant nursery|nursery]] and [[landscaping]] business.<ref name=\"About Foxx\"/>\n\nFoxx worked as a research assistant and then an English instructor at [[Caldwell Community College & Technical Institute]] and [[Appalachian State University]] before moving into university administration. From 1987 until her 1994 entry into politics, she was president of [[Mayland Community College]]. Under North Carolina Governor [[James G. Martin]], Foxx served as Deputy Secretary for Management.<ref name=\"About Foxx\"/> From 1994 to 2004, Foxx served in the [[North Carolina Senate]].<ref name=\"House Bio\">{{cite web|title=Biography|url=http://foxx.house.gov/biography/|accessdate=February 24, 2015}}</ref>\n\n==United States House of Representatives==\n\n===Committee assignments===\n* '''[[United States House Committee on Education and the Workforce|Committee on Education and the Workforce]]''' (Chairwoman)\n* '''[[United States House Committee on Oversight and Government Reform|Committee on Oversight and Government Reform]]''' <ref>[http://foxx.house.gov/biography/committees.htm Foxx official site: Committees]</ref>\n* [[Italian American Congressional Delegation]]\n\n===Caucus memberships===\n* [[Republican Study Committee]]<ref>{{cite web|title=Member List|author=|url=https://rsc-walker.house.gov/|format=|publisher=Republican Study Committee|date=|accessdate=17 November 2017}}</ref>\n* [[Congressional Constitution Caucus]]<ref>{{cite web|title=Members|author=|url=https://congressionalconstitutioncaucus-garrett.house.gov/about-us/membership|format=|publisher=Congressional Constitution Caucus|date=|accessdate=8 May 2018}}</ref>\n\n===Political campaigns===\n[[File:Virginia Foxx 2009.jpg|thumb|right|alt=Virginia Foxx |Virginia Foxx talking with constituents in Yadkinville, NC]]\nFoxx was first elected to the U.S. House in 2004, defeating [[Jim Harrell, Jr.]] with 59% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|title=Statistics of the Presidential & Congressional Election of November 2, 2004|url=http://clerk.house.gov/member_info/electionInfo/2004election.pdf|publisher=United States House of Representatives|accessdate=13 April 2016}}</ref>\n\nFoxx was briefly targeted for defeat in the [[United States House of Representatives elections in North Carolina, 2006|2006 elections]], but the [[United States Democratic Party|Democrats]]' top choice, popular Winston-Salem mayor [[Allen Joines]], decided not to run. Joines later said that he didn't have the stomach for the kind of race he felt it would take to defeat Foxx.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20070930165317/http://www.journalnow.com/servlet/Satellite?pagename=WSJ%2FMGArticle%2FWSJ_ColumnistArticle&c=MGArticle&cid=1128769268868&path=!localnews&s=1037645509099] </ref> Her 2006 opponent was [[Roger Sharpe]], whom she defeated.\n\nRoy Carter of [[Ashe County, North Carolina]] was Foxx's opponent for her seat in the 2008 election; she won by a substantial margin.\n\nIn November 2010, Foxx was reelected with about 65% of the vote.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://elections.nytimes.com/2010/results/house | work=The New York Times | title=House Results Map}}</ref>\n\nIn November 2014, Foxx was reelected with about 60% of the vote, defeating software developer Josh Brannon.<ref>{{cite news| url=\nhttp://centralnc.twcnews.com/content/politics/713619/us-rep--foxx-wins-6th-term-in-nc-s-5th-district/ }}</ref>\n\nIn November 2016, Foxx was reelected with about 59% of the vote, again over Josh Brannon.<ref>[https://www.nytimes.com/elections/results/north-carolina-house-district-5-foxx-brannon New York Times]</ref>\n\n===Tenure===\n[[File:Virginia Foxx.jpg|thumb|Foxx's late 2000's portrait]]\n\n====Hurricane Katrina====\nIn September 2005, Foxx was one of 11 members of Congress to vote against<ref>{{cite news|last=McMurray|first=Jeffrey|title=Representatives stand by their votes against hurricane aid |url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=L-kvAAAAIBAJ&sjid=8jsDAAAAIBAJ&pg=4966,7230333&dq=katrina+virginia-foxx&hl=en|newspaper=Rome News-Tribune|date=September 22, 2005}}</ref> the $51 billion aid package to victims of [[Hurricane Katrina]].\n\n====Heroes Earned Retirement Opportunities (HERO) Act====\nThe first bill sponsored by Foxx to have been signed into law since 2006, the Hero Act, signed by President Bush on Memorial Day, 2006, allows U.S. troops to increase their retirement savings by investing a portion of their combat pay into [[Individual Retirement Account]]s.\n\n====Electronic Pay Stub Act====\nThe second bill sponsored by Foxx and subsequently signed into law is the Electronic Pay Stub Act which gives federal employees the choice of receiving their pay stubs electronically. This legislation is projected to save taxpayers millions of dollars. Studies have shown that it costs 10 times more to purchase and distribute paper stubs than it does to distribute electronic stubs.<ref>Hicks, Adam. [http://www.goblueridge.net/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=4229&Itemid=1 \"Foxx-Authored Bill Passes in Congress\"]. ''GoBlueRidge.net''. July 31, 2008.</ref> This bill was signed into law in October, 2008.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.goblueridge.net/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=4810&Itemid=1 |title=Foxx\u2019s Legislation Signed into Law |publisher=Goblueridge.net |date= |accessdate=2010-08-23}}</ref>\n\n====Troubled Asset Relief Program====\n\nShortly after Congress approved the Troubled Asset Relief Program, Foxx identified a provision in the law that allowed her to force consideration of a measure to deny the second, $350 billion, tranche of the TARP bailout. On November 19, 2008, she introduced {{USBill |110|h.j.res|101}}, which met all of the parliamentary requirements for consideration once the President requested the second tranche.{{citation needed|date=October 2014}}\n\nIn the following (111th) Congress, she reintroduced the measure as {{USBill |111|h.j.res|3}}, and shortly before leaving office, President Bush requested the second tranche, thereby activating the trigger allowing her to commandeer the House floor, although she was not a member of the majority party. Her measure passed the House 270-155; the act was never addressed in the Senate.<ref>Sec. 115 of {{USPL|110|343}}</ref>\n\nDuring an interview in 2007, Foxx was quoted as saying: \"We have the best economy we have had in 50 years.\"<ref>{{cite news|title=Congresswoman Virginia Foxx to Young Turks|url=http://www.tc-america.org/in-congress/congresswoman-virginia-foxx-to-young-turks-72.htm|accessdate=April 13, 2016|work=Turk of America Magazine|publisher=Turkish Coalition of America|date=August 8, 2007}}</ref>\n\n====Opposition to Matthew Shepard and James Byrd, Jr. Hate Crimes Prevention Act====\nIn April 2009, Foxx expressed opposition to the [[Matthew Shepard and James Byrd, Jr. Hate Crimes Prevention Act]], claiming that the murder of [[Matthew Shepard]] was not a hate crime. While debating the act at the House of Representatives, she called the murder a \"very unfortunate incident\" but claimed \"we know that that young man was killed in the commitment of a robbery. It wasn't because he was gay.\" She ultimately called that allegation \"a hoax that continues to be used as an excuse for passing [[hate crimes]] bills.\"<ref>{{cite news | last =Mary Ann | first =Akers | title =Virginia Foxx: Matthew Shepard's Murder Not a Hate Crime | publisher =Washington Post | date =2009-04-29 | url =http://voices.washingtonpost.com/sleuth/2009/04/virginia_foxx_mathew_shepards.html | accessdate =2009-05-20 }}</ref>\nSome media outlets, including the ''New York Times'',<ref name=\"hoax NYT\">{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2009/05/06/opinion/06wed3.html?_r=1 |title=Matthew Shepard Act |publisher=The New York Times |date=May 5, 2009 |accessdate=2012-07-19}}</ref> ''Washington Post'',<ref name=\"hoax WaPo\">{{cite web|last=Akers |first=Mary Ann |url=http://voices.washingtonpost.com/sleuth/2009/04/virginia_foxx_mathew_shepards.html |title=The Sleuth \u2013 Virginia Foxx: Matthew Shepard's Murder Not a Hate Crime |publisher=The Washington Times |date=April 29, 2009 |accessdate=2012-07-19}}</ref> and ''Huffington Post'',<ref name=\"hoax Huff\">{{cite news | last =Ryan | first =Grim | title =Virginia Foxx: Story of Matthew Shepard's Murder A \"Hoax\" | publisher =Huffington Post | date =2009-05-30 | url =http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2009/04/29/virginia-foxx-story-of-ma_n_192971.html | accessdate =2011-12-20 }}</ref> criticized her statements. [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrat]] [[Debbie Wasserman Schultz]], a congressional colleague, did the same.<ref name=\"hoax Politico\" /> Democratic sources claimed that Matthew Shepard's mother was present at the time of Foxx's statements.<ref name=\"hoax Politico\">{{cite web|last=Thrush |first=Glenn |url=http://www.politico.com/blogs/glennthrush/0409/Matthew_Shepard_killed_in_nonbias_robbery_Foxx_says.html?showall |title=Matthew Shepard killed in non-bias \"robbery,\" Foxx says |publisher=Politico |date=April 29, 2009 |accessdate=2012-07-19}}</ref>\n\nFoxx later retracted her comments, suggesting her use of the word \"hoax\" was in bad taste.<ref name=\"hoax retraction LA Times\">{{cite web|last=Malcolm |first=Andrew |url=http://latimesblogs.latimes.com/washington/2009/04/matthew-shepard-virginia-foxx-gay.html |title=Rep. Virginia Foxx retracts word 'hoax' in Matthew Shepard murder |publisher=Los Angeles Times |date=April 30, 2009 |accessdate=2012-07-19}}</ref> She suggested that Shepard's murder was a tragedy and that his killers had received appropriate justice.<ref name=\"hoax retraction LA Times\" />\n\n====Health care debate====\nWhen commenting on the House version of the reform bill that funds counseling for end-of-life issues, Foxx said, \"Republicans have a better solution that won't put the government in charge of people's health care,\" and \"(The plan) is pro-life because it will not put seniors in a position of being put to death by their government.\"<ref name=\"fox_july30_2009\">{{cite web|url=http://www.myfox8.com/wghp-foxx-health-care-comments-090730,0,2622612.story|title=Virginia Foxx Uses Strong Words to Oppose Health Care Reform Bill|last=Tucker|first=Chad|date=July 30, 2009|work=MyFox8.com|accessdate=2009-08-09|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090802035648/http://www.myfox8.com/wghp-foxx-health-care-comments-090730,0,2622612.story|archive-date=August 2, 2009|dead-url=yes|df=mdy-all}}</ref> She later said that \"we have more to fear from the potential of the [[Affordable Health Care for America Act]] passing than we do from any terrorist right now in any country.\"<ref name=\"mediamatters_nov02_2009\">{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sA66v858_NU&feature=player_embedded|title=Rep.
