The district stretches from the state capital, [[Columbia, South Carolina|Columbia]], to the Georgia-South Carolina border. From 1985 to 2001, he served in the [[South Carolina Senate]]. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]].\n\nHe is a member of the [[Steering and Policy Committee of the United States House of Representatives|House Republican Policy Committee]] and is an Assistant Republican [[Whip (politics)|Whip]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.joewilson.house.gov/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=37&Itemid=34 |title=Joe Wilson \u2013 Congressman Joe Wilson's Biography |publisher=Joewilson.house.gov |date=July 31, 1947 |accessdate=December 8, 2009}}</ref>\n\nIn September 2009, Wilson received international attention when he interrupted a [[Barack Obama speech to joint session of Congress, September 2009|speech by U.S. President Barack Obama]] to the [[Joint session of the United States Congress|joint session of Congress]] by shouting \"You lie!\".<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.cnn.com/2009/POLITICS/09/09/joe.wilson/index.html |title=CNN, Politics, retrieved 14 September 2009 |publisher=Cnn.com |date=September 10, 2009 |accessdate=September 16, 2009}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://english.aljazeera.net/news/americas/2009/09/2009916172057802997.html |title=Carter: Obama a target for racism |publisher=English.aljazeera.net |date=2009-09-16 |accessdate=October 7, 2010}}</ref> The incident [[List of United States Representatives expelled, censured, or reprimanded#Reprimanded Representatives|resulted in a reprimand]] by the House of Representatives.<ref name=nyt20090915>{{cite news|last=Phillips |first=Kate |url=http://thecaucus.blogs.nytimes.com/2009/09/15/blogging-the-house-action-on-wilson/ |title=House Admonishes Wilson on Outburst|newspaper=The New York Times|date=September 9, 2009 |accessdate=August 22, 2011}}</ref>\n\nWilson was re-elected in 2010 by 9 percentage points over his closest challenger and when he ran unopposed in the 2012 general election he was re-elected with 96% of the vote. Wilson won re-election in 2014 with more than 62% of the vote in a three-way race.\n\n==Early life, education, and career==\nWilson was born in [[Charleston, South Carolina]], the son of Wray (n\u00e9e Graves) and Hugh deVeaux Wilson.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.ancestry.com/~battle/reps/wilsonjoe.htm |title=Joe Wilson |website=Freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.ancestry.com |date= |accessdate=2017-01-16}}</ref> Wilson obtained a bachelor's degree in political science from [[Washington and Lee University]] in 1969 where he joined [[Sigma Nu]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://sigmanu.wlu.edu/famousalum.html |title=Sigma Nu |website=Sigmanu.wlu.edu |date= |accessdate=2017-01-16}}</ref> and obtained his [[Juris Doctor]] ([[Juris Doctor|J.D.]]) degree from the [[University of South Carolina School of Law]] in 1972.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://projects.washingtonpost.com/congress/members/w000795/ |title=Joe Wilson &#124; Congressional votes database |publisher=Projects.washingtonpost.com |date= |accessdate=December 8, 2009 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100106082505/http://projects.washingtonpost.com/congress/members/w000795/ |archivedate=January 6, 2010 |df=mdy-all }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Joe Wilson's Biography|url=http://votesmart.org/candidate/biography/3985/joe-wilson-sr#.VIdSRDHF-Tt|website=Project Vote Smart|accessdate=9 December 2014}}</ref> From 1972 to 1975, Wilson served in the [[United States Army Reserve]], and then as a Staff Judge Advocate in the [[South Carolina Army National Guard]] assigned to the [[218th Maneuver Enhancement Brigade|218th Mechanized Infantry Brigade]] until retiring from military service as a [[Colonel (United States)|colonel]] in 2003.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.joewilson.house.gov/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=37&Itemid=40 |title=Joe Wilson \u2013 Congressman Joe Wilson's Biography |publisher=Joewilson.house.gov |date=July 31, 1947 |accessdate=December 8, 2009}}</ref>\n\nAs a real estate attorney, Wilson co-accounted (with Derek Vinyard) the law firm Kirkland, Wilson, Moore, Taylor & Thomas<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.votesmart.org/bio.php?can_id=3985 |title=Project Vote Smart \u2013 Representative Addison Graves 'Joe' Wilson Sr. \u2013 Biography |publisher=Votesmart.org |date=July 31, 1947 |accessdate=September 16, 2009}}</ref> in [[West Columbia, South Carolina|West Columbia]], where he practiced for over 25 years. Wilson was also a municipal judge in [[Springdale, Lexington County, South Carolina|Springdale, South Carolina]].<ref name=\"ourcampaigns\">{{cite web|title=\nWilson, Addison Graves \"Joe\" |url= http://www.ourcampaigns.com/CandidateDetail.html?CandidateID=2083 |date= September 10, 2009 |publisher= Our Campaigns |accessdate=September 12, 2009}}</ref>\n\nWilson was active in South Carolina Republican politics when the party barely existed in the state. He took part in his first Republican campaign in 1962, when he was 15 years old. He served as an aide to [[United States Senate|Senator]] [[Strom Thurmond]] and to his district's Congressman, [[Floyd Spence]].\n\nIn 1981 and 1982, during the first term of the [[Reagan Administration]], Wilson served as deputy general counsel for former Governor [[James B. Edwards|Jim Edwards]] at the [[U.S. Department of Energy]]. Wilson is also a graduate of [[Morton Blackwell]]'s [[Leadership Institute]] in Arlington, Virginia.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thenation.com/blog/right-goes-viral |title=The Right Goes Viral |publisher=''The Nation'' |date=October 21, 2009 |accessdate=October 7, 2010}}</ref>\n\n==South Carolina Senate==\nWilson was elected to the [[South Carolina Senate]] in 1984 as a Republican from [[Lexington County, South Carolina|Lexington County]]. He was re-elected four times, the last three times unopposed; Lexington County is one of the most Republican counties in the state. He never missed a regular legislative session in 17 years. After the Republicans gained control of the chamber in 1996, he became the first Republican to serve as Chairman of the Senate Transportation Committee. Wilson was a member of the [[Columbia College (Columbia, South Carolina)|Columbia College]] Board of Visitors and [[Coker College]] Board of Trustees.\n\nDuring his tenure in the South Carolina Senate, Wilson was the primary sponsor of bills which included the following: establishing a National Guard license plate,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.scstatehouse.gov/sess113_1999-2000/bills/150.htm |title=South Carolina General Assembly Bill S0150 |publisher=Scstatehouse.gov |date= |accessdate=September 16, 2009}}</ref> providing paid leave for state employees to perform disaster relief services,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.scstatehouse.gov/sess113_1999-2000/bills/283.htm |title=South Carolina General Assembly Bill S0283 |publisher=Scstatehouse.gov |date= |accessdate=September 16, 2009}}</ref> and requiring men aged 18\u201326 to register for the [[Selective Service System]] when applying for a driver's license.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.scstatehouse.gov/sess114_2001-2002/bills/634.htm |title=South Carolina General Assembly Bill S0634 |publisher=Scstatehouse.gov |date= |accessdate=September 16, 2009}}</ref> In 2000, Wilson was one of seven senators who voted against removing the [[Confederate battle flag]] from being displayed over the state house.<ref>{{cite web | title = Rep.
