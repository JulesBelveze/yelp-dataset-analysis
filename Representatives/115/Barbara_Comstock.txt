A [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]], she serves in the [[United States House of Representatives]] for [[Virginia's 10th congressional district]].\n\nFrom 2010 to 2014, she was a member of the [[Virginia House of Delegates]]. She first won election to her seat in [[Virginia elections, 2009|2009]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Blais|first1=Dianne|title=What you need to know: Virginia's 10th Congressional District race|url=http://legacy.wusa9.com/story/news/politics/elections/2014/11/04/virginia-10th-congressional-district-race/18450923/|accessdate=26 April 2016|publisher=WUSA CBS|date=November 4, 2014}}</ref> She has worked in numerous positions for various government agencies, including as chief counsel of the [[United States House Committee on Oversight and Government Reform|House Government Reform and Oversight Committee]], director of public affairs at the [[United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]], and as a Congressional staffer. She is a founding partner and co-principal of the public relations and public policy firm Corallo Comstock.<ref>{{cite web|title=Barbara Comstock's file|url=http://www.politifact.com/personalities/barbara-comstock/|website=PolitiFact.com|publisher=Richmond Times-Dispatch|accessdate=26 April 2016}}</ref>\n\n==Early life and education==\nComstock was born Barbara Jean Burns in [[Springfield, Massachusetts]], on June 30, 1959. She is the daughter of Sally Ann Burns, a teacher, and John Ferguson Burns, national manager of polymer sales for [[Shell Chemicals]].<ref name = NYT82>{{cite news |newspaper=[[The New York Times]] |accessdate=2012-08-12|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1982/10/10/style/barbara-burns-bride-of-elwyn-c-comstock.html|title=Barbara Burns Bride Of Elwyn C. Comstock  |date=October 10, 1982}}</ref> Comstock graduated from [[Westchester Academy for International Studies|Westchester High School]] in [[Houston, Texas]] in 1977. She graduated ''[[cum laude]]'' from [[Middlebury College]] in 1981. In college, Comstock spent a semester interning for Senator [[Ted Kennedy]]. While interning for Kennedy, Comstock, who was raised a Democrat, became a Republican.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Huetteman|first1=Emmarie|title=For Barbara Comstock of Virginia, a Switch From Left to Right|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2015/04/07/us/politics/for-barbara-comstock-of-virginia-a-switch-from-left-to-right.html?_r=0|work=The New York Times|date=April 6, 2015}}</ref>\n\n==Career==\nAfter working as a lawyer in private practice, Comstock served from 1991 to 1995 as a senior aide to Congressman [[Frank Wolf (politician)|Frank Wolf]]. Comstock then served as chief investigative counsel and senior counsel for the U.S. House of Representatives [[Committee on Government Reform]] from 1995 to 1999, working as one of Washington's most prominent anti-[[Bill Clinton|Clinton]] [[opposition research]]ers.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Green|first1=Joshua|title=Playing Dirty|url=https://www.theatlantic.com/magazine/archive/2004/06/playing-dirty/302960/?single_page=true|accessdate=27 April 2016|publisher=The Atlantic|date=June 2004}}</ref>\n\nComstock worked on behalf of the [[George W. Bush presidential campaign, 2000|2000 presidential campaign]] of [[George W. Bush]]. Her research team built massive stores of paper and electronic data, known as \"The Gore File,\" that were a key source of information on the former vice president for GOP publicists and ad-makers. Comstock is credited with writing the Republican \"playbook\" defending Bush nominees such as [[John Ashcroft]] for [[U.S. Attorney General]]. Comstock later served as director of public affairs for the [[United States Department of Justice|Justice Department]] from 2002 to 2003.<ref name=\"NRO\">{{cite news|first=Barbara |last=Comstock |author2=Lanny J.
