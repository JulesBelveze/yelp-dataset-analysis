He is a member of the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]]. The district formerly included, in the last congress, most of southeastern [[Queens]], including [[Jamaica, New York|Jamaica]], [[Laurelton, Queens|Laurelton]], [[Rosedale, Queens|Rosedale]], [[Cambria Heights]], [[Saint Albans, New York|Saint Albans]], [[Springfield Gardens, Queens|Springfield Gardens]], [[Far Rockaway, Queens|Far Rockaway]], and the [[John F. Kennedy International Airport]]. His district is made up mainly of middle and upper middle-class [[African-American]] and [[West Indian American]] communities, but also included a small part of [[Ozone Park, Queens, New York|Ozone Park]] and part of [[Howard Beach, New York|Howard Beach]] known as [[Old Howard Beach]], both of which are predominantly middle-class [[Italian-American]] communities. In addition, he represented much of [[Kew Gardens, Queens|Kew Gardens]] and northern [[Richmond Hill, Queens|Richmond Hill]].\n\n==Early life, education and career==\nBorn in [[East Harlem]], New York City and raised in a housing project, Meeks attended [[Adelphi University]] and obtained a [[law degree]] from [[Howard University School of Law]]. He then was Supervising Judge for the New York State Workers Compensation System.\n\nMeeks was a member of the [[New York State Assembly]] (31st D.) from 1993 to 1998, sitting in the [[190th New York
