Roybal]] (Father)\n|education       = [[California State University, Los Angeles]] ([[Bachelor of Arts|BA]])\n}}\n'''Lucille Elsa Roybal-Allard'''  ({{IPAc-en|\u02c8|r|\u0254\u026a|b|\u0259l|_|\u02c8|\u00e6|l|\u0259r|d}}; born June 12, 1941) is the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|CA|40}}, serving in Congress since 1993. She is a member of the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]]. Since redistricting in 2010, the district no longer includes downtown Los Angeles.\n\n==Early life, education and career==\nShe was born in [[Boyle Heights, California]], the daughter of Representative [[Edward R. Roybal]] and Lucille Beserra Roybal. She was educated at [[Ramona Convent Secondary School]] in Alhambra, California, and [[California State University, Los Angeles]].\n\nShe was a public relations officer and fund raising executive. She was also a member of the [[California State Assembly]] <ref name=\"CA\">[http://capitolmuseum.ca.gov/english/legislature/history/index.html Capitol Museum] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20060426003202/http://capitolmuseum.ca.gov/english/legislature/history/index.html |date=April 26, 2006 }}</ref> from 1986 to 1992.\n\n==U.S. House of Representatives==\n\n===Committee assignments===\n*'''[[United States House Committee on Appropriations|Committee on Appropriations]]'''\n**[[United States House Appropriations Subcommittee on Homeland Security|Subcommittee on Homeland Security]] (Ranking Member)\n**[[United States House Appropriations Subcommittee on Labor, Health and Human Services, Education, and Related Agencies|Subcommittee on Labor, Health and Human Services, Education, and Related Agencies]]\n\n===Caucuses===\n*[[Congressional Asian Pacific American Caucus]] (Associate Member)<ref>{{cite web|title=Members|author=|url=https://capac-chu.house.gov/members|format=|publisher=Congressional Asian Pacific American Caucus|date=|accessdate=17 May 2018}}</ref>\n*'''[[Congressional Arts Caucus]]'''<ref>{{cite web|title=Membership|author=|url=https://artscaucus-slaughter.house.gov/membership|format=|publisher=Congressional Arts Caucus|date=|accessdate=13 March 2018}}</ref>\n\nShe currently sits on the House Appropriations and Standards of Official Conduct committees. She has also served as the chair of the California Democratic congressional delegation (1998\u20131999) and of the [[Congressional Hispanic Caucus]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Members|author=|url=https://congressionalhispaniccaucus-lujangrisham.house.gov/members|format=|publisher=Congressional Hispanic Caucus|date=|accessdate=15 May 2018}}</ref> She has also been active in the Congressional Children's Caucus and on the Democratic Homeland Security and the Livable Communities task forces.\n\nShe is the first Mexican-American woman to serve in Congress. Along with [[Nydia Velazquez]] of [[New York City]], she was the second [[Latino|Latina]] after [[Ileana Ros-Lehtinen]] of [[Miami]], and with Velazquez, the first Democrat and the first elected to a full term.\n\nShe is a member of the [[Congressional Progressive Caucus]]<ref>{{cite web|title=Caucus Members|author=|url=https://cpc-grijalva.house.gov/index.cfm?sectionid=71&sectiontree=2,71|format=|publisher=Congressional Progressive Caucus|date=|accessdate=30 January 2018}}</ref> and the [[House Baltic Caucus]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Members|author=|url=http://housebalticcaucus.webs.com/members|format=|publisher=House Baltic Caucus|date=|accessdate=21 February 2018}}</ref>\n\n===Legislation===\nRoybal-Allard introduced the [[Newborn Screening Saves Lives Reauthorization Act of 2013 (H.R. 1281; 113th Congress)]] into the House on March 20, 2013. The bill would amend the [[Public Health Service Act]] to [[authorization bill|reauthorize]] grant programs and other initiatives to promote expanded [[newborn screening|screening of newborns]] and children for heritable disorders.<ref name=cbo1281>{{cite web|title=CBO - H.R. 1281|url=http://www.cbo.gov/publication/45434|publisher=Congressional Budget Office|accessdate=24 June 2014}}</ref> Roybal-Allard argued that \"newborn screening not only transforms and save lives - it saves money.\"<ref name=RoyPR22>{{cite web|title=Rep. Roybal-Allard and Rep. Simpson introduce the Newborn Screening Saves Lives Authorization Act|url=http://roybal-allard.house.gov/news/documentsingle.aspx?DocumentID=325195|publisher=House Office of Rep. Roybal-Allard|accessdate=24 June 2014|date=22 March 2013}}</ref> According to Roybal-Allard, in [[California]] \"newborns are screened for more than 40 preventable and treatable conditions \u2013 and for every one dollar California spends on screening, it yields a benefit of over $9 as we prevent disease in children who are diagnosed with these treatable conditions.\"<ref name=\"RoyPR22\"/>\n\n==Political campaigns==\nIn 1992, she won the Democratic nomination for the newly created 33rd District. She won handily in November, and has been reelected ten times as of 2012. Her district was renumbered as the 34th after the 2000 Census and the 40th after the 2010 Census.\n\n==Personal life==\nRoybal-Allard is married to Edward T. Allard III. She has two children, Ricardo Olivarez and Lisa Marie Roybal Elliott<ref name=\"press\">[http://www.house.gov/list/press/ca34_roybal-allard/photos070329b.html LRA Press Release]</ref> (born Olivarez), two stepchildren, Angela Allard and Guy Mark Allard, and four grandchildren. Her husband is a management consultant and a retired [[Marine Corps]] [[Captain (land)|captain]]. She lives in [[Downey, California]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://heavy.com/news/2012/10/downey-california-shootings-four-detained/|title=Downey, California Shootings UPDATE: Man Detained for Questioning|last=Genovario|first=Kevin|date=2012-10-26|language=en-US|access-date=2016-09-28}}</ref>\n\n==See also==\n*[[NALEO]]\n* [[Children's Act for Responsible Employment (CARE Act)]] a bill the Congresswoman introduced in 2009 to achieve parity among minor workers in the agricultural industry with that of other industries.
