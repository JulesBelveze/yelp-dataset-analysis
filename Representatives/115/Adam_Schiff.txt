A member of the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]], Schiff has served in Congress since 2001. Schiff represented the 27th and 29th Districts between 2001 and 2013, which initially included the areas of [[Alhambra, California|Alhambra]], [[Altadena, California|Altadena]], [[San Gabriel, California|San Gabriel]], [[Burbank, California|Burbank]], [[Glendale, California|Glendale]], [[South Pasadena, California|South Pasadena]], [[Temple City, California|Temple City]], [[Monterey Park, California|Monterey Park]], and [[Pasadena, California|Pasadena]]. In 2010, his district's boundaries were re-drawn to include, among others, [[La Ca\u00f1ada Flintridge, California|La Ca\u00f1ada Flintridge]], [[La Crescenta-Montrose, California|La Crescenta-Montrose]] and [[Sunland-Tujunga, Los Angeles|Sunland-Tujunga]], as well as large slices of central [[Los Angeles]] including [[Hollywood, California|Hollywood]], the [[Hollywood Hills, Los Angeles|Hollywood Hills]], [[West Hollywood, California|West Hollywood]], [[Echo Park, Los Angeles|Echo Park]], [[Silver Lake, Los Angeles|Silver Lake]], and [[Los Feliz, Los Angeles|Los Feliz]]. ([[Burbank, California|Burbank]], [[Glendale, California|Glendale]], and the western part of [[Pasadena, California|Pasadena]] all remained in his district.)\n\nSchiff is an influential voice for the Democratic Party on foreign policy and national security issues in the House of Representatives.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Neuman|first1=Johanna|title=Congressman Is Gaining a Name in Foreign Affairs|url=http://www.latimes.com/local/politics/la-me-schiff20jun20-story.html|accessdate=August 22, 2014|agency=Los Angeles Times|date=June 5, 2005}}</ref> He serves as [[ranking member]] of the [[House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence]]. He is currently on leave from the [[United States House Committee on Appropriations|House Appropriations Committee]] which he joined in 2007. The district had once been a Republican stronghold, but had been trending Democratic since the early 1990s.  In what was the most expensive House race ever at the time<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.opensecrets.org/bigpicture/topraces.php?cycle=2000&Display=allcands |title=Most Expensive Races 2000 |author=Center for Responsive Politics |accessdate=April 9, 2012}}</ref> (several elections in 2006<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.opensecrets.org/bigpicture/topraces.php?cycle=2006&Display=allcands |title=Most Expensive Races 2006 |author=Center for Responsive Politics |accessdate=April 9, 2012}}</ref> and 2008<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.opensecrets.org/bigpicture/topraces.php?cycle=2008&Display=allcands |title=Most Expensive Races 2008 |author=Center for Responsive Politics |accessdate=April 9, 2012}}</ref> later eclipsed it), Schiff unseated Rogan, taking 53 percent of the vote to Rogan's 44 percent.  He became only the second Democrat to represent this district since its creation in 1913.\n\nAfter the 2000 census, the district was renumbered as the 29th and made significantly more Democratic.  As a result, Schiff has never faced another contest nearly as close as his 2000 bid, and has been reelected eight times.
