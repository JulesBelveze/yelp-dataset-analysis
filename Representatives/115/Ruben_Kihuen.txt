He is Nevada's first Latino member of the U.S. House and Kihuen described himself as the first 'Dreamer' elected to Congress.\n\n[[Party leaders of the United States House of Representatives|House Minority Leader]] [[Nancy Pelosi]] has called on Kihuen to resign in response to sexual misconduct allegations against him by a female campaign staff member, as reported by [[BuzzFeed]].<ref name=PelosiSex>{{Cite news|last1=Nocera|first1=Kate|first2=Tarini|last2=Parti|date=December 1, 2017|url=https://www.buzzfeed.com/katenocera/she-says-she-quit-her-campaign-job-after-he-harassed-her|title=She Says She Quit Her Campaign Job After He Harassed Her. Now He\u2019s In Congress.|work=BuzzFeed|access-date=December 2, 2017|language=en}}</ref> Though he publicly refused to resign, he did not run for re-election in 2018.\n\n== Early life and education ==\nKihuen was born in [[Guadalajara]], Mexico. His family moved to the United States in 1988.<ref name=\"mjordan\">{{cite news|last1=Jordan|first1=Miriam|title=New Kingmaker: Vegas's Mr. Kihuen Will See You Now|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB118644209956489830|accessdate=July 26, 2016|publisher=Wall Street Journal|date=August 7, 2007}}</ref>\n\nKihuen's father was a laborer in [[Orange County, California]], before moving to [[Las Vegas]] in the 1990s and becoming a middle school science teacher.<ref name=\"mjordan\"/> Kihuen attended [[Rancho High School]] and was named the 1997\u20131998 Nevada \"soccer player of the year.\"<ref name=\"mjordan\"/> \n\nHe graduated from the [[University of Nevada, Las Vegas]] with a Bachelor of Science degree in education. While in high school and college, Kihuen volunteered for the campaigns of Senator [[Harry Reid]], Virginia Governor [[Mark Warner]], and Houston Mayor [[Lee P. Brown|Lee Brown]].<ref name=\"mjordan\"/>\n\nAs of 2011, Kihuen was enrolled in the [[Master of Public Administration]] degree program at the [[University of Oklahoma]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.leg.state.nv.us/Division/Research/Publications/LegManual/2013/ch1.pdf |title=Members of the Nevada Legislature |date=2011 |website=leg.state.nv.us/ |publisher=Nevada Legislature |location=Carson City, NV |page=26}}</ref>\n\n== Early career ==\nIn 2002, Kihuen became a deputy field director for the Nevada Democratic Party.<ref name=\"mjordan\" /> He then served as regional representative to Senate Majority Leader [[Harry Reid]].<ref name=\"mjordan\" /> He then worked as student recruiter and academic advisor for the [[College of Southern Nevada]] (CSN).<ref name=\"mjordan\" /> He is a past member of the Clark County Community Development Advisory Committee and the North Las Vegas Citizen's Advisory Committee.\n\n== Nevada legislature ==\nFirst elected to the state assembly in 2006, Kihuen served two terms before successfully running in 2010 to the [[Nevada State Senate]] after the incumbent was [[Term limits in the United States|term-limited]]. During the 76th legislative session in 2011 he was the chair of the Senate Select Committee on Economic Growth and Employment. In the 77th legislative session in 2013, he chaired the Senate Standing Committee on Revenue and Economic Development and served as Majority Whip for the Democrats in the Senate.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://leg.state.nv.us/App/Legislator/A/Senate/Current/10|title=Details|work=state.nv.us|accessdate=April 24, 2015}}</ref>\n\n== U.S. House of Representatives ==\n[[File:Ruben Kihuen 2012.jpg|thumb|upright|Kihuen in 2012]]\n\n=== Elections ===\n\n==== 2012 ====\n{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in Nevada, 2012#District 1}}\nIn September 2011, Kihuen announced his intention to run to represent [[Nevada's 1st congressional district]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lasvegassun.com/news/2011/nov/01/titus-being-pressured-not-run-1st-congressional-di/|title=Dina Titus being pressured not to run for 1st Congressional District seat|author=Karoun Demirjian|work=LasVegasSun.com|accessdate=April 24, 2015}}</ref> but withdrew a month before filing opened in February 2012,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lasvegassun.com/news/2012/feb/07/ruben-kihuen-out-cd1-race-clearing-way-dina-titus/|title=Ruben Kihuen drops out of CD1 race, clearing way for Dina Titus|author=Karoun Demirjian|work=LasVegasSun.com|accessdate=April 24, 2015}}</ref> allowing former Representative [[Dina Titus]] to run without a contested primary.\n\n==== 2016 ====\n{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in Nevada, 2016#District 4}}\nOn March 28, 2015, Kihuen announced he was running for Congress in {{ushr|NV|4}}, seeking to defeat first-term incumbent Republican [[Cresent Hardy]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.reviewjournal.com/politics/nevada-sen-ruben-kihuen-announces-bid-congress|author=Myers, Laura|work=Las Vegas Review-Journal|title=Nevada Sen. Ruben Kihuen announces bid for Congress|date=March 28, 2015|accessdate=April 23, 2015}}</ref> He defeated seven other candidates in the Democratic primary on June 14, 2016.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Marin|first1=Lesley|title=Ruben Kihuen wins Democratic nomination for Congressional District 4|url=http://www.ktnv.com/news/ruben-kihuen-wins-democratic-nomination-for-congressional-district-4|accessdate=June 16, 2016|publisher=KTNV|date=June 14, 2016}}</ref> Kihuen spoke at the [[2016 Democratic National Convention]], the only 2016 House recruit to do so.<ref name=\"dnc\">{{cite news|last1=Pathe|first1=Simone|title=Hispanic House Recruit Gets Prominent Speaking Slot at DNC|url=http://www.rollcall.com/news/politics/hispanic-house-recruit-gets-prominent-speaking-slot-dnc|accessdate=July 26, 2016|publisher=Roll Call|date=July 25, 2016}}</ref> During the campaign, Kihuen was on leave from his job at the Las Vegas-based public relations firm the Ramirez Group.<ref>{{cite news|title=Politically Connected PR Firm Subpoenaed in Councilman Probe|url=http://lasvegas.cbslocal.com/2016/09/29/politically-connected-pr-firm-subpoenaed-in-councilman-probe/|accessdate=November 3, 2016|agency=The Associated Press|publisher=CBS|date=September 29, 2016}}</ref> Kihuen won the election by a vote of 128,680 (48.5%) to 118,220 (44.5%).<ref name=\"2016Election\">{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/elections/results/nevada-house-district-4-hardy-kihuen|title=Nevada U.S. House 4th District Results: Ruben Kihuen Wins|date=November 9, 2016|publisher=''The New York Times''|access-date=November 9, 2016}}</ref>\n\nKihuen defeated Hardy by just over 10,000 votes. While Hardy carried six of the district's seven counties, Kihuen swamped Hardy in the district's share of Clark County, winning it by over 24,000 votes.<ref name=\"2016Election\" />  He was sworn in on January 3, 2017, becoming the first Hispanic to represent Nevada in the House of Representatives. He is also one of the first two Hispanics to represent the state in Congress; [[United States Senate|Senator]] [[Catherine Cortez Masto]] was sworn in the same day.\n\n=== Tenure ===\n\n=== Committee assignments ===\n*'''[[United States House Committee on Financial Services|Committee on Financial Services]]'''\n** [[United States House Financial Services Subcommittee on Housing and Insurance|Subcommittee on Housing and Insurance]]\n** [[United States House Financial Services Subcommittee on Terrorism and Illicit Finance|Subcommittee on Terrorism and Illicit Finance]]\n\n=== Caucus memberships ===\n* [[Congressional Hispanic Caucus]]<ref>{{cite web|title=Members|author=|url=https://congressionalhispaniccaucus-lujangrisham.house.gov/members|format=|publisher=Congressional Hispanic Caucus|date=|accessdate=15 May 2018}}</ref>\n* [[Congressional LGBT Equality Caucus]]\n* [[Congressional Progressive Caucus]]<ref>{{cite web|title=Caucus Members|author=|url=https://cpc-grijalva.house.gov/index.cfm?sectionid=71&sectiontree=2,71|publisher=Congressional Progressive Caucus|accessdate=January 30, 2018}}</ref>\n*'''[[New Democrat Coalition]]'''<ref>{{cite web|title=Members|url=https://newdemocratcoalition-himes.house.gov/members|publisher=New Democrat Coalition|accessdate=April 28, 2018}}</ref>\n*'''[[Congressional Asian Pacific American Caucus]]'''<ref>{{cite web|title=Members|author=|url=https://capac-chu.house.gov/members|format=|publisher=Congressional Asian Pacific American Caucus|date=|accessdate=17 May 2018}}</ref>\n\n== Sexual misconduct allegations ==\n{{Quote box\n |quote    =  '''Timeline of allegations'''<br />'''February 6, 2016''': Kihuen follows finance director to her car, and tells her \"You look really good, I'd like to take you out if you didn\u2019t work for me\".
