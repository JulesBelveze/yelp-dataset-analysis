{{IPAc-en|\u02c8|\u00e6|\u0261|j|\u0259|\u02cc|l|\u0251r}} (born June 19, 1979) is an American politician who is the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] from [[California's 31st congressional district|California's 31st Congressional District]]. A [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrat]], he served as Mayor of [[Redlands, California]] from 2010 to 2014 and as the President of the Inland Empire Division of the [[League of California Cities]].<ref name=\"About the Inland Empire Division of the League of California Cities\">{{cite web|last=Unknown|title=About the Inland Empire Division of the League of California Cities|url=http://www.cacities.org/Member-Engagement/Regional-Divisions/Inland-Empire|publisher=League of California Cities|accessdate=8 May 2012}}</ref> Aguilar served on the Redlands City Council from 2006 until his election to Congress.\n\nAguilar is Whip of the [[Congressional Hispanic Caucus]] and an Assistant Whip in the [[House Democratic Caucus]].<ref name=\"house.gov\">{{cite web|url=https://aguilar.house.gov/media-center/press-releases/aguilar-named-assistant-whip-house-democratic-caucus|title=Aguilar Named Assistant Whip of House Democratic Caucus|date=4 January 2017|publisher=|accessdate=21 June 2017}}</ref>\n\n==Personal==\nAguilar was born in [[Fontana, California]] and grew up in a working-class family in [[San Bernardino, California]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.peteaguilar.com/about|title=About Pete - Pete Aguilar for U.S. Congress|website=www.peteaguilar.com|accessdate=21 June 2017}}</ref> He graduated from the [[University of Redlands]] with degrees in Government and Business Administration.<ref name=\"Mayor Aguilar Biography - City of Redlands\">{{cite web|title=City Elected Officials Information Page|url=http://www.ci.redlands.ca.us/clerk/aguilar.htm|publisher=City of Redlands|accessdate=May 8, 2012}}</ref> He has lived in [[Redlands, California]] since 1997, with his wife Alisha. Together they have two sons.<ref name=Aguilar>{{cite web|title=Pete Aguilar (D-CA-31)|url=http://teapartycheer.com/bios/west/california/pete-aguilar-ca-bio/|publisher=Tea Party Cheer|accessdate=13 March 2013}}</ref>\n\n==Career==\nAguilar began his career in public service in 2001 when California Governor [[Gray Davis]] appointed him Deputy Director of the Inland Empire Regional Office of the Governor, eventually becoming the Interim Director. In 2006, Aguilar became the youngest member of the Redlands City Council in the city's 126-year history when five council members, Democrat and Republican, picked him out of 11 candidates to fill an open seat. He was elected a year later, his first election. His fellow council members appointed him mayor in 2010 and again in 2012.<ref name =\"Young Latino Mayor Pete Aguilar Could Turn a Red House Seat Blue\">{{cite
