Technological<br>University]] ([[Bachelor of Science|BS]], [[Master of Business Administration|MBA]])\n|website = {{url|estes.house.gov|House website}}\n}}\n'''Ronald Gene Estes''' (born July 19, 1956) is an American politician and member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]] who has served as the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for [[Kansas's 4th congressional district]]&nbsp;since April 25, 2017. He was previously the 39th [[Kansas State Treasurer]], serving from 2011 to 2017.\n\n==Early life, education, and career==\nEstes was born in [[Topeka, Kansas]], and is a fifth-generation Kansan.<ref name=\"StateTreasurerProfile\">{{cite news |last= |first= |date= |title=Meet Ron Estes |url=https://www.kansasstatetreasurer.com/prodweb/about_us/meet-ron-estes.php |work=Kansas State Treasurer Office of the Kansas State Treasurer and the people of Kansas |location=[[Topeka, Kansas]] |access-date=April 17, 2017 }}</ref> He earned a bachelor's degree in Civil Engineering and a master's degree in Business Administration from [[Tennessee Technological University]].<ref name=\"sedgwick\">{{cite news |last=Gruver |first=Deb |date=January 29, 2010 |title=County Treasurer Estes to run for same office at state level |url=http://www.kansas.com/news/politics-government/election/article1021871.html |work=The Wichita Eagle |location=[[Wichita, Kansas]] |access-date=April 17, 2017}}</ref>\n\nEstes worked in consulting and management roles in the aerospace, oil and gas, automotive, and several other manufacturing and service industries, working for several companies including [[Procter & Gamble]], [[Koch Industries]], and [[Bombardier Aerospace|Bombardier Learjet]].<ref name=\"CurryRollCall04122017\">{{cite news |last=Curry |first=Tom |date=April 12, 2017 |title=Estes a Stalwart but Unflashy Conservative |url=http://www.rollcall.com/news/politics/estes-profile-kansas-republican-house-race |work=[[Roll Call]] |location=[[Washington, DC]] |access-date=April 16, 2017}}</ref>\n\nHe was elected as treasurer of [[Sedgwick County, Kansas]], home to [[Wichita, Kansas|Wichita]], in 2004,<ref name=sedgwick/> and subsequently reelected in 2008.<ref name=\"CurryRollCall04122017\"/> During his political career, he also served as the Treasurer for the Kansas County Treasurer's Association, and in several posts in the Republican Party including Vice Chair of the [[Kansas Republican Party]].<ref name=\"sedgwick\"/>\n\n==Kansas State Treasurer==\nEstes ran for [[Kansas State Treasurer]] in the 2010 election, against [[incumbent]] [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrat]] [[Dennis McKinney]].<ref name=sedgwick/> Estes was the first statewide elected official from [[Wichita, Kansas]] in two decades.<ref name=\"StateTreasurerProfile\"/> He was reelected in 2014, defeating Carmen Alldritt.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Calovich |first1=Annie |last2=Lowry |first2=Bryan |date=November 4, 2014 |title=Republican Selzer to be next Kansas insurance commissioner |url=http://www.kansas.com/news/politics-government/election/article3568658.html |work=The Wichita Eagle |location=[[Wichita, Kansas]] |access-date=April 17, 2017}}</ref>\n\nAs state treasurer, Mr. Estes managed more than $24 billion in public money and he came in under budget by over $600,000.<ref name=\"TurkNYTimes04122017\"/> He made a priority telling Kansans about unclaimed money, such as funds from forgotten bank accounts.<ref name=\"CurryRollCall04122017\"/> In 2016, Estes said his office had returned $100 million in unclaimed property since 2010.<ref name=\"CurryRollCall04122017\"/>\n\nDuring the [[United States presidential election, 2016|2016 U.S. presidential election]], in February 2016, prior to the Kansas presidential caucuses, Estes endorsed Florida Senator [[Marco Rubio]] for the Republican nomination.<ref name=\"CurryRollCall04122017\"/> Estes served in the [[United States Electoral College|Electoral College]] and cast his electoral vote for [[Donald Trump]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kansascity.com/news/politics-government/article121772638.html|title=Missouri and Kansas Estes was sworn into office on April 25, 2017.\n\nIn December 2017, Estes voted in favor of the [[Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017]].<ref name=\"Almukhtar\">{{cite web |last1=Almukhtar|first1=Sarah|title=How Each House Member Voted on the Tax Bill |url=https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2017/12/19/us/politics/tax-bill-house-live-vote.html?_r=1|website=The New York Times |accessdate=December 28, 2017|date=December 19, 2017}}</ref> In an [[op-ed]] for the ''[[Wichita Eagle]]'', Estes said he is \"confident it will make a real difference for families and businesses in Kansas.\" He says it will provide economic and job growth, and that workers will see larger paychecks. He says that local companies, including [[Cessna]] and [[Spirit AeroSystems]], will be able to \"compete on a level playing field with international rivals,\" as a result of the bill, and that more jobs will return to Kansas from overseas. He says the tax-filing process has been simplified, even though the process remains the same.<ref name=\"Estes122417\">{{cite web|last1=Estes|first1=Ron|title=Rep. [[Kathleen Rice]] mid-sentence while she offered a question at a Homeland Security subcommittee hearing. Rice tweeted \"Day in the life. Worth noting there are men from both parties who don't act like this\" and included a video of the exchange. Estes explained that he was simply trying to follow committee rules after Rice's time was up.<ref>{{cite
