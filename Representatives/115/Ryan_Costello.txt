A [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]], Costello is the representative for {{ushr|PA|6}}, elected in [[United States House of Representatives elections in Pennsylvania, 2014|2014]] to the [[United States House of Representatives]]. He previously served on the [[Chester County, Pennsylvania|Chester County]] [[County commission|Board of Commissioners]] (2011\u20132015), and as its chair from 2013 to 2015. He announced his retirement in 2018 on [[MSNBC]], stating that he would not seek reelection. After announcing his retirement, CNN called him a \"rare brand in the House GOP conference: a young moderate willing to break with his leadership and his President on everything from gun control bills to repealing and replacing the Affordable Care Act.\"<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.cnn.com/2018/05/26/politics/house-retirements-ryan-costello-jeff-flake-ileana-ros-lehtinen/index.html|title=Why Republicans are calling it quits|last=CNN|first=Story by Lauren Fox, CNN; videos by Cassie Spodak, McKenna Ewen and Zachary Wasser,|work=CNN|access-date=2018-09-26}}</ref>\n\n==Early life==\nCostello was born in 1976 to schoolteacher parents.<ref>[http://www.nationaljournal.com/article/534408 Profile], nationaljournal.com; accessed November 9, 2014.</ref> Costello attended [[Ursinus College]] and [[Villanova University School of Law]].<ref name=th>{{cite web|url=http://www.timesherald.com/general-news/20140115/chester-county-commissioner-ryan-costello-to-run-for-jim-gerlachs-seat|title=Chester County Commissioner Ryan Costello to run for Jim Gerlach's seat|accessdate=November 4, 2014}}</ref>\n\n==Political career==\nCostello served on the [[Board of Supervisors]] for [[East Vincent Township, Chester County, Pennsylvania|East Vincent Township]] in [[Chester County, Pennsylvania]], for six years, serving as chairman for the last four. He was elected the Chester County [[recorder of deeds]] in 2008.<ref name=th/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.unionvilletimes.com/?p=1684|title=Costello appointed Chester County Commissioner|work=The Unionville Times|accessdate=November 4, 2014}}</ref> He was elected to the Chester County [[County commission|Board of Commissioners]] in 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.philly.com/philly/blogs/capitolinq/Chester-Republican-Costello-announces-run-for-Congress.html|title=Chester County Republican Costello announces run for Congress|work=Philly.com|accessdate=November 4, 2014}}</ref> His fellow commissioners elected him as chairman of the commission in 2013, and reappointed in 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.chestercounty.com/2014/01/14/20238/costello-re-appointed-chair-of-chester-county-commissioners|title=Costello re-appointed chair of Chester County Commissioners|accessdate=November 4, 2014}}</ref>\n\n===U.S. House elections===\n\n==== 2014 ====\nWhen [[Jim Gerlach]], the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] incumbent in {{ushr|PA|6}} in the [[United States House of |date=November 7, 2014 }}, wfmz.com; accessed November 9, 2014.</ref>\n\n==== 2016 ====\nIn 2016, Democrat Mike Parrish challenged Costello. Hacked material from Parrish was leaked during the campaign, but the Costello campaign took the position that they would not use the illicitly obtained materials during the election.<ref name=\":0\">{{Cite news|url=https://www.theatlantic.com/politics/archive/2018/05/republicans-reticent-over-use-of-hacked-documents-in-midterms/559346/|title=There\u2019s Nothing to Stop the 2018 Elections From Being Hacked|last=Bertrand|first=Natasha|work=The Atlantic|access-date=2018-05-01|language=en-US}}</ref> Vincent Galko, a consultant for Costello, said, \"When news broke that this material had likely been stolen by a foreign actor, we immediately said, \u2018We\u2019re not going to use it.'\"<ref name=\":0\" />\n\nCostello was re-elected by a wide margin of 57.2 to 42.8.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/elections/results/pennsylvania-house-district-6-costello-parrish|title=Pennsylvania U.S. House 6th District Results: Ryan A. Costello Wins|access-date=2018-09-26|language=en}}</ref>\n\n=== Tenure ===\nFor his first two terms, Costello represented a district that took in northern Chester County and western [[Montgomery County, Pennsylvania|Montgomery County]], then reached across [[Berks County, Pennsylvania|Berks County]] to take in much of heavily Republican [[Lebanon County, Pennsylvania|Lebanon County]]. However, in February 2018, the [[Pennsylvania Supreme Court]], which has a Democratic majority,<ref name=\":1\">{{Cite news|url=http://www.philly.com/philly/news/politics/pennsylvania-gerrymandering-supreme-court-congressional-map-ryan-costello-20180220.html|title=In Pa.'s new congressional map, this Republican's 'bad dream' turns into 'a nightmare' - Philly|work=Philly.com|access-date=February 22, 2018}}</ref> released a new map for the state's congressional districts to replace a map which the court had previously struck down as a Republican partisan [[gerrymander]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Prokop|first1=Andrew|title=What Pennsylvania\u2019s new congressional map means for 2018|url=https://www.vox.com/policy-and-politics/2018/2/21/17032936/pennsylvania-congressional-districts-2018|accessdate=February 23, 2018|publisher=Vox|date=February 21, 2018}}</ref> The court imposed a new map after the state legislature refused to submit a replacement. Costello was the only incumbent who retained his old district number. However, the 6th was made significantly more compact. It lost most of its heavily Republican western portion, as well as its share of Montgomery County. Instead, it now took in all of Chester County as well as most of the more Democratic portions of Berks County, including [[Reading, Pennsylvania|Reading]].<ref name=\"upshot\">{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2018/02/19/upshot/pennsylvania-new-house-districts-gerrymandering.html,%20https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2018/02/19/upshot/pennsylvania-new-house-districts-gerrymandering.html|title=The New Pennsylvania Congressional Map, District by District|last=Cohn|first=Nate|date=February 19, 2018|work=The New York Times|access-date=February 23, 2018|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref>\n\nAccording to ''[[The Philadelphia Inquirer]]'', \"Of the many Republicans who took a political blow from Pennsylvania's new congressional map, Chester County's Ryan Costello got hit the hardest.\"<ref name=\":1\"/> Costello saw his district move from having a Republican-leaning constituency to a district Hillary Clinton won by nine points.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2018/02/19/upshot/pennsylvania-new-house-districts-gerrymandering.html|title=The New Pennsylvania Congressional Map, District by District|last=Cohn|first=Nate|access-date=2018-09-26|language=en}}</ref> ''The Philadelphia Inquirer'' wrote that \"the previous, GOP-drawn map was one factor aiding Republicans as they held a firm grip on every competitive seat in the moderate Philadelphia suburbs.\"<ref name=\":1\" /> According to the ''Pottstown Mercury'', the Republican-drawn congressional map was \"widely viewed as among the nation\u2019s most gerrymandered.\"<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.pottsmerc.com/article/MP/20180220/NEWS/180229951|title=Rep. Costello calls for impeachment of Pa. justices for approving \u2018corrupt\u2019 map|access-date=February 23, 2018|language=en}}</ref> After the new congressional map was released, Costello said he supported impeaching the justices who imposed the map, calling the court corrupt and undemocratic.<ref name=\":1\" /> Republicans requested that the [[United States Supreme Court]] intervene in the redistricting dispute, although ''[[Politico]]'' reported that the Republican challenge was unlikely to be successful.<ref>{{cite news|title=Pennsylvania redistricting map challenge filed with Supreme Court|url=https://www.cbsnews.com/news/pennsylvania-redistricting-map-challenge-filed-with-supreme-court/|accessdate=February 23, 2018|agency=Associated Press|publisher=CBS News|date=February 22, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Schneider|first1=Elena|title=Republican challenge to Pennsylvania map likely to fail|url=https://www.politico.com/story/2018/02/20/pennsylvania-elections-redistricting-map-midterms-356522|accessdate=February 23, 2018|publisher=Politico|date=February 20, 2018}}</ref> In March 2018, a panel of federal judges refused to block the new congressional map from going into effect.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Kamisar|first1=Ben|title=Judges refuse GOP request to block new Pa. district boundaries|url=http://thehill.com/homenews/campaign/379127-judges-refuse-gop-request-to-block-new-pa-district-boundaries|accessdate=21 March 2018|publisher=The Hill|date=March 19, 2018}}</ref>\n\nIn March 2018, Costello filed petitions to get on the 2018 ballot,<ref>{{cite news|last1=Tamari|first1=Jonathan|last2=Otterbein|first2=Holly|last3=Seidman|first3=Andrew|title=Nearly 100 people are running for Congress in Pa. Here's how the races are shaping up.|url=http://www.philly.com/philly/news/politics/pennsylvania-congress-filing-day-costello-lamb-more-than-70-running-races-20180320.html|accessdate=21 March 2018|publisher=Philadelphia Inquirer|date=March 20, 2018}}</ref> but later that month, he announced that he was dropping his reelection bid.<ref name=\"reelection\">{{cite news| first1=Veronica| last1= Stracqualursi| first2=Eric|last2=Bradner| date=25 March 2018| title=Rep. Ryan Costello will drop bid for reelection in Pennsylvania| website=CNN| publisher=Cable News Network}}</ref> According to ''[[The Hill (newspaper)|The Hill]]'', he became frustrated with the Trump administration, which contributed to his dropping from the race.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Zanona|first1=Melanie|title=Retiring GOP lawmakers cut loose on
