A [[Democratic party (United States)|Democrat]], he has been a member of the [[United States House of Representatives]] since winning election in 2012.<ref>{{cite news|last=Nocera|first=Kate|title=Raul Ruiz win tells story of Election 2012 |url=http://www.politico.com/news/stories/1112/84020.html|accessdate=2014-05-27|newspaper=Politico|date=2012-11-18}}</ref>  In what was considered a major upset,<ref>{{cite news |last=Goad |first=Ben |date=2012-11-07 |title=Raul Ruiz unseats Mary Bono Mack in upset |url= http://www.pe.com/local-news/politics/ben-goad-headlines/20121106-congress-district-36-raul-ruiz-unseats-mary-bono-mack-in-upset.ece |dead-url=yes |newspaper=Riverside Press-Enterprise |location= |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121109171413/http://www.pe.com/local-news/politics/ben-goad-headlines/20121106-congress-district-36-raul-ruiz-unseats-mary-bono-mack-in-upset.ece |archive-date=2012-11-09 |access-date=2016-02-04}}</ref> Ruiz defeated [[gerrymandering|redistricted]], [[incumbent]] [[United States Republican Party|Republican]] representative [[Mary Bono Mack]] in the November 2012 general election in [[California's 36th congressional district]]. Ruiz was reelected to Congress in the 2014 election, after what was considered one of the most competitive congressional races in the country.<ref>{{cite news|last=Freking|first=Kevin|title=Congressional freshmen face tough challenges|url=http://www.washingtontimes.com/news/2014/may/3/congressional-freshmen-face-tough-challenges/?page=all|accessdate=2014-05-27|newspaper=Washington Times|date=2014-03-03}}</ref>\n\n==Early life and education==\nRuiz was born on August 25, 1972<ref>{{cite web|last=Kondracke |first=Morton |url=http://www.rollcall.com/new-members/raul-ruiz-d-calif-36.html |title=Raul Ruiz, D (Calif.-36) |publisher=Roll Call |date=1972-08-25 |accessdate=2013-07-22}}</ref> in [[Zacatecas]], [[Mexico]]<ref>{{cite news|last=Honore|first=Marcel|title=A look into Raul Ruiz|url=http://www.desertsun.com/article/20121208/NEWS03/312090001|dead-url=yes|newspaper=The Desert Sun|date=2012-09-12|location=Palm Springs|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140527222052/http://www.desertsun.com/article/20121208/NEWS03/312090001|archive-date=2014-05-27|access-date=2016-02-04}}</ref> and raised in [[Coachella, California]].<ref name=\"voxxi\">[http://www.voxxi.com/raul-ruiz-doctor/ Profile Raul Ruiz, first Latino to receive 3 degrees from Harvard]</ref> His parents were farmworkers.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.drraulruiz.com/about|publisher=Dr. Raul Ruiz for Congress|title=About|accessdate=2012-11-07}}</ref> He graduated from [[Coachella Valley High School]] at age 17, and went to [[UCLA]] in 1990, graduating [[magna cum laude]] before attending [[Harvard Medical School]].<ref name=voxxi/> He was the first Latino to receive three graduate degrees from [[Harvard University]]: an [[Doctor of Medicine|M.D.]] from the Harvard Medical School; an [[Master of Public Policy|M.P.P.]] from the [[Harvard Kennedy School of Government]]; and an [[Master of Public Health|M.P.H.]] from the [[Harvard School of Public Health]].<ref name=voxxi/>\n\nIn 1997, while attending Harvard as a medical student, Ruiz participated in an annual [[Thanksgiving]] protest in [[Plymouth, Massachusetts]]. Ruiz was one of 25 people arrested at the event. The charges were later dropped as part of a deal that also dismissed claims of police brutality.<ref name=\"desert\">{{cite news|last=McGinty|first=Kate|title=Mary Bono Mack and Raul Ruiz: A fact check on the congressional debate - |url=http://isun.blogs.mydesert.com/2012/10/12/mary-bono-mack-and-raul-ruiz-a-fact-check-on-the-congressional-debate/|accessdate=2014-04-25|newspaper=MyDesert.com|date=2012-10-12}}</ref>\n\nTwo years later, in 1999, Ruiz took part in another Thanksgiving protest at which he read a letter of support for [[Leonard Peltier]], who had been convicted and sentenced to two consecutive terms of life imprisonment for first-degree murder in the shooting of two [[Federal Bureau of Investigation]] (FBI) special agents during a 1975 conflict on the [[Pine Ridge Indian Reservation]]. During the 2012 congressional campaign, he was criticized for this activity by his opponent, Mary Bono Mack. In response, Ruiz's campaign stated that he did not support Peltier.<ref name=\"desert\"/><ref>{{cite news|last=Clarke|first=Chris|title=Coachella Valley Congress Race Turns Nasty|url=http://www.kcet.org/updaily/socal_focus/government/coachella-valley-congress-race-turns-nasty.html|accessdate=2014-08-19|publisher=KCET|date=2012-10-16}}</ref>\n\n==Medical career==\nAfter graduating from [[Harvard University]], Ruiz spent time working abroad in [[Mexico]], [[El Salvador]], and [[Serbia]], before taking a job as an emergency physician at the [[Eisenhower Medical Center]], a nonprofit hospital in the Coachella Valley. He founded the Coachella Valley Healthcare Initiative in 2010. In 2011, he became senior associate dean at the School of Medicine at [[University of California, Riverside]].<ref name=voxxi/><ref name=\"wt\">{{cite news|url=http://www.washingtontimes.com/campaign-2012/candidates/raul-ruiz-62342/ |publisher=The Washington Times |accessdate=7 November 2012 |title=Raul Ruiz |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130110184110/http://www.washingtontimes.com/campaign-2012/candidates/raul-ruiz-62342/ |archivedate=January 10, 2013 }}</ref>\n\nIn 2012, he received a [[Commander's Award for Public Service]] from the U.S. Army's [[82nd Airborne Division]] for his humanitarian efforts for victims of the [[2010 Haiti earthquake]].<ref name=voxxi/> Ruiz's background as a physician has gained him attention for several incidents in which he has provided medical aid to fellow airline passengers, including conservative columnist [[Lisa De Pasquale]].<ref>http://dailycaller.com/2014/07/18/dem-lawmaker-rushes-to-save-conservative-journo-during-seizure/</ref>\n\n==U.S. House of Representatives==\n\n===Elections===\n\n;2012\nRuiz ran for the [[United States House of Representatives]] in 2012 as a first-time candidate in {{ushr|CA|36}}. The district had previously been the 45th, represented by 15-year incumbent [[Mary Bono|Mary Bono Mack]]. Ruiz was initially regarded as a long shot to win.<ref name=Clinton/> He was endorsed by [[Bill Clinton]] in October 2012.<ref name=Clinton>{{cite news|last1=Terlecky|first1=Megan|title=Fmr Pres. Clinton Endorses Ruiz for Congress|url=http://www.kesq.com/news/-Fmr-Pres-Clinton-Endorses-Ruiz-for-Congress/17108768|accessdate=19 August 2014|publisher=KESQ|date=23 October 2012}}</ref> He appealed to the district's Latinos, who make up nearly half its population, by running Spanish-language ads.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Bergman|first1=Ben|title=Congressman and physician Raul Ruiz comes home to Palm Springs to treat constituents and patients|url=http://www.scpr.org/news/2013/04/23/36878/congressman-and-physician-raul-ruiz-comes-home-to/|website=KPCC|accessdate=5 May 2018}}</ref> He was elected with 52.9% of the vote, to Bono Mack's 47.1%.<ref>{{cite web|title=Rep. Raul Ruiz (D-Calif.)|url=http://www.rollcall.com/members/45281.html|website=Roll Call|accessdate=19 August 2014}}</ref>\n\n\"If the growing sway of Latinos in American politics was the story of election 2012,\" wrote ''[[Politico]]'' after the 2012 election, \"Raul Ruiz's triumph in California's 36th Congressional District was a dramatic subplot.\" Republicans \"didn't seem to fully appreciate the district's fast-growing Hispanic population until it was too late.\" Ruiz told ''Politico'' that his victory was \"a reflection of America.\"<ref>{{cite web|last1=Nocera|first1=Kate|title=Ruiz's win tells story of Election 2012|url=https://www.politico.com/story/2012/11/ruizs-win-tells-story-of-election-2012-084020|website=Politico|accessdate=6 May 2018}}</ref> Upon taking office in January 2013, Ruiz became the first Democrat to represent this district since its creation in 1983 (it had been the 37th from 1983 to 1993, the 44th from 1993 to 2003, and the 45th from 2003 to 2013).\n\n;2014\nRuiz ran for re-election in 2014. He competed in the top-two primary on June 3, 2014, finishing first with 50.3% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|title=Statement of Vote|url=http://www.sos.ca.gov/elections/sov/2014-primary/pdf/2014-complete-sov.pdf|website=Secretary of State's Office|publisher=State of California|accessdate=8 August 2014|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140804045652/http://www.sos.ca.gov/elections/sov/2014-primary/pdf/2014-complete-sov.pdf|archivedate=4 August 2014|df=}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Cahn|first1=Emily|title=Primary Results: California House Races (Updated)|url=http://atr.rollcall.com/california-primary-results-2014/|accessdate=19 August 2014|publisher=Roll Call|date=4 June 2014}}</ref> Ruiz then faced Republican nominee and state assemblyman [[Brian Nestande]] in the general election, which took place November 4, 2014.<ref>{{cite news|last=Blumenthal|first=Paul|title=Vulnerable House Incumbents Raising Big Money For 2014 Races|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/07/16/2014-election-house-incumbents_n_3604746.html|accessdate=27 May 2014|newspaper=Huffington Post|date=2013-07-16}}</ref> Despite being considered one of the most vulnerable incumbent members of the House of Representatives, Ruiz was re-elected with 54.2% of the vote, to Nestande's 45.8%.\n\n; 2016\nHis 2016 campaign focused largely on his successful attempt to secure funds for the [[Salton Sea]] Red Hill Bay restoration project and his efforts on behalf of veterans.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Marx|first1=Jesse|title=Rep. Raul Ruiz wins reelection to Congress|url=https://www.desertsun.com/story/news/politics/elections/2016/11/09/rep-raul-ruiz-leading-significant-margin/93214680/|website=The Desert Sun|accessdate=6 May 2018}}</ref>\n\nRuiz was elected to a third term in November 2016, receiving 60% of the vote.<ref name=\"wins reelection\">{{cite|newspaper=USA Today|title=Rep. Raul Ruiz wins reelection to Congress|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/politics/elections/2016/11/09/rep-raul-ruiz-leading-significant-margin/93214680/|author=Jesse Marks|date=November 9, 2016}}</ref>\n\nAfter winning on Election Night 2016, Ruiz spoke critically about \"the politics of fear\" and \"hateful rhetoric.\" Addressing his supporters in [[Rancho Mirage, California|Rancho Mirage]], he said, \"I believe that we need to come together as a nation. I believe we need to heal our wounds and put people above partisanship and solutions above ideology.\"<ref name=\"wins reelection\" />\n\n;2018\n\nIn October 2017, soap-opera actress [[Kimberlin Brown]], a pro-Trump Republican, announced that she would challenge Ruiz in 2018. Criticizing Ruiz for not passing any \"meaningful\" legislation, Brown said, \"For the first time in the history of our great country, we are not leaving something better behind for the next generation.\" Brown, known for \"[[The Bold and the Beautiful]]\", runs a design firm and has co-managed an [[avocado]] farm with her husband.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Garcia|first1=Eric|title=Soap Actress and Trump Surrogate to Challenge Ruiz|url=https://www.rollcall.com/news/politics/soap-actress-and-trump-surrogate-to-challenge-ruiz|website=Roll Call|accessdate=6 May 2018}}</ref>\n\n===Tenure===\n====Manufacturing jobs====\nIn April 2013, Ruiz introduced his first bill, the SelectUSA Authorization Act of 2013. The bill would incentivize international corporations to invest in creating manufacturing jobs in the United States rather than overseas.<ref>http://www.kesq.com/news/Rep-Raul-Ruiz-introduces-his-first-bill-in-Washington/19682182</ref> The bill has not been voted on by the House.<ref>{{cite web|title=H.R.1413: SelectUSA Authorization Act of 2013|url=http://politics.nytimes.com/congress/bills/113/hr1413|website=New York Times|accessdate=13 October 2014}}</ref>\n\n====CISPA====\nIn April 2013, Ruiz voted for [[CISPA]], which would allow for the sharing of Internet traffic information between the U.S. government and technology and manufacturing companies.<ref>{{cite web|title=H R 624|url=http://projects.washingtonpost.com/congress/113/house/1/votes/117/|website=Washington Post|accessdate=19 August 2014}}</ref>\n\n====Health care====\nHe has said he approaches the job of congressman \"through the lens of a scientist, viewing gun control and other controversial issues from a public health perspective.\"<ref name=\"wins reelection\" />\n\nIn May 2013, Ruiz voted against repeal of the [[Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act]].<ref>{{cite news | url=http://thehill.com/blogs/healthwatch/politics-elections/304727-nrcc-hits-calif-dems-over-obamacare-rates | title=NRCC hits Calif. Dems over ObamaCare rates | author=Sam Baker | date=11 June 2013 | accessdate=4 November 2013 | publisher=The Hill}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2013/roll154.xml | title=Final Vote Results for Roll Call 154 | accessdate=4 November 2013 | publisher=House.gov}}</ref> \nDuring his 2012 campaign for Congress, Ruiz stated his support for the [[Affordable Care Act]].\n\nIn 2014, Ruiz voted against the [[Pain-Capable Unborn Child Protection Act]], which would ban abortions that take place 20 or more weeks after fertilization.<ref>{{cite web|title=House Vote 251 - Approves New Abortion Restrictions|url=http://politics.nytimes.com/congress/votes/113/house/1/251|website=New York Times|accessdate=19 August 2014}}</ref>\n\nIn 2017, Ruiz described Obamacare as \"a giant step in the right direction\" while acknowledging that \"it is imperfect and needs to be improved.\" He maintained that the GOP plan would \"make premiums and deductibles go up even higher, 24 million will be uninsured...and there will be reduced reimbursement rates to hospitals and doctors for patients on Medicaid...There's nothing to reduce health care costs and out-of-pocket payments.\" Ruiz felt that Obamacare had represented \"one of the largest improvements in covering Latinos with health insurance.\"<ref>{{cite web|last1=Gamboa|first1=Suzanne|title=Raul Ruiz, Only Latino Doctor in Congress, Troubled By GOP Health Plan|url=https://www.nbcnews.com/news/latino/raul-ruiz-only-latino-doctor-congress-troubled-gop-health-plan-n736051|website=NBC News|accessdate=7 May 2018}}</ref>\n\n====Iran deal====\nDuring the 2016 campaign, Ruiz's GOP opponent, Jeff Stone, called the Iran deal \"one of the most horrific acts of Congress,\" and criticized Ruiz for having promised not to support it and having ended up voting for it. In a debate, \"Stone called Ruiz one of Iran's best friends and asked what he would say to the families of people who had been put to death by the Iranian regime for being gay, lesbian, Christian or Jewish.\" Stone stated that \"Ruiz's support of the [[Iran nuclear deal]] was the reason he got into the race.\"<ref>{{cite web|last1=Kennedy|first1=Corinne S|title=Ruiz, Stone have heated exchange on Iran nuclear deal|url=https://www.desertsun.com/story/news/politics/elections/2016/10/13/ruiz-stone-have-heated-exchange-iran-nuclear-deal/91994486/|website=The Desert Sun|accessdate=7 May 2018}}</ref>\n\n====Syrian refugees====\nRuiz voted on Nov. 19, 2015, for HR 4038, legislation that would effectively halt the resettlement of refugees from Syria and Iraq to the United participating in a Thanksgiving Day protest against the treatment of American Indians.\" She stated that Ruiz, \"dressed in Aztec warrior colors,\u201d had taken part for six years in these Thanksgiving Day protests and \"encouraged people to smash Plymouth Rock.\" At a debate, Bono Mack asked \"Dr. Ruiz, who are you?\"<ref>{{cite web|title=Police officials criticize Ruiz|url=https://www.newspapers.com/newspage/129790311/|website=The Desert Sun|accessdate=7 May 2018}}</ref>\n\nAt an October 2012 press conference, Bono Mack campaign officials released an audiotape on which Ruiz expressed solidarity with convicted police murderer [[Mumia Abu-Jamal]] and read a letter of support for [[Leonard Peltier]], who had been convicted in 1977 of murdering two FBI agents in [[South Dakota]]. And for the powerful, this is a crime, because knowing oneself with history impedes from being tossed around by this absurd machine that is in the system.\" A spokesman for Ruiz maintained that the candidate did not recall the incident, and that he did not support Peltier.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Goad|first1=Ben|title=VIDEO: Police unions question Ruiz\u2019s character,
