Comer Jr.'''<ref name=\"VoteSmart\">{{cite web |url=https://votesmart.org/candidate/biography/35169/james-comer-jr#.VT02HJOjdlE |title=James Comer, Jr.'s Biography |publisher=Votesmart.org|accessdate=May 2, 2015}}</ref> {{IPAc-en|\u02c8|k|o\u028a|m|\u0259r}} (born August 19, 1972) is an American politician from the Commonwealth of [[Kentucky]] who currently represents the state's  [[Kentucky's 1st congressional district|1st congressional district]] in the [[United States House of Representatives]]. He previously served as the Agriculture Commissioner of Kentucky from 2012 to 2016 and in the [[Kentucky House of Representatives]] from 2000 to 2012.\n\nComer sought the Republican nomination for [[Governor of Kentucky]] in the [[Kentucky gubernatorial election, 2015|2015 election]]. After losing, he successfully sought the Republican nomination for {{ushr|KY|1}} to succeed fellow Republican [[Ed Whitfield]]. On November 8, 2016, Comer won both a full term to the seat for the next Congress and also a special election which allowed him to serve the remainder of Whitfield's term.\n\n==Early life and education==\nComer is a native of [[Carthage, Tennessee]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://bioguide.congress.gov/scripts/biodisplay.pl?index=C001108|title=Biographical Directory of the United States Congress}}</ref>  He received a bachelor's degree in agriculture from [[Western Kentucky University]] in 1993.<ref name=wkyu />  In college, he served as President of the Kentucky [[Future Farmers of America]]. After college, he started James Comer, Jr. Farms,<ref name=Mays>{{cite web|url=https://maysville.kctcs.edu/news/ky-commissioner-of-agriculture-james-comer-to-keynote-farm--family-night-at-mctc.aspx|title=KY Commissioner of Agriculture James Comer to Keynote Farm  Family Night at MCTC|website=maysville.kctcs.edu}}</ref> a {{convert|950|ha|order=flip}} farm with his family,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.paducahsun.com/PrintPage/042716_PS_Comer-for-Congress|title=Comer combines experience in bid|website=www.paducahsun.com}}</ref> and he is also co-owner of Comer Land & Cattle Co.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://kyfbnewsroom.com/comer-commemorates-may-beef-month-in-kentucky/|title=Comer commemorates May beef month in Kentucky|date=May 10, 2013|publisher=}}</ref> He also served as a director of the South Central Bank for twelve years.<ref name=wkyu>{{cite web|url=http://wkyufm.org/post/james-comer-s-quest-pass-bold-agenda-gets-bumpy#stream/0|title=James Comer's Quest To 'Pass A Bold Agenda' Gets Bumpy|first=Ryland|last=Barton|publisher=}}</ref> He served as President of the Monroe County Chamber of Commerce from 1999 to 2000.<ref name=wkyu />\n\n==Kentucky House of Representatives ==\nIn 2000 Comer was elected to the [[Kentucky House of Representatives]] at the age of 27.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.courier-journal.com/story/news/politics/elections/kentucky/2015/05/07/james-comer-confident-despite-gubernatorial-campaign-turbulence/70953062/|title=Comer confident despite campaign 'turbulence'|publisher=}}</ref>\n\n==Kentucky Commissioner of Agriculture==\n[[File:James Comer Kentucky National Guard.jpg|thumb|left|Comer speaking to members of the [[Kentucky National Guard]] in 2013]]\nIn 2011, Comer ran for the position of Agriculture Commissioner; the incumbent, [[Richie Farmer]], was [[Term limits in the United States|term limited]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kentucky.com/news/politics-government/article44096538.html|title=Comer and Bob Farmer will face off for agriculture commissioner|publisher=}}</ref> In the election, Comer was the only Republican to win election to a statewide executive office,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://clintonnews.net/pages/?p=3858|title=2011 \u2013 The Year In Review|publisher=}}</ref> and worked with a team of Democratic officials and under a Democratic Governor.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kyforward.com/ag-commissioner-james-comer-ending-first-year-in-office-as-it-began-full-steam-ahead/|title=Ag Commissioner James Comer ending first year in office as it began \u2013 full steam ahead - KyForward.com|website=www.kyforward.com}}</ref> Comer had the highest percentage of the vote of any candidate on the ballot. Prior to the primary, he was endorsed by both the National Rifle Association and the US Chamber of Commerce.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://wkms.org/post/audio-james-comer-talks-bid-1st-district-congressional-seat-ahead-may-17-primary|title=[AUDIO] James Comer Talks Bid for 1st District Congressional Seat Ahead of May 17 Primary|first=John|last=Null|publisher=}}</ref> In the end he won the primary with 60.6% of the vote.<ref name=Atl /> Comer was elected to the United States House of Representatives in Kentucky's District 1 with 72.6% of the vote,<ref name=WKM>{{cite web|url=http://wkms.org/post/congressman-james-comer-talks-president-trump-trade-deals-regulations-hemp|title=Congressman James Comer Talks President Trump, Trade Deals, Regulations, Hemp|first=Matt|last=Markgraf|publisher=}}</ref> defeating his Democratic opponent Samuel L. Gaskins.
