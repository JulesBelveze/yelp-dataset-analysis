Previously she served in the [[California State Assembly]] from 2006 to 2012.<ref name=autogenerated1>[http://www.juliabrownley.com Julia Brownley Councilmember 41st Assembly District], juliabrownley.com; accessed November 15, 2014.</ref> She is a [[United States Democratic Party|Democrat]]. Before her political career, she worked in marketing and sales.<ref name=nj>{{cite news|title=California, 26th House District|url=http://www.nationaljournal.com/congress-legacy/california-26th-house-district-20121101|accessdate=August 29, 2014|publisher=National Journal|date=November 1, 2012}}</ref>\n\n==Early life, education, and career==\nBrownley grew up in Virginia, and attended [[Fairfax Hall]], an established girls [[Boarding School]] in [[Waynesboro, Virginia|Waynesboro]], for four years. She came from a Republican family. Brownley receive a bachelor's degree in political science from [[Mount Vernon College for Women]] of [[George Washington University]] in 1975 and a master's degree in business administration from [[American University]] in 1979.<ref name=nj/><ref name=wsj>{{cite web|title=Julia Brownley (D)|url=http://projects.wsj.com/campaign2012/candidates/view/julia-brownley--CA-H|publisher=Wall Street Journal|accessdate=August 29, 2014}}</ref>\n\nBrownley served on the [[Santa Monica-Malibu Unified School District]] Board of Education from 1994 to 2006. During her time on the school board, she served three terms as president.<ref>{{cite web|title=Rep. Julia Brownley (D-Calif.)|url=http://www.rollcall.com/members/45252.html|publisher=Roll Call|accessdate=August 29, 2014}}</ref>\n\n==California State Assembly (2007\u20132013)==\n[[File:Julia Brownley 2011.jpg|left|thumb|Brownley during her tenure in the California State Assembly]]\n\n===Elections===\nIn 2006, she ran for the [[California State Assembly]] in [[California's 41st Assembly District]].<ref name=wsj/> Brownley won a five-way Democratic primary with 35% of the vote.<ref name=primary>{{cite web|title=California House of Representatives elections, 2006|url=http://ballotpedia.org/California_House_of_Representatives_elections,_2006|website=Ballotpedia|publisher=Ballotpedia|accessdate=August 29, 2014}}</ref> Brownley won the general election with 62% of the vote.<ref name=primary/><ref>{{cite news|title=Election 2008: Results|url=http://articles.latimes.com/2008/nov/06/local/me-resultsb06/8|accessdate=September 13, 2014|publisher=Los Angeles Times|date=November 6, 2008}}</ref> In 2008, she won re-election to a second term with 66% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|title=November 4, 2008 General Election - Statement of Vote |url=http://www.sos.ca.gov/elections/sov/2008-general/index.htm |publisher=California Secretary of State |accessdate=August 29, 2014 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141130050219/http://www.sos.ca.gov/elections/sov/2008-general/index.htm |archivedate=November 30, 2014 |df= }}</ref> In 2010, she won re-election to a third term with 59% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|title=November 2, 2010 General Election - Statement of Vote|url=http://www.sos.ca.gov/elections/sov/2010-general/|publisher=California Secretary of State|accessdate=August 29, 2014|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140810105857/http://www.sos.ca.gov/elections/sov/2010-general/|archivedate=August 10, 2014|df=mdy-all}}</ref> Brownley was term-limited in 2012, having served the maximum of three terms in the Assembly permitted under California law.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Fauquher|first1=Tom|title=Torie Osborn Picks Up Key Endorsement in Bid for Malibu's State Assembly Seat|url=http://patch.com/california/malibu/torie-osborn-picks-up-key-endorsement-in-bid-for-mali93986a8a93#.VAAPXEh8Gnc|accessdate=29 August 2014|publisher=Malibu Patch|date=2011-06-13}}</ref>\n\nShe was endorsed by the [[League of Conservation Voters]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Candidates make final push in Assembly race|url=http://www.malibutimes.com/news/article_f134698b-fdb9-5fb1-a201-825bf0dfda7a.html?mode=jqm|accessdate=September 13, 2014|publisher=Malibu Times|date=May 31, 2006}}</ref>\n\n===Tenure===\nIn 2010, Brownley authored a bill that would have banned all plastic shopping bags. The bill did not pass.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Francis|first1=Enjoli|title=California Strikes Down Proposal to Ban Plastic Bags|url=http://abcnews.go.com/US/california-votes-plastic-bag-ban/story?id=11526792|accessdate=September 10, 2014|publisher=ABC News|date=September 1, 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Yamamura|first1=Kevin|title=Assembly Democrat wants grocery store ban on plastic bags|url=http://blogs.sacbee.com/capitolalertlatest/2013/01/assembly-democrat-wants-grocery-ban-on-plastic-bags.html|accessdate=10 September 2014|publisher=Sacramento Bee|date=2013-01-22}}</ref>\n\n===Committee assignments===\nBrownley served on the following California State Assembly committees:<ref>{{cite web|title=Julia Brownley|url=http://ballotpedia.org/Julia_Brownley|website=Ballotpedia|publisher=Ballotpedia|accessdate=August 29, 2014}}</ref>\n*Aging and Long-Term Care Committee\n*Education Committee (Chair)\n*Higher Education Committee\n*Judiciary Committee\n*Legislative Budget Committee\n*Natural Resources Joint Legislative Budget Committee\n*Select Committee on Community Colleges\n\n==U.S. House of Representatives (2013\u2013present)==\n===Elections===\n[[File:Julia Brownley 113th Congress official photo.jpg|thumb|Brownley's freshman portrait]]\n\n====2012====\n{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in California, 2012#District 26}}\nIn February 2012, Brownley announced her candidacy for [[California's 26th congressional district]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vcstar.com/news/2012/feb/19/assemblywoman-joins-26th-congressional-district/|title=Assemblywoman joins 26th Congressional District race|date=February 19, 2012|accessdate=February 20, 2012|work=[[Ventura County Star]]|first=Timm|last=Herdt}}</ref> The district had previously been the [[California's 24th congressional district|24th district]], represented by 13-term [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] [[Elton Gallegly]]. In the general election, Brownley defeated Republican [[Tony Strickland]] by a margin of 53%-47%.<ref>{{cite web|title=California 26th District - Strickland vs. Brownley|url=http://www.realclearpolitics.com/epolls/2012/house/ca/california_26th_district_strickland_vs_brownley-3282.html|publisher=Real Clear Politics|accessdate=29 August 2014}}</ref>\n\nShe was endorsed by [[Emily's List]]<ref>{{cite news|last1=Merl|first1=Jean|title=Emily's List backs Julia Brownley for Congress|url=http://latimesblogs.latimes.com/california-politics/2012/04/emilys-list-backs-julia-brownley-for-congress.html|accessdate=13 September 2014|publisher=Los Angeles Times|date=April 20, 2012}}</ref> and [[Planned Parenthood]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Brownley the better choice|url=http://www.thecamarilloacorn.com/news/2012-10-12/Letters/Brownley_the_better_choice.html|accessdate=September 13, 2014|publisher=Camarillo Acorn|date=October 12, 2012}}</ref>\n\n====2014====\n{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in California, 2014#District 26}}\nBrownley ran for re-election in 2014, facing Republican [[Jeff Gorell]] in the general election.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Modesti|first1=Kevin|title=Brownley vs. Gorell congressional race will get attention now|url=http://www.dailynews.com/opinion/20140618/brownley-vs-gorell-congressional-race-will-get-attention-now-opinion|accessdate=29 August 2014|publisher=Los Angeles Daily News|date=2014-06-18}}</ref> Brownley is a member of the [[Democratic Congressional Campaign Committee]]'s Frontline Program, which is designed to help protect vulnerable Democratic incumbents. After Republican candidates garnered over 50% of the vote in the June 2014 blanket primary, the [[Cook Political Report]] changed the rating of the race from \"Leans Democratic\" to \"Toss-up.\"<ref>{{cite news|last1=Livingston|first1=Abby|title=At the Races \u2014 Roll Call's Politics Blog DCCC Announces 26 Members on Frontline Incumbent Retention Program|url=http://atr.rollcall.com/dccc-announces-26-members-on-frontline-incumbent-retention-program/|accessdate=10 September 2014|publisher=Roll Call|date=March 5, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=2014 HOUSE RACE RATINGS FOR SEPTEMBER 12, 2014|url=http://cookpolitical.com/house/charts/race-ratings|accessdate=September 13, 2014|publisher=Cook Political Report|date=September 12, 2014}}</ref>  The election did become a close race, making for closer scrutiny of the results.  Initial results show Brownley winning 51% to 49%, with about 4,000 votes separating the candidates.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://vote.sos.ca.gov/returns/us-rep/district/26/ |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2014-11-15 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141118072104/http://vote.sos.ca.gov/returns/us-rep/district/26/ |archivedate=November 18, 2014 |df= }}</ref>\n\n===Political positions===\nBrownley supports the [[DREAM Act]], the [[Affordable Care Act]], and same-sex marriage.
