(n\u00e9e Haring) and Donald Tyler Rohrabacher.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.ancestry.com/~battle/reps/rohrabacher.htm|title=rohrabacher|publisher=freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.ancestry.com}}</ref> He attended elementary school locally, and during his college years, he lived in [[Sunset Beach, California|Sunset Beach]].\n\n[[File:U.S. Congressman Dana Rohrabacher performing guitar for an event at the University of California, Irvine.jpg|thumb|Rohrabacher plays guitar for attendees at the 2016 [[Young Americans for Liberty]] California State Convention]]\nRohrabacher graduated from [[Palos Verdes High School]] in [[Palos Verdes Estates, California]], attended Los Angeles Harbor College, and earned a [[Bachelor of Arts|bachelor's degree]] in history at [[California State University, Long Beach]] in 1969. He received his [[Master of Arts|master's degree]] in [[American Studies]] at the [[University of Southern California]].\n[[File:FRC President Tony Perkins presents the \"True Blue\" award to Congressman Dana Rohrabacher.jpg|thumb|right|Rohrabacher receiving the True Blue award from [[Family Research Council|FRC]] President [[Tony Perkins (politician)|Tony Perkins]] in 2003]]\n[[File:U.S. Congressman Dana Rohrabacher, President Ronald Reagan's speechwriter, and Congressman Ed Royce with Statue of Reagan in Newport Beach, CA in 2011.jpg|thumb|Rohrabacher and [[Ed Royce]] with Statue of President [[Ronald Reagan]] in [[Newport Beach, California]] in 2011]]\nWhile in graduate school and during the early 1970s, he had a side activity as a folk singer.<ref>{{cite book|last=Doherty|first=Brian|title=[[Radicals for Capitalism: A Freewheeling History of the Modern American Libertarian Movement]]|date=April 28, 2009|publisher=PublicAffairs}}</ref> He was also a writer for the ''[[Orange County Register]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Dana Rohrbacher \u2013 Early Life|url=http://www.rohrabacher.house.gov/about/full-biography}}</ref> During this time he was considered a [[Anarcho-capitalism|free-market anarchist]] and [[Libertarianism in the United States|libertarian]] activist after his previous membership in [[Young Americans for Freedom]]. Libertarian author [[Samuel Konkin]] recalled Rohrabacher as \"a charismatic campus activist, radicalized by [[Robert LeFevre]] who provided him with small funding to travel the country with his instrument and folk songs from campus to campus, converting YAF chapters into [[Libertarian Alliance]]s and [[International Society for Individual Liberty|SIL]] chapters.\"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.spaz.org/~dan/individualist-anarchist/software/konkin-interview.html|title=Interview With Samuel Edward Konkin III|publisher=spaz.org|accessdate=June 22, 2015}}</ref>\n\n==U.S. House of Representatives==\n\n===Elections===\nRohrabacher left the Reagan administration in 1988 to pursue the open House seat recently vacated by [[Dan Lungren]].
