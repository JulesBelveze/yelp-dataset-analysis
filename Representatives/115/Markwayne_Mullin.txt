He owns several businesses, which he took over at twenty, when his father became ill. Mullin, a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]], was elected to the U.S. House of Representatives in the [[United States House of Representatives elections in Oklahoma, 2012|2012 elections]], succeeding [[Democratic Party (US)|Democratic]] representative [[Dan Boren]].\n\n==Early life and education==\nMullin was born on July 26, 1977 in [[Tulsa, Oklahoma]].<ref name=rollcall/> He graduated from [[Stilwell High School]] in [[Stilwell, Oklahoma]].<ref name=newsok>{{cite web |url=http://blog.newsok.com/politics/2012/10/11/markwayne-mullin-tapped-to-give-national-republican-address/ |title=Markwayne Mullin Tapped to Give National Republican Address &#124; .Politics |publisher=Blog.newsok.com |date=2012-10-16 |accessdate=2012-11-08 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://archive.is/20121216104445/http://blog.newsok.com/politics/2012/10/11/markwayne-mullin-tapped-to-give-national-republican-address/ |archivedate=2012-12-16 |df= }}</ref> He attended [[Missouri Valley College]] in 1996, but did not graduate.<ref name=rollcall/>  Mullin received an [[Associate in Applied Science|associate in applied sciences]] degree in construction technology from [[Oklahoma State University Institute of Technology]], in 2010.<ref name=rollcall/><ref>[http://bioguide.congress.gov/scripts/biodisplay.pl?index=M001190 MULLIN, Markwayne, (1977 - )] Biographical Directory of the United States Congress. 1774-Present. Retrieved April 13, 2017</ref>\n\n==Business career==\nMullin took over his family's business, Mullin Plumbing, at the age of twenty, when his father fell ill. He also owns Mullin Properties, Mullin Farms and Mullin Services.<ref>{{cite news | url=http://www.kjrh.com/dpp/news/local_news/markwayne-mullin-wins-district-2-congressional-seat | title=Markwayne Mullin wins District 2 Congressional seat | work=KJRH 2 | date=November 7, 2012 | accessdate=November 8, 2012 | publisher=Scripps TV Station Group | deadurl=yes | archiveurl=https://archive.is/20130127212939/http://www.kjrh.com/dpp/news/local_news/markwayne-mullin-wins-district-2-congressional-seat | archivedate=January 27, 2013 | df= }}</ref>\n\nHe hosted ''House Talk'', a [[home improvement]] radio program, on Tulsa station [[KFAQ]] and syndicated across Oklahoma.<ref name=rollcall>{{cite web|author=November 7, 2012 |url=http://www.rollcall.com/new-members/markwayne-mullin-house-r-okla-2.html |title=Markwayne Mullin |publisher=Roll Call |date=1977-07-26 |accessdate=2012-11-08}}</ref><ref name=\"about\">{{cite web|url=http://www.mullinforcongress.com/about-mullin|title=Meet the Mullin Family|publisher=Markwayne Mullin for Congress|accessdate=August 9, 2013|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130614090725/http://www.mullinforcongress.com/about-mullin/|archivedate=June 14, 2013|df=}}</ref>\n\n==U.S. House of Representatives==\n\n===2012 election===\n{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Oklahoma, 2012}}\nIncumbent [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] U.S. congressman [[Dan Boren]] decided to retire in 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.krmg.com/news/news/local/markwayne-mullin-makes-congressional-bid-official/nDfR2/ |title=Markwayne Mullin makes Congressional bid official |publisher=www.krmg.com |date= |accessdate=2012-11-08}}</ref> Mullin declared his candidacy for the [[United States House of Representatives elections in Oklahoma, 2012|2012 elections]] to the [[United States House of Representatives]] to represent [[Oklahoma's 2nd congressional district]] in September 2011. In the six-candidate Republican primary, Mullin ranked first with 42% of the vote, failing to reach the over 50% threshold. State representative [[George Faught]] ranked second with 22% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=735568 |title=OK District 2 \u2013 R Primary Race \u2013 Jun 26, 2012 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2013-08-30}}</ref> In the run-off primary election, Mullin defeated Faught 57%\u201343%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=771199 |title=OK District 2 \u2013 R Runoff Race \u2013 Aug 28, 2012 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2013-08-30}}</ref><ref>[http://www.ok.gov/elections/support/ok_results_seb.html State Election Results, Runoff], [http://www.ok.gov/elections Oklahoma State Elections Board].</ref> He branded himself as an outsider; his campaign slogan was \"A rancher. A businessman. Not a politician!\"<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20111211151101/http://www.mullinforcongress.com:80/about-mullin/ Archive] of Mullin's campaign site from 2012</ref>\n\nThe second has historically been a classic [[Yellow Dog Democrat|\"Yellow Dog\" Democratic]] district.  However, it has steadily trended Republican, as [[Tulsa]]'s suburbs have spilled into the northern portion of the district. For these reasons, Mullin was thought to have a good chance of winning the election. In the general election, Mullin defeated the Democratic candidate, Rob Wallace, a former [[District Attorney|district attorney]], 57%\u201338%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=705055 |title=OK \u2013 District 02 Race \u2013 Nov 06, 2012 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2013-08-30}}</ref> He became the first Republican to represent the district, since [[Tom Coburn]] in 2001,<ref name=\"tulsa\">{{cite news | url=http://www.tulsaworld.com/news/article.aspx?subjectid=262&articleid=20121107_16_EC3_CUTLIN289219 | title=Republican Markwayne Mullin voted into 2nd District Seat | work=Tulsa World | date=November 7, 2012 | accessdate=November 8, 2012 | author=Krehbiel, Mark}}</ref> and only the second since 1921.\n\n===Miami Tribe revocation===\n\nOn February 5, 2014, Mullin introduced the bill [[To revoke the charter of incorporation of the Miami Tribe of Oklahoma at the request of that tribe (H.R. 4002; 113th Congress)]], which would accept the request of the [[Miami Tribe of Oklahoma]] to revoke the charter of incorporation issued to that tribe and ratified by its members on June 1, 1940.<ref name=4002sum>{{cite web|title=H.R. 4002 \u2013 Summary|url=https://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/4002|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=23 June 2014}}</ref>\n\n===2017 town hall comments===\nIn April 2017, Mullin drew criticism when he was recorded during a town hall meeting telling his constituents that it was \"bullcrap\" that taxpayers pay his salary.
