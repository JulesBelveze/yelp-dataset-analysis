He was a member of both the [[Governorship of George W. Bush|gubernatorial]] and [[Presidency of George W. Bush|presidential]] administrations of [[George W. Bush]].<ref name=\"tech leader\">{{cite web|url=http://lubbockonline.com/local-news/2010-11-28/tech-leader-looks-back-katrina|title=Tech leader looks back on Katrina|work=Lubbock Online - Lubbock Avalanche-Journal|accessdate=April 28, 2016}}</ref> Arrington was named appointments manager for Governor Bush in 1996. In 2000, he was appointed Special Assistant to the President and Associate Director of Presidential Personnel.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-srv/onpolitics/transcripts/whitehousesalaries_high_low.htm|title=OnPolitics (washingtonpost.com)|publisher=|accessdate=April 28, 2016}}</ref> In December 2001, [[Donald E. Powell]], the 18th Chairman of the [[Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation]] hired Arrington as the agency's chief of staff.<ref name=\"johnhopebryant.com\">{{cite web|url=http://www.johnhopebryant.com/john_hope_bryant_/2005/11/new_golf_coast_.html|title=New Golf Coast Recovery Chief is a Friend to HOPE|work=The John Hope Bryant Blog|accessdate=April 28, 2016}}</ref>\n\nHe later served as deputy federal coordinator for the [[Office of the Federal Coordinator for Gulf Coast Rebuilding]]. In 2006, Arrington left the coastal rebuilding office to return to his alma mater, [[Texas Tech University]] as its system chief of staff and later as vice chancellor for research and commercialization. Until his election to Congress, Arrington was the president of Scott Laboratories in Lubbock.\n\n==Early life and education==\nArrington was reared in [[Plainview, Texas|Plainview]] in [[Hale County, Texas|Hale County]] on the South Texas Plains to Gene and Betty Arrington. His father played [[basketball]] at Texas Tech,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.texastech.com/sports/m-baskbl/archive/text-m-baskbl-a-lettermen.html|title=TEXASTECH.COM  - Texas Tech University Official Athletic Site|publisher=|accessdate=April 28, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.texastech.com/sports/m-baskbl/archive/text-m-baskbl-a-confindrec.html|title=TEXASTECH.COM  - Texas Tech University Official Athletic Site|publisher=|accessdate=April 28, 2016}}</ref> having lettered in 1958, 1959, and 1960<ref>http://www.lostlettermen.com/texas_tech-basketball-big_12/1960/</ref> under coach [[Polk Robison]]. In high school, Arrington was a multi-sport athlete and a state-ranked tennis player.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.myplainview.com/news/back_in_time/article_b51fde9c-8526-5c24-972f-43d22000369d.html|title=Back in Time 03-31-09|work=MyPlainview.com|accessdate=April 28, 2016}}</ref>\n\nAfter graduating from [[Plainview High School (Texas)|Plainview High School]], Arrington attended Texas Tech, where he was a member of the [[Phi Delta Theta]] men's [[fraternity]]. He also walked on to the [[American football|football]] team under [[Spike Dykes]]. He graduated in 1994 with a [[Bachelor of Arts]] in [[political science]] but remained at Texas Tech to pursue a [[Master of Public Administration]] degree, which he completed in 1997. In 2004, he earned a Certificate of International Business Management from the [[McDonough School of Business]] at [[Georgetown University]] in [[Washington, D.C.]]\n\n==Governor's Office==\nIn 1996, Arrington was hired by Governor Bush as an appointments manager.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.breitbart.com/texas/2014/07/04/robert-duncan-resigns-texas-senate-seat-campaign-to-replace-him-begins/|title=Robert Duncan Resigns Texas Senate Seat, Campaign to Replace Him Begins - Breitbart|date=July 4, 2014|newspaper=Breitbart|language=en-US|access-date=February 2, 2017}}</ref>\n\n==White House==\nAfter Governor Bush's election as President in 2000, Arrington was asked to join the [[White House]] staff as Special Assistant to the President and Associate Director of Presidential Personnel,<ref>http://xroads.virginia.edu/~ma02/amacker/administrations/Bush,%20George%20W/Bush%2043%20Working%20File/Bush%2043%20Staff%20and%20Advisers%20(drafts)/Bush%2043%20Staff%20and%20Advisers%20(draft).doc</ref> where he served under [[Clay Johnson III]]. For the next year, Arrington briefed and made recommendations to the President, [[Vice President of the United States|Vice President]] [[Dick Cheney]], and Chief of Staff [[Andy Card]]. During his time in the Office of Presidential Personnel, Arrington managed an executive search team that helped the Office fill more than five thousand executive level, board, and commission positions.{{citation needed|date=April 2016}} He specialized in appointments relating to energy, the environment, and natural resources.<ref>[http://lubbockonline.com/stories/071301/loc_0713010055.shtml]{{dead link|date=April 2018}}</ref>\n\n==Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation==\nIn late December 2001, at the age of twenty-eight, Arrington became one of the youngest chiefs of staff in the history of the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G1-80482908.html|title=Washington People|publisher=|accessdate=April 28, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fdic.gov/about/learn/symbol/index.html|title=FDIC: Who is the FDIC?|publisher=|accessdate=April 28, 2016}}</ref> where he served under the 18th chairman, [[Donald E. Powell]].<ref name=\"fdic.gov\">{{cite web|url=http://www.fdic.gov/about/learn/advisorycommittee/minutes.html |title=Error 404b |publisher= |accessdate=April 28, 2016 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20121203032019/http://www.fdic.gov/about/learn/advisorycommittee/minutes.html |archivedate=December 3, 2012 |df= }}</ref> As Powell's chief of staff, Arrington managed and oversaw the offices of the Chairman, Policy Development, and Public Affairs, all of which he reorganized to increase efficiency. In 2002, Arrington began chairing the FDIC Board Appeals Committee<ref name=\"fdic.gov\"/> and served in Powell's place on the Audit Committee.{{citation needed|date=April 2016}}\n\n==Gulf Coast rebuilding==\nIn 2005, in the wake of FEMA's response to [[Hurricane Katrina]], President Bush established by [[executive order]] the Office of the Federal Coordinator for Gulf Coast Rebuilding.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://georgewbush-whitehouse.archives.gov/news/releases/2005/11/20051101-9.html|title=Executive Order: Creation of the Gulf Coast Recovery and Rebuilding Council|date=November 1, 2005|publisher=|accessdate=April 28, 2016}}</ref> Bush appointed Don Powell as Federal Coordinator, who appointed Arrington as Deputy Federal Coordinator and Chief Operating Officer. In this role, Arrington worked with the Governors of the affected states, as well as military officials, local authorities and charitable organizations.<ref name=\"tech leader\"/><ref name=\"johnhopebryant.com\"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.insurancejournal.com/news/southeast/2006/08/24/71707.htm|title=Ala. Gov. Riley Tours Shrimping Community One Year After Katrina|date=August 24, 2006|work=Insurance Journal|accessdate=April 28, 2016}}</ref> Powell and Arrington were responsible for developing and executing the Federal Government's recovery efforts, as well as coordinating with local, state and federal officials. By the end of Arrington's first year in the Gulf Coast, he had aided Powell in the procurement and implementation of much of the $120 billion spent on infrastructure and assistance relief.<ref name=\"tech leader\"/>\n\n==Texas Tech==\nAfter a year with the Gulf Coast position, Arrington returned to Texas Tech to serve as its system chief of staff.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.irs.ttu.edu/reports/statereports/.../BoardMinutes0511.pdf|title=Institutional Research - Institutional Research - TTU|first=State of Texas and Texas Tech|last=University|website=www.irs.ttu.edu|accessdate=April 16, 2018}}</ref> The Tech System includes [[Texas Tech University]], [[Texas Tech Health Sciences Center]] and [[Angelo State University]]. He also served as the primary liaison to the vice chancellors throughout the system.{{citation needed|date=April 2016}}\n\nIn 2011, Arrington was named Vice Chancellor for Research and Commercialization at Texas Tech University System. During his seven-year tenure with the Texas Tech University System, Arrington was chairman on the Task Force for Enrollment Growth and was the chief architect of \"Leading the Way,\" the strategic plan for the universities within the TTU System.<ref>{{cite web|title = Arrington Leaves TTU System for Private Sector {{!}} Texas Tech University System|url = http://www.texastech.edu/stories/14-06-arrington-leaves-ttu-system-for-private-sector.php|website = www.texastech.edu|accessdate = January 4, 2016}}</ref>\n\nArrington worked to secure the naming rights to the Laura W. Bush Institute for Women's Health for the university health sciences center.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ttuhsc.edu/lwbiwh/history.aspx|title=Laura Bush Institute|publisher=|accessdate=April 28, 2016}}</ref> Since securing the naming rights in 2007, the institute has been responsible for raising hundreds of thousands of dollars for health care issues affecting women.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.angelo.edu/services/communitywed/laura_bush_institute.php|title=Laura W. Bush Institute for Women\u2019s Health Home|website=www.angelo.edu|language=en|access-date=2018-05-25}}</ref>\n\n== Scott Laboratories ==\nIn 2014, Arrington became president of Scott Laboratories in Lubbock. As President of a healthcare innovation holding company, which includes a comprehensive health system, Arrington's primary role is launching and growing new ventures as well as supporting new revenue opportunities at the health system.\n\nUntil his full-time job in Congress, he was focused on developing a tele-health startup, launching an innovative insurance product, and establishing a digital marketing platform for the health system.<ref>{{cite web|title = New Medical Facility Coming to Lubbock|url = http://www.kcbd.com/story/2574690/new-medical-facility-coming-to-lubbock|website = www.kcbd.com|accessdate = January 4, 2016}}</ref>\n\n== Political career ==\nArrington ran unsuccessfully in 2014 in a [[special election]] for the [[Texas State Senate]] [[Texas Senate, District 28|District 28]]. He was defeated by fellow Republican [[Charles Perry (Texas politician)|Charles Perry]], 53 to 30 percent, who still holds the seat.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.texastribune.org/2014/09/09/voters-have-pick-six-senate-special-election/|title=Perry Wins Special Election for Senate Seat|work=The Texas Tribune|accessdate=April 28, 2016}}</ref>\n\nWith Republican Representative [[Randy Neugebauer]] of {{ushr|TX|19}} retiring in 2016, Arrington decided to run for his seat. [[Glen Robertson]] led a nine-candidate field in the [[primary election]] held on March 1 with 27,791 (26.7 percent) of the ballots cast, followed by Arrington's 26,980 (26 percent). In third place was [[Michael Bob Starr]], the former commander of [[Dyess Air Force Base]] who led handily in [[Abilene, Texas|Abilene]] and finished with 22,256 votes (21.4 percent). Laredo surgeon Donald R. May finished fourth with 9,592 votes (9.2 percent).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://enrpages.sos.state.tx.us/public/mar01_273_state.htm|title=Republican primary returns|date=March 1, 2016|publisher=[[Texas Secretary of State]]|accessdate=March 3, 2016|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160306124107/https://enrpages.sos.state.tx.us/public/mar01_273_state.htm|archivedate=March 6, 2016|df=mdy-all}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://lubbockonline.com/local-news/2016-03-01/robertson-and-arrington-make-runoff-election-congressional-race#.VthbAo-cH3x|title=Robertson and Arrington make runoff election in Congressional race|author=Matt Dotray|date=March 2, 2016|publisher=''[[Lubbock Avalanche-Journal]]''|accessdate=March 3, 2016}}</ref>\n\nIn the [[runoff election]] held on May 24, 2016, Arrington defeated Robertson, 25,214 (53.7 percent) to 21,769 (46.3 percent) to become the Republican nominee.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://enrpages.sos.state.tx.us/public/may24_316_state.htm?x=0&y=841&id=99|title=Election Returns|date=May 24, 2016|publisher=[[Texas Secretary of State]]|accessdate=May 24, 2016|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160609171619/https://enrpages.sos.state.tx.us/public/may24_316_state.htm?x=0&y=841&id=99|archivedate=June 9, 2016|df=mdy-all}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.reporternews.com/story/news/politics/elections/2016/11/08/arrington-grabs-big-early-lead-cruises-congress-win/93473928/|title=Arrington grabs big early lead, cruises to Congress win|publisher=|accessdate=April 16, 2018}}</ref> He faced no [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] opponent in the congressional [[general election]] on November 8, 2016. Arrington polled 176,314 votes (86.7 percent); the [[Libertarian Party (United States)|Libertarian]] Troy Bonar trailed with 17,376 votes (8.5 percent), and the [[Green Party (United States)|Green]] candidate, Mark Lawson, polled 9,785 votes (4.8 percent).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://elections.sos.state.tx.us/elchist319_state.htm|title=Election Results|date=November 8, 2016|publisher=Texas Secretary of State|accessdate=December 17, 2016}}</ref> However, the 19th is so heavily Republican that any Democratic challenger would have faced nearly impossible odds in any event. With a [[Cook Partisan Voting Index]] of R+26, the 19th is the third-most Republican district in Texas and the 12th-most Republican district in the nation.\n\nWhen Arrington took office on January 3, 2017, he became only the fifth person to represent this district since its creation in 1935.\n\n===Political stances===\n\n====National security====\nArrington supported President [[Donald Trump]]'s 2017 [[Executive Order 13769|executive order]] curtailing [[Middle East]]ern [[immigration]]. He stated that \"It is important that our commander in chief puts the safety of Americans first. Given concerns about the inadequate vetting of refugees and problems with our immigration system, this temporary pause is intended to ensure the safety of our citizens.\"<ref name=\"Blake1\">{{cite web|last1=Blake|first1=Aaron|title=Coffman, Gardner join Republicans against President Trump's travel ban; here's where the rest stand|url=http://www.denverpost.com/2017/01/29/republicans-on-trump-travel-ban/|website=Denver Post|accessdate=January 30, 2017}}</ref>\n\n====Unemployment benefits====\nIn defending a proposal to cut access to the SNAP program (food stamps),  Arrington cited the biblical passage Thessalonians 3-10.  \"He says even when we were with you we give you this rule, 'If a man will not work he shall not eat.' And he goes on to say 'We heard that some of you are idle.' I think that every American, Republican or Democrat wants to help the needy among us. And I think it's a reasonable expectation that we have work requirements. I think that gives more credibility, frankly, to SNAP.\"<ref>{{cite
