A [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]], he is currently the [[United States House of Representatives|United States Representative]] for [[New York's 11th congressional district]], first elected in a May 2015 special election. The district includes all of [[Staten Island]], as well as portions of southwest [[Brooklyn]]. He is the only Republican representing a significant portion of [[New York City]] in Congress.\n\n==Early life and career==\nDonovan was born into a [[working-class]] [[Roman Catholic]] family in [[Staten Island, New York]] in 1956. His [[Irish-American]] father, Daniel Michael Donovan, was a [[longshoreman]] and lifelong Democrat; his [[Polish-American]] mother, Katherine Bolewicz Donovan, was a [[garment worker]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Gray|first=Geoffrey|url=http://nymag.com/news/intelligencer/encounter/68321|title=110 Minutes With Republican Attorney General Candidate Dan Donovan|publisher=[[New York (magazine)|New York]]|date=September 19, 2010|accessdate=May 6, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.feetintwoworlds.org/2010/10/12/half-polish-catholic-ag-candidate-wants-ny-polish-community-to-know-his-background|title=Half-Polish & Catholic AG candidate wants NY Polish community to know his background|accessdate=2011-04-02|deadurl=bot: unknown|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20101221020114/http://news.feetintwoworlds.org/2010/10/12/half-polish-catholic-ag-candidate-wants-ny-polish-community-to-know-his-background|archivedate=December 21, 2010}}</ref>\n\nHe was raised in the [[Tompkinsville, Staten Island|Tompkinsville]] section of the borough.<ref name=\"dandonovan1\">{{cite web|title=Meet Dan Donovan|publisher=Donovan New York Attorney General|url=http://www.dandonovan.org/meet_dan.php|accessdate=June 4, 2010|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100710132803/http://www.dandonovan.org/meet_dan.php|archivedate=July 10, 2010}}</ref> He attended [[Monsignor Farrell High School]], an all-boys Catholic school, graduating in 1974. He went to study Criminal Justice at [[St. John's University (New York)|St. John's University]]. After graduating from there he attended [[Fordham University School of Law]], earning his [[juris doctor]] in 1988.<ref name=\"dandonovan1\"/>\n\nIn 1989, Donovan became an Assistant District Attorney in the office of [[Robert M. Morgenthau]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://rcda.nyc.gov/meet.html|title=Richmond County District Attorneys Office|publisher=Rcda.nyc.gov|date=January 1, 2004|accessdate=May 6, 2015}}</ref> He served in the office of the [[New York County District Attorney]] under Morgenthau until 1996. Later that year Donovan became Chief of Staff to then Staten Island [[Borough President]] [[Guy V. Molinari]].<ref name=\"autogenerated1\">{{cite web|url=http://brooklyn.ny1.com/content/news_beats/politics/118626/da-donovan-set-to-announce-ag-run/?ap=1&MP4|title=DA Donovan Set To Announce AG Run|publisher=ny1.com|accessdate=2017-05-04|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110714215917/http://brooklyn.ny1.com/content/news_beats/politics/118626/da-donovan-set-to-announce-ag-run/?ap=1&MP4|archivedate=July 14, 2011}}</ref> He remained in that position until 2002 when he was sworn in as Deputy Borough President of Staten Island; he had been appointed by his immediate predecessor and the then new Borough President [[James Molinaro]].<ref name=\"autogenerated1\"/>\n\n==Richmond County District Attorney==\nIn 2003, twenty-year incumbent Democrat [[William L. Murphy]] decided not to seek re-election. Donovan announced his intention to run to succeed him.
