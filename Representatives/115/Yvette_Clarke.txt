Clarke was first elected to Congress in 2006. From 2007 to 2013, her district was labeled the [[New York's 11th congressional district|11th]] until it was redrawn. Before entering Congress in 2007, Clarke was a member of the [[New York City Council]] representing the 40th council district in [[Brooklyn]].\n\n==Early life, family, and education==\nClarke was born in [[Flatbush, Brooklyn]], on November 21, 1964, to Lesley Clarke and former city councilwoman [[Una S. T. Clarke|Una Clarke]], both immigrants from [[Jamaica]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://clarke.house.gov/about/biography/|title=Biography - Congresswoman Yvette Clarke|work=Congresswoman Yvette Clarke|access-date=2018-08-04|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://observer.com/2006/12/the-clarke-family/|title=The Clarke Family|date=2006-12-18|work=Observer|access-date=2018-08-04|language=en-US}}</ref> She graduated from [[Edward R.
