Rosen is the Democratic nominee for the U.S. Senate in the [[United States Senate election in Nevada, 2018|2018 election]].\n\n==Early life and career==\nRosen was born on August 2, 1957 in [[Chicago]], the daughter of Carol, a [[homemaker]],<ref>{{cite web |url=http://obits.reviewjournal.com/obituaries/lvrj/obituary.aspx?n=carol-spektor&pid=152877756&fhid=12209|title=Carol Spektor Obituary|publisher=''Las Vegas Review-Journal''|date=August 2, 2011|accessdate=August 29, 2018}}</ref> and Leonard Spektor, a [[car dealership]] owner who had served in the [[U.S. Army]] during the [[Korean War]].<ref name=\"Leonard\"/> She attended the [[University of Minnesota]] and graduated with a bachelor's degree in psychology in 1979.<ref name=\":1\">{{Cite news |url=https://www.reviewjournal.com/news/politics-and-government/nevada/heller-ad-claim-against-rosen-prompts-new-disclosure-of-degree/|title=Heller ad claim against Rosen prompts new disclosure of degree|last=Lochhead|first=Colton|date=July 18, 2018|work=[[Las Vegas Review-Journal]]|access-date=July 18, 2018}}</ref> While she attended college, her parents moved to [[Las Vegas]], where she also moved after graduating. She took a job with [[Summa Corporation]], and worked as a waitress at [[Caesars Palace]]. In the 1980s, she began to work for [[Southwest Gas]], and then opened her own consulting business.<ref name=newcomer/> While working for Summa, she attended [[College of Southern Nevada|Clark County Community College]] (now the College of Southern Nevada) and received an associate's degree in computing and information technology in 1985.<ref name=\":1\" />\n\nA former [[programmer|computer programmer]] with no prior political experience, she was asked in 2016 by then-[[Senate Majority Leader]] [[Harry Reid]] to run for the U.S. House seat vacated by Republican [[Joe Heck]].<ref name=\":0\">{{Cite news |url=https://www.rgj.com/story/news/politics/2018/05/04/u-s-senate-hopeful-jacky-rosen-announces-biden-endorsement/580624002/|title=Former Vice President Joe Biden endorses U.S. Senate hopeful Jacky Rosen|work=Reno Gazette-Journal |access-date=May 23, 2018|language=en}}</ref> She has served as the president of Ner Tamid, a [[Reform Jewish]] synagogue in Las Vegas, and cited ''[[tikkun olam]]'' as a key part of her decision to enter politics.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.jta.org/2017/07/06/news-opinion/politics/jacky-rosen-announces-for-nevadas-senate-slot|title=Jewish congresswoman Jacky Rosen announces run for Nevada Senate seat|publisher=Jewish Telegraphic Agency|date=July 6, 2017|access-date=June 12, 2018}}</ref>\n\n==Political career==\n===U.S. House of Representatives===\n====2016 election====\n{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Nevada, 2016#District 3}}\nRosen declared her candidacy for the [[United States House of Representatives]] seat in {{ushr|NV|3}} in the [[United States House of Representatives elections in Nevada, 2016|2016 elections]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.rgj.com/story/news/politics/2016/01/26/democrat-jacky-rosen-launches-bid-rep-hecks-house-seat/79351294/|title=Democrat Jacky Rosen launches bid for Rep.
