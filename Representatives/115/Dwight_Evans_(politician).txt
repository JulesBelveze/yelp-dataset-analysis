Evans''' (born May 16, 1954) is an American politician of the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]] serving as a member of the [[United States House of Representatives]] from [[Pennsylvania's 2nd congressional district]] since 2016. He defeated incumbent [[Chaka Fattah]] in the Democratic primary election, and won a special election on November 8, 2016, following Fattah's resignation from Congress after he faced corruption charges. He previously served in the [[Pennsylvania House of Representatives]], representing the [[Pennsylvania House of Representatives, District 203|203rd district]] located in [[Philadelphia County, Pennsylvania|Philadelphia County]].\n\n==Early life and education==\nEvans grew up in the [[Germantown, Philadelphia, Pennsylvania|Germantown]] and [[West Oak Lane]] sections of [[Philadelphia]]<ref name=Dwight>{{cite web |url=http://www.phillymag.com/articles/the_dwight_stuff/|title=Power: The Dwight Stuff? - Philadelphia Magazine|date=20 March 2007}}</ref> and is a graduate of the [[Community College of Philadelphia]] and [[La Salle University]]. After graduation, he became a teacher in the [[School District of Philadelphia]] and a community activist for the [[Urban League]].\n\n==Early political career==\n\n===Pennsylvania House of Representatives===\nEvans was first elected to office in 1980. The district, which encompasses West Oak Lane in Philadelphia, was heavily Democratic with a 95% African-American population.<ref name=Dwight/> As a result, he had minimal GOP opposition in winning re-election to his seat.\n\nEvans was elected as the Democratic Chairman of the House Appropriations Committee in 1990 <ref name=quiet>[http://www.phillymag.com/articles/the_quiet_connection/ S.A. Paolantonio, ''The Quiet Connection'', Philadelphia Magazine]</ref> and served in that capacity until November 2010.\n\nIn 2010, the [[Philadelphia Tribune]] named Evans one of the 10 most influential African-Americans in the city.<ref name=influence>{{cite web|url=http://www.prweb.com/releases/2010/09/prweb4530584.htm|title=Philadelphia Tribune Names City\u2019s 10 Most Influential African Americans|publisher=}}</ref>\n\n===Unsuccessful elections===\n[[File:Dwight Evans at a rally outside City Hall.jpg|thumb|left|Evans speaking during his 2007 mayoral campaign]]\nPrior to his election to Congress, Evans ran for higher office four times unsuccessfully. [[Pennsylvania gubernatorial election, 1986|In 1986]], he sought the Democratic nomination for [[Lieutenant Governor of Pennsylvania|Lieutenant Governor]] but finished third in the primary election to future Lieutenant Governor [[Mark Singel]].<ref name=lieutenant>Carol Morello, Singel Emerges as Winner Over Flaherty and Evans, Philadelphia Inquirer, May 21, 1986</ref>\n\n[[Pennsylvania gubernatorial election, 1994|In 1994]], Evans became the first African American candidate to seek the office of [[Governor of Pennsylvania]]. In the Democratic primary election, Evans faced off with [[Lynn Yeakel]] and Lt. Governor Singel. He won endorsements from ''[[The Philadelphia Inquirer]]'', ''[[Philadelphia Daily News]]'', and ''[[Pittsburgh Post-Gazette]]''. This time, Evans finished second in the primary with 22%.<ref name=governor>[https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9B05E5D81339F932A25756C0A962958260, Michael DeCoursey Hinds, ''Pennsylvania's No. 2 Official Is Nominated for Governor'', New York Times, May 11, 1994]</ref>\n\nEvans made two runs for [[Mayor of Philadelphia]]. [[Philadelphia mayoral election, 1999|In 1999]], in the race to succeed [[Ed Rendell]], Evans finished fifth with 4.7% of the vote in a crowded primary won by [[John F. Street|John Street]].<ref name=99election>[http://www.fandm.edu/x2311.xml G. Terry Madonna], 1999 Philadelphia Mayoral Primary, Franklin and Marshall College, Center for Politics & Public Affairs</ref> [[Philadelphia mayoral election, 2007|In 2007]], despite comments by Governor Rendell that Evans was the \"best qualified\" for mayor,<ref name=qualified>Amy Worden, ''Rendell Likes Evans but Won't Endorse'', Philadelphia Inquirer, May 3, 2007</ref> he finished fifth again taking only 7.82% of the vote.<ref name=07election>{{cite web|url=http://www.seventy.org/pastelections/2007/mayoffresults.pdf|title=Certified Election Results, Committee of Seventy, June 18, 2007|publisher=|accessdate=20 September 2018}}</ref>\n\n==U.S. House of Representatives==\n\n===Elections===\n\n====2016 special election====\n[[File:Dwight Evans swearing in.jpg|thumb|Evans being sworn in by Speaker [[Paul Ryan]]]]\nIn November 2015, Evans announced that he would run for [[Pennsylvania's 2nd congressional district]] in [[United States House of Representatives elections in Pennsylvania, 2016#District 2|2016]] against [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] incumbent [[Chaka Fattah]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Brennan|first=Chris|url=http://articles.philly.com/2015-11-04/news/67990386_1_congressional-district-west-oak-lane-community-events|title=Dwight Evans says he'll challenge Fattah in '16|work=[[Philadelphia Media Network]]|date=November 4, 2015|accessdate=July 9, 2016}}</ref> In an upset, Evans beat Fattah for the Democratic nomination\u2013the real contest in this heavily Democratic, black-majority district\u2013on April 26, 2016. He won mainly by running up his margins in his [[Olney-Oak Lane, Philadelphia|Olney-Oak Lane]] stronghold. Fattah later resigned amid a corruption scandal.<ref>{{cite web|last=McCrone|first=Brian X.|url=http://www.nbcphiladelphia.com/news/local/Chaka-Fattah-Dwight-Evans-wins-2nd-Congressional-race-for-US-House-seat-377169041.html|title=Fattah Concedes as Evans Scores Upset Win in 2nd Congressional Democratic Primary|work=[[WCAU]]|date=April 27, 2016|accessdate=July 9, 2016}}</ref>\n\nAs a result, Evans ran in two elections on November 8, 2016\u2013a special election for the balance of Fattah's sixth term, and a regular election for a full two-year term. Evans won both elections and was sworn in on Monday, November 14, 2016. This led to Evans having more seniority than other new members who won the 2016 congressional election.<ref>{{cite web|last=Tamari|first=Jonathan|url= http://www.philly.com/philly/news/20161115_Dwight_Evans_sworn_in_to_represent_Philly-based_2nd_District_in_U_S__House.html|title=Dwight Evans sworn in to represent Philly-based 2nd District in U.S. House |work=Philly News|date=November 15, 2016|accessdate=November 15, 2016}}</ref>\n\n===Tenure===\n\nIn August 2017, following the aftermath of the [[2017 Unite the Right rally]] in [[Charlottesville, Virginia]], Evans and New York Representative [[Adriano Espaillat]] introduced legislation banning [[Confederate monuments]] on federal property.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thehill.com/homenews/house/347026-democrats-unveil-bill-to-ban-confederate-monuments-on-federal-property|title=Dems unveil bill to ban Confederate monuments on federal property|first=Cristina|last=Marcos|date=17 August 2017|publisher=|accessdate=20 September 2018}}</ref>\n\nHe is a member of the [[Congressional Progressive Caucus]]<ref>{{cite web|title=Caucus Members|author=|url=https://cpc.grijalva.house.gov/index.cfm?sectionid=71&sectiontree=2,71|format=|publisher=Congressional Progressive Caucus|date=|accessdate=25 October 2017}}</ref> and the [[Congressional Black Caucus]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Membership|author=|url=https://cbc.house.gov/membership/|format=|publisher=Congressional Black Caucus|date=|accessdate=7 March 2018}}</ref>\n\n=== Issues ===\n'''Crime'''\n\nIn September 2018, Evans voted against HR 6691, the Community Safety and Security Act of 2018. The bill would amends the definition of \"crime of violence\". Within the definition of \"crime of violence\" is fleeing a police officer in a vehicle or on foot.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://votesmart.org/bill/25260/64187/9128/dwight-evans-voted-nay-passage-hr-6691-community-safety-and-security-act-of-2018#64187|title=HR 6691 - Community Safety and Security Act of 2018 - National Key Vote|last=|first=|date=|website=Vote Smart|language=en-US|archive-url=|archive-date=|dead-url=|access-date=2018-09-26}}</ref>\n\n'''Environment'''\n\nOn September 24, 2018, Evans was rated 100% by the Clean Water Action group.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://votesmart.org/candidate/9128/dwight-evans#.W6vt7hMzZ5M|title=Dwight Evans' Political Summary|last=|first=|date=|website=Vote Smart|language=en-US|archive-url=|archive-date=|dead-url=|access-date=2018-09-26}}</ref>\n\n==See also==\n* [[List of African-American United States Representatives]]\n\n==References==\n{{reflist|30em}}\n\n==External links==\n* [https://evans.house.gov/ Congressman Dwight Evans] official US House website\n* [http://www.dwightevans.com/ Dwight Evans for Congress] official campaign website\n* {{Dmoz|Regional/North_America/United_States/Pennsylvania/Government/Federal/US_House_of_Representatives/Dwight_Evans_%5BD-2%5D|Dwight Evans}}\n{{CongLinks | congbio = E000296 | votesmart = 9128 | fec = H6PA02171 | congress = dwight-evans/E000296 }}\n* {{C-SPAN|dwightevans}}\n* [http://www.legis.state.pa.us/cfdocs/legis/BiosHistory/MemBio.cfm?ID=269&body=H Pennsylvania House of Representatives - Dwight Evans] official PA House website (1981-2016)\n\n{{s-start}}s\n{{s-par|us-pa-hs}}\n{{succession box | before=[[James F.
