On November 7, 2017, he won a [[Utah's 3rd congressional district special election, 2017|special election]] to replace [[Jason Chaffetz]] in Congress, after Chaffetz resigned.\n\n==Early life==\nJohn Curtis was born May 10, 1960 in Salt Lake City, Utah. His parents were Jesse Duckworth \"Dee\" Curtis (March 22, 1927 \u2013 June 16, 2015), and his mother Hazel Dawn Curtis (n\u00e9e Ream) (November 9, 1925 \u2013 May 15, 2016). They married in 1955.\n\nCurtis attended high school at [[Skyline High School (Utah)|Skyline High School]], where he met his future wife, Sue Snarr. He attended [[Brigham Young University]] and graduated with a degree in business management. He worked for OC Tanner and the Citizen Watch Company before taking a position as the COO of a Provo-based company, [[Action Target]], in 2000.\n\nCurtis ran for the Utah State Senate in 2000 as a Democrat against [[Curt Bramble]], losing 33% to 66%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.utahcounty.gov/dept/clerkaud/Elections/ElectRslts/2000_gen/INDEX.asp|title=Utah County 2000 General Election Results|website=www.utahcounty.gov|accessdate=December 21, 2017}}</ref> From 2002 to 2003, Curtis served as vice chairman and chairman of the Utah County Democratic Party.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.deseretnews.com/article/865685772/No-political-party-has-exclusivity-on-everything-thats-good.html|title=John Curtis: No political party has 'exclusivity on everything that's good'|last=Roche|first=Lisa Riley|date=2017-07-30|work=DeseretNews.com|access-date=2018-07-19|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.deseretnews.com/article/975560/Utah-County-Democrats-pick-a-chief.html|title=Utah County Democrats pick a chief|date=2003-04-09|work=DeseretNews.com|access-date=2018-07-19|language=en}}</ref>\n\n==Mayor of Provo==\nCurtis ran successfully for mayor of [[Provo, Utah|Provo]] City in 2009 defeating former legislator Stephen Clark with 53% of the vote and took office on January 5, 2010 on a platform of safety, prosperity, and unity.<ref>{{cite web|title=Curtis wins Provo mayor race; He also assisted with the purchase of iProvo, Provo City's existing fiber internet network, by Google Fiber.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.heraldextra.com/news/local/central/provo/provo-mayor-announces-google-fiber-for-small-businesses-in-state/article_183b7b1f-dfc4-5553-a126-0542a93c39d0.html|title=Provo mayor announces Google Fiber for Small Businesses in State of City|last=Pugmire|first=Genelle|work=Daily Herald|access-date=July 18, 2017|language=en}}</ref> Curtis was re-elected for a second four-year term in 2013 with 86.49% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|title=Curtis soars to victory and second term in Provo|url=http://www.heraldextra.com/news/local/govt-and-politics/elections/curtis-soars-to-victory-and-second-term-in-provo/article_54d3421f-84df-54eb-946d-3ca6c15562f8.html}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://universe.byu.edu/2013/11/06/provo-city-mayor-john-curtis-wins-re-election/|title=Provo City Mayor John Curtis wins re-election \u2013 The Daily Universe|access-date=May 18, 2016}}</ref> In November 2016, Curtis announced he would not seek re-election for the Mayorship of Provo. John Curtis, Other Provo Officials Accused Of Ignoring Complaints Against Ex-Police Chief|last=Press|first=Associated|access-date=2018-07-16|language=en}}</ref> Plaintiffs accused King of a broad range of sexual misconduct, including staring at their breasts, making inappropriate comments, uninvited touching, groping and, in one case, rape.<ref name=\"Piper\">{{Cite news|url=https://www.deseretnews.com/article/900016300/provo-responds-to-sexual-assault-allegations-against-former-police-chief.html|title=Provo responds to sexual assault allegations against former police chief|last=Piper|first=Matthew|date=2018-04-19|work=DeseretNews.com|access-date=2018-07-16|language=en}}</ref>\n\nCity Council members told ''the Deseret News'' that they held a closed-door meeting about King\u2019s conduct in late 2015 or early 2016. The lawsuit alleged that Congressman John Curtis, then Provo\u2019s mayor, chilled reporting by telling police department supervisors in fall 2014 that \u201che did not want to receive any more complaints about Chief King.\u201d The complaint read, \"Chief King was going to remain chief of the department as long as Curtis was in office and there was nothing the supervisors could do about it\u201d.<ref name=\"deseretnews.com\">{{Cite news|url=https://www.deseretnews.com/article/900013394/provo-officials-heard-about-sexual-misconduct-by-former-chief-john-king-years-before-alleged-rape.html|title=Provo officials heard about sexual misconduct by former Chief John King years before alleged rape|last=Piper|first=Matthew|date=2018-03-20|work=DeseretNews.com|access-date=2018-07-16|language=en}}</ref> Councilman Gary Winterton confirmed the council had such a discussion about King, with the chief present, but he said he could not say much about the meeting because it was a closed session. Winterton said he could not say what type of administrative action, if any, was taken. The lawsuit stated the meeting did not result in any discipline of King.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.sltrib.com/news/2018/03/20/new-lawsuit-describes-provo-officials-ignoring-complaints-of-police-chiefs-sexual-misconduct/|title=New lawsuit describes now-Rep. John Curtis, other Provo officials as ignoring complaints of police chief\u2019s sexual misconduct|work=The Salt Lake Tribune|access-date=2018-07-16|language=en-US}}</ref>\n\nIn March 2017, King resigned after the rape allegation, making his exit at the request of then-Mayor and current 3rd District Congressman John Curtis, even though Salt Lake County District Attorney Sim Gill had declined to bring charges.<ref name=\"Piper\"/> In an email obtained by ''the Deseret News'' through a records request, King sent Curtis a March 17 email thanking him for his support: \u201cYou did your best to protect me at the press conference,\u201d he said. \u201cI am deeply sorry for putting you in this terrible position.\u201d<ref name=\"deseretnews.com\"/>\n\nOn March 23, 2018 Curtis clarified that as Provo\u2019s mayor, he heard three, not just two complaints of inappropriate sexual conduct by John King. The first time, Curtis said, he warned King that even if his alleged actions had been misinterpreted, the former police chief shouldn\u2019t put himself in positions where his actions could be misinterpreted. The second time, he ordered King to retake sexual harassment training, and reiterated that he should not go past a certain point while visiting women at the department\u2019s dispatch center. The third time, after a student volunteer accused King of rape in early 2017, Curtis asked for King\u2019s resignation.<ref name=\"ReferenceA\">{{Cite news|url=https://www.deseretnews.com/article/900013820/curtis-says-he-heard-about-3-incidents-of-sexual-misconduct-by-former-provo-police-chief.html|title=Curtis says he heard about 3 incidents of sexual misconduct by former Provo police chief|last=Piper|first=Matthew|date=2018-03-23|work=DeseretNews.com|access-date=2018-07-16|language=en}}</ref>\n\nIn response Curtis stated, \u201cOne of the things I\u2019m learning, is what women expect is more than checking the boxes, legally,\u201d he said. \u201cThey need a lot of emotional support and understanding. And we don\u2019t talk a lot about that portion of what do you do when these things happen. So, in a way, if you think about this, I\u2019m seeing, like, \u2018OK, my primary responsibility is to get this into the right hands.\u2019 I read her comments about how what I did made her feel, and it was clear to me that she expected more from me than just getting it into the right hands.
