She served as Chief of the [[Orlando Police Department]], the first woman to hold the position. She was the Democratic nominee in both 2012 and 2016 to represent Florida's 10th congressional district in the United States House of Representatives, the latter of which Demings won.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.nytimes.com/elections/results/florida-house-district-10-demings-lowe | title=Florida U.S. House 10th District Results: Val Demings Wins | work=The New York Times | accessdate=November 8, 2016}}</ref>\n\n==Early life==\nValdez Venita Butler was born on March 12, 1957,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://info.cqrollcall.com/rs/764-XAC-282/images/CQ-NewMemberGuide-115thCongress.pdf |title=Guide to the New Congress |publisher=[[Roll Call]] |accessdate=January 3, 2017}}</ref> one of seven children born to a poor family; her father worked in [[orange (fruit)|orange]] [[grove (nature)|groves]], while her mother was a [[housekeeping|housekeeper]]. They lived in [[Mandarin (Jacksonville)|Mandarin]], a neighborhood in [[Jacksonville, Florida]]. She attended [[Segregation in the United States|segregated schools]] in the 1960s, graduating from [[Wolfson High School]] in the 1970s.<ref name=sentinel2007>{{cite web|url=http://articles.orlandosentinel.com/2007-12-16/news/Val16_1_val-demings-orlando-police-chief-jerry-demings|title=Val Demings takes over as Orlando's police chief Monday|publisher=Articles.orlandosentinel.com|date=December 16, 2007|accessdate=October 4, 2012}}</ref>\n\nHer desire for a career in law enforcement came when Demings served in the \"school patrol\" at Dupont Junior High School. She attended [[Florida State University]], graduating with a degree in [[criminology]] in 1979.<ref name=sentinel2007/>\n\n==Career==\nAfter graduating from college, Demings worked as a [[social worker]] in Jacksonville for 18 months.<ref name=sentinel2007/><ref name=msnbc/> In 1983, she applied for a job with the [[Orlando Police Department]] (OPD), and she began with the department on [[patrol]] on Orlando's west side.<ref name=sentinel2007/>\n\nDemings was appointed as Chief of the OPD in December 2007, becoming the first woman to lead the department.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://articles.orlandosentinel.com/2011-05-05/news/os-ed-val-demings-retirement-050511-20110504_1_mercury-capsule-val-demings-50th-anniversary|title=Val Demings' retirement opinion: Orlando Police Chief Val Demings is retiring|work=Orlando Sentinel|date=May 5, 2011|accessdate=October 4, 2012}}</ref> As Chief, she was credited with reducing violent crime in Orlando. She retired from the position effective June 1, 2011, after serving with the OPD for 27 years.<ref name=retire>{{cite web |last=Schlueb |first=Mark |url=http://articles.orlandosentinel.com/2011-05-03/news/os-orlando-police-chief-retires-20110503_1_police-chief-val-demings-jerry-demings-new-orlando-police|title=Orlando Police Chief Val Demings retiring: Orlando Police Chief Val Demings is retiring|work=Orlando Sentinel|date=May 3, 2011|accessdate=October 4, 2012}}</ref>\n\n==Political career==\n===2012 U.S. House campaign===\nDemings was the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]] nominee for the [[United States House of Representatives]] in [[Florida's 10th congressional district]] in the [[United States House of Representatives elections in Florida, 2012#District 10|2012 elections]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theledger.com/article/20121001/POLITICS/121009944|title=Rep.
