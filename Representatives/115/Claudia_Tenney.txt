Tenney previously represented the 101st Assembly District in the [[New York State Assembly]] for six years.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://tenney.house.gov/about|title=About|date=December 3, 2012|work=Congresswoman Claudia Tenney|access-date=August 12, 2017|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.rollcall.com/news/politics/party-candidate-elected-in-new-yorks-22nd-district|title=Republican Claudia Tenney Elected in New York's 22nd District|date=November 8, 2016|access-date=December 28, 2017|language=en}}</ref> She is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]]. \n\n==Early life and education==\nTenney is a native of [[New Hartford, New York|New Hartford]] and the daughter of former [[New York State Supreme Court]] [[Judge|Justice]] John R. Tenney. She graduated from [[List of Colgate University people|Colgate University]] in 1983 and received her law degree from the [[University of Cincinnati College of Law|Taft College of Law]] at the [[University of Cincinnati]].\n\n==Career before politics==\nEarly in her career, Tenney was the only American employed by the [[Consulate General]] of [[Yugoslavia]]. She acted as intermediary between [[ABC Sports]] and the Yugoslavian government leading up to the [[1984 Winter Olympics]] in [[Sarajevo]].<ref name=\"syracuse\">{{cite news|last1=Groom|first1=Debra|title=Assemblywoman Claudia Tenney -- a master of many jobs|url=http://www.syracuse.com/news/index.ssf/2011/03/assemblywoman_claudia_tenney_-.html|accessdate=November 14, 2016|publisher=Syracuse.com|date=March 5, 2011}}</ref>\n\nTenney was a co-owner of Mid-York Press, a commercial printing company started by her mother's family in 1946. Mid-York Press is located in [[Sherburne (town), New York|Sherburne]] in [[Chenango County]].<ref name=\"Press\">{{cite news|url=http://romesentinel.com/county/congressional-race-near-boiling-point/QBqpkb!azJQqTyrMfoqt0TpaaXsaw/|title=Congressional race near boiling point|author=Staff|first=|date=November 4, 2016|newspaper=Rome Sentinel|accessdate=November 25, 2016|location=Rome, New York}}</ref> \n\nTenney maintained a private law practice in Clinton. Prior to owning her own firm, she was a partner at the [[Utica, New York|Utica]] area law firm of Groben, Gilroy, Oster and Saunders.<ref name=syracuse/>\n\nIn January 2001, she began co-hosting \"Common Cents\", a radio and television program that airs weekly across Oneida and most of [[Herkimer County, New York|Herkimer County]]. In February 2010, Tenney began co-hosting \"First Look\" on [[WIBX|WIBX 950 Radio]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Biography|url=http://nyassembly.gov/mem/Claudia-Tenney/bio/|website=Assemblywoman Claudia Tenney|publisher=New York State Assembly|accessdate=November 14, 2016}}</ref>\n\nIn 2009, Tenney ran for Oneida County Surrogate Court Judge. She ran as a Republican against incumbent Democrat, Louis Gigliotti. Tenney was defeated by Gigliotti, receiving 45% of the vote to Gigliotti's 55%.<ref>{{cite news|newspaper=Rome Sentinel|title=Tenney Loses Surrogate Judge Election|url=http://romesentinel.com/news?newsid=20091104-141200|accessdate=May 10, 2016}}</ref>\n\n==New York State Assembly==\nAfter Assemblyman David Townsend announced in 2010 that he was running for Oneida County Sheriff, Tenney decided to run for New York State Assembly.
