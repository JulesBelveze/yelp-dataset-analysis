A [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]], Walker has represented [[North Carolina's 6th congressional district]] in the [[United States House of Representatives]] since 2015. In 2017, Walker was elected to head the [[Republican Study Committee]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Member List|author=|url=https://rsc-walker.house.gov/|format=|publisher=Republican Study Committee|date=|accessdate=22 January 2018}}</ref>\n\n==Early life and career==\nWalker was born on May 20, 1969, in [[Dothan, Alabama]].<ref name=\"RCProfile\">[http://data.rollcall.com/members/52926 Rep. Mark Walker, R-N.C.: Member of the House (2nd term)], ''Roll Call'' (accessed January 30, 2017).</ref> His father was an [[Independent Baptist]] minister, and was the chaplain of a prison in Alabama.<ref name=\"wegmann\">{{cite web |url=http://dailysignal.com/2016/04/20/a-preacher-turned-lawmaker-and-his-new-campaign-to-win-the-old-war-on-poverty/ |title=A Preacher Turned Lawmaker and His New Campaign to Win the Old War on Poverty |last1=Wegmann |first1=Philip |date=April 20, 2016 |website=The Daily Signal |publisher=[[The Heritage Foundation]] |access-date=April 23, 2016}}</ref>\n\nWalker eventually attended [[Trinity Baptist College]] for a time before moving with his family to [[Houston, Texas]]. From there, Walker moved to the [[Piedmont Triad]]. He worked in business and finance for several years.<ref name=\"Vote Smart\">{{cite web |url=https://votesmart.org/candidate/biography/146255/mark-walker#.V85XjZMrKRs | title=Mark Walker Bio}}</ref>  Walker eventually returned to college to pursue the ministry and attend Piedmont Baptist College, now [[Piedmont International University]], graduating with a B.A. in Biblical Studies. Walker was ordained in the [[Southern Baptist]] denomination.  His career in ministry began at Calvary Baptist Church in Winston-Salem. From there he has worked for and led churches in North Carolina and Florida. He has served as a worship pastor, executive pastor and lead pastor.\n\nIn 2008, Walker started with Lawndale Baptist Church in [[Greensboro, North Carolina|Greensboro]] as its Pastor of Arts and Worship.<ref>[http://www.greensboro-nc.gov/modules/showdocument.aspx?documentid=18056 Biography and Resume at the City of Greensboro, North Carolina website]</ref> The church has a membership of several thousand congregants.<ref>Emily Cahn, [http://www.rollcall.com/news/home/religious-right-congress-jody-hice-georgia-2014#sthash.USzP0h8r.dpuf Megachurches Prove Mega-Influential in GOP Primaries], ''Roll Call'' (July 20, 2014).</ref>\n\n==U.S. House of Representatives==\n\n===Elections===\n\n;2014\nRepublican [[Howard Coble]] had represented the 6th district since 1985 when he announced his retirement at age 83. [https://www.nytimes.com/2015/01/07/us/politics/as-power-in-congress-shifts-to-gop-lives-of-freshmen-in-transition.html As Power in Congress Shifts to G.O.P., Lives of Freshmen in Transition], ''New York Times'' (January 6, 2015).</ref> He is an avowed opponent of the [[Affordable Care Act]], and has led the conservative Republican Study Committee's efforts to repeal the [[Health care reform in the United States|health care reform]] legislation.<ref>MJ Lee & Deirdre Walsh, [http://www.cnn.com/2017/01/04/politics/conservatives-repeal-and-replace-obamacare-bill/ Conservative House Republicans unveil repeal and replace Obamacare bill], CNN (January 4, 2017).</ref> He has called for \"full repeal\" of the legislation, and criticized 2015 Republican-sponsored legislation that would repeal only part of the act.<ref>Alexander Bolton, [http://thehill.com/homenews/senate/260153-obamacare-repeal-teeters-in-senate ObamaCare repeal teeters in Senate], ''The Hill'' (November 16, 2015).</ref>\n\nIn December 2016, Walker was one of only 33 Republican U.S. Representatives to vote \"no\" on a short-term stopgap funding measure that would appropriate millions of dollars in federal disaster relief spending in the wake of [[Hurricane Matthew]]. Walker said that he opposed such stopgap funding bills.<ref>Anna Douglas, [http://www.newsobserver.com/news/politics-government/article119919723.html Why a congressman voted against a bill with money for disaster relief in his district], ''News & Observer'' (December 9, 2016).</ref>\n\nWalker has led efforts to improve the Republican Party's outreach to [[African American]]s, and organized a February 2017 conference between the [[College president|presidents and chancellors]] of [[historically black colleges and universities]] (HBCUs) and Republican congressional leaders.<ref>William Douglas, [http://www.charlotteobserver.com/news/politics-government/article129213204.html Will GOP\u2019s latest effort to reach out to black community last?],  ''News & Observer'' (January 30, 2017).</ref> He also worked with fellow Representative [[Alma Adams]], a Democrat from North Carolina, to start an internship program for students from HBCUs.<ref name=\"McPherson\">Lindsey McPherson, [http://www.rollcall.com/news/politics/walker-wants-gop-lead-criminal-justice-immigration Walker Wants GOP to Lead on Criminal Justice, Immigration: Head of Republican Study Committee says new approach warranted], ''Roll Call'' (January 23, 2017).</ref> He is supportive of [[criminal justice reform]] initiatives, and has called for a shift in Republican approach to this issue.<ref name=\"McPherson\"/>\n\nIn 2017, Walker became co-chair of the Congressional Prayer Caucus.<ref>[http://www.courier-tribune.com/news/20170111/walker-co-chairs-prayer-caucus Walker co-chairs prayer caucus], ''Asheboro Courier-Tribune'' (January 11, 2017).</ref>\n\nWalker has played for the Republicans in the annual [[Congressional Baseball Game]], serving as [[Baseball pitcher|pitcher]] in the 2016 game.<ref>Alex Gangitano & Bridget Bowman, [http://www.rollcall.com/news/hoh/republicans-turn-back-democrats-thriller-8-7 Republicans Turn Back Democrats in Thriller, 8-7], ''Roll Call'' (June
