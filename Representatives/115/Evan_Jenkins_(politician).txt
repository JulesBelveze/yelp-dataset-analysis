He is a member of the [[U.S. House Committee on Appropriations]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://wvmetronews.com/2015/01/04/new-year-new-congress/|title=WV MetroNews  \u2013 New Year, new Congress|work=wvmetronews.com|accessdate=February 5, 2015}}</ref> He is a [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]], having switched his party affiliation from Democrat in 2013.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.politico.com/story/2013/07/evan-jenkins-party-switch-2014-election-094972|title=Dem joins GOP to run against Rahall|work=POLITICO|access-date=2017-06-02}}</ref>\n\nJenkins was a member of the [[West Virginia Senate]] from the 5th District, which contains [[Cabell County, West Virginia|Cabell County]] and a small portion of [[Wayne County, West Virginia|Wayne County]].  He served in both chambers of the [[West Virginia Legislature]] in Charleston over the course of 20 years, having been elected as a member of the House in 1994, and elected to the Senate in 2002.<ref name=\"Jim Workman\">{{cite web|url=http://www.wowktv.com/story/25508919/rahall-jenkins-set-to-face-off-in-3rd-district-congressional-race|title=Rahall, Jenkins set to face off in 3rd District Congressional Ra \u2013 WOWK 13 Charleston, Huntington WV News, Weather, Sports|author=Jim Workman|date=May 13, 2014|publisher=|accessdate=October 2, 2014}}</ref> He gave up his seat to run in the  2014 congressional election, defeating incumbent [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrat]] [[Nick Rahall]].<ref name=\"auto1\">{{cite web|url=http://thehill.com/blogs/ballot-box/house-races/222908-rahall-loses-house-seat-in-west-virginia|title=Dem Rahall loses House seat after 38 years|author=Timothy Cama|work=TheHill}}</ref>\n\nJenkins was a candidate for the [[U.S. Senate]] in [[United States Senate election in West Virginia, 2018|2018]], losing to [[West Virginia Attorney General]] [[Patrick Morrisey]] in the primary election.<ref>https://www.rollcall.com/news/politics/patrick-morrisey-wins-west-virginia-gop-senate-primary</ref> Jenkins is currently a candidate in the election to be held on November 6, 2018 to fill the remaining six years term as a Justice of the [[Supreme Court of Appeals of West Virginia]] due to the resignation of [[Robin Davis]].<ref>http://wvmetronews.com/2018/08/14/more-candidates-lining-up-to-run-for-open-supreme-court-seats/</ref> \n\n==Early life==\nJenkins, a lifelong resident of [[Huntington, West Virginia|Huntington]], is the son of Dorothy C. He had actually grown up as a Republican, but became a Democrat in 1992 prior to his run for the House of Delegates.  On switching parties, Jenkins stated that: \"West Virginia is under attack from Barack Obama and a Democratic Party that our parents and grandparents would not recognize.\"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://atr.rollcall.com/democrat-switches-parties-to-challenge-rahall/|title=Democrat Switches Parties to Challenge Rahall #WV03|date=July 31, 2013|accessdate=July 31, 2013|work=[[Roll Call]]|first=Abby|last=Livingston}}</ref> West Virginia's 3rd district had long been a Democratic stronghold, but had been swept up in the growing Republican tide that had swept the state since the turn of the century. In 2012, it went for [[Mitt Romney]] 66-32 percent, making it the second-most Republican district in the nation to be represented by a Democrat.<ref name=post/>  Jenkins and Rahall had contributed to each other's campaigns in the decade's previous election cycles.<ref name=\"Contributions\">{{cite news | url=http://www.charlestondailymail.com/News/statenews/201307290182 | title=Nick Rahall, Evan Jenkins contributed to each other's campaigns | work=[[Charleston Daily Mail]] | date=July 30, 2013 | accessdate=October 6, 2014 | author=Boucher, Dave}}</ref>\n\nJenkins ran unopposed in the Republican primary.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pocahontastimes.com/beard-mclaughlin-win-primary-election/|title=Beard, McLaughlin win primary election|publisher=|accessdate=October 2, 2014}}</ref>  He faced Rahall in the general election in November 2014.
