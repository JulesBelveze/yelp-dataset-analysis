She is a member of the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]]. The district, numbered as the 12th District from 2008 to 2013, includes the northern two-thirds of [[San Mateo County]] and the southwest quarter of [[San Francisco]]. She represents much of the territory that had been represented by her political mentor, [[Leo Ryan]].\n\nShe is also a former member of the [[California State Senate]] who represented parts of San Francisco and San Mateo counties. On April 8, 2008, she won the [[California's 12th congressional district special election, 2008|special election]] for the vacated [[United States House of Representatives]] seat of late [[United States Congress|Congressman]] [[Tom Lantos]].<ref name=\"SFC 4-9-08\">{{cite news | title= Voters send Jackie Speier to Washington|url=http://www.sfgate.com/bayarea/article/Voters-send-Jackie-Speier-to-Washington-3288063.php|author=John Wildermuth|date=April 9, 2008|work=San Francisco Chronicle|accessdate=5 September 2015}}</ref>\n\nA [[Caltrain]] [[Caltrain Express|\"Baby Bullet\" express]] locomotive is named in her honor.\n\n==Early life and education==\nSpeier was born in 1950 in [[San Francisco]], and grew up in an apolitical family, the daughter of Nancy (n\u00e9e Kanchelian) and Manfred \"Fred\" Speier.<ref>{{cite news | title= MANFRED SPEIER Obituary - San Francisco, CA|url=http://www.legacy.com/obituaries/sfgate/obituary.aspx?pid=159673775|author=|date=|newspaper=San Francisco Chronicle}}</ref> Her mother was of [[Armenia]]n descent and a survivor of the [[Armenian Genocide]], while her father was an immigrant from [[Germany]]. Speier took Jacqueline as her [[Confirmation#Confirmation name|confirmation name]] after [[Jacqueline Kennedy Onassis|Jackie Kennedy]].<ref name=\"Moving Up\">{{cite news |last=Haddock|first=Vicki|title=Jackie Speier\u2013 moving on, moving up: Survivor of Jonestown ambush plans run for lieutenant governor|work=[[San Francisco Chronicle]]|date=November 16, 2003|url=http://www.sfgate.com/opinion/article/Jackie-Speier-moving-on-moving-up-Survivor-2511901.php|accessdate=January 27, 2008}}</ref> She is a graduate of [[Mercy High School (Burlingame, California)|Mercy High School]] in [[Burlingame, California|Burlingame]]. She earned a [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] degree from the [[University of California, Davis]], and a [[Juris Doctor|J.D.]] degree from the [[University of California, Hastings College of the Law]] in 1976.<ref>{{cite web|last=Staff|title=Alumni News|work=[[University of California Hastings College of the Law]]|date=April 2007|url=http://www.uchastings.edu/site_files/newsletter/eNewsletterApr.html|accessdate=January 27, 2008|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20070503194352/http://www.uchastings.edu/site_files/newsletter/eNewsletterApr.html|archivedate=May 3, 2007|df=mdy-all}}</ref>\n\n==Marriage and family==\nSpeier's first marriage was to Dr. Steven Sierra, an emergency-room doctor, in 1987.<ref name=\"NNDB\" /><ref name=\"SierraDead\">{{cite news |url=http://articles.latimes.com/1994-01-27/news/mn-16007_1_assemblywoman-jackie-speier|title=Auto Accident Kills Husband of Jackie Speier |date=January 27, 1994|work=[[Los Angeles Times]]|accessdate=July 28, 2014}}</ref> In 1988, they had a son Jackson Kent, while she was serving as a member of the California State Assembly.<ref name=\"Moving Up\" /> Dr. Sierra died in a car accident in 1994 at the age of 53.<ref name=\"NNDB\">[http://www.nndb.com/people/846/000168342/ Jackie Speier] at National Names DataBase.</ref> At the time, Jackie was two months pregnant with their second child, a daughter she named Stephanie.<ref name=\"Moving Up\" />\n\nIn 2001, Speier married Barry Dennis, an investment consultant.<ref name=\"NNDB\" /><ref name=\"CAMPAIGN\">[http://www.jackieforcongress.com/about/ Jackie For Congress: Bio], biography page at 2008 campaign website.</ref>\n\n==Jonestown shooting==\n[[File:Leo Ryan.jpg|thumb|right|179x179px|Congressman Leo Ryan]]\nSpeier entered politics by serving as a congressional staffer for Congressman [[Leo Ryan]]. Speier was part of his November 1978 fact-finding mission organized to investigate allegations of [[human rights abuses|human-rights abuses]] by [[Jim Jones]] and his [[Peoples Temple]] followers, almost all of whom were American citizens who had moved to [[Jonestown]], [[Guyana]], with Jones in 1977 and 1978.<ref name=\"Moving Up\"/> Speier was one of two members of the mission who made wills before traveling to Jonestown.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.rollcall.com/news/jonestown-murders-jackie-speier-37-years|title=Congresswoman Left for Dead at Jonestown Recalls the Massacre, 37 Years Later|last=Gale|first=Rebecca|date=November 18, 2015|work=Roll Call|access-date=2017-12-15}}</ref>\n\nSeveral Peoples Temple members ambushed the investigative team and others boarding the plane to leave Jonestown on November 18. Five people died, including Congressman Ryan. While trying to shield herself from rifle and shotgun fire behind small airplane wheels with other team members, Speier was shot five times and waited 22 hours before help arrived.<ref name=\"Honored Guest\">{{cite press release|last=Staff|title=Senator Jackie Speier one of honored guests at banquet|publisher=Armenian National Committee of America Western Region|date=October 2006|url=http://www.anca.org/press_releases/press_releases.php?prid=644|accessdate=January 27, 2008|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20071209222317/http://www.anca.org/press_releases/press_releases.php?prid=644|archivedate=December 9, 2007|df=mdy-all}}</ref> That same day, over 900 remaining members of the [[Peoples Temple]] died in Jonestown and [[Georgetown, Guyana|Georgetown]] in a mass murder-suicide.\n\n==Political career==\n\n===San Mateo County===\nSpeier's political career began with an unsuccessful run to fill the vacancy caused by the death of Congressman Ryan (the seat she holds now).<ref name=\"Moving Up\" /> She lost the Democratic primary to another former Ryan staffer, G. W. \"Joe\" Holsinger. He lost to the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] candidate [[William Royer|Bill Royer]], San Mateo County [[Board of Supervisors|Supervisor]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://library.cqpress.com/cqalmanac/document.php?id=cqal79-861-26157-1182533|title=CQ Almanac Online Edition|website=library.cqpress.com|access-date=2017-10-13}}</ref>\n\nSpeier won her first election in 1980, when she ran for the San Mateo County Board of Supervisors and defeated a 20-year incumbent. At the time, she was the youngest person ever elected to the board. She was reelected in 1984, and was later selected as chairwoman.<ref name=\"Honored Guest\" />\n\n===California State Assembly===\nIn 1986, midway through her second term on the Board of Supervisors, Speier ran for the [[California State Assembly]] from a district in northern San Mateo County. She won by a few hundred votes. She was reelected five more times, the last time as the nominee of both the Democratic and Republican parties.<ref name=\"HR 56\">{{cite web|url=http://www.leginfo.ca.gov/pub/95-96/bill/asm/ab_0051-0100/hr_56_bill_960805_introduced.html|title=HR 56 Assembly House Resolution - INTRODUCED|publisher=California government}}</ref>\n\n===California State Senate===\n[[File:Jackie Speier 11 2005.jpg|left|thumb|250x150px|Speier, California state senator]]\n[[File:Jackie Speier Caltrain.jpg|right|thumb|150x150px|Caltrain [[locomotive]] named after Jackie Speier]]\nState law prevented Speier from running for reelection to the Assembly in 1996, but in 1998 she was elected to the California State Senate. In 2002, she was elected to a second term with 78.2% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://vote2002.sos.ca.gov/Returns/stsen/0859.htm|title=California Secretary of State, Vote2002, State Senate District 8|accessdate=February 15, 2008|deadurl=unfit|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20070814080526/http://vote2002.sos.ca.gov/Returns/stsen/0859.htm|archivedate=August 14, 2007}}</ref> As a state senator, Speier was instrumental in securing $127&nbsp;million funding to start the [[Caltrain Express|\"Baby Bullet\" express service]] for [[Caltrain]], for which the commuter rail agency named a new locomotive (no. 925) after her. Speier also focused on representing [[consumer rights]].<ref>{{cite news |last=Yates|first=Dana|title=Yee looking to make mark|work=The Daily Journal|publisher=San Mateo County's homepage|date=|url=http://www.smdailyjournal.com/article_preview.php?id=67838|accessdate=January 27, 2008}}</ref> Senator Speier was termed out of the [[California State Senate]] in 2006. During her last term, she served as assistant president [[pro tempore]] of the California State Senate.\n\n===Candidate for Lieutenant Governor of California===\nIn 2006, Speier ran in the Democratic primary for [[Lieutenant Governor of California]] against insurance commissioner [[John Garamendi]] and state senator [[Liz Figueroa]]. In the June 6, 2006 elections, Garamendi defeated Speier in a close race. Garamendi received 42.5%, Speier received 39.7%, and Figueroa received the remaining 17.8% of the vote.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=112293|title=Our Campaigns - CA Lieutenant Governor - D Primary Race - Jun 06, 2006|website=www.ourcampaigns.com|access-date=2017-12-13}}</ref>\n\n===2008 presidential campaign===\nSpeier endorsed [[Hillary Clinton]]'s [[Hillary Clinton presidential campaign, 2008|bid for president]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://thehill.com/homenews/campaign/269333-female-lawmakers-rally-around-clinton|title=Female lawmakers rally around Clinton's White House bid|last=Segal|first=Cheryl|date=February 16, 2016|work=TheHill|access-date=December 15, 2017}}</ref>\n\n===Member of the U.S. House of Representatives===\nOn January 13, 2008, Speier announced she was running in the Democratic primary for the 12th District, Ryan's old district. The seat was being vacated by 14-term incumbent and fellow Democrat [[Tom Lantos]], who announced on January 2, 2008, that he was not seeking re-election. Speier had spent much of 2007 building support to challenge Lantos in the Democratic primary.<ref>{{cite news|last=Kapochunas|first=Rachel|title=California Dems Expected to Vie for Lantos Seat|work=CQ Politics|publisher=Congressional Quarterly Inc.|date=January 2, 2008|url=http://www.cqpolitics.com/wmspage.cfm?docID=news-000002651163|accessdate=January 27, 2008|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080302225659/http://www.cqpolitics.com/wmspage.cfm?docID=news-000002651163|archivedate=March 2, 2008|df=mdy-all}}</ref>\n\nOn January 17, 2008, Lantos endorsed Speier as his successor. She also picked up endorsements from Congresswoman [[Anna Eshoo]], Congressman [[Mike Thompson (California politician)|Mike Thompson]] and San Francisco Mayor [[Gavin Newsom]].\n\nLantos died February 11, 2008. Speier won a special primary election on April 8, 2008 to fill the remainder of his term, which ended in January 2009. She won an outright majority, avoiding a runoff that would have been held on June 3, coinciding with the regular primary election.<ref>{{cite news |author=John Wildermuth|newspaper=San Francisco Chronicle|date=February 13, 2008|title=April 8 primary set to pick Lantos' successor|url=http://www.sfgate.com/bayarea/article/April-8-primary-set-to-pick-Lantos-successor-3295085.php|accessdate=February 15, 2008}}</ref> She was elected to a full term in November with 75 percent of the vote and has been reelected three more times with no substantive opposition.\n\nOn July 11, 2008, Speier introduced her first bill, the Gasoline Savings and Speed Limit Reduction Act, which would set a national speed limit of 60&nbsp;mph in urban areas and 65&nbsp;mph on less-populated stretches of highway.<ref>{{Cite web|last= |first= | title =H.R.6458 -
