The district includes several suburban and rural areas east of [[Nashville]]. A [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]], she was previously elected to the [[Tennessee Senate]], serving as floor leader of the Republican Caucus. She unsuccessfully ran for the Republican nomination in the [[Tennessee gubernatorial election, 2018|2018 Tennessee gubernatorial election]].\n\n==Early life and education==\nDiane Lynn Warren was born on January 16, 1951 in [[Baltimore, Maryland]], to Joseph and Audrey Warren.  Warren graduated from Andover High School in [[Linthicum, Maryland]], in 1969 and was the first member of her family to earn a college degree.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thehill.com/capital-living/new-member-of-the-week/240911-rep-blacks-healthcare-prescription-doesnt-include-the-government|title=Rep. Beard|work=TheHill}}</ref> Following graduation from [[Anne Arundel Community College]] in [[Arnold, Maryland]] with an [[associate's degree]] in [[nursing]], went on to [[Belmont University]], earning a [[bachelor's degree]] in nursing in 1991.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tennessean.com/article/20121003/NEWS/310020091/Rep-Diane-Black-won-t-end-push-more-spending-cuts?nclick_check=1|title=Rep. Diane Black won't end push for more spending cuts|work=The Tennessean}}</ref><ref name=\"wapobio\">{{cite news |url= |title=Diane Black (R-Tenn.) |accessdate=May 23, 2012 | work=The Washington Post |first1=Nia-Malika |last1=Henderson |first2=Jackie |last2=Kucinich}}</ref>\n\n==Career==\nBlack worked as a [[registered nurse]] until she ran for the [[Tennessee House of Representatives]] in 1998. Later she served as an educator at [[Volunteer State Community College]]<ref name=\"About Diane\"/><ref>[http://health.state.tn.us/licensure/Practitioner.aspx?ProfessionCode=5005&LicenseNumber=13146&FileNumber=13146 Practitioner Profile Data \u2013 Tennessee Department of Health] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150226010715/http://health.state.tn.us/licensure/Practitioner.aspx?ProfessionCode=5005&LicenseNumber=13146&FileNumber=13146 |date=2015-02-26 }}</ref> in [[Gallatin, Tennessee]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.dianeblack.org/ContentPage.aspx?WebPageId=23954&GroupId=825 |title=Biography |work=Diane Black for State Senate |accessdate=2010-07-12}}</ref>\n\n=== Tennessee legislature ===\nBefore becoming a [[Tennessee Senate|state Senator]] in 2004, she had previously served as a [[Tennessee House of Representatives|state Representative]] for six years from 1998. Prior to her election as a Republican state representative, Black had voted in two [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] primaries in 1996.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.tennessean.com/story/news/politics/tn-elections/2018/06/27/tennessee-election-candidates-voting-records-governor/736368002/|title=What the voting records reveal about Tennessee's candidates for governor|work=The Tennessean|access-date=2018-07-02|language=en}}</ref> Black was the Assistant Floor Leader of the Senate Republican Caucus, a member of the Senate Government Operations Committee, and the Vice-Chairwoman of the Senate General Welfare, Health and Human Resources Committee.
