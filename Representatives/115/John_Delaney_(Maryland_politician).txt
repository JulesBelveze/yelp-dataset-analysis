Representatives==\n\n===2012 election===\n{{BLP sources section|date=September 2016}}\n{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in Maryland, 2012#District 6}}\nAfter redistricting, Delaney decided to run for the newly redrawn 6th District against 10-term Republican incumbent [[Roscoe Bartlett]]. The district had long been a Republican stronghold, but it had been significantly reconfigured. The [[Maryland General Assembly]] shifted heavily Republican [[Carroll County, Maryland|Carroll County]] and a mostly Republican section of [[Frederick County, Maryland|Frederick County]] to the heavily Democratic [[Maryland's 8th congressional district|8th district]]. It shifted Republican-tilting sections of [[Harford County|Harford]] and [[Baltimore County|Baltimore]] counties into the already heavily Republican [[Maryland's 1st congressional district|1st district]]. Taking their place was a heavily Democratic spur of western Montgomery County, which ended just two blocks from Delaney's home in [[Potomac, Maryland|Potomac]].\n\nOn paper, this dramatically altered the district's demographics, turning it from a heavily Republican district into a Democratic-leaning district.  While [[John McCain]] carried the 6th with 57 percent of the vote in 2008,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.swingstateproject.com/diary/4161/|title=Swing State Project: Presidential Results by Congressional District, 2000-2008|date=16 October 2015|publisher=|deadurl=bot: unknown|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151016181827/http://www.swingstateproject.com/diary/4161/|archivedate=16 October 2015|df=}}</ref> [[Barack Obama]] would have carried the new 6th with 56 percent.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1xn6nCNM97oFDZ4M-HQgoUT3X4paOiSDsRMSuxbaOBdg/edit?usp=sharing&usp=embed_facebook|title=Daily Kos Elections 2008 & 2012 presidential election results for congressional districts used in 2012 & 2014 elections|website=Google Docs}}</ref> The Montgomery County share of the district has three times as many people as the rest of the district combined.\n\nThe shifts were quite controversial, as Republicans accused Democrats of shifting district boundaries in their favor, and former Governor [[Martin O'Malley]] later admitted the redrawn districts would favor Democrats. \"That was my hope,\" O'Malley told attorneys in a deposition. \"It was also my intent to create ... a district where the people would be more likely to elect a Democrat than a Republican.\"<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.baltimoresun.com/news/maryland/politics/bs-md-redistricting-case-20170601-story.html|title=Lawsuit forces Maryland Democrats to acknowledge the obvious: Redistricting was motivated by politics|last1=Lake|first1=Brett|date=June 1, 2017|publisher=''Baltimore Sun''|accessdate=July 28, 2017}}</ref>\n\nDuring the primary, Delaney was endorsed by former President [[Bill Clinton]], U.S. Congresswoman [[Donna Edwards]], Comptroller [[Peter Franchot]], the ''[[Washington Post]]'', and the ''Gazette''.<ref>{{cite news|author=Ben Pershing|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/local/md-politics/delaney-md-democrats-work-to-show-unified-front-after-newcomers-primary-win/2012/04/04/gIQA6u0GvS_story.html|title=Delaney, Md. Democrats work to show unified front after newcomer's primary win|publisher=The Washington Post|date=April 4, 2012|accessdate=November 15, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|author=Editorial Board|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/opinions/john-delaney-for-mds-6th-district/2012/03/08/gIQAg9Sy3R_story.html|title=John Delaney for Md.'s 6th District|publisher=The Washington Post|date=March 10, 2012|accessdate=November 15, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gazette.net/article/20120321/OPINION/703219529/1014/delaney-bartlett-for-congress-in-6th-district&template=gazette |title=Delaney, Bartlett for Congress in 6th District |publisher=Gazette.Net |accessdate=November 15, 2012 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120709064852/http://www.gazette.net/article/20120321/OPINION/703219529/1014/delaney-bartlett-for-congress-in-6th-district%26template%3Dgazette |archivedate=July 9, 2012 |df= }}</ref>\n\nOn April 3, 2012, Delaney won the five-candidate Democratic primary field with 54% of the vote. The next closest opponent, State Senator [[Robert J. Garagiola]], received 29% of the vote, 25 points behind Delaney.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=748169|title=MD District 06 - D Primary Race|date=April 3, 2012|publisher=Our Campaigns|accessdate=May 26, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.elections.state.md.us/elections/2012/results/primary/gen_detail_results_2012_3_DEM00806.html|title=Maryland State Board of Elections|publisher=Elections.state.md.us|accessdate=November 15, 2012}}</ref>\n\nIn the November 6, 2012 general election, Delaney defeated Bartlett by 59%\u201338%, a 21-point margin. He won mostly on the strength of a nearly 56,000-vote margin in Montgomery County, which accounted for almost all of the overall margin of 58,900 votes.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=705236|title=MD - District 06 Race|date=November 6, 2012|publisher=Our Campaigns|accessdate=May 26, 2016}}</ref>\n\n===2014 election===\nDelaney faced a closer-than-expected contest for reelection against Republican [[Dan Bongino]], the Republican candidate for Senate in 2012. He ultimately won by just over 2,200 votes, due mainly to swamping Bongino in the Montgomery County portion of the district by over 20,500 votes.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.cnn.com/election/2014/results/state/MD/house/06/ |title=Maryland House results |publisher=CNN |department=Politics |date=November 4, 2014 |access-date=November 4, 2017 }}</ref> [[Larry Hogan]] carried the district in his successful run for governor.\n\n===2016 election===\nDelaney won a third term in 2016, taking 56 percent of the vote to Republican Amie Hoeber's 40 percent.\n\n===Tenure===\n\nSince his election to Congress, Delaney introduced legislation to end partisan gerrymandering. The [[Open Our Democracy Act of 2017]] would appoint independent redistricting commissions nationwide to end partisan gerrymandering, make Election Day a federal holiday and create an open top-two primary system.<ref name=\"United States Congress\">{{cite web|title=Delaney Introduces Bill to End Gerrymandering, Reform Elections|url=https://delaney.house.gov/news/press-releases/delaney-introduces-bill-to-end-gerrymandering-reform-elections|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=July 4, 2017}}</ref>\n\nDelaney was ranked as the 53rd most bipartisan member of the U.S. House of Representatives during the [[114th United States Congress]] (and the most bipartisan member of the U.S. House of Representatives from Maryland) in the Bipartisan Index created by [[Richard Lugar#The Lugar Center|The Lugar Center]] and the [[McCourt School of Public Policy]] that ranks members of the United States Congress by their degree of bipartisanship (by measuring the frequency each member's bills attract co-sponsors from the opposite party and each member's co-sponsorship of bills by members of the opposite party).<ref>{{Citation|url=http://www.thelugarcenter.org/assets/htmldocuments/The%20Lugar%20Center%20-%20McCourt%20School%20Bipartisan%20Index%20114th%20Congress%20House%20Scores.pdf|title=The Lugar Center - McCourt School Bipartisan Index|publisher=[[Richard Lugar#The Lugar Center|The Lugar Center]]|date=March 7, 2016|accessdate=April 30, 2017}}</ref>  In 2015, a similar ranking by the nonpartisan site GovTrack ranked Delaney third highest for bipartisanship among all House Democrats.<ref>{{Citation|url=https://www.govtrack.us/congress/members/john_delaney/412544/report-card/2015|title=John Delaney Report Card 2015|publisher=[[Govtrack.us]]|date=January 9, 2016}}</ref>\n\n===2020 presidential bid===\n{{main|John Delaney presidential campaign, 2020}}\n\nDespite a rumored bid to run against incumbent governor [[Larry Hogan]] in 2018, Delaney plans to bypass the [[United States elections, 2018|2018 elections]] altogether.
