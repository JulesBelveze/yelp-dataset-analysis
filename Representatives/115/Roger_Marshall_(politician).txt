Marshall''' (born August 9, 1960) is an American politician and [[Obstetrics|obstetrician]]. He is a [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] and the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|KS|1}}. He was elected in [[United States House of Representatives elections in Kansas, 2016#District 1|2016]].\n\n==Pre-political life==\nMarshall was born in [[El Dorado, Kansas]].<ref>{{cite web|author=Special to the Sentinel Roger Marshall's office |url=http://m.mcphersonsentinel.com/article/20150528/news/150529357 |title=Marshall announces republican candidacy |publisher=M.mcphersonsentinel.com|date=2015-05-28|accessdate=2016-08-03}}</ref> He received a degree in biochemistry from [[Kansas State University]] of Agriculture and Applied Sciences and a Doctor of Medicine (M.D.) degree from the [[University of Kansas School of Medicine]]. He completed a residency in obstetrics and gynecology at Bayfront Medical Center in St. Petersburg, Florida.\n\nMarshall has served as chairman of the Board of Great Bend Regional Hospital and has been a district governor of [[Rotary International]]. He also served seven years in the [[United States Army Reserve]] reaching the rank of captain.<ref name=\"GBT1\">{{cite web|url=http://www.gbtribune.com/section/69/article/98209/ |title=Marshall Wins in Upset|author=Hogg, Dale|publisher=''Great Bend Tribune''|date=August 2, 2016}}</ref>\n\n==U.S. House of Representatives==\n[[File:Roger Marshall, 115th official photo.jpg|thumb|200px|Marshall's first official portrait]]\nOn January 31, 2018, Dr. Marshall was a passenger on a chartered Amtrak train involved in the [[2018 Republican Train Wreck]]. He administered first aid and CPR to the injured.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.kwch.com/content/news/Republican-lawmaker-Train-carrying-GOP-House-members-to-retreat-in-West-Virginia-has-hit-a-truck-471967243.html|title=Dr. Roger Marshall performs CPR on train-crash patient|last=KWCH|access-date=2018-05-15|language=en}}</ref>\n\n===2016 campaign===\nMarshall ran against incumbent [[Tim Huelskamp]] in the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]] [[primary election]] for {{ushr|KS|1}} in the [[United States House of Representatives]]. Marshall ran with the support of many of the state's agricultural groups, who were angered at Huelskamp losing his seat on the [[United States House Committee on Agriculture|House Agriculture Committee]], the first time in a century that a Kansan had not been on that panel.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.mcclatchydc.com/news/politics-government/congress/article91303977.html|title=Firebrand Kansas congressman feels heat in Republican primary|last=Tate|first=Curtis|publisher=[[McClatchy Washington Bureau]]|date=2016-07-22}}</ref>\n\nOn August 2, 2016, Marshall defeated Huelskamp in the Republican primary, 56 percent to 44 percent. No Democrat even filed.<ref name=\"KSS1\">{{cite web|url=http://www.kansascity.com/news/politics-government/election/article93390117.html|title=Tea party's Tim Huelskamp ousted by challenger Roger Marshall in Kansas congressional race|author= Robertson, Joe| publisher=''[[The Kansas City Star]]''|date=August 2, 2016}}</ref> In the general election, Marshall won handily, defeating independent Alan LaPolice and Libertarian Kerry Burt with 65.9 percent of the vote. However, the 1st is so heavily Republican that any Democratic challenger would have faced nearly impossible odds in any event. With a [[Cook Partisan Voting Index]] of R+24, it is the 18th most Republican district in the nation, and among districts in the Midwest, only {{ushr|NE|3}} is more Republican.\n\nMarshall's candidacy was endorsed by the [[United States Chamber of Commerce]], the Kansas Livestock Association, the [[National Association of Wheat Growers]], and the Kansas Farm Bureau, an affiliate of the [[American Farm Bureau Federation]].<ref name=\"KSS1\"/><ref name=\"Guard1\">{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2016/aug/03/roger-marshall-wins-kansas-republican-primary-against-tea-party-incumbent|title=Roger Marshall wins Kansas Republican primary against Tea Party incumbent|author=Staff |publisher=''[[The Guardian]]''|date=August 2, 2016}}</ref>\n\n===Tenure===\nRep. ===\n\nMarshall supports government funding for [[renewable energy]].<ref name=\"VS2016\"/>\n\n=== Environment ===\n\nMarshall has criticized the [[Environmental Protection Agency]] and supports reducing its authority.<ref name=\"Brydum\"/> Marshall supports the [[Renewable Fuel Standard (United States)|federal Renewable Fuel Standard]], which requires corn-based [[ethanol]] to be blended with [[gasoline]]; corn is produced in Marshall's district.<ref name=\"TateJuly2016\"/>\n\n=== Health care===\n\nMarshall supports repealing the [[Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act]].<ref name=\"MarchInterview\">{{cite news |author=Lev Facher |url=https://www.statnews.com/2017/03/03/roger-marshall-kansas-obamacare/ |title=Two months ago, this doctor was delivering babies. Now he's at the nexus of the Obamacare fight |work=''Stat'' via [[Boston Globe Media]] |date=March 3, 2017 |accessdate=May 6, 2017}}</ref> Like many other Republicans who want the \"health care system to rely on the free market rather than Obamacare's regulations\", Marshall \"measures success in how many people can afford to leave the Medicaid program and enter the private insurance market.\"<ref name=\"MarchInterview\" /> In opposing the Act's [[Medicaid expansion]], Marshall said in an interview in March 2017, \"Just, like, homeless people... I think just morally, spiritually, socially, (some people) just don't want health care. The Medicaid population, which is [on] a free credit card, as a group, do probably the least preventive medicine and taking care of themselves and eating healthy and exercising. And I'm not judging, I'm just saying socially that's where they are. So there's a group of people that even with unlimited access to health care are only going to use the emergency room when their arm is chopped off or when their pneumonia is so bad they get brought [into] the ER.\"<ref name=\"MarchInterview\" /> Those who support Medicaid, say that Marshall did not understand how Kansas Medicaid operates. The program provides \"medical coverage for low-income families and disabled Kansans\".<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.miamiherald.com/news/politics-government/article137269363.html |title=Poor 'just don't want health care,' congressman says, and the backlash begins |author=Bryan Lowry |work=Miami Herald |date=May 5, 2017 |accessdate=May 6, 2017 |location=[[Great Bend, Kansas]]}}</ref><ref name=\"MarchInterview\"/> Marshall's remark attracted widespread attention and criticism.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.kansascity.com/news/politics-government/article137189318.html |title=Poor 'don't want health care,' Kansas congressman says, and the backlash begins |work=Kansas City Star|date=March 8, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |author=[[Jonathan Chait]] |url=http://nymag.com/daily/intelligencer/2017/03/obamacare-repealer-explains-poor-dont-want-to-be-healthy.html |title=Republican Congressman: Repeal Obamacare Because Poor People Don't Want to Be Healthy |work=New York |date=March 3, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |author=Peter Sullivan |url=http://thehill.com/policy/healthcare/322285-gop-lawmaker-some-people-just-dont-want-health-care |title=GOP rep: Some people 'just don't want healthcare' |work=The Hill |date=March 3, 2017}}</ref>\n\n=== Economic issues ===\n\n==== Subsidies====\nMarshall, who is represents a rural district, supports [[farm subsidies]], such federal [[crop insurance]]. The KFB opposed Huelskamp's own opposition to some farm subsidies.<ref>Justin Wingerter, [http://cjonline.com/news-local-state/2016-07-08/kansas-farm-bureau-endorses-roger-marshall-over-rep-tim-huelskamp Kansas Farm Bureau endorses Roger Marshall over Rep. such legislation is supported by the agriculture lobby, which is powerful in Marshall's district.<ref name=\"TateJuly2016\">Curtis Tate, [http://www.mcclatchydc.com/news/politics-government/congress/article91303977.html Firebrand Kansas congressman feels heat in Republican primary], McClatchy DC (July 22, 2016).</ref><ref>Justin Wingerter, [http://cjonline.com/news-local/2015-07-16/congressional-challenger-roger-marshall-supports-paths-immigrants-block-grants Congressional challenger Roger Marshall supports paths for immigrants, block grants to replace ACA], ''Topeka Capital-Journal'' (July 16, 2015).</ref>\n\n===Social issues===\n\n==== Abortion====\nMarshall is [[Anti-abortion movement|anti-abortion]].<ref name=\"Brydum\">Sunnivie Brydum, [http://www.advocate.com/election/2016/8/03/antigay-kansas-rep-tim-huelskamp-wont-be-returning-congress Antigay Kansas Rep.
