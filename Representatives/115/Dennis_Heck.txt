The district is based in the state capital of [[Olympia, Washington|Olympia]], and also includes much of eastern [[Tacoma, Washington|Tacoma]].\n\nHeck was previously the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] nominee for U.S. Representative for the 3rd district in 2010, but was defeated by [[Jaime Herrera Beutler]], a Republican. In 2012 Heck ran and won in the newly created 10th district, defeating Republican [[Dick Muri]].<ref name=\"Schrader\">{{cite web|last=Schrader|first=Jordan|author2=Shannon, Brad|title=Democrats Derek Kilmer, Denny Heck win Congressional races|url=http://www.thenewstribune.com/2012/11/06/2358023/democrats-derek-kilmer-denny-heck.html|work=thenewstribune.com|publisher=Tacoma News, Inc.|accessdate=9 November 2012|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130921054748/http://www.thenewstribune.com/2012/11/06/2358023/democrats-derek-kilmer-denny-heck.html|archivedate=21 September 2013|df=}}</ref>\n\nHeck served as Chief of Staff to Washington Governor [[Booth Gardner]] from 1990 to 1993.  Previously, he was Majority Leader of the [[Washington State House of Representatives]] and Chief Clerk of the House.  He founded and served as CEO of [[TVW (Washington)|TVW]], the state equivalent of [[C-SPAN]].  From 1999 to 2012, he served on the board of directors of Intrepid Learning Solutions,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.intrepidls.com|title=Intrepid Learning Inc.'s corporate learning platform|publisher=|accessdate=23 November 2016}}</ref> which he co-founded with [[Christopher Hedrick]].\n\n==Early life, education, and business career==\nHeck was born in [[Vancouver, Washington]] in 1952, and raised in the [[Lake Shore, Washington|Lake Shore]] area of [[Clark County, Washington|Clark County]] where he graduated from [[Columbia River High School]]. He graduated from [[The Evergreen State College]] in [[Olympia, Washington|Olympia]] in 1973.\n\nHeck was the co-founder and from 1999 to 2012 was board director of [[Intrepid Learning Solutions]],<ref>[http://www.intrepidls.com/company/directors/ Intrepid Learning Solutions] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100918230211/http://www.intrepidls.com/company/directors/ |date=2010-09-18 }}</ref> a company specializing in business oriented education and training programs. He helped found [[Digital Efficiency]]<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.digitalfnc.com/whoweare.html|title=Digital Efficiency|publisher=|accessdate=23 November 2016 |deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110710124856/http://www.digitalfnc.com/whoweare.html|archivedate=10 July 2011}}</ref> which specializes in aiding businesses and medical facilities in transferring toward an all digital format. Heck helped found [[TVW (Washington)|TVW]],<ref>[http://www.tvw.org/about/history.cfm?CFID=619481&CFTOKEN=72224912&bhcp=1 TVW]{{dead link|date=January 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> the public affairs network for the state. TVW provides coverage of the [[Washington State Legislature]] and sessions of the [[Washington Supreme Court]].\n\nHeck is the author of ''Challenges and Opportunities:  The Transformation of Washington's Schools'', published in 1987.\n\n==Washington House of Representatives==\nStarting in 1976, Heck was elected to five terms in the [[Washington House of Representatives]], representing the [[Washington's 17th legislative district|17th Legislative District]] in Clark, [[Skamania County, Washington|Skamania]], and [[Klickitat County, Washington|Klickitat]] Counties. During that time he was elected Majority Leader, the second-ranking position in the House. He also co-chaired the Education Committee and wrote the state\u2019s historic Basic Education Act.\n\nHeck served as [[Chief of staff#Government|Chief of Staff]] for Governor [[Booth Gardner]] during
