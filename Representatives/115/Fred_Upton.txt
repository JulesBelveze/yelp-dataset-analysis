A member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]] and former Chairman of the [[United States House Committee on Energy and Commerce|Committee on Energy and Commerce]], he has played a major role in shaping post-Obamacare health-care legislation. The district stretches along the Michigan-[[Indiana]] border in the southwestern part of the state. If re-elected in 2018, he will become the dean of Michigan's congressional delegation upon the retirement of [[Sander Levin]].\n\n==Early life, education, and early political career==\nUpton was born in [[St. Joseph, Michigan]], the son of Elizabeth B. (n\u00e9e Vial) and Stephen Edward Upton.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.ancestry.com/~battle/reps/upton.htm|title=Frederick Stephen Upton|publisher=rootsweb.ancestry.com|accessdate=February 21, 2012}}</ref> Upton attended [[Shattuck-Saint Mary's]], graduating in 1971.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nndb.com/people/862/000040742|title=Fred Upton|accessdate=February 7, 2017}}</ref> He earned a [[Bachelor's degree|B.A.]] in journalism from the [[University of Michigan]] in 1975.
