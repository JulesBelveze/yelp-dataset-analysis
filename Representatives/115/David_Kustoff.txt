He served as a [[United States Attorney]] for the Western District of Tennessee from 2006 until 2008, and is the member of the [[United States House of Representatives]] for [[Tennessee's 8th congressional district]]. The district includes much of [[West Tennessee]], including the eastern fourth of [[Memphis, Tennessee|Memphis]] and most of its suburbs.\n\n==Early life and career==\nKustoff was born on October 8, 1966 in Memphis,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://info.cqrollcall.com/rs/764-XAC-282/images/CQ-NewMemberGuide-115thCongress.pdf|title=Guide to the New Congress |publisher=[[Roll Call]]|accessdate=January 3, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://books.google.ca/books?id=ydNvZI6Ncc4C&q=%22Kustoff,+David+Frank%22+tennessee&dq=%22Kustoff,+David+Frank%22+tennessee&hl=en&sa=X&ved=0ahUKEwiv9bnLnbvPAhUL2IMKHZpWBuIQ6AEIHDAA|title=1987 OUTSTANDING YOUNG MEN OF AMERICA|date=April 4, 1987|publisher=|accessdate=April 4, 2018|via=Google Books}}</ref> and raised in [[Shelby County, Tennessee]]. He graduated from Memphis' [[White Station High School]] in 1985.<ref name=leadership/> Kustoff attended the [[University of Memphis]], graduating with a bachelor's degree in business administration in 1989. He then attended the [[Cecil C. Humphreys School of Law]], graduating in 1992.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.memphis.edu/magazinearchive/v28i1/feat3.htm|title=Leading by example|work=The University of Memphis Magazine|first=Blair |last=Dedrick|access-date=September 14, 2016}}</ref>\n\nIn 1998, Kustoff opened a law firm with [[Jim Strickland (politician)|Jim Strickland]], who he met at the University of Memphis. Both would become active in Tennessee politics; Strickland was elected [[List of mayors of Memphis, Tennessee|Mayor of Memphis]] in [[Memphis mayoral election, 2015|2015]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.commercialappeal.com/news/government/city/profile-strickland-runs-on-humor-faith-ep-1229321977-326675461.html|title=Profile: Strickland runs on humor, faith|work=The Commercial Appeal|first=Ryan|last=Poe|date=August 14, 2015|accessdate=September 15, 2016}}</ref>\n\nKustoff became active in politics during the 1990s, in which he was chair of the Republican Party of Shelby County. He continued his political career in the 2000s, chairing [[George W. Bush]]'s election campaigns in [[United States presidential election, 2000|2000]] and [[United States presidential election, 2004|2004]], as well as chairing the campaign of [[Lamar Alexander]] for one of Tennessee's seats in the [[United States Senate|U.S. Senate]] in [[United States Senate election in Tennessee, 2002|2002]].<ref name=\"parispi.net\">{{Cite web|url=http://www.parispi.net/news/local_news/article_0763e006-0198-11e6-8843-b327fb2136a5.html|title=PARIS TN: Kustoff hopes to set self apart in Congress race|access-date=September 14, 2016}}</ref>\n\nKustoff unsuccessfully ran for Congress in the Republican primary for {{ushr|TN|7}} in 2002.  At the time, the district stretched from eastern Memphis to the [[Nashville]] suburbs.  However, with two other Memphians, [[Tennessee Senate|State Senator]] [[Mark Norris (American politician)|Mark Norris]] and city councilman Brent Taylor, splitting the Memphis area's vote, the primary was won by the only major candidate from the Nashville suburbs, State Senator [[Marsha Blackburn]], who went on to win the [[United States House of Representatives elections, 2002|general election]].<ref name=\":0\">{{Cite web|url=https://www.memphisdailynews.com/news/2016/aug/8/kustoff-victory-caps-tv-outsider-heavy-congressional-campaign/|title=Kustoff Victory Caps TV, Outsider Heavy Congressional Campaign|work=Memphis Daily News|date=August 8, 2016|access-date=September 14, 2016}}</ref>\n\nKustoff is a member of the [[Republican Study Committee]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Member List|author=|url=https://rsc-walker.house.gov/|format=|publisher=Republican Study Committee|date=|accessdate=21 December 2017}}</ref>\n\n== U.S. Attorney ==\nIn 2006, President Bush appointed Kustoff Kustoff|work=wmcactionnews5.com|access-date=September 14, 2016}}</ref>\n\nKustoff resigned as U.S. Attorney in shortly before the 2008 election, choosing to return to his private practice.<ref name=huckabee/>\n\n== 2016 congressional campaign ==\n\n=== Republican primary ===\nIn February 2016, [[Stephen Fincher]] announced that he would not run for re-election in Tennessee's 8th congressional district.  Redistricting four years earlier had shifted eastern Memphis and most of its suburbs to the 8th. Kustoff joined a crowded Republican primary\u2014the real contest in this heavily Republican district. He faced off against local leaders such as Shelby County Mayor Mark Luttrell, physician and former County Commissioner George Flinn, Shelby County Register of Deeds Tom Leatherwood, and State Senator [[Brian Kelsey]]. Flinn self-financed his campaign, spending $2.9 million, much of which went to TV ads.<ref name=\":0\" />\n\nKustoff emphasized the themes of law and order and reducing crime, as well as campaigning on reducing illegal immigration and bringing down [[West Tennessee]]'s high poverty rate. He praised Tennessee Governor [[Bill Haslam]]'s Tennessee Promise program, which offers two years of free community college education to qualifying Tennessee residents.<ref name=\"parispi.net\"/> Kustoff began to emerge from the crowded pack when he was endorsed by former [[Arkansas]] Governor and Republican Presidential candidate [[Mike Huckabee]], who filmed ads for Kustoff and campaigned with him.<ref name=huckabee/> Kustoff won the Republican primary, outperforming better-funded and more well-known candidates. The race reflected the lowest primary turnout in the district since 2004.<ref name=\":0\" />\n\n=== General election ===\nKustoff faced Democrat Rickey Hobson, a [[Delta Air Lines]] manager and [[Somerville, Tennessee]] resident. This district is one of the most Republican districts in the nation, and Kustoff was heavily favored to win. In spite of this, Kustoff ran an active campaign, kicking off a tour of the district's 15 counties and urging skeptical Republicans to support [[Donald Trump]] for the presidency.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.kustoffforcongress.com/2016/08/26/david-kustoff-launches-general-election-kick-off-tour-in-tennessees-8th-district/|title=David Kustoff Launches General Election \"Kick-Off Tour\" in Tennessee\u2019s 8th District \u2013 David Kustoff for Congress|website=Catch Digital Strategy|access-date=September 14, 2016}}</ref> Kustoff defeated Hobson in the general election.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.jacksonsun.com/story/news/politics/elections/2016/11/08/kustoff-wins-district-8-seat/93494710/|title=Kustoff wins District 8 seat|publisher=|accessdate=April 4, 2018}}</ref>\n\n==Political stances==\n===National security===\n\nKustoff supported President [[Donald Trump]]'s 2017 [[Executive Order 13769|executive order]] to impose a temporary ban on entry to the U.S. to citizens of seven Muslim-majority countries. He stated that \u201cI believe President Trump is putting American safety first, and I will encourage a long-term plan that is consistent with the values and compassion on which our great nation was founded.\u201d<ref name=\"Blake1\">{{cite web|last1=Blake|first1=Aaron|title=Coffman, Gardner join Republicans against President Trump\u2019s travel ban; here\u2019s where the rest stand|url=http://www.denverpost.com/2017/01/29/republicans-on-trump-travel-ban/|website=Denver Post|accessdate=30 January 2017}}</ref>\n\n===Health care===\n\nKustoff voted for the [[American Health Care Act]] in May 2017. \"[O]ur current health care system is failing Tennesseans\", he said afterwards. Later that month, a woman angrily confronted him about that vote during a [[town hall meeting]] at the [[University of Tennessee at Martin]]; after the meeting ended and Kustoff along with some of his staff got into their car, she gave chase and allegedly attempted to [[road rage|run them off the road]], then confronted them again about Kustoff's vote, reportedly banging on the windows of his car in the process. Police later arrested her on a [[felony]] charge of [[reckless endangerment]].<ref name=\"road rage incident\">{{cite news|last=Nashrulla|first=Tasneem|title=A Woman Allegedly Tried To Run A Republican Congressman Off The Road Over His Vote To Repeal Obamacare|url=https://www.buzzfeed.com/tasneemnashrulla/a-woman-allegedly-tried-to-run-a-republican-congressman-off|newspaper=[[Buzzfeed]]|date=May 14, 2017|accessdate=May 14, 2017}}</ref>\n\n== Personal life ==\nDavid Kustoff is married to Roberta Kustoff, who is also a lawyer at the Kustoff and Strickland Firm.
