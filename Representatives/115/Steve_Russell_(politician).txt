[[Legion of Merit]]<br>[[Bronze Star Medal|Bronze Star]] (with [[\"V\" device|valor]])<br>[[Combat Infantryman Badge]]<br>[[Ranger tab]]\n}}\n'''Steven Dane Russell''' (born May 25, 1963) is a retired American soldier and politician. A [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]], he represents {{ushr|OK|5}} (Oklahoma City) in the [[United States House of Representatives]].<ref>Casteel, Chris. \"[http://newsok.com/republican-steve-russell-elected-to-represent-oklahoma-city-in-congress/article/5363552 Republican Steve Russell elected to represent Oklahoma City in Congress],\" 'The Oklahoman' November 4, 2014.</ref> He is a former member of the [[Oklahoma Senate]].\n\n==Early life==\nRussell was born in [[Oklahoma City, Oklahoma]] to Clyde E. \"Gene\" Russell and Donna J. Porter Russell on May 25, 1963. Russell grew up in [[Del City, Oklahoma]]. The youngest of three, Russell nearly died at birth due to an opposite blood type with his mother. After weeks of intensive care, he recovered and would face several other brushes with death. At the age of seven, Russell was nearly killed by the deadly tornado that hit near Lake Eufala, Oklahoma on June 11, 1970 while visiting his grandparents. Later that year, Russell nearly died from a ruptured appendix and underwent two surgeries with weeks of intensive care at Midwest City Hospital.\n\nRussell attended school at Del City Elementary and Kerr Junior High. He became a Christian reading a [[Gideons International|Gideon's]] [[New Testament]] he picked up at Kerr in 1977. In 1980, Russell was an exchange student to Germany, living in Kasseedorf near Eutin in Schleswig-Holstein. Graduating from [[Del City High School]] in 1981, Russell was President of his senior class and voted most likely to succeed.<ref>Aquila, Vol. 29, Del City High School, 1981/</ref> After earning a four-year [[Army ROTC]] scholarship, Russell attended [[Ouachita Baptist University]] in [[Arkadelphia, Arkansas]]. After graduating with a degree in Public Speaking, Russell was commissioned a [[Second Lieutenant|second lieutenant]] in the [[U.S. Army]] [[infantry]].\n\n==Military career==\nReceiving his Infantry training at [[Fort Benning]], [[Georgia (U.S. state)|Georgia]], Russell married Cindy Myers, whom he met at Ouachita, on December 21, 1985. Russell would graduate from the U.S. Army Infantry Officers' Basic Course, Airborne School, Mortar School and a year later from the [[U.S. Army Ranger School]] in Class 11\u201387.<ref>http://www.benning.army.mil/infantry/rtb/Graduates/images/11-87.jpg.  Russell is standing 3rd row down from the 'G'/</ref>\n\nRussell served 21 years in airborne, light and mechanized assignments in the [[Arctic]], the desert, [[Pacific Ocean|the Pacific]], in [[Europe]] and in the [[Continental United States]]. He served more than seven years overseas and deployed operationally to [[Kosovo]], [[Kuwait]], [[Afghanistan]] and [[Iraq]].\n\nDuring [[Operation Iraqi Freedom]], Russell commanded the 1st Battalion, [[22nd Infantry Regiment (United States)|22nd Infantry]] 'Regulars' and conducted combat in [[Tikrit, Iraq]] from the spring of 2003 to the spring of 2004. His task force was a part of Colonel [[James Hickey (soldier)|James Hickey]]'s 1st Brigade, 4th Infantry Division. Russell's battalion was broadly covered during the first year of the war by [[CNN]], [[Fox News Channel]], [[ABC News|ABC]], [[NBC News|NBC]], [[CBS News|CBS]], ''[[Time (magazine)|TIME]]'', [[Associated Press]] and [[Reuters]]. His unit was a central player in the hunt and capture of [[Saddam Hussein]] and has been featured in the [[Discovery Channel]]'s ''Ace in the Hole''<ref>Ace in the Hole {{cite web |url=https://video.search.yahoo.com/video/play?p=Ace+in+the+Hole+Documentary&vid=637d55195372e13b036f0aadf4111b32&l=1%3A29%3A30&turl=http%3A%2F%2Fts1.mm.bing.net%2Fth%3Fid%3DV.4837624790649732%26pid%3D15.1&rurl=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.youtube.com%2Fwatch%3Fv%3DSznP8bD-I3E&tit=Ace+in+the+Hole+-+Capturing+Saddam+Hussein.mp4&c=0&sigr=11aeq80fv&sigt=11el856c5&ct=p&age=0&&tt=b}}</ref> and BBC Panorama's 'Saddam on the Run' documentaries.\n\n[[File:Peter Jennings with Steve Russell.png|thumb|left|Russell with [[Peter Jennings]] in Tikrit, Iraq in March 2004]]\n\nRussell is highly decorated,<ref name=\"infantry1\">{{cite web|url=http://1-22infantry.org/current/russell.htm |title=1st Battalion 22nd Infantry Former CO elected to senate |publisher=1-22infantry.org |date=August 27, 2008 |accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref> having received the [[Legion of Merit]], the [[Bronze Star]] Medal with Valor Device<ref>{{cite web|author=iPad iPhone Android TIME TV Populist The Page |url=http://content.time.com/time/video/player/0,32068,4769005001_1866333,00.html |title=Revisiting a Tikrit Ambush - Video - TIME.com |publisher=Content.time.com |date= |accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref> and Oak Leaf Cluster, the [[Combat Infantryman's Badge]] and his unit in Iraq was awarded the [[Valorous Unit Award]] for its role in the hunt and capture of Saddam Hussein.<ref>Valorous Unit Award, April 15, 2003 to Mar 15, 2004, 1st Battalion 22nd Infantry cited; Permanent Orders 031-14, 2008, Streamer embroidered IRAQ 2003\u20132004</ref>\n\nRussell continued in command of the 1st Battalion, 22nd Infantry until June, 2005, when he was selected to become the Chief of Tactics at the US Army Infantry School at Ft. Benning, GA. Turning down a [[Queen's University]] Fellowship to [[Kingston, Canada]] for the [[U.S. Army War College]] and promotion to [[colonel]], Russell retired as a [[Lieutenant Colonel|lieutenant colonel]] in 2006. He returned to [[Oklahoma]] with his wife and five children.{{Citation needed|date=September 2015}}\n\n[[File:RussellwUSandSaddamUniforms.png|thumb|Congressman Steve Russell appearing with uniforms on loan to the Oklahoma History Center. The American uniform is one he wore during the hunt for Saddam Hussein and the green uniform was one worn by Saddam Hussein himself while President of Iraq. The uniforms are part of a special collection on display of Saddam related artifacts.]]\n\n==Post-military career==\nRussell began an active veterans advocacy career upon retirement and worked closely with other veterans to bolster the soldiers voice in the wars in Iraq and Afghanistan. He formed Vets for Victory with several decorated warriors holding rallies in several states to embolden the public to support the troops.<ref>{{cite web|author=Brian C. Scherr, 2006 |url=http://vets4victory.com/rally_okla.html |title=rally_okla |publisher=Vets4victory.com |date= |accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref> Russell also toured the country with many distinguished and highly decorated veterans such as [[David Bellavia]], author of ''[[House to House]]''; [[Marcus Luttrell]], author of ''[[Lone Survivor (book)|Lone Survivor]]''; Jeremiah Workman, author of ''Shadow of the Sword''; and Sean Parnell, author of ''[[Outlaw Platoon]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vetsforfreedom.org/heroestour/|title=Vets for Freedom|work=vetsforfreedom.org|accessdate=December 8, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.defendfreedomtour.com/vets-on-board/ |title=Vets on Board |publisher=Defend Freedom Tour |date= |accessdate=February 28, 2014 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://archive.is/20140221172728/http://www.defendfreedomtour.com/vets-on-board/ |archivedate=February 21, 2014 |df=mdy-all }}</ref>\n\nAs a motivational speaker, Russell embarked on a successful speaking career in hundreds of cities since 2007. He has been featured as a [[keynote]] speaker at such notable events as the Ring Banquet at the [[U.S. Air Force Academy]] and was honored as the [[Paul R. Smith]] Award recipient for his distinguished service in Iraq at the American Veterans Center 15th Annual Awards Gala in 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.americanveteranscenter.org/2013/01/2012-awards-gala-broadcast/ |title=2012 Awards Gala Broadcast |publisher=American Veterans Center |accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref> He is managed by Premiere Speakers Bureau of [[Franklin, Tennessee]].<ref>{{cite web|author=Steve Russell |url=http://premierespeakers.com/steve_russell |title=Steve Russell &#124; Premiere Motivational Speakers Bureau |publisher=Premierespeakers.com |date=July 25, 2012 |accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref>\n\n[[File:We Got Him! Display.jpg|thumbnail|Display of Russell's book We Got Him! published by Simon & Schuster]]\n\nRussell has also been a successful writer and author. His opinion pieces have appeared in many major publications, and he is the author of ''We Got Him!: A Memoir of the Hunt and Capture of Saddam Hussein'', published by [[Simon & Schuster]] in December 2011. An earlier version of the work was published by Deeds Publishing that year. The book has received critical acclaim for its vivid portrayal of combat and the events surrounding the hunt for the Iraqi dictator and his capture.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nyjournalofbooks.com/book-review/we-got-him-memoir-hunt-and-capture-saddam-hussein |title=a book review: We Got Him! A Memoir of the Hunt and Capture of Saddam Hussein |publisher=Nyjournalofbooks.com |date=December 6, 2011 |accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref> It received numerous endorsements to include General [[Tommy Franks]] and the forward was written by General [[Ray Odierno]].\n\nRussell also has appeared on many national television and radio shows as a military and foreign affairs commentator. In addition, he has made appearances on Spike TV's ''[[Deadliest Warrior]]'' and four episodes of [[Outdoor Channel]]'s ''[[Gun Stories]]''.\n\nRussell is also the owner and founder of Two Rivers Arms, a small rifle manufacturing business that makes copies of the Iraqi Tabuk AK-47 and Iraqi Tabuk Sniper Rifle.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.tworiversarms.com |title=index.html |publisher=Tworiversarms.com |date= |accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.news9.com/category/116601/video-page?clipId=8210573&autostart=true |title=Videos \u2013 News9.com \u2013 Oklahoma City, OK \u2013 News, Weather, Video and Sports &#124; |publisher=News9.com |date=February 4, 2014 |accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.news9.com/story/20929806/del-city-gun-shop-working?autoStart=true&topVideoCatNo=default&clipId=8299842 |title=Del City Gun Shop Struggling To Keep Up With Demand \u2013 News9.com \u2013 Oklahoma City, OK \u2013 News, Weather, Video and Sports &#124; |publisher=News9.com |date= |accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref> The company built half a dozen special Iraqi rifle replicas for Clint Eastwood's film ''[[American Sniper (film)|American Sniper]]''.<ref>http://www.theakforum.net/forums/109-two-rivers-arms/165803-two-rivers-arms-makes-enemy-rifles-upcoming-clint-eastwood-film-american-sniper.html</ref><ref>Guns & Ammo AK-47 Issue, p. 82 & ff., September 2015, Guns & Ammo 2016 Annual, p. 42 & ff., Guns & Ammo Combat Arms Fall 2016, p. 122 & ff. Outdoor Sportsman Group, NY, NY; AK-47 & Soviet Weapons Issue #179, p. 34 & ff., Harris Publications Inc., NY, NY</ref>\n\n==Political career==\nRussell began his service to the public in 2008 when he defeated four opponents in one of the most contested races in the [[Oklahoma State Senate]] that year. Russell won the initial Republican primary with 42% of the vote, leading by 15 points. He won the Republican run off with 67% of the vote.<ref name=\"infantry1\"/>\n\nAs a state senator Russell authored the Soldiers Relief Act that made Oklahoma\u2019s serving military tax exempt, affecting over 50,000 soldiers and their families.<ref>{{cite web|author=Julie Bisbee \u2022 |url=http://newsok.com/editor-comments-on-military-tax-break/article/3846413 |title=Editor comments on military tax break |publisher=News OK |date=June 16, 2009 |accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref>  He also authored the Soldiers Credentialing Act, a law allowing credit for soldiers skills through direct certification rather than additional schooling allowing them to quickly enter the work force. Additionally, Russell authored a number of bills that became law related to the 2nd Amendment. Shortly after the publication of his book, Russell announced he would not seek reelection and he decided to focus on his business, public speaking and veterans advocacy.<ref>{{cite web|last=Associated |first=The |url=http://newsok.com/oklahoma-state-senator-wont-seek-another-term/article/3612973 |title=Oklahoma state senator won't seek another term |publisher=News OK |date=October 13, 2011 |accessdate=February 28, 2014}}</ref>\n\nIn January 2014, Russell announced he would seek the seat of the [[United States House of Representatives]] in {{ushr|OK|5}}, which was being vacated by [[James Lankford]] in his US Senate bid.<ref name=\"autogenerated1\">{{cite web |url=http://kfor.com/2014/01/21/former-state-sen-steve-russell-announces-plans-to-run-for-lankfords-congressional-seat/ |title=Former State Sen. vote.<ref>https://www.nytimes.com/elections/results/oklahoma-house-district-5-primary-election</ref>\n\n'''Tenure'''\n\nRep. Russell was the main sponsor of H.R. 194, Federal Agency Mail Management Act of 2017, which became law on November 21, 2017 during the 115th Congress, 1st Session (2017). This bill would require the General Services Administration (GSA) to provide guidance and assistance to federal agencies for effective mail processing, leading to a more efficient, cost-saving system.<ref>https://www.congress.gov/bill/115th-congress/house-bill/194</ref>\n\n==Committee assignments==\n* '''[[United States House Committee on Armed Services]]'''\n* '''[[United States House Committee on Oversight and Government Reform|Committee on Oversight and Government Reform]]'''\u2014serves as Vice Chairman of Committee\n** [[United States House Oversight Subcommittee on National Security]]\u2014serves as Vice Chairman of Subcommittee\n* '''[[House Republican Steering Committee]]'''\n\n==Personal life==\nRussell is a fifth-generation Oklahoman. His hobbies include teaching Sunday school, reading and studying history, playing the guitar and singing, as well as collecting military antiques and firearms. Russell has been married to the former Cindy Myers of [[Columbus, Arkansas]] since 1985. They have five grown children, three of whom were adopted from [[Hungary]]. They are members of First Southern Baptist Church in Del City.\n\n==Education==\nRussell is a graduate of Del City High, Class of 1981. He was inducted into the Del City Hall of Fame in its very first class along with professional football player [[Bob Kalsu]] who was killed in Vietnam and two-time Olympic Gold Medalist [[John Smith (wrestler)|John Smith]].\n\nRussell graduated from [[Ouachita Baptist University]] with a BA in Public Speaking. He was the president and pledge master of his fraternity and cadet corps commander of the [[Army Reserve Officers' Training Corps|ROTC Cadets]].\n\nRussell also earned a [[Masters of Military Art and Science|MMAS]] in Military History with a focus on East Asia from the [[Command and General Staff College]] at [[Fort Leavenworth]], [[Kansas]] in 1998. His other military education includes the Infantry Officers Basic Course, the Armored Officers Career Course, the Combined Arms Staff School and the Battalion Commanders Course.\n\nRussell's military skills schools include, Airborne School, Ranger School, Northern Warfare Summer and Winter Phase, Mortar School, and the Bradley Gunners and Commanders Courses.\n\n[[File:Russellmedals.jpg|thumbnail|Awards, Decorations and Badges of Steve Russell]]\n\n==Military Awards and Decorations==\nRussell has been decorated for campaign service, meritorious achievement, and valor. His decorations include the [[Legion of Merit]], the [[Bronze Star Medal]] for valor and service, the [[Meritorious Service Medal]] (six awards), the [[Joint Service Commendation Medal]], the [[Army Commendation Medal]] (three awards), the [[Army Achievement Medal]] (four awards), the [[National Defense Service Medal]] (two awards), the [[Armed Forces Expeditionary Medal]], the [[Kosovo Campaign Medal]], the [[Afghanistan Campaign Medal]], the [[Iraq Campaign Medal]], the [[Global War on Terrorism Expeditionary Medal]], the [[Global War on Terrorism Service Medal]], the [[Military Outstanding Volunteer Service Medal]], the [[NATO Medal]] for [[Kosovo]], and Russell is a member of the [[Military Order of the Loyal Legion of the United States]]. He was awarded the [[Ranger Tab]], the [[Combat Infantryman Badge]] and the [[US Army and Korean Parachutists Badge]].<ref>{{Cite web|title = LTC Steven D.
