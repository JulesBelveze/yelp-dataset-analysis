Gottheimer''' ({{IPAc-en|\u02c8|\u0261|\u0252|t|h|a\u026a|m|\u0259r}}; born March 8, 1975) is an American lawyer, writer, public policy adviser, and the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|NJ|5}}. He has been active in the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]] as a [[speechwriter]] for [[Bill Clinton]] and as an adviser to the presidential campaigns of [[Wesley Clark]], [[John Kerry]], and [[Hillary Clinton]]. He has also worked for [[Burson-Marsteller]], the [[Federal Communications Commission]], [[Ford Motor Company]], and [[Microsoft]]. On November 8, 2016, he defeated incumbent [[Scott Garrett]] in the congressional race for New Jersey's 5th District.\n\n==Career==\n[[File:Josh Gottheimer FCC.jpg|thumb|Gottheimer in 2012]]\nGottheimer was born in [[Livingston, New Jersey]] on March 8, 1975.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://info.cqrollcall.com/rs/764-XAC-282/images/CQ-NewMemberGuide-115thCongress.pdf |title=Guide to the New Congress |publisher=[[Roll Call]] |accessdate=January 3, 2017}}</ref> At the age of 16, Gottheimer served as a [[United States Senate Page]] for [[Frank Lautenberg]], a Senator from [[New Jersey]]. Through high school and college, Gottheimer held internships with [[C-SPAN]], the [[Secretary of the United States Senate]], and [[Tom Foley]], the [[Speaker of the United States House of Representatives]].<ref name=phone/>\n\nGottheimer graduated from [[West Essex High School]], the [[University of Pennsylvania]], and [[Harvard Law School]]. While at the University of Pennsylvania, he served on the \"rapid response team\" for [[Bill Clinton]]'s [[United States presidential election, 1996|1996 reelection campaign]]. Following Clinton's relection, Gottheimer attended [[Pembroke College, Oxford]], on a [[Thouron Award|Thouron Fellowship]], studying toward a [[Doctor of Philosophy|DPhil]] in modern history. He joined the Clinton [[Presidency of Bill Clinton|administration]] as a [[speechwriter]] in 1998, at the age of 23,<ref name=phone>{{cite web|last=Palmer|first=Joanne|url=http://jewishstandard.timesofisrael.com/and-then-the-phone-rang/|title='And then the phone rang\u2026'; Wyckoff man\u2019s adventures in politics and public service|work=[[The Times of Israel]]|date=February 14, 2014|accessdate=January 13, 2016}}</ref> working in the administration until its end in 2001. While attending law school, Gottheimer worked as an adviser for [[Wesley Clark]]'s [[Wesley Clark presidential campaign, 2004|2004 presidential campaign]], [[John Kerry]]'s [[John Kerry presidential campaign, 2004|2004 presidential campaign]], and [[Hillary Clinton]]'s [[Hillary Clinton presidential campaign, 2008|2008 presidential campaign]].<ref name=protege>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2015/12/26/nyregion/protege-of-hillary-and-bill-clintons-targets-us-congressional-seat.html?_r=0|title=Prot\u00e9g\u00e9 of Clintons Targets U.S. Congressional Seat|first=Alexander|last=Burns|work=The New York Times|date=December 25, 2015|accessdate=January 13, 2016}}</ref> After the 2004 election, Gottheimer worked for the [[Ford Motor Company]], and then became executive vice president for [[Burson-Marsteller]].<ref name=phone/> From 2010 to 2012, he worked for the [[Federal Communications Commission]],<ref>{{cite press release|url=https://apps.fcc.gov/edocs_public/attachmatch/DOC-314747A1.pdf|format=pdf|title=Josh Gottheimer, Senior Counselor to the Chairman, to step down; Jordan Usdan named Acting Director of Public-Private Initiatives|publisher=Federal Communications Commission|date=June 20, 2012|accessdate=January 13, 2016}}</ref> where he led an initiative related to [[broadband]] internet.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rrmediagroup.com/News/NewsDetails/NewsID/8029|title=FCC\u2019s Gottheimer to Lead New Broadband Public/Private Initiative|publisher=RadioResource Media Group|date=March 7, 2012|accessdate=January 13, 2016}}</ref> He then joined [[Microsoft]] as a strategist.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.business-standard.com/article/technology/former-washington-political-brawler-now-battles-for-microsoft-112121600065_1.html|title=Former Washington political brawler now battles for Microsoft|first1=Nick|last1=Wingfield|first2=Claire|last2=Cain Miller|date=December 16, 2012|publisher=Business Standard|accessdate=January 13, 2016}}</ref>\n\n==Congressional career==\nIn the [[United States House of Representatives elections in New Jersey, 2016|2016 elections]], Gottheimer ran for the House of Representatives in {{ushr|NJ|5}}, a seat held by Republican [[Scott Garrett]]. the Times of Israel, Gottheimer explained his inability to support President Obama's [[Iran deal]], saying that it was not \"in the best interest of the United States or our allies in the region,\" mainly because it would \"not preclude Iran from developing a nuclear weapon.\" He challenged what he described as five myths that had \"developed around the deal,\" among them the notion that it would prevent Iran from getting a nuclear weapon and the belief that without the deal Iran would \"be left to operate without any economic sanctions.\"<ref>{{cite web |last1=Gottheimer |first1=Josh
