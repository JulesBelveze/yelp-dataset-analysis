2014.]]\n\n==Early life and career==\n\nJim Banks was born in [[Columbia City, Indiana]]. He earned his undergraduate from [[Indiana University]] and his [[MBA]] from [[Grace College]]. He worked in the [[real estate]] and [[construction]] industry in [[Fort Wayne, Indiana]] prior to his role in elected office.<ref name=\"About\">{{cite web|title=About|url=https://banks.house.gov/about|website=Congressman Jim Banks|accessdate=28 December 2017|language=en|date=3 December 2012}}</ref>\n\n==Military career==\n\nBanks serves in the [[U.S. Navy Reserve]] as a supply corps officer. From 2014 to 2015, he took a leave of absence from the Indiana State Senate to serve in Afghanistan.<ref name=\"About\"/>\n\n==Political career==\nFrom 2008 to 2010, Banks served on the [[Whitley County, Indiana|Whitley County]] Council from the At Large district.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100921060710/http://www.whitleygov.com:80/department/?fDD=5-0|title=Whitley County, Indiana / County Council|date=2010-09-21|access-date=2018-05-16}}</ref> He won the primary after defeating incumbent County Councilman, Scott Darley.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://talkofthetownwc.com/blog/2008/05/|title=Talk of the Town - Whitley County: May 2008 Archives|website=talkofthetownwc.com|access-date=2018-05-16}}</ref> He was succeeded by Paula Reimers on the County Council.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://busconews.com/reimers-wins-council-seat/|title=Reimers wins Council seat {{!}} Busco News|website=busconews.com|language=en-US|access-date=2018-05-16}}</ref> Banks also served as Chair of the Whitley County Republican Party from 2007-2011.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://ballotpedia.org/Jim_Banks_(Indiana)|title=Jim Banks (Indiana) - Ballotpedia|access-date=2018-05-16|language=en-US}}</ref> He was succeeded by Matt Boyd as Party Chair.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://talkofthetownwc.com/blog/2011/06/matt_boyd_named_new_whitley_co.html|title=Talk of the Town - Whitley County: Matt Boyd named new Whitley County GOP chairman, caucus chooses Paul Zilz for secretary|website=talkofthetownwc.com|access-date=2018-05-16}}</ref>\n\nWith assistance from the [[American Legislative Exchange Council]] (ALEC) he has supported [[Right-to-work law|right-to-work legislation]] in Indiana.<ref>{{cite news|title=National Group Pushes Indiana 'Right-To-Work' Law: American Legislative Exchange Council Backs Bill |url=http://www.theindychannel.com/news/politics/national-group-pushes-indiana-right-to-work-law |newspaper=WRTV Indianapolis|date=December 7, 2011}}</ref>\n\nBanks addressed the 2014 [[Conservative Political Action Conference]] in Washington, D.C., on March 9, 2014, after he was selected as one of their Top 10 Conservatives Under 40.<ref>{{cite news|title=Sen.
