Zeldin''' (born January 30, 1980) is an American attorney and politician currently serving as a [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] member of the [[United States House of Representatives|Congress]] for {{ushr|NY|1}}, elected in 2014. The 1st district includes Central and Eastern [[Suffolk County, New York|Suffolk County]], including most of [[Smithtown, New York|Smithtown]], as well as the entirety of the towns of [[Brookhaven, New York|Brookhaven]], [[Riverhead (town), New York|Riverhead]], [[Southold, New York|Southold]], [[Southampton (town), New York|Southampton]], [[East Hampton (town), New York|East Hampton]], and [[Shelter Island (town), New York|Shelter Island]], and a small portion of [[Islip (town), New York|Islip]].\n\n==Early life, education, and military service==\nZeldin was raised in [[Shirley, New York|Shirley, Suffolk County, New York]] and graduated from [[William Floyd High School]] in 1998. He received a bachelor's degree ''cum laude'' in political science from the [[University at Albany, SUNY|University at Albany]] in 2001 and earned his [[Juris Doctor]] from [[Albany Law School]] of [[Union University (New York)|Union University, New York]] in May 2003.<ref name=\"Official bio\">{{cite web|title=Lee Zeldin|url=https://zeldin.house.gov/about|publisher=house.gov|accessdate=February 1, 2016}}</ref>\n\nAfter law school, Zeldin received an [[Army ROTC]] commission as a Second Lieutenant, assigned to the Military Intelligence Corps of the [[United States Army]]. In January 2004, he was admitted to the [[New York State Bar]]. In 2006, he was deployed to [[Iraq]] with an infantry battalion of paratroopers from the [[82nd Airborne Division]]. Zeldin also served as a lawyer in the [[Judge Advocate General's Corps, United States Army|Judge Advocate General's Corps]]. In 2007, he transitioned from active duty to the [[United States Army Reserve|Army Reserve]] where he currently serves with the rank of Major.{{Citation needed|date=September 2018}} In 2008, Zeldin started a law practice in [[Smithtown, New York]], which he operated full-time until being elected to [[New York's 3rd State Senate district]] in 2010.<ref name=\"Official bio\"/>\n\n==New York Senate==\n\n===Elections===\nIn 2010, Zeldin ran in the [[New York Senate|New York State Senate]]'s 3rd district, challenging [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] incumbent [[Brian X.
