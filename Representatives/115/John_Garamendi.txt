Garamendi was previously the [[California Insurance Commissioner]] from 1991 to 1995 and 2003 to 2007, the [[United States Deputy Secretary of the Interior|U.S. Deputy Secretary of the Interior]] from 1995 to 1998, and the [[List of Lieutenant Governors of California|46th Lieutenant Governor of California]] from 2007<!--This date is correct. See http://www.ltg.ca.gov/biography/aboutjohn.asp for verification.--> until his election to Congress.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sos.ca.gov/elect-results/cd10-results.htm |title=Congressional District 10 Special General Election Results |publisher=[[Secretary of State of California]] |date=November 3, 2009 |accessdate=November 3, 2009 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20091019140853/http://www.sos.ca.gov/elect-results/cd10-results.htm |archivedate=October 19, 2009 }}</ref>\n\nGaramendi was born in [[Camp Blanding, Florida]] and raised in [[Mokelumne Hill, California]]. He earned a [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] in business from the [[University of California, Berkeley]] and an [[Master of Business Administration|M.B.A.]] from [[Harvard Business School]], then served in the [[Peace Corps]] in [[Ethiopia]] from 1966 to 1968. He was elected to the [[California State Assembly]] in 1974, serving a single term before being elected in 1976 to the [[California State Senate]], where he served for four terms until 1990. During this time he had a spell as Majority Leader and ran unsuccessfully for the Democratic nominations for [[Governor of California]] in [[California gubernatorial election, 1982|1982]] and for [[California State Controller]] in 1986.\n\nIn 1990, he became the first elected [[California Insurance Commissioner]], serving from 1991 to 1995. Previously, Insurance Commissioners had been appointed by the Governor. Rather than seek re-election, he ran for governor in the [[California gubernatorial election, 1994|1994]] election, losing in the Democratic primary. He left public office and served as [[President of the United States|President]] [[Bill Clinton]]'s [[United States Deputy Secretary of the Interior|Deputy Secretary of the Interior]] from 1995 to 1998, then worked for the Peace Corps again. He was elected insurance commissioner again in 2002 and briefly ran for governor again in the [[California gubernatorial recall election|2003 recall]], before dropping out to support Lieutenant Governor [[Cruz Bustamante]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.garamendi.org/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=47:garamendi-bid-press-release&catid=3:press-releases&Itemid=55 |title=Garamendi for Congress |website=Garamendi.org |accessdate=January 14, 2016}}</ref> In 2006, he was elected lieutenant governor to succeed the term-limited Bustamante.\n\nGaramendi had planned to run for governor for a fourth time in [[California gubernatorial election, 2010|2010]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://books.google.co.uk/books?id=Pf3UpAu73iAC&pg=PA182 |title=California After Arnold |author1=Stephen D. Cummings |author2=Patrick B. Reddy |page=182 |website=Books.google.co.uk |date= |accessdate=January 14, 2016}}</ref> but after Democratic U.S. Representative [[Ellen Tauscher]] of {{ushr|CA|10}} resigned to become [[Under Secretary of State for Arms Control and International Security]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mercurynews.com/breakingnews/ci_11942936/ |title=Tauscher accepts State Department post - San Jose Mercury News |publisher=Mercurynews.com|accessdate=August 22, 2010}}</ref> Garamendi won a [[California's 10th congressional district special election, 2009|November 2009]] special election to succeed her.<ref>[http://uk.truveo.com/John-Garamendi-announces-for-Congress/id/657387170] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110717110225/http://uk.truveo.com/John-Garamendi-announces-for-Congress/id/657387170 |date=July 17, 2011 }}</ref> He was re-elected in 2010 and, after redistricting, for the {{ushr|CA|3}} in 2012 and 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nationaljournal.com/almanac/member/1775#biography |title=NationalJournal |website=NationalJournal.com |accessdate=January 14, 2016}}</ref>\n\n==Early life and education==\nGaramendi was born in Camp Blanding, Florida, and was raised in [[Mokelumne Hill, California]], the son of Mary Jane (n\u00e9e McSorley) and Raymond V. Garamendi. His paternal grandparents were [[Basque people|Basque]] immigrants from [[Spain]] and his mother was of half Irish and half Italian ancestry.<ref name=Origin>[http://www.euskosare.org/euskal_mundua/john_garamendi_state_capitol_intvw]  {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080526002050/http://www.euskosare.org/euskal_mundua/john_garamendi_state_capitol_intvw |date=May 26, 2008 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.ancestry.com/%7Ebattle/reps/garamendi.htm |title=John Garamendi ancestry |website=Freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.ancestry.com |date= |accessdate=January 14, 2016}}</ref> Garamendi received a [[Bachelor of Arts]] in [[business]] from the [[University of California, Berkeley]], where he was both a football player and wrestler, and a [[Master of Business Administration]] degree from [[Harvard Business School]].<ref name=\"House bio\">{{cite web |title=Biography |url=http://garamendi.house.gov/about/biography.shtml |publisher=US House of Representatives |accessdate=January 8, 2011 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110106091115/http://garamendi.house.gov/about/biography.shtml |archivedate=January 6, 2011}}</ref> He served in the [[Peace Corps]] in [[Ethiopia]] from 1966 to 1968.<ref>[http://www.peacecorps.gov/index.cfm?shell=learn.whatispc.notable.gov ] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20060929190134/http://www.peacecorps.gov/index.cfm?shell=learn.whatispc.notable.gov |date=September 29, 2006 }}</ref> Garamendi is an [[Eagle Scout (Boy Scouts of America)|Eagle Scout]]. Six-term Republican incumbent [[William T. Bagley]] did not stand for re-election, instead running for [[California State Controller]] and losing in the general election to Democratic State Assemblyman [[Kenneth Cory]]. In the election to succeed Bagley, Garamendi faced Republican State Assemblyman [[Douglas F. Carter]]. Carter had won a special election in the [[California's 12th State Assembly district|12th Assembly district]] in July 1973 to succeed Republican [[Robert T. Monagan]], who had resigned to become [[United States Secretary of Transportation|United States Assistant Secretary of Transportation]]. In the general election, Garamendi defeated Carter in a landslide, by 60,380 votes (64.08%) to 33,842 (35.92%) as the Democrats won a supermajority in the [[California State Assembly]] for the first time since the 1800s.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=549314 | title=Our Campaigns - CA State Assembly 07 1974|publisher=Our Campaigns|accessdate=January 25, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.newspapers.com/newspage/47186063/ | title=The Times Standard from Eureka, California \u00b7 Page 18|publisher=The Times Standard|date=November 7, 1974|accessdate=January 25, 2015}}</ref>\n\n===State Senate===\nIn 1976, he decided to retire after one term to run in [[California's 13th State Senate district]]. He defeated Republican Bob Whitten 53%-47%.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=543766 | title=Our Campaigns - CA Senate 13 | publisher=Our Campaigns | accessdate=April 15, 2013}}</ref> He won re-election in 1980 (60%),<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=500139 |title=CA State Senate 13 Race - Nov 04, 1980 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=January 14, 2016}}</ref> 1984 (69%),<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=443280 |title=CA State Senate 05 Race - Nov 06, 1984 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=January 14, 2016}}</ref> and 1988 (69%).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=374790 |title=CA State Senate 05 Race - Nov 08, 1988 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=January 14, 2016}}</ref>\n\nWhile in the [[California Senate]], he served as Majority Leader. His sponsored legislation reformed health care, welfare, and the state tax code. He also protected [[Lake Tahoe]] and [[Mono Lake]].<ref name=\"garamendi.house.gov\">[http://garamendi.house.gov/about/biography.shtml ] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110106091115/http://garamendi.house.gov/about/biography.shtml |date=January 6, 2011 }}</ref> He was Chairman of the Joint Committee on Science and Technology, the Senate Health and Welfare Committee, and the Senate Revenue and Taxation Committee.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/CandidateDetail.html?CandidateID=3652 |title=Candidate - John Garamendi |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=January 14, 2016}}</ref>\n\n===1982 gubernatorial election===\n{{See also|California gubernatorial election, 1982}}\nHe ran for [[Governor of California]] for the first time in 1982. In the Democratic primary, [[Los Angeles Mayor]] [[Tom Bradley (American politician)|Tom Bradley]] defeated him 61%-25%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=389358 |title=CA Governor - D Primary Race - Jun 08, 1982 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=January 14, 2016}}</ref>\n\n===1986 Controller election===\nIn the 1986 Democratic primary for [[California State Controller]], Garamendi lost to Assemblyman [[Gray Davis]], 50%-38%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=563457 |title=CA Controller - D Primary Race - Jun 03, 1986 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=January 14, 2016}}</ref>\n\n==Insurance Commissioner (1991\u20131995)==\n[[File:John Garamendi.jpg|upright|left|thumb|Garamendi as California Insurance Commissioner]]\n\n===1990 election===\nHe ran for [[California Insurance Commissioner]] for the first time in 1990. He won the Democratic primary with a plurality of 36% of the vote. His closest challenger was radio talk show host [[Bill Press]], who got 28% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=554017 |title=CA Insurance Commissioner - D Primary Race - Jun 05, 1990 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=January 14, 2016}}</ref> In the general election, he defeated Republican [[Wes Bannister]] 52%-38%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=331382 |title=CA Insurance Commissioner Race - Nov 06, 1990 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=January 14, 2016}}</ref>\n\n===Tenure===\nIn 1991, while serving as the newly instituted California Insurance Commissioner, Garamendi seized Executive Life, a failing life insurance company, and resold it to French investors who turned out to be fronts for a government-owned French bank. They made billions of dollars selling off the company's portfolio of [[junk bonds]] while the Californians with Executive Life policies were very negatively affected. This situation erupted into a scandal in both the U.S. and France, with the U.S. government filing criminal charges, because it was illegal for a government-owned bank to own a U.S. insurer. Policyholders blamed Garamendi for putting them in this position.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Kristof |first1=KATHY M. |title=Executive Life Buyout Rejected by Garamendi |url=http://articles.latimes.com/1991-11-07/news/mn-1397_1_executive-life |website=Los Angeles Times |accessdate=July 21, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Hiltzik |first1=Michael |title=Exec Life Saga Still Shadows Garamendi |url=http://www.latimes.com/la-me-election13oct13-story.html |website=Los Angeles Times |accessdate=July 21, 2018}}</ref>\n\nEventually the U.S. government reached a settlement with the French bankers, and in 2005 Garamendi too agreed to a settlement, the terms of which further angered Executive Life policyholders, who, according to the ''[[Sacramento Bee]]'', \"had been damaged to the tune of perhaps $5 billion.\" A leader of the policyholders' interest group, Sue Watson, said, \"We are shocked and outraged that the largest financial fraud in California history would be settled for so little and without even a fight.\" ''The Bee'' editorialized that \"Garamendi cannot simultaneously assert that the sale was a good deal and a multibillion-dollar fraud, and then defend a cents-on-the-dollar settlement that left the buyers with billions of dollars in windfall profits. It just does not make sense.\"<ref>{{cite web |last1=Walters |first1=Dan |title=Investigate a shameful insurance deal |url=http://legacy.sandiegouniontribune.com/uniontrib/20050221/news_lz1e21walters.html |website=San Diego Tribune |publisher=The Sacramento Bee |accessdate=July 21, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=McMenamin |first1=Brigid |title=Executive Life |url=https://www.forbes.com/global/1999/1227/0226024a.html#3746acc25039 |website=Forbes |accessdate=July 21, 2018}}</ref>\n\n===1994 gubernatorial election===\n{{See also|California gubernatorial election, 1994}}\nGaramendi ran for California governor a second time in 1994. In the Democratic primary, [[California State Treasurer|State Treasurer]] [[Kathleen Brown]] defeated him 48%-33%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=113619 |title=CA Governor - D Primary Race - Jun 07, 1994 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=January 14, 2016}}</ref><ref>[http://www.lk.cs.ucla.edu/LK/Presentations/facultylecture/calelection/returns.html]   {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20050419204848/http://www.lk.cs.ucla.edu/LK/Presentations/facultylecture/calelection/returns.html |date=April 19, 2005 }}</ref>\n\n==1995\u20132003==\nAfter his tenure as insurance commissioner, [[President of the United States|President]] [[Bill Clinton]] appointed Garamendi to serve as [[United States Deputy Secretary of the Interior|Deputy Secretary of the Interior]], the second-highest post in the [[United States Department of the Interior|U.S. Department of the Interior]]. He resigned in 1998 and led groups of [[Peace Corps]] volunteers that helped negotiate peace in the [[Eritrean\u2013Ethiopian War]] and [[Second Congo War|Congo Civil War]] from 1998 to 2000.<ref name=autogenerated1>{{cite web |url=http://garamendi.house.gov/about/biography.shtml |title=Biography, Representative John Garamendi |accessdate=March 11, 2012 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120211091350/http://garamendi.house.gov/about/biography.shtml|archivedate=February 11, 2012}}</ref> He then spent time in the private sector.\n\n==Insurance commissioner (2003\u20132007)==\n\n===2002 election===\n{{See also|California Insurance Commissioner election, 2002}}\nHe ran for California insurance commissioner for a second time in 2002. He won the Democratic primary with 39% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=375996 |title=CA Insurance Commissioner - D Primary Race - Mar 05, 2002 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=January 14, 2016}}</ref> In the general election, he defeated Republican Gary Mendoza 46%-42%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=1611 |title=CA Insurance Commissioner Race - Nov 05, 2002 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=January 14, 2016}}</ref>\n\n===Tenure===\nHe created a ''Home Owners Bill of Rights.'' This helped create laws that reduced workers compensation costs 58% and reduced premiums by over $500 million. He re-established an anti-fraud task force. He also developed a report analyzing California\u2019s health care system with 43 recommended improvements.<ref name=\"garamendi.house.gov\"/>\n\n===2003 gubernatorial election===\n{{See also|California gubernatorial election, 2003}}\nOn August 7, 2003 Garamendi announced his candidacy for governor a third time in the [[2003 California recall|gubernatorial recall election]], but dropped out two days later to endorse [[Lieutenant Governor of California|Lieutenant Governor]] [[Cruz Bustamante]].\n\n==Lieutenant governor (2007\u20132009)==\n\n===2006 election===\n{{See also|California lieutenant gubernatorial election, 2006}}\nOn July 16, 2004, Garamendi announced his candidacy to run for [[Lieutenant Governor of California]], the first and only time he would run for this position. Incumbent Cruz Bustamante was term-limited and decided to run to replace Garamendi as insurance commissioner. Garamendi's endorsers included former [[Vice President of the United States|Vice President]] [[Al Gore]], [[Sierra Club]], the [[California Teachers Association]], the [[California League of Conservation Voters]], the Peace Officers Research Association of California, the California Professional Firefighters Association, former U.S. Senate Majority Leader [[Tom Daschle]], and former U.S. Interior Secretary [[Bruce Babbitt]]. Garamendi won the Democratic primary by defeating state senators [[Jackie Speier]] and [[Liz Figueroa]] 43%-40%-18%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=112293 |title=CA Lieutenant Governor - D Primary Race - Jun 06, 2006 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=January 14, 2016}}</ref> In the general election, he defeated Republican State Senator [[Tom McClintock]] 49%-45%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=112362 |title=CA Lieutenant Governor Race - Nov 07, 2006 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=January 14, 2016}}</ref>\n\n===Tenure===\nAs [[Lieutenant Governor of California]], Garamendi was the ''de jure'' President of the State Senate, regent of the [[University California]], Trustee of the [[California State University System]], member of the [[California State Lands Commission]], Chairman of the [[California Commission for Economic Development]], and acting governor.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.garamendi.org/bio |title=Meet John and Patti &#124; Garamendi for Congress |website=Garamendi.org |date= |accessdate=January 14, 2016}}</ref>\n\n==U.S. House of Representatives (2009\u2013present)==\n\n===Elections===\n'''2009'''\n{{Main|California's 10th congressional district special election, 2009}}\nDespite living outside of [[California's 10th congressional district]], Garamendi announced his intention to run for the 2009 special election in the 10th district, where Democratic U.S. Congresswoman [[Ellen Tauscher]] had vacated her seat. There was some confusion about the location of Garamendi's residence. Garamendi said: \"My front yard is in the district, our bedroom is not.\"<ref name=\"Changes course\">{{cite news|last=Marinucci|first=Carla|title=Garamendi changes course for Tauscher's seat|url=http://www.sfgate.com/cgi-bin/article.cgi?f=/c/a/2009/04/22/BAAF1776NO.DTL|accessdate=June 7, 2012|newspaper=SF Gate|date=April 23, 2009}}</ref>  Garamendi continued to fuel speculation about the location of his residence when he made similar comments to ''[[The New York Times]]'' in July, stating the same thing. Garamendi ranked first among Democrats with 26% of the vote, defeating State Senator [[Mark DeSaulnier]] (18%) and Assemblymember [[Joan Buchanan]] (12%). In the November run-off special election, Garamendi defeated Republican nominee David Harmer 53%-43%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=557824 |title=CA - District 10 - District 10 Race - Nov 02, 2010 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=January 14, 2016}}</ref>\n\n'''2012'''\n{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in California, 2012#District 3}}\nAfter redistricting, Garamendi filed papers in March 2012 to run in the newly redrawn [[California's 3rd congressional district]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thereporter.com/rss/ci_20086052?source=rss |title=Solano County's Congressman John Garamendi files election papers |website=Thereporter.com |date= |accessdate=January 14, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://woodlandrecord.com/garamendi-is-a-candidate-for-new-third-congressional-district-p2454-1.htm |title=Garamendi is a candidate for new third congressional district |website=Woodlandrecord.com |date=July 15, 2009 |accessdate=January 14, 2016}}</ref>\n\nThe old 10th Congressional District represented by Garamendi made up just 23% of the new 3rd district. While the old 10th Congressional District traditionally favored Democrats, the new 3rd District is now considered swing district by some.<ref name=Redistricting>{{cite web|title=California Redistricting: The Democrats' Proposal|url=http://www.swingstateproject.com/diary/8409/california-redistricting-the-democrats-proposal|publisher=Swing State Project|accessdate=June 7, 2012}}</ref> Garamendi defeated Republican Kim Vann 54.2%-45.8%.\n\n'''2014'''\n{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in California, 2014#District 3}}\nGaramendi was re-elected.\n\n===Political issues===\n====Trump====\nGaramendi has heavily criticized and mocked President Trump, suggesting in December 2016, prior to Trump's inauguration, that because of his international real-estate business Trump is weighted down with conflicts of interest, stating that Trump was \"not even bothering to take his daily intelligence briefings,\" maintaining that there was \"no doubt\" that Russia played a role in Trump's victory, and saying that he was \"very, very concerned\" about a potential waiver allowing General [[James Mattis]] to become [[Secretary of Defense]], and, more broadly, disturbed by the placement of \"generals, ex-generals in every one of the key positions dealing with the military, dealing with international affairs.\"<ref>{{cite web |last1=Blitzer |first1=Wolf |title=The Situation Room - Interview With California Congressman John Garamendi |url=http://transcripts.cnn.com/TRANSCRIPTS/1612/02/sitroom.02.html |website=CNN |accessdate=July 15, 2018}}</ref>\n\nAt a July 2017 town hall in [[Davis, California]], Garamendi said that current developments in the Trump administration were \"far more serious\" than [[Watergate]]. \"This is developing far more rapidly than any of us expected,\" he said. \"Is there a cover-up? An [[obstruction of justice]]? Well, we'll see.
