signature.jpg\n}}\n'''Walter Kurt Schrader''' (born October 19, 1951) is an [[United States|American]] politician who has been the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|OR|5}} since 2009. He is a member of the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]] and previously served in both houses of the [[Oregon Legislative Assembly]].\n\n==Early life, education, and early career==\nSchrader was born in Connecticut and received a Bachelor of Arts degree from [[Cornell University]] in 1973. While at Cornell, Schrader met [[Martha Schrader|Martha Northam]] and the two were married in 1975.<ref name=kohler>{{cite news|title=Vet makes a house call|last=Kohler|first=Vince|date=May 1, 1997|work=[[The Oregonian]]}}</ref> Schrader earned his [[Doctor of Veterinary Medicine]] degree from the [[University of Illinois]] in 1977. A year later, the Schraders moved to Oregon, and Kurt opened the Clackamas County Veterinary Clinic in [[Oregon City, Oregon|Oregon City]], to begin his veterinary practice.<ref name=kohler/>\n\nSchrader served for 16 years on the Canby Planning Commission.{{citation needed|date=April 2015}}\n\n==Oregon Legislature==\n\n===Elections===\nAfter winning his first election in 1996, Schrader served four terms in the [[Oregon House of Representatives]]. Schrader ran for the [[Oregon House of Representatives]] in 1994, where he lost to Republican [[Jerry Grisham]] in the general election by 38 votes.<ref name=hunsberger>{{cite news|title=More absentees vote but alter few races|last=Hunsberger|first=Brent|work=[[The Oregonian]]|date=November 11, 1996}}</ref> In 1996, Schrader ran again; and this time defeated Paul Kraxburger.<ref name=kohler2>{{cite news|title=Grisham wins by 38 votes|last=Kohler|first=Vince|work=[[The Oregonian]]|date=November 15, 1994}}</ref> He was subsequently reelected to the House in 1998 and 2000.\n\nIn 2002, Schrader ran for the [[Oregon State Senate]] seat vacated by the retiring [[Verne Duncan]], representing the 20th district in southwestern [[Clackamas County, Oregon|Clackamas County]], including the cities of [[Barlow, Oregon|Barlow]], [[Canby, Oregon|Canby]], [[Gladstone, Oregon|Gladstone]], [[Johnson City, Oregon|Johnson City]], [[Oregon City, Oregon|Oregon City]] and portions of [[Milwaukie, Oregon|Milwaukie]]. He defeated fellow Oregon House member Kathy Lowe in a contentious Democratic primary, and then faced no Republican opposition in the general election.<ref name=mayes>{{cite news|title=Schraders ahead in legislative races|work=[[The Oregonian]]|last=Mayes|first=Steve|date=May 22, 2002}}</ref> [[Martha Schrader]] was the Democratic nominee to succeed her husband, but lost in the general election to [[Wayne Scott]].<ref name=mayes/> She served as a Clackamas County commissioner until 2009, when she was appointed by the same commission (with Martha recusing herself from voting) to replace her husband in the State Senate.<ref name=schrader2>{{cite news|url=http://www.oregonlive.com/clackamascounty/index.ssf/2009/01/martha_schrader_will_replace_h.html|publisher=[[The Oregonian]]|date=January 9, 2009|accessdate=January 10, 2009|title=Martha Schrader will replace husband in State Senate}}</ref>\n\n===Committee assignments===\nIn the Oregon Senate, Schrader served as co-chair of the Joint the election with 54 percent of the vote to Erickson's 38 percent.  Schrader won all seven of the counties in the 5th congressional district, though he posted a plurality win in Polk County.\n\n[[File:Kurt Schrader, Official Portrait, 111th Congress.jpg|thumb|left|150px|Official portrait, 2010]]\n;2010\n\nSchrader was challenged by Republican nominee and [[Oregon House of Representatives|Oregon State Representative]] [[Scott Bruun]] and Pacific Green nominee Chris Lugo. Despite several polls showing Bruun ahead and pollster [[Nate Silver]] of [[FiveThirtyEight]] predicting Schrader would likely lose his bid for reelection, the final vote tally had Schrader winning by a fairly comfortable five-point margin, picking up 51% of the vote to Bruun's 46%. It was the closest House race in Oregon in 2010, a year in which Republicans picked up at least 63 House seats, but only [[United States House of Representatives elections in Washington, 2010#Third congressional district|one]] on the [[U.S. West Coast|West Coast]].\n\n'''2012'''\n\nSchrader won re-election 54.1% to 42.6%.\n\n'''2014'''\n\nSchrader won 53.9% to 39.4%\n\n'''2016'''\n\nSchrader won 53.6% to 43.1%.
