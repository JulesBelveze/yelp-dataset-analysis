farmers from the agricultural [[Lobbying|lobby]], the [[Louisiana Farm Bureau Federation]]. He told the farmers, \"Food security is national security. Agriculture is at the forefront of the fight because any interruption in the food supply or a compromise in its safety goes right to the heart of the nation.\"<ref name=lfbf>{{cite web|url=\nhttp://www.thenewsstar.com/story/news/2017/03/17/congressman-abraham-ag-americas-thin-green-line/99264888/|title=Rep. Abraham, Louisiana's only member on the House Agriculture Committee, defined his job in part to \"educating members whose districts might be deeply metropolitan and who have no perspective on the importance of agriculture.\"<ref name=lfbf/> In 2018, Abraham was named to the conference committee for the 2018 Farm Bill. The conference committee will resolve differences in the House and the Senate version of the Farm Bill.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thenewsstar.com/story/news/2018/07/18/abraham-craft-final-farm-bill-language/797847002/|title=Abraham to craft final Farm Bill language|last=Hilburn|first=Greg|date=July 18, 2018|work=The Monroe News-Star|access-date=July 20, 2018}}</ref>\n\n==== Gun law====\n\nIn the wake of the [[2016 Orlando nightclub shooting]], Abraham offered thoughts and prayers. He stressed that the shooter had pledged allegiance to [[Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant|ISIS]] and asked for the public to prayer for law enforcement and service members who fight terrorism.<ref name=\"Berkowitz61616\">{{cite web|last1=Berkowitz|first1=Bonnie|last2=Cai|first2=Weiyi|last3=Lu|first3=Denise|last4=Gamio|first4=Lazaro|title=Everything lawmakers said (and didn't say) after the Orlando mass shooting|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/graphics/national/how-lawmakers-responded-to-the-orlando-mass-shooting/|website=Washington Post|accessdate=31 December 2017}}</ref>\n\n==== Health care ====\n\nAbraham believes the [[Affordable Care Act]] should be repealed. He opposes the expansion of [[Medicaid]].<ref name=debate>{{cite web |url=http://www.thenewsstar.com/story/news/politics/2014/11/20/mayo-abraham-face-th-district-debate/70035864/ |title=Mayo, Abraham face off in 5th District debate |author=Kaleb Causey |date=November 20, 2014 |work=The Monroe News-Star |accessdate=December 13, 2014}}</ref>\n\n===Economic issues ===\n\n==== Tax reform ====\n\nHe supports simplifying the tax code.<ref name=debate/>\n\n==== Jobs ====\n\nAbraham supports [[equal pay for women]].<ref name=debate/>\n\n=== International issues ===\n\n==== Gas &  oil ====\n\nAbraham was in favor of the [[Keystone Pipeline]].<ref name=debate/>\n\n==== Immigration ====\n\nRegarding [[illegal immigration]], Abraham opposes [[amnesty]] and supports strengthening border security.<ref name=debate/>  Abraham supported President [[Donald Trump]]'s 2017 [[Executive Order 13769|executive order]] to temporarily halt [[immigration]] from the seven specified nations until the development of more enhanced screening methods. In May 2005, he stated \"As a doctor, I know and I can attest that this bill is backed by scientific research showing that babies can indeed feel pain at 20 weeks, if not before\" .<ref name=\"Morris10417\">{{cite web|last1=Morris|first1=Meagan|title=Passed abortion ban cited 'fetal pain,' but what does science say about it?|url=https://www.metro.us/news/the-big-stories/abortion-ban-can-fetuses-feel-pain|website=Metro US|accessdate=31 December 2017|language=en|date=4 October 2017}}</ref>\n\n==== Cannabis ====\n\nAbraham has a \"D\" rating from [[NORML]] for his voting history regarding [[cannabis]]-related causes. Abraham opposes veterans having access to medical marijuana if recommended by their [[Veterans Health Administration]] doctor and if it is legal for medicinal purposes in their state of residence.<ref name=\"NORML\">{{cite web|title=Louisiana Scorecard|url=http://norml.org/congressional-scorecard/louisiana|website=NORML|accessdate=31 December 2017|language=en-us}}</ref>\n\n==== LGBT ====\n\nAbraham supports efforts to ban [[transgender]] people from serving in the  military \"100 percent.\"<ref name=\"Gamard8917\" />\n\n==Election of 2016==\nIn his bid for reelection in 2016, Abraham defeated one challenger, fellow Republican Billy Burkette<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.thenewsstar.com/story/news/local/2016/11/08/abraham-reelected-5th-district-seat/93447078/|title=Abraham reelected in 5th district seat|last=Mott|first=Ashley|date=November 9, 2016|website=Monroe News Star|publisher=|access-date=November 13, 2016}}</ref> of [[Baton Rouge]], a former [[constable]] in [[East Feliciana Parish]] and a former chairman of the Louisiana Band of [[Choctaw]] Indians.
