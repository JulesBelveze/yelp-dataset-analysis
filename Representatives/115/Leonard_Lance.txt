Lance''' (born June 25, 1952) is the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] for {{ushr|NJ|7}}, serving since 2009. He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]]. He previously served in the [[New Jersey Senate]] and the [[New Jersey General Assembly]].\n\n==Early life, education, and early political career==\nLance was born in [[Easton, Pennsylvania]], into a political family. His parents were Anne M. (n\u00e9e Anderson) and [[Wesley Lance|Wesley Leonard Lance]], who was a State Senator.<ref>[http://region.princeton.edu/about_advisory_bio_54.html Hon. Leonard Lance (MPA '82)] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070210110150/http://region.princeton.edu/about_advisory_bio_54.html |date=February 10, 2007 }}, [[Princeton University]] Policy Research Institute for the Region. Accessed May 11, 2007.</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.ancestry.com/~battle/reps/lance.htm|title=leonard lance|publisher=}}</ref> His great-uncle, H. Kiefer Lance, was also active in New Jersey politics.\n\nAfter attending [[North Hunterdon High School]] in [[Annandale, New Jersey]], Lance received a [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] from [[Lehigh University]] in 1974, a [[Juris Doctor|J.D.]] from [[Vanderbilt University Law School]] in 1977 and an [[Master of Public Administration|M.P.A.]] from the [[Woodrow Wilson School of Public and International Affairs]] at [[Princeton University]] in New Jersey in 1982.<ref name=NJLEG>[http://www.njleg.state.nj.us/members/BIO.asp?Leg=71 Senator Lance's legislative web page], [[New Jersey Legislature]]. Retrieved April 16, 2008.</ref>\n\nLeonard Lance served as the law clerk to the [[Warren County, New Jersey|Warren County]] Court in 1977 and 1978. He was assistant counsel for county and municipal matters to [[Governor of New Jersey]] [[Thomas Kean]] from 1983 to 1990. He was a member of the New Jersey Council on the Humanities during the [[Christine Todd Whitman|Whitman]] Administration by appointment of the Governor.\n\n==New Jersey legislature==\nLance served in the [[New Jersey General Assembly]] for 11 years (1991\u20132002) and served in the [[New Jersey Senate]] for 7 years (2002\u20132009). In 2002 he was elected to the [[New Jersey Senate]] and held the position of [[Minority Leader]] from 2004 to 2008.<ref name=NJLEG/> While serving in the state legislature, he gained a reputation as a moderate Republican.<ref name=twsSLedger898>Tom Moran, May 22, 2011, ''The Star-Ledger'', [http://blog.nj.com/njv_tom_moran/2011/05/the_curious_transformation_of.html The curious transformation of N.J. He lost the Republican primary, ranking third with 17% in New Jersey's 23rd District.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=723038 |title=NJ General Assembly 23 \u2013 R Primary Race \u2013 Jun 02, 1987 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=October 6, 2012}}</ref> Lance was appointed to the [[New Jersey General Assembly]] in February 1991 when then-Assemblyman [[William E. After redistricting, Lance ran for the newly redrawn 23rd District in 1991, and won the Republican primary. In the general election, he ranked second with 30%, winning a seat. Incumbent Republican State Assemblyman [[Chuck Haytaian]] ranked first in the district with 33%.<ref name=\"ReferenceA\">{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=241047 |title=NJ General Assembly 23 Race \u2013 Nov 05, 1991 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=October 6, 2012}}</ref> In 1993, Lance won re-election to a second term with 40%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=149409 |title=NJ General Assembly 23 Race \u2013 Nov 02, 1993 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=October 6, 2012}}</ref> In 1995, he won re-election to a third term with 34%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=218787 |title=NJ General Assembly 23 Race \u2013 Nov 07, 1995 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=October 6, 2012}}</ref> In 1997, he won re-election to a fourth term with 30%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=719396 |title=NJ General Assembly 23 Race \u2013 Nov 04, 1997 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=October 6, 2012}}</ref> In 1999, he won re-election to a fifth term with 36%.<ref name=\"ReferenceB\">{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=36972 |title=NJ General Assembly 23 Race \u2013 Nov 04, 1999 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=October 6, 2012}}</ref>\n\nAfter redistricting, he ran for the [[New Jersey Senate]] in 2001 in the 23rd District. Cook 69%\u201331%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=3603 |title=NJ State Senate 23 Race \u2013 Nov 06, 2001 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=October 6, 2012}}</ref> In 2003, he won re-election to a second term with 68%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=20012 |title=NJ State Senate 23 Race \u2013 Nov 04, 2003 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=October 6, 2012}}</ref> In 2007, he won re-election to a third term with 67%.<ref name=\"ourcampaigns.com\">{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=338935 |title=NJ State Senate 23 Race \u2013 Nov 06, 2007 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=October 6, 2012}}</ref>\n\n== U.S. House of Representatives ==\n\n===Elections===\n;1996\nIn 1996, Leonard Lance sought the Republican nomination to replace Rep. Ferguson in 2006 and lost narrowly.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.westfieldleader.com/06nov09/06nov09.pdf |title=Incumbent Menendez Maintains Seat, Kean Loses Senate Race |publisher=Westfield Leader |date=November 6, 2005}}</ref> ''[[The Cook Political Report]]'' rated the race as a \"toss up.\"<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20160529123927/http://cookpolitical.com/archive/chart/house/race-ratings/2008-11-04_13-32-49 ]</ref> Lance was endorsed by ''[[The New York Times]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=For the House|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2008/10/25/opinion/25sat4.html|website=The New York Times}}</ref> On November 4, Lance defeated Stender by 51% to 41%.
