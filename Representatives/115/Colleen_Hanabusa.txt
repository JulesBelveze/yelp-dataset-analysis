grew up in [[Wai'anae, Hawai'i|Wai{{okina}}anae]] with her two younger brothers, her parents, and her grandparents. Her parents Isao and June owned a gas station.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180717032446/https://www.hanabusaforgovernor.com/about-colleen-hanabusa/|title=About Colleen Hanabusa}}</ref> Her maternal grandfather was confined at the [[Honouliuli Internment Camp]] on Oahu during World War II.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://bigislandnow.com/2018/02/20/rep-hanabusas-statement-on-the-76th-anniversary-of-the-forced-relocation-of-japanese-americans/ |title=Rep. She received a B.A. in economics and sociology in 1973 and an M.A. in sociology in 1975 from the [[University of Hawai'i]] and in 1977 received a J.D. from the University of Hawai'i's [[William S. Richardson School of Law]].<ref name=\"HW Rees\">{{citation | last = Rees | first = Robert M. | title = Queen of the Senate | newspaper=[[Honolulu Weekly]] | date = June 12, 2002 | url=http://www.honoluluweekly.com/archives/coverstory%20%202002/06-12-02%20Hanabusa/06-12-02%20Hanabusa.html | archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110927085027/http://www.honoluluweekly.com/archives/coverstory%20%202002/06-12-02%20Hanabusa/06-12-02%20Hanabusa.html | archive-date=September 27, 2011}}</ref>\n\n==Law career==\nHanabusa is a labor lawyer with almost 30 years of experience, and a corporate officer in a family-run corporation. She has been recognized in The Best Lawyers in America, Woodward and White, Inc., served as a delegate to the Hawai`i State Judicial Conference, and was noted in ''[[Honolulu Magazine]]'' as one of Hawai`i's A+ Attorneys in 1993 and subsequent years.\n\n==Hawaii Senate==\nIn November 1998, Hanabusa was elected as the [[Hawaii Senate|state senator]] from the 21st District. The 21st District includes [[Wai'anae, Hawaii|Wai'anae]], where her family has resided for four generations, as well as [[Ko Olina Resort|Ko Olina]], [[Kahe Point]], [[Nanakuli, Hawaii|Nanakuli]], [[M\u0101'ili, Hawai'i|Ma'ili]], [[Makaha, Hawaii|Makaha]], [[Makua, Hawaii|Makua]] and [[Kaena Point|Ka'ena Point]].\n\nOne of Hanabusa's first acts upon being elected was to organize senators to vote against the second-term confirmation of [[Attorney General of Hawaii|Hawaii Attorney General]] [[Margery Bronster]].<ref name=\"HW Rees\" />\n\nHanabusa served as Senate Majority Leader before being elected the first woman President of the Senate in 2006&nbsp;\u2013 making her the first Asian American woman to preside over a state legislative chamber in the United States.<ref name=\"emilyslist\"/> In 2003 she was named one of Hawaii\u2019s \u201ctop ten political power brokers\u201d, along with the state\u2019s governor and two U.S. senators, by ''[[Hawaii Business Magazine]].''<ref name=businessmagazine>{{citation | title = Hawaii\u2019s Powerbrokers (List) | newspaper=Honolulu Business Magazine | date = October 2003 | url=http://www.hawaiibusiness.com/Hawaii-Business/October-2003/Hawaiias-Powerbrokers-List/|access-date=May 14, 2010}}</ref>\n\nHanabusa ran unsuccessfully in a special election held in January 2003 to replace the late [[Patsy T. Mink]] as U.S. Representative from [[Hawaii's 2nd congressional district|Hawai'i's 2nd congressional district]], losing to [[Ed Case]], a [[Blue Dog Coalition|Blue Dog]] Democrat.<ref>{{citation| last=Gima| first=Craig| title=Victorious Case sees end of old-style politics| date=January 6, 2003| newspaper=Honolulu Star-Bulletin| url=http://archives.starbulletin.com/2003/01/06/news/index1.html| access-date=January 7, 2011}}</ref> In 2006 she ran for the same seat after Case retired to unsuccessfully challenge Senator [[Daniel Akaka]] in the Democratic primary. Hanabusa was again unsuccessful, losing in the Democratic primary to former [[Lieutenant Governor of Hawaii|Lieutenant Governor]] [[Mazie Hirono]] by 844 votes.<ref>{{citation| last=Reyes| first=B.J.| title=Statewide name recognition gives Hirono the advantage| date=September 25, 2006| newspaper=Honolulu Star-Bulletin| url=http://archives.starbulletin.com/2006/09/25/news/story03.html| access-date=January 7, 2011}}</ref>\n\n===Leadership positions===\n*Serving the Leeward Coast as State Senator since 1998\n*State Senate President since 2007\n*State Senate Majority Leader since 2007\n*Chair, Judiciary and Hawaiian Affairs Committee\n*Co-chair, Joint Senate House Task Force on Ice and Drug Abatement\n*Senate's first statewide hearings on Rice v. When Governor [[Ben Cayetano]] vetoed the tax credit bill, Hanabusa took the unprecedented step of suing to overturn the veto.<ref name=KoOlina>{{citation| last=Pang | first=Gordon Y.K. | title = Is 2nd time charm for U.S. House candidate? |newspaper=[[Honolulu Advertiser]] | date=August 29, 2006 | url=http://the.honoluluadvertiser.com/article/2006/Aug/29/ln/FP608290346.html/|access-date=May 14, 2010}}</ref><ref>{{citation| title=Senator sees no conflict in many ties to Ko Olina | first=Jim | last=Dooley | newspaper=Honolulu Advertiser | date=March 4, 2004 | url=http://the.honoluluadvertiser.com/article/2004/Mar/04/ln/ln03a.html | access-date=June 28, 2010}}</ref>\n\nWithin months, Hanabusa's then-fianc\u00e9 John Souza received a preferential deal in purchasing one of Stone's homes in Ko Olina. Souza and Hanabusa, who were engaged at the time and married in 2008, then bought a $1-million home in another Ko Olina subdivision developed by Centex Homes of Texas.<ref>{{citation| last=Dooley | first=Jim | title=Exclusive Report: Close Ties Between Congressional Candidate Colleen Hanabusa and Ko Olina Developer Rake in Funds | newspaper=Hawaii Reporter | date=October 28, 2010 | url=http://www.hawaiireporter.com/exclusive-report-close-ties-between-congressional-candidate-colleen-hanabusa-and-koolina-developer-rake-in-funds | access-date=February 7, 2011}}</ref>\n\nWhen State Rep.
