He is a member to the [[United States House of Representatives]] for {{ushr|wa|4}} and was sworn in on January 3, 2015.\n\n==Career==\nNewhouse graduated from [[Washington State University]]  with a bachelor of science degree in [[agricultural economics]] in 1977.<ref name=wsu>{{cite web|url=http://wsm.wsu.edu/s/index.php?id=961#.UT4xbVf77hM|title=Dan Newhouse \u201977 \u2013 Farm to director's office|author=Washington State Magazine|accessdate=November 5, 2014}}</ref> During his time at WSU Dan was a member of [[Alpha Gamma Rho]] fraternity.<ref>{{Cite journal|date=2017-05-09|title=Alpha Gamma Rho|url=https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Alpha_Gamma_Rho&oldid=779449375|journal=Wikipedia|language=en}}</ref>  He also is a graduate of the Washington Agriculture and Forestry Leadership Program. He served four terms in the [[Washington House of Representatives]] from 2003 through 2009, representing the 15th Legislative District. In 2009, [[Christine Gregoire]], the then-Democratic [[Governor of Washington]], appointed the Republican Newhouse to head the state's agriculture department.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://producenews.com/9-news-section/story-cat/3524-3321|title=Dan Newhouse tapped to head Washington state Agriculture Department|work=The Produce News \u2013 Covering fresh produce around the globe since 1897.|accessdate=November 5, 2014}}</ref>\n\nIn February 2014, Newhouse entered the Republican primary for {{ushr|WA|4}}.  The incumbent, fellow 10-term Republican [[Doc Hastings]], did not run in the [[United States House of Representatives elections in Washington, 2014|2014 elections]].<ref name=yakima>{{cite web|url=http://www.yakimaherald.com/community/lowervalley/sunnysidecontent/1944182-8/newhouse-declares-for-hastings-seat-in-congress|archive-url=https://archive.is/20140221202416/http://www.yakimaherald.com/community/lowervalley/sunnysidecontent/1944182-8/newhouse-declares-for-hastings-seat-in-congress|dead-url=yes|archive-date=February 21, 2014|title=Former state ag director Newhouse officially joins 4th District race|publisher=yakimaherald.com|accessdate=February 22, 2014}}</ref> He and fellow Republican [[Clint Didier]] advanced to the November election after finishing in the top two in the August [[primary election]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blog.seattlepi.com/seattlepolitics/2014/08/05/clint-didier-dan-newhouse-all-republican-contest-for-congress|title=Clint Didier, Dan Newhouse: All-Republican contest for Congress|work=Strange Bedfellows \u2013 Politics News|accessdate=November 5, 2014}}</ref> marking the first time that two Republican candidates would square off in a general election since the state adopted the \"top two\" primary system. The race on November 4 was very close, and was seen as a battle between the factions of the GOP; Newhouse is considered a mainstream Republican, while Didier openly identified with the [[Tea Party movement]]. On November 4, 2014, Newhouse defeated Didier, by a margin of 51%-49%.<ref>[http://www.khq.com/story/27330702/newhouse-beats-fellow-goper-didier-in-4th Newhouse beats Didier in 4th congressional district], khq.com; accessed November 30, 2014.</ref><ref>[http://blog.seattlepi.com/seattlepolitics/2014/11/07/tea-party-turned-back-newhouse-likely-states-new-congressman \"Tea Party turned back\"], blog.seattlepi.com/seattlepolitics; accessed November 30, 2014.</ref>\n\nNewhouse faced Didier in a rematch [[United States House of Representatives elections in Washington, 2016|in 2016]], placing first in the blanket primary with 44,720 votes (45.77%) to Didier's 26,892\t(27.53%).
