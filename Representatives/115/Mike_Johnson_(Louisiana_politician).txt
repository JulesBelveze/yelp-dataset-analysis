He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]]. From 2015 to January 2017, Johnson served as a representative in the [[Louisiana House of Representatives]] for district 8 in [[Bossier Parish, Louisiana|Bossier Parish]]. During his tenure he proposed the Louisiana Marriage and Conscience Act; the bill was ultimately tabled.\n\n==Early life and education==\nJohnson was born in [[Shreveport, Louisiana|Shreveport]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.legaldirectories.com/Johnson-J-Michael-838210-Atty.aspx|title=J. Michael Johnson - Lawyer in Bossier City, Louisiana (LA) Bossier County - legaldirectories.com|website=www.legaldirectories.com}}</ref> the oldest of four children of Jeanne Johnson and the late James Patrick Johnson, a [[firefighter]] who founded the [[nonprofit organization]], the Percy R. Johnson Burn Foundation, after having been critically burned and disabled in the line of duty. Johnson has two brothers, Chris and Josh, and a sister, Laura.<ref name=\"fathersobit\">{{cite web|url= http://www.legacy.com/obituaries/shreveporttimes/obituary.aspx?pid=183039473#sthash.Bgl9ucQ8.dpuf|title=James Patrick Johnson|publisher=''[[The Times (Shreveport)|The Shreveport Times]]''|accessdate=December 13, 2016}}</ref>\n\nJohnson graduated from [[Captain Shreve High School]] in Shreveport. He received an undergraduate degree in [[Business Administration]] from [[Louisiana State University]] in [[Baton Rouge, Louisiana|Baton Rouge]], where he was a member of the [[Order of Omega]] honor society and [[Kappa Sigma]] [[fraternity]]. He holds a [[Juris Doctor]] from [[Louisiana State University Law Center]],<ref name=\"bio\">{{cite web|url=http://www.mikejohnsonlouisiana.com/#!portfolio/cee5|title=About Mike |publisher=mikejohnsonlouisiana.com|accessdate=March 30, 2015}}</ref> and worked as [[Lawyer|constitutional attorney]] in [[Benton, Louisiana|Benton]], in  [[Bossier Parish]] seat of government located north of [[Bossier City]], [[Louisiana]].\n\nJohnson is a member of the [[Council for National Policy]], the [[National Rifle Association]], the [[American Bar Association]], and the [[Louisiana Family Forum]].<ref name=\"bio\" />\n\n==Commentator==\nJohnson has served as a talk [[radio]] host and conservative columnist. He has appeared on ''[[The O'Reilly Factor]]'', ''[[Fox and Friends]]'', ''[[Good Morning America]]'', ''[[The Today Show]]'', and [[National Public Radio]].<ref name=bio/>\n\n==Legal career==\n Prior to his election to Congress, Johnson was also a partner in the [[Graydon K. Kitchens Jr.|Kitchens Law Firm]] and a senior attorney and national media spokesman for the [[Alliance Defense Fund]], now known as [[Alliance Defending Freedom]], which describes itself as \"a [[non-profit organization|non-profit]] legal defense and advocacy organization dedicated to religious liberty, traditional family values, and the value and sanctity of life.\"<ref name=mjohnson/>  Johnson was also formerly chief counsel of the non-profit law firm Freedom Guard.<ref name=stimes>{{cite web|url=http://www.shreveporttimes.com/story/news/local/2015/01/13/looming-session-leave-little-wiggle-room-johnson/21695793/|title=Looming session leaves little wiggle room for Johnson|author=Alexandria Burris|date=January 16, 2015|publisher=''The Shreveport Times''|accessdate=March 30, 2015}}</ref>\n\nJohnson served as co-counsel for the state [[defendant]]s in the lawsuit challenging the constitutionality of the Louisiana state ban on same-sex marriage in both 2004 and again in 2015.  He was driving home from a hearing on the matter before the [[United States Court of Appeals for the Fifth Circuit]] in [[New Orleans]] when he learned from a radio broadcast that he would run unopposed in his bid to succeed Thompson.<ref name=mjohnson>{{cite web|url=http://www.arklatexhomepage.com/story/d/story/conservative-republican-walks-into-louisiana-legis/29275/stOTzOyAiU6l-uIul9MOdw|title=Conservative Republican walks into Louisiana Legislature's District 8 seat unopposed|date=January 10, 2015|author=Nancy Cook|publisher=[[KTAL-TV]] ([[NBC]]): arklatexhomepage.com|accessdate=March 30, 2015}}</ref>\n\nJohnson served as a trustee of the [[Ethics & Religious Liberty Commission|Ethics and Religious Liberty Commission]] within the [[Southern Baptist Convention]] from 2004-2012.\n\n==Louisiana House of Representatives==\nJohnson ran unopposed in the [[special election]] called for February 21, 2015 to select a successor to [[Jeff R. Thompson]], a Republican who had resigned to become a [[judge]] in Louisiana's 26th Judicial District Court.
