He is a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]].\n\n==Early life, education, and career==\nScott's father, Jim, is an orthopedic surgeon and his mother, Becky, is a teacher in the public school system. Scott graduated from the [[University of Georgia]] with a B.B.A. in risk management and insurance. He passed the Series 7 Exam.<ref>{{cite web|title=Austin Scott \u2013 Biography|url=http://www.austinscott.house.gov/full-biography/}}</ref>\n\nScott is president of the Southern Group, LLC and a partner in Lockett Station Group, LLC.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.legis.state.ga.us/legis/2009_10/house/bios/scottAustin/scottAustinBio.htm|title=Representative Austin Scott|publisher=Legis.state.ga.us|date=|accessdate=2010-08-22}}</ref>\n\n==Georgia legislature==\nScott was first elected to the [[Georgia House of Representatives]] at the age of 26. He was the chairman of the Governmental Affairs Committee. He also served on the Appropriations, Rules, and Ways and Means Committee, where he was the chairman of the Public Policy Subcommittee. The District he represented consists of [[Tift County, Georgia|Tift]] and [[Turner County, Georgia|Turner]] counties.\n\n==U.S. House of Representatives==\n\n===Elections===\n====2010====\n[[File:Austin fire.jpg|thumb|left|300px|Austin Scott meets with members of the Carrollton, GA fire department on July 20, 2009 during the Walk of Georgia.]]\n\nScott challenged Democratic incumbent [[Jim Marshall (Georgia politician)|Jim Marshall]] in Georgia's eighth congressional district. He defeated Marshall in the general election on November 2, 2010, with 53% of the vote to Marshall's 47%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.politico.com/2010/maps/#/House/2010/GA|title=2010 Election results|publisher=Politico|date=2010-11-05|accessdate=2011-08-09}}</ref>\n\nScott originally planned to campaign for Governor of Georgia, announcing his campaign in January 2009. freshman portrait]]\nThe Georgia General Assembly redrew the 8th district after the [[2010 United States Census|2010 census]]. Notably, most of [[Macon, Georgia|Macon]] and surrounding [[Bibb County, Georgia|Bibb County]] (except for a sliver in the north) were drawn into the neighboring [[Georgia's 2nd congressional district|2nd district]]. Macon had been the heart of the 8th and its predecessors for more than a century. To make up for the loss of population, the General Assembly pushed the 8th all the way to the [[Florida]] border, adding [[Thomasville, Georgia|Thomasville]] and most of [[Valdosta, Georgia|Valdosta]] from the old 2nd. The old 8th already had a significant Republican lean, with a [[Cook Partisan Voting Index]] of R+10. However, the new 8th has a CPVI of R+15, making it the 11th most Republican district in the [[Eastern Time Zone]] and one of the most Republican districts in the country.\n\nScott was unopposed in both the primary and general elections.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://results.enr.clarityelections.com/GA/42277/112424/en/summary.html|title=GA \u2013 Election Results|accessdate=November 17, 2012}}</ref>\n\n====2014====\nScott was unopposed for a third term.\n\n====2016====\nIn 2016, Scott faced a Democratic opponent for the first time since his initial run for the seat when private investigator James Neal Harris declared for the race.
