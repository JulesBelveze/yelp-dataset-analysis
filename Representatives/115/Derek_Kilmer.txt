A member of the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]], Kilmer previously served as a member of the [[Washington House of Representatives]] from 2005 to 2007 and as a member of the [[Washington State Senate]] from 2007 to 2013, representing the 26th district.\n\nOn March 5, 2012, Kilmer announced he was running to succeed [[Norm Dicks]] for the seat representing Washington's 6th congressional district.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://publicola.com/2012/03/05/state-sen-kilmer-running-for-us-rep-dicks-open-seat |title=State Sen. Kilmer Running for US Rep. Dicks\u2019 Open Seat}}</ref> On November 6, 2012, Kilmer won the [[general election]] to become the district's next representative.<ref>{{cite web|last=Schrader|first=Jordan|author2=Shannon, Brad|title=Democrats Derek Kilmer, Denny Heck win Congressional races|url=http://www.theolympian.com/2012/11/07/2311365/democrats-derek-kilmer-denny-heck.html|work=theolympian.com|publisher=The Olympian|accessdate=November 8, 2012|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://archive.is/20130204105332/http://www.theolympian.com/2012/11/07/2311365/democrats-derek-kilmer-denny-heck.html|archivedate=February 4, 2013|df=mdy-all}}</ref> He received 186,661 votes, to opponent Bill Driscoll's 129,725.<ref>{{cite web|last=Reed|first=Sam|title=6th Congrressional District Results|url=http://vote.wa.gov/results/current/CongressionalDistrict6.html|work=2012 election results|publisher=WA STATE SEC OF STATE}}</ref>\n\n==Early life, education, and business career==\nKilmer was born and raised in [[Port Angeles]], Washington. Both of his parents were public school teachers. Kilmer earned a BA in public affairs with a certificate in American Studies from [[Princeton University]]'s [[Woodrow Wilson School of Public and International Affairs]]. He won a [[Marshall Scholarship]] to obtain his PhD in [[Social Policy|Comparative Social Policy]] from the [[Department of Social Policy and Intervention, University of Oxford]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://votesmart.org/candidate/biography/51516/derek-kilmer#.UaFUI5xTDKc|title=Project Vote Smart - The Voter's Self Defense System|work=Project Vote Smart|accessdate=October 19, 2014}}</ref>\n\nKilmer is a former business consultant for [[McKinsey and Company]]. He was also a business retention manager for the Economic Development Board for [[Tacoma, Washington|Tacoma]]-[[Pierce County, Washington|Pierce County]]. He is also a trustee for [[Tacoma Community College]] and served on the board of Peninsula Schools Education Foundation.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.princeton.edu/paw/archive_old/PAW95-96/08_9596/0124note.html |title=Princeton Alumni Weekly&nbsp;\u2014 January 24, 1996 |publisher=Princeton University |date=1996-01-24 |accessdate=2012-03-05}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://kilmer.house.gov/about/full-biography|title=Full Biography|publisher=|accessdate=October 19, 2014}}</ref>\n\n==Washington legislature==\n\n===Elections===\nIn 2004, Kilmer challenged incumbent Republican State Representative Lois McMahan of Washington's 26th house district, seat 2. He defeated her 50%-48%, a difference of 1,009 votes.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=96953|title=Our Campaigns - WA District 26 Seat 2 Race - Nov 02, 2004|publisher=|accessdate=October 19, 2014}}</ref>\n\nIn 2006, Republican State Senator Bob Oke decided to retire. Kilmer decided to run for Washington's 26th senate district. He defeated Republican [[Jim Hines (Washington politician)|Jim Hines]] 60%-40%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=206006|title=Our Campaigns - WA State Senate District 26 Race - Nov 07, 2006|publisher=|accessdate=October 19, 2014}}</ref> In 2010, he won re-election to a second term, defeating Marty McClendon 59%-41%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=687542|title=Our Campaigns - WA State Senate District 26 Race - Nov 02, 2010|publisher=|accessdate=October 19, 2014}}</ref>\n\n===Tenure===\nIn 2007, he was one of just three Democratic state senators to vote against the bill that would allow labor unions to spend non-members' bargaining fees on political causes without first getting their permission.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.seattlepi.com/local/article/Unions-get-OK-for-political-spending-1234307.php#ixzz2ULze6Vd2|title=Unions get OK for political spending|work=seattlepi.com|accessdate=October 19, 2014}}</ref>\n\nHe sponsored the senate bill that would increase fines to pay for a new $849 million Tacoma Narrows bridge in his district.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://komonews.com/news/local/senate-approves-fine-for-drivers-who-skip-bridge-toll|title=Senate approves fine for drivers who skip bridge toll|work=KOMO News|date=25 February 2007|accessdate=21 July 2016}}</ref>\n\nBusiness groups have praised Kilmer for being one of the most pro-business Democrats in Olympia. He is the three-time recipient of the \"LEADER Award\" from the Washington Economic Development Association. He was named the \"Legislative Business Star Award\" from Enterprise Washington's Business Institute. He was named \"Legislator of the Year\" by the Department of Veterans Affairs. He was recognized by the Northwest Chapter of the [[Paralyzed Veterans of America]] as \"Legislator of the Year\". He was also named \"Legislator of the Year\" by the Washington Council of Police & Sheriffs. He was named an \"Honorary Fire Chief\" by the Washington Fire Chiefs.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.derekkilmer.com/about-derek/ |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2013-05-26 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130522183625/http://www.derekkilmer.com/about-derek/ |archivedate=May 22, 2013 |df=mdy-all }}</ref>\n\n===Committee assignments (State of Washington)===\n;Senate\n*Capital Budget Committee (Chair)\n*Economic Development, Trade, & Innovation Committee\n*Higher Education & Workforce Development Committee\n*Ways and Means Committee (Vice Chair)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ballotpedia.org/wiki/index.php/Derek_Kilmer|title=Derek Kilmer|publisher=|accessdate=October 19, 2014}}</ref>\n\n==U.S. House of Representatives==\n\n===2012 election===\n{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Washington, 2012#District 6}}\nAfter redistricting, Democratic U.S. Congressman [[Norm Dicks]] decided to retire. Kilmer decided to run for the newly redrawn [[Washington's 6th congressional district]]. He won the endorsements from ''[[The Seattle Times]]'', which called him \"a problem solver who can be bipartisan.\" [[News Tribune|The News Tribune of Tacoma]] praised him for having \"an uncommon understanding of trade, business taxation, smart regulation, job creation and other fundamentals of economic growth.\" Port Angeles, Rep. Kilmer's hometown and an area for which he was elected to represent, suffers from an unemployment rate 2.3 percent higher than the Washington State average, consonant with the rate of increase recorded before he took office.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.konp.com/local/8450|title=KONP / Local News / Unemployment rate up again in Clallam County|publisher=|accessdate=October 19, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.homefacts.com/unemployment/Washington/Clallam-County/Port-Angeles.html|title=Port Angeles, WA Unemployment - Homefacts|publisher=|accessdate=October 19, 2014}}</ref>  In the November general election, he defeated Republican nominee Bill Driscoll 59%\u201341%. He won all six counties in the district.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=773794|title=Our Campaigns - WA District 6 Race - Nov 06, 2012|publisher=|accessdate=October 19, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nationaljournal.com/almanac/member/3533#biography|title=Login|publisher=|accessdate=October 19, 2014}}</ref>\n\n===Israel policy===\n\nDerek Kilmer was a cosponsor of the [[United States\u2013Israel Strategic Partnership Act of 2013]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thomas.loc.gov/cgi-bin/bdquery/z?d113:HR00938:@@@D&summ2=m&|title=Bill Summary & Status  -  113th Congress (2013 - 2014)  - H.R.938 - CRS Summary - THOMAS (Library of Congress)|publisher=|accessdate=October 19, 2014}}</ref>\n\nKilmer was part of a 37-member congressional delegation that visited Israel. The trip was sponsored by the lobby group, American Israel Education Foundation, with the stated goal of working to strengthen strategic economic and military cooperation between Israel and the United States.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://jewishsound.org/janis-siegel-jtnews-correspondent-38/|title=In the Middle East, a congressman learns that nothing\u2019s simple|publisher=|accessdate=October 19, 2014}}</ref>\n\n===Co-sponsorship of the United States\u2013Israel Partnership Act of 2013===\nThrough his co-sponsorship of the United States\u2013Israel Strategic Partnership Act of 2013,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thomas.loc.gov/cgi-bin/bdquery/z?d113:HR00938:@@@D&summ2=m&|title=Bill Summary & Status  -  113th Congress (2013 - 2014)  - H.R.938 - CRS Summary - THOMAS (Library of Congress)|publisher=|accessdate=October 19, 2014}}</ref> Rep. Kilmer supports spending U.S. tax revenue to fund Israel's military, and to provide assistance for collaboration in the fields of energy, water, homeland security, agriculture, and alternative fuel technologies.\n\n===Legislation===\nOn October 29, 2013, Kilmer introduced the [[American Savings Promotion Act]] (H.R. 3374; 113th Congress), a bill that would authorize some financial institutions to conduct a contest, known as a \"savings promotion [[raffle]],\" in which the sole consideration required for a chance of winning designated prizes is obtained by the deposit of a specified amount of money in a savings account or program, where each ticket or entry has an equal chance of being drawn.<ref name=3374sum>{{cite web|title=H.R. 3374 -
