A member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]], he was a [[South Carolina House of Representatives|South Carolina House Representative]] from 2005 to 2007 and from 2009 until 2017.<ref>[http://www.scstatehouse.net/members/bios/1421590739.html South Carolia House] {{webarchive|url=https://archive.is/20041227042152/http://www.scstatehouse.net/members/bios/1421590739.html |date=2004-12-27 }}, Ralph Norman biography.</ref> \n\nIn February 2017, Norman vacated his seat to launch a [[South Carolina's 5th congressional district special election, 2017|U.S. congressional bid]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Marchant|first=Bristow |url=http://www.thestate.com/news/politics-government/article133163614.html |title=SC legislator resigns seat to run in special 5th District congressional race |publisher=The State |date=2017-02-16 |accessdate=2017-05-19}}</ref> Norman won the [[South Carolina's 5th congressional district special election, 2017|2017 5th congressional district special election]], assuming office in the U.S. House of Representatives in June 2017.\n\n==Early life and career==\nHe was born in [[York County, South Carolina]], and currently resides in [[Rock Hill, South Carolina|Rock Hill]], where he is a real estate developer at the Warren Norman Company, a business founded by and named after Norman's father. He and his wife, Elaine have 4 children and 16 grandchildren.{{citation needed|date=February 2017}}\n\n==South Carolina House of Representatives==\nIn 2004, Norman was elected to serve District 48 in the South Carolina House of Representatives, winning a three-way Republican primary outright with 52% of the vote.
