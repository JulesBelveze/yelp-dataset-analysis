She was also the first [[United States Republican Party|Republican]] woman elected to the House from Florida, and is currently the [[List of current United States Representatives by seniority|most [http://www.miamiherald.com/news/local/community/miami-dade/article147718764.html Ileana Ros-Lehtinen to Retire from Congress], ''[[Miami Herald]]'', April 30, 2017.</ref>\n\n==Early life, education, and early career==\nIleana Ros y Adato was born in [[Havana, Cuba]], one of two children born to [[Enrique Ros]] (1924\u20132013), who later became a businessman and [[opposition to Fidel Castro|anti-Fidel Castro]] activist, and his wife, Amanda Adato. The family immigrated to the [[United States]] when Ileana was seven years old. She received her [[Bachelor of Arts]] in [[education]] and her [[Master of Arts]] in educational leadership from [[Florida International University]]. She attended the [[University of Miami]] where she earned an [[Doctor of Education|Ed.D]] in [[higher education]].<ref name=\"vote-smart.org\">{{cite web|url=http://www.vote-smart.org/bio.php?can_id=26815|title=Project Vote Smart \u2013 Representative Ileana Ros-Lehtinen|publisher=Vote-smart.org|date=1952-07-15|accessdate=2010-08-23|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20071224181639/http://www.vote-smart.org/bio.php?can_id=26815|archivedate=2007-12-24|df=}}</ref>\n\nRos-Lehtinen was raised Catholic and is now an [[Episcopal Church (United States)|Episcopalian]].<ref name=\"vote-smart.org\"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://miamiherald.typepad.com/gaysouthflorida/2010/11/gay-rights-advocate-us-rep-ileana-ros-lehtinen-to-head-house-foreign-affairs-committee.html|title=Gay-rights advocate U.S. Rep. Ileana Ros-Lehtinen to head House Foreign Affairs Committee|publisher=|accessdate=19 November 2014}}</ref> Ros-Lehtinen's maternal grandparents were [[Sephardi Jews|Sephardic]] [[Jew]]s, originally from the [[Ottoman Empire]], who had been active in [[History of the Jews in Cuba|Cuba's Jewish community]].<ref name=ref1>{{cite web|last=Nir|first=Ori|url=http://www.forward.com/articles/2056|title=Miami Pol Closes In on Key Foreign Policy Post \u2013 The Jewish Daily Forward|publisher=Forward.com|date=2005-10-14|accessdate=2010-08-23}}</ref> Her maternal grandfather left the city of [[K\u0131rklareli]] for Cuba in 1913, fleeing the devastation and economic collapse caused by the [[First Balkan War]].<ref>[https://ros-lehtinen.house.gov/chairwoman-ros-lehtinen-meets-turkish-relatives-istanbul Chairwoman Ros-Lehtinen Meets Turkish Relatives in Istanbul] Jan 31, 2012</ref> Her mother later converted to [[Catholicism]] to marry her father.<ref name=ref1/><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.jpost.com/Features/In-Thespotlight/Ileana-Ros-Lehtinen-Ready-to-play-hardball |title=Ileana Ros-Lehtinen: Ready to play hardball |author=Hilary Leila Kreiger |publisher=''Jerusalem Post'' |date= 2010-12-23}}</ref>\n\nRos-Lehtinen was an educator and the owner/operator of a private school in [[Miami-Dade County]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2007/04/11/AR2007041100168.html|work=The Washington Post|title=Rep. Ileana Ros-Lehtinen (R-Fla.)|date=April 15, 2007|accessdate=May 5, 2010}}</ref> She was elected to the [[Florida House of Representatives]] in 1982, where she met State Representative [[Dexter Lehtinen]] (D-Miami). They married on June 9, 1984, after Dexter switched parties. They both served in the State House until 1986. That year, they were both elected to the [[Florida Senate]], where he was elected to District 40 and she was elected in District 34.<ref name=flsenate>{{cite web|url=http://www.flsenate.gov/data/Publications/Archive/SenateHandbooks/pdf/86-88%20Senate%20Handbook.pdf|title=86\u201388 Senate Handbook|format=PDF|accessdate=February 29, 2012}}</ref><ref>\"Senator called A Brainy Tough Guy\". ''The Miami Herald''. June 11, 1988, p. 1A</ref> In 1988, Dexter Lehtinen resigned his seat to become U.S. Attorney of South Florida. In 1989, Ros-Lehtinen resigned her seat to become a U.S. Representative.\n\n==Personal life==\n\nRos-Lehtinen has two children, [[Rodrigo Lehtinen|Rodrigo]], a [[transgender]] [[LGBT rights]] activist, and Patricia Marie.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2014/11/19/ileana-ros-lehtinen-transgender-son_n_6185310.html|title=Ileana Ros-Lehtinen Opens Up About Her Son, The First Trans Child Of A Member Of Congress|work=The Huffington Post|accessdate=19 November 2014}}</ref> She is also step-mother to Katherine and Douglas Lehtinen.\n\n==U.S. House of Representatives==\n[[File:Florida-representatives-take-oath-miami-federal-courthouse.jpg|thumb|Ros-Lehtinen (right) being sworn in as a member of Congress by Chief Judge [[Kevin Michael Moore]] (left), along with Florida colleagues [[Carlos Curbelo (politician)|Carlos Curbelo]], [[Frederica Wilson]] and [[Mario D\u00edaz-Balart]]. (February 2015)]]\n\n===Tenure===\nAfter incumbent Democrat U.S. Congressman [[Claude Pepper]] died on May 30, 1989, there was a [[Florida's 18th congressional district special election, 1989|special election scheduled for August 29, 1989]]. State Senator Ros-Lehtinen defeated Democrat Gerald Richman 53%\u201347%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=29161|title=FL District 18 \u2013 Special Election Race|publisher=Our Campaigns|date=August 29, 1989|accessdate=February 29, 2012}}</ref> She was the first [[Cuban American]] elected to the [[United States Congress]] and the first Republican woman elected from Florida. Ros-Lehtinen was unaware that she was also the first [[Hispanic and Latino Americans|Latina]] elected to Congress until after she was elected.<ref name=HRCInterview /> In 1990, she won re-election to a full term with 60% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=28795|title=FL District 18 Race|publisher=Our Campaigns|date=November 6, 1990|accessdate=February 29, 2012}}</ref> In total, she has been elected to fourteen full terms (1990, 1992, 1994, 1996, 1998, 2000, 2002, 2004, 2006, 2008, 2010, 2012, 2014, and what will turn out to have been the election for her final full term, 2016, scheduled to be serving through January 3, 2019), never winning with less than 58%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/CandidateDetail.html?CandidateID=1061|title=Candidate: Ileana Ros-Lehtinen|publisher=Our Campaigns|accessdate=February 29, 2012}}</ref>\n\nRos-Lehtinen joined Majority Leader [[Steny Hoyer]] ([[U.S. Democratic Party|D]]-[[Maryland]]) on a congressional delegation to the [[United Nations]] in order to encourage international support for an end to the [[genocide in Darfur]]. In addition, when Ros-Lehtinen returned from a trip to Darfur in April 2007 where she visited [[Sudan]]ese refugee camps, she strongly encouraged the United States and the international community to find a solution to this humanitarian crisis.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://foreignaffairs.republicans.house.gov/apps/list/press/foreignaffairs_rep/pr_070404_darfur.shtml |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2007-08-28 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20070829173121/http://foreignaffairs.republicans.house.gov/apps/list/press/foreignaffairs_rep/pr_070404_darfur.shtml |archivedate=2007-08-29 |df= }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://foreignaffairs.republicans.house.gov/apps/list/press/foreignaffairs_rep/070723undarfur.shtml |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2007-08-28 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20070829173910/http://foreignaffairs.republicans.house.gov/apps/list/press/foreignaffairs_rep/070723undarfur.shtml |archivedate=2007-08-29 |df= }}</ref>\n\nFollowing the 2008 elections, then President-elect [[Barack Obama]] rang Ros-Lehtinen to congratulate her on her re-election. She hung up on him, believing that it was a [[prank call]] from a radio station.  She did the same to Obama's chief of staff [[Rahm Emanuel]] when he rang to confirm the original call was genuine, and only accepted the call after Congressman [[Howard Berman]] managed to speak to her.<ref>[http://blogs.abcnews.com/politicalpunch/2008/12/an-actual-press.html Press Release from the Office of Rep. She reportedly invoked concerns about the legislation's cost and that funds could be used to promote abortion.<ref name=\"The Washington Post\">{{cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2010/12/22/AR2010122204815.html|date=2010-12-22|publisher=The Washington Post|work=The Cable Foreign Policy Magazine|title=Who killed the bill to prevent forced child marriages|accessdate=2010-12-23}}</ref>\n\nRos-Lehtinen was Chairwoman of the [[United States House Committee on Foreign Affairs|House Foreign Affairs Committee]] from [[112th United States Congress|2011\u20132013]]. But on March 20, 2011, the day after the [[NATO]] strikes to enforce the [[No fly zone#Libya|no-fly-zone]] began, she expressed a different view in a press release: \"I am concerned that the President has yet to clearly define for the American people what vital United States security interests he believes are currently at stake in Libya.\"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blogs.abcnews.com/thenote/2011/03/gingrich-not-alone-in-no-fly-zone-contradiction.html|title=Gingrich Not Alone In No-fly Zone Contradiction|publisher=[[ABC News]]|date=March 24, 2011|accessdate=March 28, 2011|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110326165356/http://blogs.abcnews.com/thenote/2011/03/gingrich-not-alone-in-no-fly-zone-contradiction.html|archivedate=March 26, 2011|df=}}</ref> The congresswoman has also been a forerunner in cutting U.S. aid to foreign lands, including the State Department, The Peace Corps,  the Asia Foundation, the U.S. Institute of Peace and the East-West Center. Ros-Lehtinen also advances strongly held views on Cuba, and has lobbied against ending the [[United States embargo against Cuba|United States embargo]] against that country. In 2006, she received a 100% rating from the [[National Right to Life Committee]] for her abortion-related voting record. She is against allowing federal funds and federal health coverage for abortions. She wants to stop [[embryonic stem cell]] research. She has voted to make it a crime to harm a [[fetus]] while committing a crime. She is against [[partial-birth abortions]] unless it is necessary to save the mother's life.
