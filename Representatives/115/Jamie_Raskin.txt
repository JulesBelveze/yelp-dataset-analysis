The district is anchored in [[Montgomery County, Maryland|Montgomery County]], an affluent suburban county northwest of [[Washington, D.C.]].\n\nAs a [[law professor]], he taught constitutional law and legislation at the [[American University]] and the [[Washington College of Law]]. He served as the director of the college's [[Master of Laws|LL.M.]] program on Law and Government until his election to Congress.\n\n==Early life==\nRaskin was born in Washington, D.C. on December 13, 1962<ref name=\"raskin campaign bio\">{{cite web|last=Friends of Jamin Raskin|year=2006 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20060827203242/http://www.jaminraskin.com/bio.php|archivedate=August 27, 2006 |url=http://www.jaminraskin.com/bio.php|title=Biography|publisher=Jamie Raskin for State Senate campaign|accessdate=May 4, 2006}}</ref> to a [[American Jews|Jewish]] family,<ref name=MarylandSenate /><ref>Rachel Levmore (May 2, 2012). [http://forward.com/articles/155597/should-the-government-get-involved/ \"Should the Government 'Get' Involved? - State Laws Target Men Who Refuse to Grant Religious Divorce\"], ''The Forward''</ref> the son of progressive activist [[Marcus Raskin]], a former staff aide to President [[John F. Kennedy]] on the [[United States National Security Council|National Security Council]] and co-founder of the [[Institute for Policy Studies]], and Barbara (n\u00e9e Bellman) Raskin, a journalist and novelist. He graduated from [[Georgetown Day School]] in 1979, received his B.A. degree from [[Harvard College]] (''[[magna cum laude]]'') in 1983, and received his [[Juris Doctor|J.D.]] degree from [[Harvard Law School]] (''magna cum laude'') in 1987. He is a past editor of the ''[[Harvard Law Review]]''.<ref>[https://archive.org/details/100HarvLRev99s Editorial Board listing] in \"Front Matter.\" ''Harvard Law Review'', vol. 100, no. 1, 1986, p. 99.</ref>\n\n==Maryland legislature==\nIn November 2006, he was elected as a [[Maryland Senate|Maryland State Senator]] for District 20,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.elections.state.md.us/elections/2006/results/general/office_State_Senator.html|title=Official 2006 Gubernatorial General Election results for State Senator|publisher=Maryland State Board of Elections|year=2006|accessdate=October 20, 2010}}</ref> representing parts of [[Silver Spring, Maryland|Silver Spring]] and [[Takoma Park]] in Montgomery County. In 2012 he was named the [[whip (politics)|Majority Whip]] for the Senate, and was the [[Montgomery County, Maryland|Chairman of the Montgomery County Senate Delegation]], Chairman of the Select Committee on Ethics Reform, and a Member of the Judicial Proceedings Committee.<ref name=MarylandSenate>{{cite web|title=Jamin B. (Jamie) Raskin|work=Maryland Manual Online|publisher=Maryland State Archives|url=http://msa.maryland.gov/msa/mdmanual/05sen/html/msa14610.html|accessdate=January 17, 2017}}</ref>\n\nRaskin was a strong proponent of [[Modern liberalism in the United States|liberal]] issues in the Maryland Senate. Vice President Joseph Biden ruled the objection out of order because it had to be sponsored by at least one member of each chamber, and there was no Senate sponsor.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/powerpost/wp/2017/01/06/trump-victory-becomes-official-as-congress-tallies-electoral-college/|title='It is over': Biden quiets Democrats as Congress meets to make Trump victory official|publisher=washingtonpost.com|accessdate=July 1, 2017}}</ref>\n\nIn late June 2017 Raskin was the chief sponsor of legislation to establish a congressional \"oversight\" commission with the authority to declare a President \"incapacitated\" and removed from office under the [[25th Amendment to the United States Constitution]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.yahoo.com/news/bill-create-panel-remove-trump-office-quietly-picks-democratic-support-124521145.html|title=Bill to create panel that could remove Trump from office quietly picks up Democratic support|date=June 30, 2017|accessdate=July 1, 2017|work=[[Yahoo! News]]|first=Michael|last=Isikoff}}</ref>\n\nIn April 2018, Raskin, together with [[Jared Huffman]], [[Jerry McNerney]], and [[Dan Kildee]], launched the [[Congressional Freethought Caucus]]. Its stated goals include \"pushing public policy formed on the basis of reason, science, and moral values\", promoting the \"separation of church and state,\" opposing discrimination against \"atheists, agnostics, humanists, seekers, religious and nonreligious persons\", among others. Huffman and Raskin will act as co-chairs.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Manchester|first1=Julia|title=Dem lawmakers launch 'Freethought' congressional
