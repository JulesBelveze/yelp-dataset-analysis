A former [[United States Marine Corps|Marine Corps]] [[Military Officer|officer]], he is a member of the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]].\n\nAfter graduating from [[Harvard University]] in 2001 with a [[Bachelor of Science]] in [[physics]], Moulton joined the [[United States Marine Corps]]. He served four tours in [[Iraq War|Iraq]] and then went on to earn his [[master's degree]]s in [[Master of Business Administration|business]] and [[Master of Public Policy|public policy]] in a [[Double degree|dual program]] at Harvard. He entered politics in 2014, running for Massachusetts's 6th congressional district. He defeated incumbent U.S. Representative [[John F. Tierney]] in the Democratic primary and then defeated former Republican State Senator [[Richard Tisei]] in the general election.\n\n==Early life, education, and commission==\nSeth Wilbur Moulton was born on October 24, 1978, in [[Salem, Massachusetts]], the son of Lynn Alice (n\u00e9e Meader), a secretary, and Wilbur Thomas Moulton, Jr., a real estate attorney.<ref name=madepeace/><ref>{{cite web|title=0695. Lynn Alice Meade|url=http://www.themeaderfamily.org/test/showPage.php?ID=0695 |publisher=Meader Family Organization|accessdate=November 6, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|last1=Gomes|first1=Peter J.|title=The Good Life: Truths That Last in Times of Need|date=2003|publisher=HarperOne|page=365|url=http://librarum.org/book/24442/365|accessdate=November 6, 2014|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141106043435/http://librarum.org/book/24442/365|archivedate=November 6, 2014|df=mdy-all}}</ref> Moulton grew up in [[Marblehead, Massachusetts]], as the oldest of three siblings.<ref name=madepeace>{{cite news |url=http://www.bostonmagazine.com/2008/01/how-the-moultons-made-peace-with-the-war|title=How the Moultons Made Peace with the War|work=[[Boston Magazine]]|author=Phyllis Karas |date=February 2008}}</ref> He graduated from [[Phillips Academy]], a co-educational boarding and day [[university-preparatory school]] in [[Andover, Massachusetts|Andover]], [[Massachusetts]] in 1997,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.andover.edu/calendar/pages/moultontalk2014.aspx|work=[[Phillips Academy]]|title=Seth Moulton '97 talks about service on April 22}}</ref> and attended [[Harvard University]], where he earned his [[Bachelor of Arts]] in [[physics]] in 2001. He gave the Undergraduate English Oration at his [[graduation|commencement]], focusing on the importance of service.<ref name=warprofiles>{{cite news|url=http://www.thecrimson.com/article/2003/6/5/war-profiles-seth-w-moulton-01|title=War Profiles: Seth W. Moulton '01, 2nd Lieutenant, U.S. Marine Corps|work=[[Harvard Crimson]]|date=June 5, 2003}}</ref>\n\nMoulton joined the Marine Corps after graduation, a few months before the [[September 11 attacks]],<ref name=disillusioned>{{cite news |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2015/02/09/us/politics/disillusioned-in-iraq-but-prodded-to-serve-again.html|title=Disillusioned in Iraq, but Prodded to Serve Again|work=[[New York Times]] |author=Jeremy W. Peters|date=February 8, 2015}}</ref> and attended the [[Officer Candidate School]] in [[Quantico, Virginia]]. After graduating in 2002 with the rank of [[second lieutenant]], Moulton was among the first service members to enter [[Baghdad]] at the beginning of the [[Iraq War]].<ref name=madepeace/><ref name=warprofiles/>\n\n==Military career==\nDuring the [[2003 invasion of Iraq]], Moulton led one of the first infantry platoons to enter [[Baghdad]]. He served a total of four tours of duty in [[Iraq]] from 2003 to 2008.<ref name=madepeace/> Moulton took part in the 2003 [[Battle of Nasiriyah]], leading a platoon that cleared a hostile stronghold. In that action, he went to the aid of a Marine wounded by friendly fire, and for his actions he was awarded the [[Commendation Medal|Navy and Marine Corps Commendation Medal]] for valor.<ref name=underplays>{{cite news|url=https://www.bostonglobe.com/metro/2014/10/17/moulton-underplays-military-service/lY9FfmOrviwL2LAFHr61dO/story.html|title=Seth Moulton underplays military service|first=Walter V. |last=Robinson |date=October 18, 2014|work=[[The Boston Globe]]}}</ref> Moulton was active in combat against insurgent forces in Iraq, including the 2004 [[Battle of Najaf (2004)|Battle of Najaf]] against the militia of [[Muqtada al-Sadr]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.pbs.org/now/shows/316/No-End-in-Sight.html|work=[[PBS]]|date=April 20, 2007|title=Director's Interview: Charles Ferguson}}</ref> Over two days, he \"fearlessly exposed himself to enemy fire\" as his platoon was pinned down under heavy fire and then directed the supporting fire that repelled the attack. He received the [[Bronze Star Medal]] for his actions in this battle.<ref name=underplays/>\n\nHe told only his campaign manager, a former Marine, about these awards, keeping them secret even from his parents. When ''[[Boston Globe]]'' reporter [[Walter V. Robinson]] disclosed in October 2014 that Moulton had earned the Bronze Star and the Commendation Medal, Moulton said that \"[t]here is a healthy disrespect among veterans who served on the front lines for people who walk around telling war stories\". He said he was uncomfortable calling attention to his own awards out of respect to \"many others who did heroic things and received no awards at all.\" He asked Robinson not to refer to him as a hero: \"Look, we served our country, and we served the guys next to us. And it's not something to brag about.\" The ''Globe'' reported that \"his voice choked with emotion\" as he added: \"The greatest honor of my life was to lead these men in my platoon, even though it was a war that I and they disagreed with.\"<ref name=underplays/>\n\nIn 2008, General [[David Petraeus]] requested that Moulton be assigned to work as a special liaison with tribal leaders in Southern Iraq during his fourth tour of duty in Iraq.
