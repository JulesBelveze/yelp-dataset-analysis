M\u00e8ng Zh\u0101ow\u00e9n\n|showflag = p\n}}\n'''Grace Meng''' {{IPAc-en|\u02c8|m|\u025b|\u014b}} ({{zh|c=\u5b5f\u662d\u6587}}, born October 1, 1975) is an American [[lawyer]] and a [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] member of the [[United States House of Representatives]], representing [[New York's 6th congressional district]] in the [[New York City]] borough of [[Queens]], which includes neighborhoods like [[Bayside, Queens|Bayside]], [[Flushing, Queens|Flushing]], [[Forest Hills, Queens|Forest Hills]], [[Rego Park, Queens|Rego Park]], [[Fresh Meadows, Queens|Fresh Meadows]], [[Glendale, Queens|Glendale]], [[Kew Gardens, Queens|Kew Gardens]], and [[Maspeth, Queens|Maspeth]]. Previously, she served as a member of the [[New York State Assembly]], representing the 22nd assembly district in Flushing, Queens. She is the first [[Asian American]] to represent part of New York in Congress, and also current Vice Chair of the [[Democratic National Committee]].\n\n==Early life and education==\nGrace Meng was born on October 1, 1975 in Queens, where she was also raised.<ref name=\"Capitol Info\"/> She is of [[Taiwanese American|Taiwanese]] descent, and is the daughter of [[Jimmy Meng]], an Assemblyman, and Shiao-Mei Meng.<ref name=kye>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2005/06/12/fashion/weddings/grace-meng-and-wayne-kye.html|title=Grace Meng and Wayne Kye|website=The New York Times|date=June 12, 2005|accessdate=November 1, 2016}}</ref> She attended [[Nathaniel Hawthorne Middle School 74]] and is a graduate of [[Stuyvesant High School]]. She received a [[B.A.]] degree from the [[University of Michigan]] and a [[Juris Doctor]] from the [[Benjamin N. Cardozo School of Law]] at [[Yeshiva University]].<ref name=\"Official bio\" />\n\n==New York Assembly==\n\n===Elections===\nGrace Meng's father Jimmy Meng was elected in 2004 to New York's 22nd assembly district, becoming the first Asian American to be elected to the legislature in [[New York State]] history.<ref>[http://www.chinadaily.com.cn/english/doc/2004-11/05/content_389005.htm \"First Asian American in the NY State Assembly\"], ChinaDaily, 05-11-2004. Retrieved on 16-02-2007</ref> Jimmy served only one term, having decided to not run for re-election in 2006 following a scandal regarding election irregularities in his first campaign. He subsequently pleaded guilty to bribery charges. He only served one month in jail for his crime. Grace Meng decided to run to succeed her father, but was taken off the ballot when Democrat [[Ellen Young (politician)|Ellen Young]], challenged her residency status.<ref>{{cite web|last=Stirling |first=Stephen |url=http://www.timesledger.com/stories/2008/30/flushing_times_newsyoungmengw07242008.html |title=Young, Meng won't fight over primary signatures \u2022 TimesLedger |publisher=Timesledger.com |date=2008-07-25 |accessdate=2014-08-13}}</ref> Subsequently her district residency issues were resolved.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nationaljournal.com/thenextamerica/politics/new-york-6th-house-district-grace-meng-d-20121106 |title=New York, 6th House District: Grace Meng (D) |publisher=NationalJournal.com |date=2012-11-06 |accessdate=2014-08-13 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150110064820/http://www.nationaljournal.com/thenextamerica/politics/new-york-6th-house-district-grace-meng-d-20121106 |archivedate=2015-01-10 |df= }}</ref> Ellen Young succeeded Jimmy Meng in 2006.\n\nIn 2008, Grace decided to challenge Young again. On September 9, 2008, Grace defeated the incumbent in the Democratic primary 59%-41%.<ref>Noah C. Zuss, \"Meng Beats Young in Primary for Flushing Seat.\" \"Southeast Queens Press,\" Sept. 12-18, 2008, p. 11</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=463724 |title=Ourcampaigns.com |publisher=Ourcampaigns.com |date= |accessdate=2014-08-13}}</ref> She went on to win the 2008 November election, defeating Young again, this time as an [[Independence Party of New York|Independence Party]] nominee, 88%-12%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=469000 |title=Ourcampaigns.com |publisher=Ourcampaigns.com |date= |accessdate=2014-08-13}}</ref> In 2010, she won re-election to a second term unopposed.<ref name=\"elect2008\" /><ref name=\"elect2010\" /><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=692593 |title=Ourcampaigns.com |publisher=Ourcampaigns.com |date= |accessdate=2014-08-13}}</ref>\n\nIn 2008, she was named one of City & State's \"New York City Rising Stars: 40 Under 40\" for being a young influential member in New York City politics.\n\n===Tenure===\nShe was the author of the [[Reverse Mortgage]] Act of 2009, that prohibited proceeds received from reverse mortgages from being considered as income, so senior citizens can get their partial property tax exemption. Seven other of her pieces of legislation were signed into law.<ref>[http://www.gracemeng.org/biography.html ] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121107183712/http://www.gracemeng.org/biography.html |date=November 7, 2012 }}</ref>\nIn 2017, Meng boycotted Trump's inauguration in protest to his inflammatory rhetoric.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dnainfo.com/new-york/20170118/forest-hills/grace-meng-boycott-presidential-inauguration-donald-trump-womens-march-on-washington|title=Grace Meng Is Latest Elected Official to Boycott Trump Inauguration|website=DNAinfo New York|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20170126142558/https://www.dnainfo.com/new-york/20170118/forest-hills/grace-meng-boycott-presidential-inauguration-donald-trump-womens-march-on-washington|archivedate=2017-01-26|df=}}</ref>\n\n===Committee assignments===\n*House Appropriations Committee<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ballotpedia.org/wiki/index.php/Grace_Meng |title=Grace Meng |publisher=Ballotpedia |date= |accessdate=2014-08-13}}</ref>\n\n==U.S. House of Representatives==\n\n===2012 election===\nIn June 2012, Meng faced fellow Assembly member [[Rory Lancman]] and [[New York City Council]] member [[Elizabeth Crowley]] in a primary election for New York's 6th congressional district and won. She received the endorsement of the Queens County Democratic party.<ref name=\"elect2012\" /> On November 6, 2012, Meng won the race for New York Congressional District 6 against Republican member of the New York City Council [[Dan Halloran]], making her the first Asian American elected to Congress from New York.<ref>{{cite web|title=Grace Meng, Michael Grimm Win Seats In Congress |url=http://www.ny1.com/content/top_stories/171920/grace-meng--michael-grimm-win-seats-in-congress |archive-url=https://archive.is/20130130061541/http://www.ny1.com/content/top_stories/171920/grace-meng--michael-grimm-win-seats-in-congress |dead-url=yes |archive-date=30 January 2013 |accessdate=7 November 2012 }}</ref>\n\n===Tenure===\nShe was inaugurated on January 3, 2013. Meng has co-formed the Bipartisan Freshman Caucus saying \"The American people are just sick and tired of blaming each other without getting anything done.\"<ref>{{cite web|last=Schneier |first=David |url=http://www.qchron.com/editions/central/meng-talks-nation-s-business-at-th/article_182c8a3d-3940-5bd2-89c3-c3e62aab0078.html |title=Meng talks nation's business at 112th - Queens Chronicle: Central/Mid Queens News |publisher=Qchron.com |date=2013-02-28 |accessdate=2014-08-13}}</ref>\n\nHer district includes the Queens neighborhoods of [[Auburndale, Queens|Auburndale]], Bayside, [[Briarwood, Queens|Briarwood]], [[Elmhurst, Queens|Elmhurst]], Flushing, Forest Hills, Fresh Meadows, Glendale, Kew Gardens, [[Kew Gardens Hills, Queens|Kew Gardens Hills]], Maspeth, [[Middle Village, Queens|Middle Village]], and Rego Park.\n\nOn February 10, 2014, Meng introduced the bill [[To amend the International Religious Freedom Act of 1998 to include the desecration of cemeteries among the many forms of violations of the right to religious freedom (H.R. 4028; 113th Congress)]] into the House.<ref name=4028sum>{{cite web|title=H.R. 4028 - Summary|url=http://beta.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/4028|publisher=United States Congress|accessdate=28 May 2014}}</ref> The bill would amend the findings of the [[International Religious Freedom Act of 1998]] by including the [[desecration]] of [[cemetery|cemeteries]] among the various violations of the right to [[Freedom of religion|religious freedom]].<ref name=\"4028sum\"/><ref name=NextWeekHill>{{cite news|last=Marcos|first=Cristina|title=Next week: Appropriations, VA reform, intelligence authorization|url=http://thehill.com/blogs/floor-action/house/207115-next-week-appropriations-va-reform-intelligence-authorization|accessdate=29 May 2014|newspaper=The Hill|date=23 May 2014}}</ref> Meng said that \"this legislation would be a new and important tool in our fight against the desecration of cemeteries\" because it would \"combat religiously-motivated vandalism of cemeteries and also prevent developers from building over cemeteries, a new and emerging threat in places where there are no Jewish communities left to protect burial grounds.\"<ref name=JPupdates29may>{{cite news|title=House Passes Meng Legislation to Make Desecration of Cemeteries a Violation of Religious Freedom|url=http://jpupdates.com/2014/05/29/house-passes-meng-legislation-make-desecration-cemeteries-violation-religious-freedom/|accessdate=29 May 2014|newspaper=Jewish Political News and Updates|date=29 May 2014|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140529190648/http://jpupdates.com/2014/05/29/house-passes-meng-legislation-make-desecration-cemeteries-violation-religious-freedom/|archivedate=29 May 2014|df=}}</ref>\n\nRepresentative Meng became Vice Chair of the Democratic National Committee (DNC) in February 2017. Members of the Democratic Party elected her as DNC Vice Chair in Atlanta.<ref>\nhttps://observer.com/2017/02/nyc-congressman-and-assemblyman-score-dnc-vice-chairmanships/amp/</ref>\n\n=== Committee assignments ===\n*'''[[United States House Committee on Foreign Affairs|Committee on Foreign Affairs]]'''\n**[[United States House Foreign Affairs Subcommittee on the Middle East and North Africa|Subcommittee on the Middle East and North Africa]]\n*'''[[United States House Committee on Small Business|Committee on Small Business]]'''\n**[[United States House Small Business Subcommittee on Contracting and Workforce|Subcommittee on Contracting and Workforce]] (Ranking Member)\n\n=== Caucus memberships ===\n*'''[[Congressional Asian Pacific American Caucus]]'''<ref>{{cite web|title=Members|author=|url=https://capac-chu.house.gov/members|format=|publisher=Congressional Asian Pacific American Caucus|date=|accessdate=17 May 2018}}</ref>\n\n==Personal life==\nMeng married Wayne Kye, a dentist of Korean descent, in June 2005.<ref name=kye/> The couple resides in Queens with their two sons, Tyler and Brandon.<ref name=\"Official bio\"/>\n\nIn November 2013, Meng was robbed and assaulted by a purse-snatcher in the [[Eastern Market, Washington, D.C.|Eastern Market]] area of Washington, D.C.<ref name=robbery>{{cite news|last1=O'Keefe|first1=Ed|last2=Williams|first2=Clarence|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/post-politics/wp/2013/11/20/rep-grace-meng-attacked-robbed-reports-say/|title=Rep.
