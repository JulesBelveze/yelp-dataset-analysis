A member of the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]], he previously served in the [[California State Assembly]] from 1998 to 2004, representing the 54th Assembly district, and then as a member of the [[California State Senate]], representing the 27th district from 2004 to 2012. In both posts, he represented the city of [[Long Beach, California|Long Beach]] and its surrounding suburbs.\n\n==Early life, education, and academic career==\nLowenthal was born and raised in [[Queens, New York|Queens]], [[New York City]]. His family is Jewish. He graduated with a B.A. from [[Hobart and William Smith Colleges|Hobart College]] and earned a Ph.D. from [[Ohio State University]]. In 1969, Lowenthal moved to [[Long Beach, California|Long Beach]] and became a professor of [[community psychology]] at [[California State University, Long Beach]]. He went on leave to become a Long Beach City Councilman in 1992. He remained on leave for several years until retiring in 1998.<ref>http://lowenthal.house.gov/biography/biography.htm#.UifDnT-8G2k</ref>\n\n==California Assembly==\n\n===Elections===\nIn 1998, Lowenthal decided to run for the [[California's 54th State Assembly district|54th district]] of the [[California State Assembly]]. He defeated Republican nominee Julie Alban 50%-47%.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=280672</ref> In 2000, he won re-election to a second term defeating Republican nominee [[Rudy Svorinich]], a [[Los Angeles City Council]] member, 59%-39%.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=209624</ref> In 2002, he won re-election to a third term defeating Republican nominee Cesar Castellanos, 60%-40%.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=5705</ref>\n\n===Tenure===\nIn the Assembly, Lowenthal authored [[Gun control in the United States|gun control in California]] that would ban gun selling in people's homes.<ref>http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=LB&p_theme=lb&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=0EAE8C3DC10F421B&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM</ref> He was also a founding member of the Assembly's Bipartisan Caucus and authored legislation for the creation of California\u2019s first ever [[California Citizens Redistricting Commission|Bipartisan Citizens Redistricting Commission]].<ref>http://lowenthal.house.gov/biography/biography.htm#.UifDnT-8G2k</ref>\n\nSenator Lowenthal was named Legislator of the Year by the California Firefighters Association in 2002 after he passed a law that limited liability for organizations that donated firefighting equipment. The [[League of California Cities]] named him Legislator of the Year in 2001 due to his advocacy on behalf of local governments. He has also received the \"Rivie\" Award from the Friends of the Los Angeles River due to his efforts to help clean up the troubled river.\n\n==California Senate==\n\n===Elections===\nIn 2004, he decided to run for the [[California Senate]] in the [[California's 27th State Senate district|27th Senate district]]. He won the Democratic primary unopposed. In the general election, he defeated Republican nominee Cesar Navarro Castellanos 63%-37%.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=71765</ref> In 2008, he won re-election to a second term, defeating Allen Wood 67%-33%.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=331944</ref>\n\n===Tenure===\nThe 47th District includes the [[Los Angeles County, California|Los Angeles County]] communities of [[Avalon, California|Avalon]], [[Long Beach, California|Long Beach]], Signal Hill, [[Lakewood, California|Lakewood]], [[Cerritos, California|Cerritos]], [[Artesia, California|Artesia]], [[Bellflower, California|Bellflower]], [[Downey, California|Downey]], [[South Gate, California|South Gate]], [[Lynwood, California|Lynwood]], [[Paramount, California|Paramount]], [[Hawaiian Gardens, California|Hawaiian Gardens]], Florence-Graham and [[Willowbrook, California|Willowbrook]].\n\nOther legislation that Senator Lowenthal has had signed into law include a law to reduce diesel emissions at the ports by limiting idling time for trucks conducting transactions at the ports, a bill that established a grant program to provide financial incentives for purchasing or leasing electric vehicles, and a bill to protect highways.\n\nWith Bonnie Lowenthal's election to the Assembly and Alan Lowenthal's reelection in 2008, Senator and Assemblywoman Lowenthal were the first divorced husband and wife to serve concurrently in the [[California State Legislature]].\n\nLowenthal provided significant commentary throughout the documentary ''[[Who Killed the Electric
