A [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] and supporter of [[Donald Trump]],<ref name=twsCNN494/><ref name=twsNBC1/><ref name=twsPolitico44/> he served as the [[United States Ambassador to the Holy See]] from 2005 until 2008. He is the former [[Chief executive officer|CEO]] of Rooney Holdings (formerly known as Rooney Brothers Company), an investment and holding company based in [[Naples, Florida]].\n\n==Early life and education==\n\nHe is a graduate of the [[Georgetown Preparatory School]], [[Georgetown University]] (A.B. 1975) and [[Georgetown University Law Center]] (J.D. 1978). Rooney is the oldest of Laurence Francis and Lucy Turner Rooney's six children. His younger siblings are Patrick T. Rooney, Timothy P. Rooney, Lucy Rooney Kapples, James H. Rooney, and Rebecca Rooney. He is not related to fellow Republican representative [[Tom Rooney (politician)|Tom Rooney]].\n\nRooney holds honorary degrees from the [[University of Notre Dame]] (2006)<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://news.nd.edu/news/trustees-to-confer-honorary-degrees-during-meetings-in-rome/|title=Trustees to confer honorary degrees during meetings in Rome|last=Dame|first=Marketing Communications: Web // University of Notre|website=Notre Dame News|language=en-US|access-date=2018-08-11}}</ref> and the [[University of Dallas]] (2010).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://francisrooney.house.gov/biography/|title=Biography {{!}} U.S. Representative Francis Rooney|website=francisrooney.house.gov|language=en|access-date=2018-08-11}}</ref>\n\n==Business career==\nFrancis Rooney III, served as the Chief Executive Officer and President at Rooney Holdings, Inc. since 1984.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/research/stocks/private/person.asp?personId=592982&privcapId=4245231|title=L. Francis Rooney III, J.D.|last=|first=|date=|website=Bloomberg|archive-url=|archive-date=|dead-url=|access-date=2018-08-14}}</ref> Rooney is the majority owner of [[Manhattan Construction Company]]; he is the fourth generation of his family to own that company. Manhattan Construction built the new [[Cowboys Stadium]] in Texas, the U.S. [[Capitol Visitors Center]] at the [[United States Capitol]], the [[Oklahoma State Capitol]], the [[George Bush Presidential Library]], the [[Cato Institute]] headquarters, [[New Orleans Sports Arena]], and [[Reliant Stadium]]. The company is working on the [[George W. Bush Presidential Library]] earning it the distinction to be the only construction company to work on two presidential libraries. They have also done extensive projects for [[Morehouse College]], [[George Washington University]], [[Oklahoma State University]], and the [[University of Georgia]]. The subsidiary won an estimated $100 million in [[The Pentagon|Pentagon]] contracts in 2003, nearly four times the amount the company won in 2002, according to the [[Center for Public Integrity]].\n\n=== Activities in Central America ===\nRooney is a member of the Advisory Board of the [[Panama Canal Authority]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.pancanal.com/eng/pr/press-releases/2008/10/21/pr299.html|title=Panama Canal Board of Directors meets with Advisory Board|last=|first=|date=|website=www.pancanal.com|language=en|archive-url=|archive-date=|dead-url=|access-date=2018-08-11}}</ref> He was among the U.S. delegation led by [[Colin Powell]] to the inauguration of [[List of Presidents of Panama|Panamanian president]] [[Mart\u00edn Torrijos]].\n\n== Political activities ==\nRooney is a major Republican donor, having contributed to the campaign of [[George W. Bush]]. Soon afterward, Rooney entered the Republican primary to succeed him\u2014the real contest in this heavily Republican district. Rooney spent $4 million of his own money and far outspent his opponents in an election that was shortened due to the timing of Clawson's late announcement.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fec.gov/fecviewer/CandidateCommitteeDetail.do|title=Campaign finance data - FEC.gov|website=FEC.gov|accessdate=December 27, 2017}}</ref> In the August 30 primary, he defeated Sanibel Councilman Chauncey Goss and radio personality [[Dan Bongino]].
