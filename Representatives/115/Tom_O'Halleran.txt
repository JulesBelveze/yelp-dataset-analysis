He began his political career as a Republican, but is now a [[United States Democratic Party|Democrat]].\n\n==Early career==\nO'Halleran served with the [[Chicago Police Department]] from 1966 to 1979.  He then became a member of the [[Chicago Board of Trade]], operating his own futures trading business.<ref name=bio>{{Cite web|url=http://www.tomohalleran.com/about-tom|title=Meet Tom - Tom O'Halleran for Congress |website=www.tomohalleran.com|accessdate=2016-07-30}}</ref>\n\n==Arizona Legislature==\nO'Halleran, then a [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]], served in the [[Arizona House of Representatives]] from 2001 to 2006. He then served in the [[Arizona Senate|Arizona State Senate]], representing the 1st District from 2007 to 2009. In a 2008 primary election, he was unseated by [[Steve Pierce]].\n\nAfter leaving the Arizona State Legislature, O'Halleran hosted a radio show on [[KAZM]] in [[Sedona, Arizona|Sedona]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Bell|first1=David|title=Former state Rep. O'Halleran joins CD1 candidate field |url=http://www.eacourier.com/news/former-state-rep-o-halleran-joins-cd-candidate-field/article_f9153f5e-3af5-11e5-8da2-9b95e830e54c.html|accessdate=March 1, 2016|publisher=Eastern Arizona Courier|date=August 7, 2015}}</ref>\n\nIn 2014 he left the Republican Party, citing its policies on education, water, and child welfare as reasons for his resignation. He then ran for the 6th District State Senate seat as an Independent, losing by 3%.<ref>{{cite web|title=Tom O'Halleran|url=https://ballotpedia.org/Tom_O'Halleran|publisher=[[Ballotpedia]]|accessdate=March 1, 2016}}</ref>\n\n==U.S. House of Representatives==\n===2016 campaign===\n{{main|United States House of Representatives elections in Arizona, 2016#District 1}}\nOn August 6, 2015, O'Halleran announced his candidacy as a Democrat for [[Arizona's 1st congressional district]].<ref>{{cite news|title=O\u2019Halleran announces candidacy in 1st Congressional District|url=http://azcapitoltimes.com/news/2015/08/05/ohalleran-announces-candidacy-in-1st-congressional-district/|accessdate=1 March 2016|agency=Associated Press|publisher=Arizona Capitol Times|date=August 5, 2015}}</ref>\n\nHe explained his switch of party affiliation as a result of his positive attitude toward government, and, in particular, of his support for government regulations that would increase the use of wind and solar energy.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Sanders |first1=Rebekah L. |title=Tom O\u2019Halleran running for Congress as Democrat |url=https://www.azcentral.com/story/news/arizona/politics/2015/08/04/tom-ohalleran-running-congress-democrat/31087643/ |website=azCentral |accessdate=26 September 2018}}</ref>\n\nIn May 2016, O'Halleran was named to the [[Democratic Congressional Campaign Committee]]'s Red to Blue list, indicating that his race was a priority for the group.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://dccc.org/red-blue/|title=Red to Blue - DCCC|language=en-US|access-date=2016-07-30}}</ref>\n\nOn August 30, 2016, O'Halleran beat Miguel Olivas in the Democratic primary.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Reagan|first1=Kevin|title=Bennett joins Babeu in pledging to term limits|url=http://www.trivalleycentral.com/arizona_city_independent/news/bennett-joins-babeu-in-pledging-to-term-limits/article_529226b2-58d9-11e6-80e8-bf1ef634e93e.html|accessdate=9 August 2016|publisher=Arizona City Independent|date=August 3, 2016}}</ref> He faced Republican [[Paul Babeu]] and Green Party candidate Ray Parrish<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://2016.azvoterguide.com/candidates/2016/federal/us-house-of-representatives/ray-parrish/|title=Ray Parrish {{!}} azvoterguide.com|website=2016.azvoterguide.com|access-date=2016-09-24}}</ref> in the general election on November 8, 2016.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://kjzz.org/content/358303/its-republican-paul-babeu-and-democrat-tom-ohalleran-monstrous-cd-1-race|title=It's Republican Paul Babeu And Democrat Tom O'Halleran In Monstrous CD-1 Race|date=31 August 2016|publisher=|accessdate=7 December 2016}}</ref> O'Halleran won, receiving 51% of the vote to Babeu's 44%.<ref>{{cite news|title=Democrat Tom O'Halleran defeats Pinal County Sheriff Paul Babeu in CD-1|url=http://www.abc15.com/news/state/democrat-tom-ohalleran-defeats-pinal-county-sheriff-paul-babeu-in-cd-1|accessdate=11 November 2016|publisher=ABC 15|date=November 8, 2016}}</ref>\n\nHe is a member of the [[Blue Dog Coalition]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Members|author=|url=https://bluedogcaucus-costa.house.gov/members|publisher=Blue dog coalition|accessdate=26 October 2017}}</ref>\n\n===2018 campaign===\nIn May 2017, Republican State Senator [[Steve Smith (Arizona politician)|Steve Smith]] of Maricopa announced that he was running for O'Halloran's seat.
