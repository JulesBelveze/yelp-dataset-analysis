She is the U.S. Representative for [[Oregon's 1st congressional district]] and was first elected in a [[Oregon's 1st congressional district special election, 2012|special election]] on January 31, 2012.  The district includes most of [[Portland, Oregon|Portland]] west of the [[Willamette River]], as well as all of [[Yamhill County, Oregon|Yamhill]], [[Columbia County, Oregon|Columbia]], [[Clatsop County, Oregon|Clatsop]], and [[Washington County, Oregon|Washington]] counties.\n\nBonamici previously represented District 17 in the [[Oregon State Senate]] from 2008 to 2011.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://blog.oregonlive.com/mapesonpolitics/2011/11/suzanne_bonamici_makes_legisla.html | work=[[The Oregonian]] | title=Suzanne Bonamici makes legislative resignation official | date=November 21, 2011}}</ref> She was first elected to the [[Oregon House of Representatives]] in 2006.\n\n==Early life, education, and law career==\nBonamici was born in [[Detroit]] and raised in a small Michigan town. Her father worked for a local bank and her mother was a small-business owner and piano teacher. She earned an A.A. from [[Lane Community College]], a B.A. in journalism from the [[University of Oregon]], and a J.D. from the [[University of Oregon School of Law]].\n\nDuring college, she was a legal assistant at [[Lane County, Oregon|Lane County]] [[Legal aid in the United States|Legal Aid]] in the city of [[Eugene, Oregon|Eugene]]. After getting her law degree, she became a [[Consumer protection laws|consumer protection attorney]] for the [[Federal Trade Commission]] in [[Washington, D.C.|the nation's capital]]. She went into private practice in [[Portland, Oregon|Portland]] and represented small businesses.<ref>http://www.bonamiciforcongress.com/about/</ref>\n\n==Oregon legislature==\n===Elections===\nIn 2006, incumbent Democratic State Representative [[Brad Avakian]] decided to retire to run for the [[Oregon Senate]]. She ran for the open seat in Oregon's 34th House District and defeated Republican Joan Draper 62%-36%.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=307673</ref>\n\nOn April 30, 2008 Bonamici was appointed by Commissioners from both [[Washington County, Oregon|Washington]] and [[Multnomah County, Oregon|Multnomah]] counties to represent Oregon's 17th Senate District. The seat became vacant when Avakian was appointed Commissioner of the [[Oregon Bureau of Labor and Industries]].<ref name=senate>{{cite news\n|title=Bonamici named to replace Avakian in state Senate\n|date=May 1, 2008\n|url=http://www.beavertonvalleytimes.com/news/story.php?story_id=120958893605414700S\n|work=Beaverton Valley Times\n}}</ref> She was sworn in on May 19, 2008. \n\nBonamici was unopposed [[Oregon legislative elections, 2008|in the November 2008 special election]] for the balance of Avakian's four-year term, and was elected with 97 percent of the vote.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=504691</ref> In 2010, she won re-election with 64% of the vote.<ref>http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=681595</ref>\n\n===Committee assignments===\nIn the 2009 Legislative Session, Bonamici served as the Chair of the Senate Consumer
