Previously, she was the Senior Vice-President for Outreach and Engagement at [[Ohio State University]]. A [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrat]], Beatty was a member of the [[Ohio House of Representatives]] from 1999 to 2008, representing the 27th House District; in the Ohio House of Representatives, she served for a time as [[Minority Leader]]. Her husband is [[Otto Beatty, Jr.]], who is also a former Ohio State Representative.\n\nIn 2012, she ran for the newly redrawn [[Ohio's 3rd congressional district]], based in the City of [[Columbus, Ohio|Columbus]], and won the Democratic primary by defeating former U.S. Congresswoman [[Mary Jo Kilroy]].<ref name=\"politico1\">[http://www.politico.com/2012-election/map/#/House/2012/Primary/OH 2012 Election Results Map by State - Live Voting Updates - POLITICO.com<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> Beatty went on to win the [[general election]] against Chris Long.<ref>{{cite web|title=Ex-Ohio Rep. Beatty wins new US House district|url=http://www.sfgate.com/news/article/Ex-Ohio-Rep-Beatty-wins-new-US-House-district-4015659.php|work=sfgate.com|accessdate=8 November 2012}}</ref>  [[File:Dayton-ohio-skyline.jpg|thumb|100px|alt=text|Dayton, Ohio]]\n\n==Early life, education, and early political career==\nBeatty has a [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] in speech from [[Central State University]], an [[Master of Science|M.S.]] in [[counseling psychology]] from [[Wright State University]], and has studied at the doctoral level at the [[University of Cincinnati]]. Beatty served as the Montgomery County Health and Human Services Director responsible for administering the county's health levy and area public nursing homes, including Stillwater Nursing Home. In 2003, she received an [[honorary doctorate]] from the [[Ohio Dominican University]]. Beatty served as a delegate for [[John Kerry]] on the [[Ohio]] delegation to the [[2004 Democratic National Convention]] in [[Boston]].\n\n==Ohio House of Representatives (1999-2009)==\n\n===Elections===\nIn 1999, longtime State Representative [[Otto Beatty Jr.]] of Ohio's 21st House District decided to resign early to begin an opportunity in the private sector.  His wife, Joyce Beatty, was appointed to his seat.  She won a full term in 2000 with 82% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=196394 |title=OH State House 21 Race - Nov 07, 2000 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2012-03-15}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.sos.state.oh.us/SOS/elections/electResultsMain/2000ElectionsResults/OHHouseReps11072000.aspx | title=2000 general election results | accessdate=September 13, 2011}}</ref> After redistricting, she decided to run in the newly redrawn Ohio's 27th House District and won re-election to a second term in 2002 with 82% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=49128 |title=OH State House 27 Race - Nov 05, 2002 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2012-03-15}}</ref> In 2004, she won re-election to a third term unopposed.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=71909 |title=OH State House 27 Race - Nov 02, 2004 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2012-03-15}}</ref> In 2006, she won re-election to a fourth term with 87% of the vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=304026 |title=OH State House 27 Race - Nov 07, 2006 |publisher=Our Campaigns |date= |accessdate=2012-03-15}}</ref> [[Term limits]] kept Beatty from seeking another term in 2008, but her leadership helped Democrats to obtain the majority in the [[128th Ohio General Assembly]].\n\n===Tenure===\nAfter [[Chris Redfern]] left to become chairman of the [[Ohio Democratic Party]], Beatty was named Minority Leader.  She served in that capacity for the entire [[Ohio 127th General Assembly]]. She was the first female Democratic House Leader in Ohio history.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://beattyforcongress.com/about |title=Beatty For Congress |publisher=Beatty For Congress |date=2009-03-25 |accessdate=2012-03-15}}</ref>\n\n===Committee assignments===\n\n==Ohio State University (2008-2012)==\nFollowing her time in the House, she became Senior Vice-President for Outreach and Engagement at [[Ohio State University]].\n\n==U.S. House of Representatives==\n\n===Elections===\n;2012\n{{See also|United States House of Representatives elections in Ohio, 2012#District 3}}\nOn March 6, 2012, Beatty defeated former Congresswoman [[Mary Jo Kilroy]], Columbus city councilwoman Priscilla Tyson, and state representative [[Ted Celeste]] 38%-35%-15%-12% to win the Ohio 3rd Congressional District Democratic Primary.<ref name=\"politico1\"/> Beatty received early support from the Ohio Legislative Black Caucus, [[List of mayors of Columbus, Ohio|Columbus Mayor]] [[Michael B. Coleman]], and various other Central Ohio political figures including Rep. [[Tracy Maxwell Heard]] and former Rep. [[W. Carlton Weddington]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://beattyforcongress.com/endorsements |title=Beatty For Congress |publisher=Beatty For Congress |date=2009-03-25 |accessdate=2012-03-15}}</ref>\n\n===Committee assignments===\n*'''[[United States House Committee on Financial Services|Committee on Financial Services]]'''\n**[[United States House Financial Services Subcommittee on Housing and Insurance|Subcommittee on Housing and Insurance]]\n**[[United States House Financial Services Subcommittee on Oversight and Investigations|Subcommittee on Oversight and Investigations]]\n\n==Personal life==\nBeatty is married to attorney and former State Representative [[Otto Beatty, Jr.]]  She has been a national spokesperson for the [[American Heart Association]]. She served on the [[American Heart Association|Columbus American Heart Association Board]], Ohio Democratic Committee, Women's Fund, [[NAACP]], and [[Delta Sigma Theta Sorority]]. In addition, she was a legislative chair of The Links and was a Chairwoman of the Columbus Urban League Board. She won the 2002 YWCA Woman of Achievement Award, The Ohio Health Speaking of Women Health Award, NAACP Freedom Award, Woman of Courage Award, and the Urban League Leadership Recognition Award.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ohioladiesgallery.org/PublicOfficial.aspx?personId=83540 |title=Ohio Ladies Gallery |publisher=Ohio Ladies Gallery |date= |accessdate=2012-03-15}}</ref>\n\n==Pension==\nOn June 21, 2013,  the [[National Journal]] published an article, \"Nearly One in Five Members of Congress Gets Paid Twice\".
